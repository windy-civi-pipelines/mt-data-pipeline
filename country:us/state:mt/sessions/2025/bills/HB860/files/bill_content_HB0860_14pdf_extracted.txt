7 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A VETERINARY TRAINING LOAN PROGRAM;
8 PROVIDING THAT THE LOAN PROGRAM PROVIDES LOANS TO VETERINARY STUDENTS WHO
9 COMMIT TO PRACTICING IN UNDERSERVED AREAS IN MONTANA; PROVIDING PROGRAM
10 REQUIREMENTS, INCLUDING A PREFERENCE FOR APPLICANTS WHO ARE MONTANA RESIDENTS;
11 PROVIDING FOR REPAYMENT OF THE LOAN IF THE PARTICIPANT DOES NOT FULFILL THE PROGRAM
12 REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING THAT A FORGIVEN LOAN IS NOT CONSIDERED INCOME FOR INCOME
13 TAX PURPOSES; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; ESTABLISHING
14 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS; AMENDING SECTION 15-30-2120, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
15 DATE.”

17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 10] may be cited as the "Rural Montana
20 Veterinary Workforce Act".
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purpose of [sections 1 through 10] is to establish a
23 veterinary training program for underserved areas in rural Montana. The program provides incentives for
24 students pursuing a doctor of veterinary medicine program to locate their veterinary practice in rural Montana
25 communities and to meet the needs of those owning animals in underserved areas.
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 10], unless the context
28 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:

18 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Veterinary training loan program -- eligibility. (1) There is a veterinary
19 training loan program as provided in [sections 1 through 10] for students enrolled in a veterinary medicine
20 degree program who commit to practice in an underserved area. The commissioner shall administer the loan
21 program.
22 (2) To qualify for the loan program, a student must:
23 (a) be enrolled in an accredited college of veterinary medicine;
24 (b) have successfully fulfilled the course requirements for at least one term of instruction and be in
25 good standing at an accredited college of veterinary medicine; and
26 (c) certify at the time of application that the student has not defaulted on any education loans.
27 (3) Preference must be given to students who are Montana residents. It is the intent of the
28 legislature that the commissioner apply the board of regents' residency policy to determine if an applicant is a

3 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Veterinary training loan program -- limits -- payment required. (1)
4 The commissioner shall select, in consultation with the advisory committee provided for in [section 9], not more
5 than four new students to participate in the loan program each year. Once enrolled, a participant may receive a
6 loan for each year that the participant continues to meet the requirements of the loan program.
7 (2) Each participant may receive a total loan not to exceed $100,000 as follows:
8 (a) a maximum of $25,000 each year for a student enrolled in a 4-year degree program
9 culminating in a doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent degree; or
10 (b) a maximum of $33,000 each year for a student enrolled in a 3-year degree program
11 culminating in a doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent degree.
12 (3) A participant shall request a loan payment following the completion of each academic year and
13 provide an academic progress report documenting the participant's good standing and progression. The
14 commissioner shall pay the loan upon the request of the participant and a showing of good standing and
15 progression.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Program agreement. (1) Each student selected as a program participant
18 shall enter into a program agreement with the commissioner that includes a commitment to:
19 (a) complete all curriculum requirements to obtain a doctor of veterinary medicine degree or its
20 equivalent;
21 (b) obtain and maintain the license necessary to practice veterinary medicine in Montana;
22 (c) engage in full-time practice of veterinary medicine in an underserved area for 4 years in
23 Montana; and
24 (d) begin the full-time practice of veterinary medicine in an underserved area within 180 days after
25 completion of:
26 (i) the doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent program degree; or
27 (ii) a postdegree training program, such as an internship, a residency, or additional graduate
28 studies.

1 (2) If a participant fails to complete the degree requirements to earn a doctor of veterinary
2 medicine degree, the commissioner shall secure recovery of loan payments made to the participant plus
3 interest, late fees, and collection costs. The program agreement must outline the repayment requirements if the
4 participant fails to meet the requirements of the program agreement. The repayment requirements must provide
5 for payment of the loan within 10 years from the date of entering into the program agreement.
6 (3) The rate of interest on a loan may not exceed 8% annually.
7 (4) A program agreement may specify a period of postponement of the program agreement or
8 criteria for postponing the program agreement.

10 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Loan forgiveness and repayment. (1) For each period of 12
11 consecutive months that the participant practices veterinary medicine full time in an underserved area, a
12 participant must receive loan forgiveness equal to one-quarter of the participant's total loan amount.
13 (2) Except as provided in subsections (3) through (5), if a participant fails to meet the requirements
14 of the program agreement provided for in [section 6], the participant shall repay, within 10 years from the date
15 of entering into the program agreement, the amount of the loan not forgiven pursuant to subsection (1) plus
16 interest, late fees, and costs associated with collection efforts. The rate of interest on a loan may not exceed
17 8% annually. The amount due must be prorated based on the amount of time the participant practiced
18 veterinary medicine full time in an underserved area.
19 (3) If a location is no longer considered an underserved area after a participant entered into the
20 program agreement, a participant who continues to practice in the former underserved area is eligible for loan
21 forgiveness if the other requirements of the program agreement are met.
22 (4) The requirements of a program agreement must be postponed at the request of the participant
23 during:
24 (a) a period of temporary medical disability during which the participant is unable to practice
25 veterinary medicine; or
26 (b) a period of postponement agreed to or determined in accordance with criteria included in the
27 program agreement.
28 (5) The repayment provisions of subsection (2) do not apply and a loan must be forgiven if:

2 (b) the participant dies.

5 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Administration of veterinary training loan program. The
6 commissioner shall:
7 (1) establish procedures for initially determining and periodically updating underserved areas;
8 (2) establish application and selection processes and timelines;
9 (3) to the extent possible seek to replicate relevant processes used to administer the Montana
10 rural physician incentive program provided for in 20-26-1503;
11 (4) adopt policies and procedures for the administration of the veterinary training loan program
12 consistent with [sections 1 through 10]; and
13 (5) select students for the loan program in consultation with the advisory committee provided for in
14 [section 9] by giving preference to applicants who are Montana residents and who agree to practice in an
15 underserved area and by considering the following:
16 (a) an applicant's ability and willingness to practice in an underserved area; and
17 (b) an applicant's date of availability to begin practicing in an underserved area.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Advisory committee. (1) There is a veterinary training loan program
20 advisory committee attached to the office of the commissioner of higher education for administrative purposes
21 only as described in 2-15-121. The purpose of the advisory committee is to provide input to the commissioner
22 on the veterinary training loan program provided for in [sections 1 through 10].
23 (2) The advisory committee is composed of the following members:
24 (a) a representative of the board of livestock appointed by the presiding officer of the board of
25 livestock;
26 (b) a representative from the Montana state university department of animal and range sciences
27 appointed by the head of the department;
28 (c) a representative from a school of veterinary medicine in Montana appointed by the governor;

2 (e) a representative of the Montana department of labor and industry with a background in
3 workforce development in rural areas appointed by the director of the Montana department of labor and
4 industry;
5 (f) a veterinarian licensed and practicing in Montana appointed by governor; and
6 (g) a member of the public appointed by the governor.

14 (a) advise the commissioner on matters related to the loan program;
15 (b) provide input to the commissioner regarding underserved areas in Montana; and
16 (c) make recommendations to the commissioner on the selection of students for the loan program.

18 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Report to legislature. The commissioner shall report biennially, in
19 accordance with 5-11-210, to the economic affairs interim committee and the education interim committee on
20 the veterinary training loan program. The report must include the following information:
21 (1) the selection, progress, and retention of students and practicing participants;
22 (2) changes to the areas qualifying as underserved areas; and
23 (3) how the level of veterinary services is affected by the loan program.
