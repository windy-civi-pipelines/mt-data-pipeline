6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A VETERINARY TRAINING LOAN PROGRAM;
7 PROVIDING THAT THE LOAN PROGRAM PROVIDES LOANS TO VETERINARY STUDENTS WHO
8 COMMIT TO PRACTICING IN UNDERSERVED AREAS IN MONTANA; PROVIDING PROGRAM
9 REQUIREMENTS, INCLUDING A PREFERENCE FOR APPLICANTS WHO ARE MONTANA RESIDENTS;
10 PROVIDING FOR REPAYMENT OF THE LOAN IF THE PARTICIPANT DOES NOT FULFILL THE PROGRAM
11 REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING THAT A FORGIVEN LOAN IS NOT CONSIDERED INCOME FOR INCOME
12 TAX PURPOSES; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; ESTABLISHING
13 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS; AMENDING SECTION 15-30-2120, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
14 DATE.”
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 10] may be cited as the "Rural Montana
19 Veterinary Workforce Act".
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purpose of [sections 1 through 10] is to establish a
22 veterinary training program for underserved areas in rural Montana. The program provides incentives for
23 students pursuing a doctor of veterinary medicine program to locate their veterinary practice in rural Montana
24 communities and to meet the needs of those owning animals in underserved areas.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 10], unless the context
27 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
28 (1) "Accredited college of veterinary medicine" means a veterinary medicine program at an

17 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Veterinary training loan program -- eligibility. (1) There is a veterinary
18 training loan program as provided in [sections 1 through 10] for students enrolled in a veterinary medicine
19 degree program who commit to practice in an underserved area. The commissioner shall administer the loan
20 program.
21 (2) To qualify for the loan program, a student must:
22 (a) be enrolled in an accredited college of veterinary medicine;
23 (b) have successfully fulfilled the course requirements for at least one term of instruction and be in
24 good standing at an accredited college of veterinary medicine; and
25 (c) certify at the time of application that the student has not defaulted on any education loans.
26 (3) Preference must be given to students who are Montana residents. It is the intent of the
27 legislature that the commissioner apply the board of regents' residency policy to determine if an applicant is a
28 Montana resident.

3 The commissioner shall select, in consultation with the advisory committee provided for in [section 9], not more
4 than four new students to participate in the loan program each year. Once enrolled, a participant may receive a
5 loan for each year that the participant continues to meet the requirements of the loan program.
6 (2) Each participant may receive a total loan not to exceed $100,000 as follows:
7 (a) a maximum of $25,000 each year for a student enrolled in a 4-year degree program
8 culminating in a doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent degree; or
9 (b) a maximum of $33,000 each year for a student enrolled in a 3-year degree program
10 culminating in a doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent degree.
11 (3) A participant shall request a loan payment following the completion of each academic year and
12 provide an academic progress report documenting the participant's good standing and progression. The
13 commissioner shall pay the loan upon the request of the participant and a showing of good standing and
14 progression.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Program agreement. (1) Each student selected as a program participant
17 shall enter into a program agreement with the commissioner that includes a commitment to:
18 (a) complete all curriculum requirements to obtain a doctor of veterinary medicine degree or its
19 equivalent;
20 (b) obtain and maintain the license necessary to practice veterinary medicine in Montana;
21 (c) engage in full-time practice of veterinary medicine in an underserved area for 4 years in
22 Montana; and
23 (d) begin the full-time practice of veterinary medicine in an underserved area within 180 days after
24 completion of:
25 (i) the doctor of veterinary medicine or equivalent program degree; or
26 (ii) a postdegree training program, such as an internship, a residency, or additional graduate
27 studies.
28 (2) If a participant fails to complete the degree requirements to earn a doctor of veterinary

9 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Loan forgiveness and repayment. (1) For each period of 12
10 consecutive months that the participant practices veterinary medicine full time in an underserved area, a
11 participant must receive loan forgiveness equal to one-quarter of the participant's total loan amount.
12 (2) Except as provided in subsections (3) through (5), if a participant fails to meet the requirements

15 interest, late fees, and costs associated with collection efforts. The rate of interest on a loan may not exceed
16 8% annually. The amount due must be prorated based on the amount of time the participant practiced
17 veterinary medicine full time in an underserved area.
18 (3) If a location is no longer considered an underserved area after a participant entered into the
19 program agreement, a participant who continues to practice in the former underserved area is eligible for loan

22 during:

24 veterinary medicine; or

26 program agreement.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Administration of veterinary training loan program. The
5 commissioner shall:
6 (1) establish procedures for initially determining and periodically updating underserved areas;
7 (2) establish application and selection processes and timelines;
8 (3) to the extent possible seek to replicate relevant processes used to administer the Montana
9 rural physician incentive program provided for in 20-26-1503;
10 (4) adopt policies and procedures for the administration of the veterinary training loan program
11 consistent with [sections 1 through 10]; and
12 (5) select students for the loan program in consultation with the advisory committee provided for in
13 [section 9] by giving preference to applicants who are Montana residents and who agree to practice in an
14 underserved area and by considering the following:
15 (a) an applicant's ability and willingness to practice in an underserved area; and
16 (b) an applicant's date of availability to begin practicing in an underserved area.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Advisory committee. (1) There is a veterinary training loan program
19 advisory committee attached to the office of the commissioner of higher education for administrative purposes
20 only as described in 2-15-121. The purpose of the advisory committee is to provide input to the commissioner
21 on the veterinary training loan program provided for in [sections 1 through 10].
22 (2) The advisory committee is composed of the following members:
23 (a) a representative of the board of livestock appointed by the presiding officer of the board of
24 livestock;
25 (b) a representative from the Montana state university department of animal and range sciences
26 appointed by the head of the department;
27 (c) a representative from a school of veterinary medicine in Montana appointed by the governor;
28 (d) a representative from the livestock industry appointed by the governor;

20 (1) the selection, progress, and retention of students and practicing participants;
21 (2) changes to the areas qualifying as underserved areas; and
22 (3) how the level of veterinary services is affected by the loan program.

17 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Veterinary training loan program -- eligibility. (1) There is a veterinary
18 training loan program as provided in [sections 1 through 10] for students enrolled in a veterinary medicine
19 degree program who commit to practice in an underserved area. The commissioner shall administer the loan
20 program.
21 (2) To qualify for the loan program, a student must:
22 (a) be enrolled in an accredited college of veterinary medicine;
23 (b) have successfully fulfilled the course requirements for at least one term of instruction and be in
24 good standing at an accredited college of veterinary medicine; and
25 (c) certify at the time of application that the student has not defaulted on any education loans.
26 (3) Preference must be given to students who are Montana residents. It is the intent of the
27 legislature that the commissioner apply the board of regents' residency policy to determine if an applicant is a
28 Montana resident.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Administration of veterinary training loan program. The
