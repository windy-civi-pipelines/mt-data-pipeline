12 Section 2. Section 17-7-304, MCA, is amended to read:
13
14 "17-7-304. Disposal of unexpended appropriations. (1) All money appropriated for any specific
15 purpose except that appropriated for the university system units listed in subsection (2) and except as provided
16 in subsection (4) must, after the expiration of the time for which appropriated, revert to the several funds and
17 accounts from which originally appropriated. However, any unexpended balance in any specific appropriation
18 may be used for the years for which the appropriation was made or may be used to fund the provisions of 2-18-
19 1203 through 2-18-1205 and 19-2-706 in the succeeding year.
20
21 (2) Except as provided in 17-2-108 and subsection (3) of this section, all money appropriated for
22 the university of Montana campuses at Missoula, Butte, Dillon, and Helena and the Montana state university
23 campuses at Bozeman, Billings, Havre, and Great Falls, the agricultural experiment station with central offices
24 at Bozeman, the forest and conservation experiment station with central offices at Missoula, the cooperative
25 extension service with central offices at Bozeman, and the bureau of mines and geology with central offices in
26 Butte must, after the expiration of the time for which appropriated, revert to an account held by the board of
27 regents. The board of regents is authorized to maintain a fund balance and to use the funds held in this account
28 in accordance with a long-term plan for major and deferred maintenance expenditures and equipment or fixed
- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 944

1 assets purchases prepared by the affected university system units and approved by the board of regents. The
2 affected university system units may, with the approval of the board of regents, modify the long-term plan at any
3 time to address changing needs and priorities. The board of regents shall communicate the plan to each
4 legislature, to the finance committee when requested by the committee, and to the office of budget and program
5 planning.
6
7 (3) Subsection (2) does not apply to reversions that are the result of a reduction in spending
8 directed by the governor pursuant to 17-7-140 .. Any amount that is a result of a reduction in spending directed
9 by the governor must revert to the fund or account from which it was originally appropriated.
10
11 (4) (a) Subject to subsection (4)(b) and except as provided in subsection (4)(c ), after the end of a
12 fiscal year, 30% of the money appropriated to an agency for that year by the general appropriations act for
13 personal services, operating expenses, and equipment, by fund type, and remaining unexpended and
14 unencumbered at the end of the year may be reappropriated to be spent during the following 2 years for any
15 purpose, except for increases in pay, that is consistent with the goals and objectives of the agency. The dollar
16 amount of the 30% amount that may be carried forward and spent must be determined by the office of budget
17 and program planning.
18
19 (b) (i) Any portion of the 30% of the unexpended and unencumbered money referred to in
20 subsection (4)(a) that was appropriated to a legislative branch entity may be deposited in the account
21 established in 5-11-407 ..
22
23 (ii) After the end of a biennium, any portion of the unexpended and unencumbered money
24 appropriated for the operation of the preceding legislature in a separate appropriation act may be deposited in
25 the account established in 5-11-407 .. The approving authority shall determine the portion of the unexpended
26 and unencumbered money that is deposited in the account.
27
28 (iii) Any portion of the 30% of the unexpended and unencumbered money referred to in subsection
- 5 - Authorized Print Version – HB 944
- 2025

1 (4)(a) that was appropriated to the legislative audit division may be deposited in the account established in 5-
2 13-404 ..
3
4 (c) For each year of the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, all unexpended and unencumbered
5 funds for contracted services for the department of military affairs must revert to the general fund at the end of
6 that fiscal year.
7 (5) When the carryforward appropriation authority is established on the accounting system, and
8 prior to spending funds pursuant to subsection (4), an agency must report to the approving authority how those
9 funds will be spent in the following 2 years."
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Referrals to legislative auditor. The general government budget
12 committee may make referrals and recommendations to the legislative auditor.
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Termination. (1) [Section 1] terminates September 15, 2026.
17 (2) [Section 2] terminates June 30, 2027.
