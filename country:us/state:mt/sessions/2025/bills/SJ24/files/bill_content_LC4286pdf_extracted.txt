4 A JOINT RESOLUTION OF THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF
5 MONTANA SUPPORTING HAVING THE STATE JOIN THE NATIONAL POPULAR VOTE INITIATIVE.
6
7 WHEREAS, the first proposal to change the electoral college system by constitutional amendment was
8 introduced in 1800, and since then more than 700 proposals to reform or eliminate the electoral college have
9 been introduced in Congress; and
10 WHEREAS, the electoral college does not provide for direct democratic elections; and
11 WHEREAS, the electoral college results in presidential elections predict most state election outcomes
12 before each election and direct the overwhelming majority of campaign resources and attention to the voters of
13 a few states; and
14 WHEREAS, the electoral college makes it possible for candidates to win an electoral college majority
15 and the presidency while gaining fewer votes nationwide than their principal opponents in the popular election;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, under the current system, 5 of the nation's 47 presidents won the election without winning
18 the popular vote; and
19 WHEREAS, the National Popular Vote Initiative was founded in 2006, promoting an interstate compact
20 that would establish direct popular election of the President and Vice President without a constitutional
21 amendment while retaining the structure of the electoral college system; and
22 WHEREAS, the National Popular Vote would guarantee that the presidential candidates who win the
23 most popular votes nationwide always win the presidency; and
24 WHEREAS, the National Popular Vote would encourage candidates to extend their focus beyond
25 winning battleground states and campaign more widely and devote greater attention to issues of concern in all
26 parts of the country; and
27 WHEREAS, the National Popular Vote would ensure that every voter, in every state, has an equal vote
28 in every presidential election; and
- 1 - LC 4286
69th Legislature 2025 LC 4286
1 WHEREAS, as of 2024, 17 states and the District of Columbia have passed the National Popular Vote
2 law, representing 209 of the 270 electoral votes needed to make the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact
3 take effect; and
4 WHEREAS, the National Popular Vote does not require an amendment to Montana's Constitution or
5 the United States Constitution but does require the state to join the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact;
6 and
7 WHEREAS, Article II, section 1, clause 2, of the United States Constitution gives each state legislature
8 the exclusive power to decide how its electoral votes are awarded.
9
10 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF
11 THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12 That the Legislature recommends that the State of Montana join the National Popular Vote Interstate
13 Compact to elect the President and Vice President by national popular vote for all future presidential elections.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Secretary of State send a copy of this resolution to each
15 member of the Montana Congressional Delegation and to each federally recognized tribal government in
16 Montana.
17 - END -
