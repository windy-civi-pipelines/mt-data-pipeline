4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO FUNDING FOR
5 CONSERVATION; REVISING THE ALLOCATION OF THE MARIJUANA STATE SPECIAL REVENUE
6 ACCOUNT; ESTABLISHING A HABITAT LEGACY ACCOUNT; ESTABLISHING STATE SPECIAL REVENUE
7 ACCOUNTS FOR WILDLIFE HABITAT CONSERVATION, WILDLIFE IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS, AND
8 WILDLIFE CROSSINGS; REVISING MEMBERSHIP FOR THE WILDLIFE HABITAT IMPROVEMENT
9 ADVISORY COUNCIL; REVISING THE KELLY FLYNN MONTANA WILDLIFE HABITAT IMPROVEMENT
10 ACT; REVISING RANKING CRITERIA FOR WILDLIFE HABITAT IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS; REVISING
11 RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AMENDING SECTIONS 16-12-111, 87-5-
12 801, 87-5-802, 87-5-803, 87-5-804, 87-5-806, AND 87-5-808, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Habitat legacy account. (1) There is a habitat legacy account in the
17 state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102. The account is administered by the department of fish,
18 wildlife, and parks. All funds received pursuant to 16-12-111(4)(b) must be deposited in the account.
19 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), at the end of each fiscal year the state treasurer shall
20 make the following transfers from this account:
21 (a) 75% to the land and wildlife stewardship account established in [section 2] to be used solely,
22 together with the interest and income from the money, as funding to secure wildlife habitat pursuant to 87-1-
23 209;
24 (b) 20% to the account established in [section 3]; and
25 (c) 5% to the account established in [section 4].
26 (3) If the account established in [section 2] has a balance of unobligated and unexpended funds
27 that exceeds $50 million, then the transfers provided for in subsection (2) may not be made, and the

4 Section 5. Section 16-12-111, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "16-12-111. Marijuana state special revenue account -- operating reserve -- transfer of excess
6 funds. (1) There is a dedicated marijuana state special revenue account within the state special revenue fund
7 established in 17-2-102, to be administered by the department.
8 (2) The account consists of:
9 (a) money deposited into the account pursuant to this chapter;
10 (b) the taxes collected pursuant to Title 15, chapter 64, part 1;
11 (c) license and registered cardholder fees deposited into the account pursuant to this chapter;
12 (d) taxes deposited into the account pursuant to 16-12-310; and
13 (e) civil penalties collected under this chapter.
14 (3) Except as provided in subsection (4), money in the account must be used by the department
15 for the purpose of administering the provisions of this chapter.
16 (4) At the end of each fiscal year, the department shall transfer funds in excess of a 3-month
17 operating reserve necessary to fund operating costs at the beginning of the next fiscal year in the following
18 order:
19 (a) an amount not to exceed $6 million 11% must be transferred to the healing and ending
20 addiction through recovery and treatment (HEART) account established in 16-12-122;
21 (b) the net balance remaining after distribution to the HEART account must be distributed as
22 follows:
23 (i) 20% to the credit of the department of fish, wildlife, and parks to be used solely as funding for
24 wildlife habitat in the same manner as funding generated under 87-1-242 (3) and used pursuant to 87-1-209
25 habitat legacy account provided for in [section 1];
26 (ii) 4% to the state park account established in 23-1-105(1);
27 (iii) 4% to the trails and recreational facilities account established in 23-2-108;

24 (4) "Habitat" means the natural environment where a species lives or thrives.
