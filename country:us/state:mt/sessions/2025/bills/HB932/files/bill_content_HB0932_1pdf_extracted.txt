16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Habitat legacy account. (1) There is a habitat legacy account in the
17 state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102. The account is administered by the department of fish,
18 wildlife, and parks. All funds received pursuant to 16-12-111(4)(b) must be deposited in the account.
19 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), at the end of each fiscal year the state treasurer shall
20 make the following transfers from this account:
21 (a) 75% to the land and wildlife stewardship account established in [section 2] to be used solely,
22 together with the interest and income from the money, as funding to secure wildlife habitat pursuant to 87-1-
23 209;
24 (b) 20% to the account established in [section 3]; and
25 (c) 5% to the account established in [section 4].
26 (3) If the account established in [section 2] has a balance of unobligated and unexpended funds
27 that exceeds $50 million, then the transfers provided for in subsection (2) may not be made, and the
28 department shall instead make the following transfers:

8 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Land and wildlife stewardship state special revenue account. There
9 is a land and wildlife stewardship account in the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102 that is
10 administered by the department of fish, wildlife, and parks for the benefit of the department of fish, wildlife, and
11 parks. All funds received pursuant to [section 1] must be deposited in the account.
12 (2) Funds in the account may be used, together with the interest and income from the money, only
13 as funding to secure wildlife habitat pursuant to 87-1-209.
14 (3) The account retains its own interest.
15 (4) Any unspent or unencumbered money in the account at the end of a fiscal year must remain in
16 the account.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Wildlife habitat improvement project state special revenue account.
19 There is a wildlife habitat improvement project account in the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-
20 102 that is administered by the department of fish, wildlife, and parks for the benefit of the department of fish,
21 wildlife, and parks. All funds received pursuant to [section 1] must be deposited in the account.
22 (2) Funds in the account may be used only as funding for wildlife improvement projects in the
23 same manner as funding under 87-5-806.
24 (3) The account retains its own interest.
25 (4) Any unspent or unencumbered money in the account at the end of a fiscal year must remain in
26 the account.

1 account -- purpose -- funds invested. (1) There is a big game and wildlife highway crossings and

13 Section 7. Section 87-5-802, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "87-5-802. Definitions. As used in this part, unless the context indicates otherwise, the following
15 definitions apply:
16 (1) "Commission" means the fish and wildlife commission established in 2-15-3402.
17 (2) "Council" means the wildlife habitat improvement advisory council established in 87-5-803.
18 (2)(3) "Department" means the department of fish, wildlife, and parks established in 2-15-3401.
19 (3) "Native plant" means a plant indigenous to the state of Montana.
20 (4) "Habitat" means the natural environment where a species lives or thrives.
21 (4)(5) "Noxious weed" means a weed has the same meaning as provided defined in 7-22-2101.
22 (5) "Noxious weed management" means an integrated weed management program for the
23 containment, suppression, and, where possible, eradication of noxious weeds. The term includes a long-term
24 management approach toward noxious weeds that includes but is not limited to hand-pulling, cultivation,
25 herbicide application, mechanical or biological control, prescribed grazing or burning, prevention, and
26 revegetation."
