10 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CANDIDATE FILING LAWS; REQUIRING
11 CANDIDATES FOR LOCAL AND STATE OFFICE TO FILE FOR OFFICE PRIOR TO THE PRIMARY
12 ELECTION; PROHIBITING UNSUCCESSFUL PRIMARY CANDIDATES FROM SEEKING NOMINATION IN
13 THE GENERAL ELECTION; INCREASING THE SIGNATURE PERCENTAGE REQUIRED FOR A THIRD-
14 PARTY OR INDEPENDENT CANDIDATE PETITION; AMENDING SECTIONS 13-1-403, 13-1-502, 13-10-201,
15 13-10-211, 13-10-501, 13-10-502, AND 13-10-503, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE
16 DATE.”
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
19
20 Section 1. Section 13-1-403, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "13-1-403. Election deadlines for candidate filing, write-in candidacy, and withdrawal -- election
22 cancellation -- election by acclamation. (1) Consistent with the candidate filing deadline in 13-10-201(7) for
23 primary elections and except as provided in subsection (2) for a write-in candidate, the candidate filing deadline
24 for election to a local government office is no sooner than 145 105 days and no later than 85 90 days before the
25 election.
26 (2) (a) A declaration of intent to be a write-in candidate must be filed with the election administrator
27 by 5 p.m. on the 65th 90th day before the date of the election.

1 (d) by electronic mail.
2 (5)(6) A declaration is not valid until the filing fee required pursuant to 13-10-202 is received by the
3 secretary of state or the election administrator.
4 (6)(7) A write-in candidate who files a declaration of intent for a general election may not file with a
5 partisan, nonpartisan, or independent designation."
6
7 Section 5. Section 13-10-501, MCA, is amended to read:
8 "13-10-501. Petition for nomination by independent candidates or political parties not eligible
9 to participate in primary election. (1) Except as provided in 13-10-504, nominations for public office by an
10 independent candidate or a political party that does not meet the requirements of 13-10-601 may be made by
11 filing a declaration of intent and paying the filing fee in accordance with 13-10-201 and by circulating a petition
12 for nomination.
13 (2) The petition must contain the same information and the oath of the candidate required for a
14 declaration for nomination.
15 (3) If a petition is filed by a political party, it must contain the party name and, in five words or less,
16 the principle that the body represents.
17 (4) The form of the petition must be prescribed by the secretary of state, and the secretary of state
18 shall furnish sample copies to the election administrators and on request to any individual.
19 (5) Each sheet of a petition must contain signatures of electors residing in only one county.
20 (6) An unsuccessful candidate for office at a primary election may not seek nomination by write-in
21 vote or petition for the same office at the general election."
22
23 Section 6. Section 13-10-502, MCA, is amended to read:
24 "13-10-502. Signature requirements for petition. (1) The petition for nomination must be signed by
25 active electors as defined in 13-1-101 residing within the state and district or political subdivision in which the
26 officer or officers are to be elected. Each signature line must contain spaces for the signature, post-office
27 address, and printed last name of the signer.

6 (3) If the office sought is a new office or the boundaries of the district or political subdivision in
7 which the election is to be held have changed since the last election for the office, the officer with whom
8 nominations for the office sought are filed shall determine the number of signatures required for a petition of
9 nomination for that office. The number of signatures may not be less than is required in subsection (2)."
10
11 Section 7. Section 13-10-503, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "13-10-503. Filing deadlines. (1) (a) A petition for nomination, and the affidavits of circulation
13 required by 13-27-302, and the required filing fee must be filed with the same officer with whom other
14 nominations for the office sought are filed. Petitions must be submitted, at least 1 week before the deadline for
15 filing, to the election administrator in the county where the signer resides for verification and certification by the
16 procedures provided in 13-27-303 through 13-27-306.
17 (b) If sufficient signatures are verified and certified pursuant to 13-10-502, the county election
18 administrator shall file the petition for nomination with the same officer with whom other nominations for the
19 office sought are filed.
20 (c) If the petition for nomination is not certified, the county election administrator shall refund the
21 filing fee paid in accordance with 13-10-201.
22 (2) Except as provided in 13-10-504, each petition for nomination and the required filing fee must
23 be filed before the scheduled primary election or the filing deadline for the special or general election if a
24 primary election is not scheduled."
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
