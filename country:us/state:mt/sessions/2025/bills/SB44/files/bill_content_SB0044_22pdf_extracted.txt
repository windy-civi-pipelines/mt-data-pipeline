15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Separation of powers doctrine -- government powers generally --
16 limits. (1) The powers of the LEGISLATIVE BRANCH, THE executive branch, and THE judicial branch are limited to
17 those enumerated in the Montana constitution and granted by law.
18 (2) The legislature may not enact a law that is contrary to the constitution or exercise
19 executive branch powers provided for in [section 5] or judicial branch powers provided for in [section 6].
20 (3) The executive branch may not exercise legislative branch powers provided for in [sections 2
21 through 4] or judicial branch powers provided for in [section 6].
22 (4) The judicial branch may not:
23 (a) exercise legislative branch powers provided for in [sections 2 through 4];
24 (b) amend the effective date of an act of the legislature before the law becomes effective;
25 (c) declare a law unenforceable as applied toward anyone other than the parties involved
26 in a case or controversy; or
27 (d) exercise executive branch powers provided for in [section 5].
28 (5) The power of the board of regents is defined as provided in [section 7].

22 NEW SECTION.Section 7. Executive branch power of board of regents and board of public
23 education -- definitions. (1) The board of regents and the board of public education are agencies of the
24 executive branch of state government. As such, they are required to enforce, within their respective spheres,
25 laws adopted by the legislature or by the people.
26 (2) The oversight of the Montana University System entrusted by the state constitution to the board
27 of regents refers to the kind of oversight customarily exercised by executive branch officers over operations
28 assigned to them and does not extend to overriding state statutes. The board of regents' power regarding the
- 10 - Authorized Print Version – SB 44
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0044.2
1 government, control, supervision, coordination, and management of the university system and other public
2 educational institutions is assigned by law.
3 (3) The duties entrusted by the state constitution to the board of public education are limited to
4 executive branch functions and do not extend to overriding state statutes. The board of public education
5 exercises general supervision over the public school system and other public educational institutions as
6 assigned by law.
7 (4) The rights of the people protected in the United States and Montana constitutions extend fully
8 to geographical areas under the control of the board of regents and the board of public education. The boards
9 are obligated to respect and protect those rights and to implement laws that protect or enhance them.
10 (5) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
11 (a) "Board of public education" means the board of public education created in Article X, section
12 9(3), of the Montana constitution and 2-15-1507.
13 (b) "Board of regents" means the board of regents of higher education established by Article X,
14 section 9(2), of the Montana constitution and 2-15-1505.
15 (c) "Coordination" means the process of organizing people or groups so that they work together
16 properly and well.
17 (d) "General supervision" means general instructions are given and tasks are undertaken to
18 achieve required outcomes or objectives.
19 (e) "Government" means the authority to supervise, coordinate, manage, and control.
20 (f) "Manage" or "management" means an activity inclusive of control that is performed on a daily
21 basis.
22 (g) "Supervision" means regular monitoring of the administrative, clinical, or clerical work
23 performance of a staff member, trainee, student, volunteer, or employee on contract by a person with the
24 authority to give direction and require change.
