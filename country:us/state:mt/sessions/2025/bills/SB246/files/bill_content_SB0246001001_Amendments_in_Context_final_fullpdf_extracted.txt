1 procedures or by direct purchase. Montana-produced food products may be procured by direct purchase when:
2 (i) the quality of available Montana-produced food products is substantially equivalent to the
3 quality of similar food products produced outside the state;
4 (ii) a vendor is able to supply Montana-produced food products in sufficient quantity; and
5 (iii) a bid for Montana-produced food products either does not exceed or reasonably exceeds the
6 lowest bid or price quoted for similar food products produced outside the state. A bid reasonably exceeds the
7 lowest bid or price quoted when, in the discretion of the person charged by law with the duty to purchase food
8 products for a governmental body, the higher bid is reasonable and capable of being paid out of that
9 governmental body's existing budget without any further supplemental or additional appropriation.
10 (b) The department shall adopt any rules necessary to administer the optional procurement
11 exception established in this subsection (4).
12 (5) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
13 (a) "Food" means articles normally used by humans as food or drink, including articles used for
14 components of articles normally used by humans as food or drink.
15 (b) "Produced" means planted, cultivated, grown, harvested, raised, collected, processed, or
16 manufactured."
