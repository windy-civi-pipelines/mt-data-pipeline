4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO THE USE OF PARTY
5 AFFILIATION FOR CERTAIN NONPARTISAN JUDICIAL RACES; ALLOWING CANDIDATES FOR CHIEF
6 JUSTICE OF THE SUPREME COURT OR JUSTICE OF THE SUPREME COURT TO INDICATE A POLITICAL
7 PARTY AFFILIATION ON A DECLARATION FOR NOMINATION AND TO HAVE THAT AFFILIATION
8 APPEAR ON THE PRIMARY AND GENERAL BALLOTS; ALLOWING CANDIDATES FOR CHIEF JUSTICE
9 OF THE SUPREME COURT OR JUSTICE OF THE SUPREME COURT TO ANNOUNCE POLITICAL
10 AFFILIATIONS AND ENDORSEMENTS; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AMENDING SECTIONS 13-10-
11 209, 13-10-602, 13-12-203, 13-14-112, 13-14-115, AND 13-14-212, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
12 DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Indication of political party affiliation by candidate for chief justice
17 or supreme court justice. A candidate for the office of chief justice of the supreme court or the office of justice
18 of the supreme court may indicate the candidate's political party affiliation on the candidate's declaration for
19 nomination.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Announcement of political party affiliation or endorsement by
22 candidate for chief justice or supreme court justice. A candidate for the office of chief justice of the
23 supreme court or the office of justice of the supreme court may announce the candidate's political party
24 affiliation or a campaign endorsement that the candidate has received.
25
26 Section 3. Section 13-10-209, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "13-10-209. Arrangement and preparing of primary ballots. (1) (a) Ballots for a primary election
28 must be arranged and prepared in the same manner and number as provided in chapter 12 for general election

1 ballots, except that there must be separate ballots for each political party entitled to participate. The name of
2 the political party must appear at the top of the separate ballot for that party and need not appear with each
3 candidate's name.
4 (b) Nonpartisan offices and ballot issues may be prepared on separate ballots or may appear on
5 the same ballot as partisan offices if:
6 (i) each section is clearly identified as separate; and
7 (ii) the nonpartisan offices and ballot issues appear on each party's ballot.
8 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), an election administrator does not need to prepare a
9 primary ballot for a political party if:
10 (a) the party does not have candidates for more than half of the offices to appear on the ballot; and
11 (b) no more than one candidate files for nomination by that party for any of the offices to appear on
12 the ballot.

14 candidate files for a precinct committee office from a party that will not have a primary ballot prepared, that
15 party shall select the candidate to fill the office.

17 ballot for a political party is not prepared, the secretary of state shall certify that a primary election is
18 unnecessary for that party and shall instruct the election administrator to certify the names of the candidates for
19 that party for the general election ballot only.
20 (5) The separate ballots for each party must have the same appearance. Each set of party ballots
21 must bear the same number. If prepared as a separate ballot, the nonpartisan ballot may have a different
22 appearance than the party ballots but must be numbered in the same order as the party ballots.
23 (6) (a) Regardless of whether the election administrator has prepared separate ballots for
24 nonpartisan offices or has listed nonpartisan candidates on the same ballots as partisan candidates, if a
25 candidate for the office of chief justice of the supreme court or the office of justice of the supreme court
26 indicates the candidate's political party affiliation on a declaration for nomination, the candidate's name must
27 appear under the title of the office sought, with the name of the candidate's political party affiliation, if any, in no
28 more than three words appearing opposite or below the candidate's name.

9 Section 4. Section 13-10-602, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "13-10-602. Use of party name. (1) Except as provided in subsection (3), a political party and its
11 regularly nominated candidates, members, and officers have the sole and exclusive right to the use of the party
12 name. A Except as provided in [section 1], a candidate for office may not use any word of the name of any
13 other political party or organization other than that by which the candidate is nominated in a manner that
14 indicates or implies the individual is a candidate of the nonnominating party.
15 (2) An independent or nonpartisan candidate, except as provided in [section 1] or subsection (3) of
16 this section, may not use any word of the name of any existing political party or organization in the candidacy in
17 a manner that indicates or implies that the individual is a candidate of that party or organization.
18 (3) A candidate for an elective office under an elected county official government provided for in 7-
19 3-111 who is running in an election conducted after electors have approved a change pursuant to 7-3-103(4) in
20 the type of election held from partisan to nonpartisan may use a party name or symbol in the candidate's
21 campaign material."
22
23 Section 5. Section 13-12-203, MCA, is amended to read:
24 "13-12-203. Appearance of candidate's name and party designation on ballot. (1) Subject to 13-
25 12-202 and except as provided in 13-10-209 for nonpartisan offices and 13-10-303 for certain other candidates,
26 in partisan elections, candidates' names must appear under the title of the office sought, with the name of the
27 party in not more than three words appearing opposite or below the name.

2 designation appearing with the name unless partisan and nonpartisan offices appear on the same ballot. In
3 such a case, the names of nonpartisan candidates must appear with the word "Nonpartisan".
4 (b) In an election for chief justice of the supreme court or for justice of the supreme court, the
5 candidate's name must appear under the title of the office sought, with the name of the candidate's political
6 party affiliation, if any, in no more than three words appearing opposite or below the candidate's name. If a
7 candidate for chief justice of the supreme court or for justice of the supreme court did not indicate a political
8 party affiliation, the name of the candidate must appear with the word "Nonpartisan".
9 (3) Except as otherwise provided by this section, information about the candidate other than the
10 candidate's name may not appear on the ballot, including a title, accomplishment, award, or degree."

12 Section 6. Section 13-14-112, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "13-14-112. Declarations for nomination -- fee -- filing. (1) Nonpartisan candidates shall file
14 declarations for nomination as required by the primary election laws in a form prescribed by the secretary of
15 state except as provided in 13-14-113. Except for a candidate covered under 7-1-205, a candidate may not file
16 for more than one public office.
17 (2) (a) Declarations Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), a declaration may not indicate political
18 affiliation. The, and the candidate may not state in the declaration any principles or measures that the candidate
19 advocates or any slogans.
20 (b) A candidate for chief justice of the supreme court or for justice of the supreme court may
21 indicate the candidate's political party affiliation on the declaration. A candidate who does not indicate a political
22 party affiliation on the declaration may list "Nonpartisan".
23 (3) Each individual filing a declaration shall pay the fee prescribed by law for the office that the
24 individual seeks.
25 (4) Declarations must be filed:
26 (a) in the office of the secretary of state or the appropriate election administrator as provided in 13-
27 10-201; and
28 (b) within the filing period provided in 13-10-201(7) for the office that the individual seeks."

2 Section 7. Section 13-14-115, MCA, is amended to read:
3 "13-14-115. Preparation and distribution of nonpartisan primary ballots -- determination on
4 conducting primary. (1) The election administrators shall arrange, prepare, and distribute primary ballots for
5 nonpartisan offices, designated "nonpartisan primary ballots". The ballots must be arranged and prepared as
6 provided in 13-10-209 and be without political designation, except as provided in 13-10-209 and 13-12-203 for
7 a candidate for the office of chief justice of the supreme court or the office of justice of the supreme court.
8 (2) (a) Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), the election administrator of a political subdivision
9 may determine that a local nonpartisan portion of a primary election need not be held if:
10 (i) the number of candidates for an office exceeds three times the number to be elected to that
11 office in no more than one-half of the offices on the ballot; and
12 (ii) the number of candidates in excess of three times the number to be elected is not more than
13 one for any office on the ballot.
14 (b) The election administrator may determine that a primary election for a nonpartisan county office
15 need not be held if fewer than three candidates have filed for that office.
16 (c) If the election administrator determines that a primary election must be held pursuant to
17 subsection (2)(a) or (2)(b), the election administrator shall conduct the primary election only for the nonpartisan
18 offices that have a sufficient number of candidates that have filed to be elected to that office.
19 (d) If the election administrator determines that a primary election need not be held pursuant to
20 subsection (2)(a), (2)(b), or (2)(c) for a nonpartisan office, the administrator shall give notice to the governing
21 body that a primary election will not be held for that office.
22 (3) The governing body may require that a primary election be held for a nonpartisan office if it
23 passes a resolution not more than 10 days after the close of filing by candidates for election stating that a
24 primary election must be held for that office."
25
26 Section 8. Section 13-14-212, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "13-14-212. Form of ballot on retention of certain incumbent judicial officers. (1) If the
28 incumbent is the only candidate for the office of chief justice, supreme court justice, district court judge, or
- 5 - Authorized Print Version – ****
****

1 justice of the peace, the election administrator may not include a nonpartisan designation or write-in space for
2 the office on the general election ballot. The name of the incumbent must be placed on the official ballot for the
3 general election as follows:
4 Shall (insert title of officer) (insert name of the incumbent officer) of the (insert title of the court) of the
5 state of Montana be retained in office for another term?
6 (2) If the incumbent is the only candidate for the office of chief justice of the supreme court or the
7 office of justice of the supreme court, the election administrator may not include a nonpartisan or political party
8 designation or a write-in space for the office on the general election ballot. The name of the incumbent must be
9 placed on the official ballot for the general election as follows:
10 Shall (insert title of officer) (insert name of the incumbent officer) of the (insert title of the court) of the
11 state of Montana be retained in office for another term?
12 (2)(3) Following the question, provision must be made, subject to rules adopted pursuant to 13-12-
13 202, for a voter to indicate a "yes" or "no" vote."
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Appropriation. There is appropriated $5,000 from the general fund to
16 the secretary of state for the purposes of updating the secretary of state's website and performing computer
17 programming related to judicial elections.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Codification instruction. (1) [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
20 integral part of Title 13, chapter 14, part 2, and the provisions of Title 13, chapter 14, part 2, apply to [section 1].
21 (2) [Section 2] is intended to be codified as an integral part of Title 13, chapter 35, part 2, and the
22 provisions of Title 13, chapter 35, part 2, apply to [section 2].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Applicability. [This act] applies to primary and general elections for the
27 office of chief justice of the supreme court or the office of justice of the supreme court held on or after January
28 1, 2026.
