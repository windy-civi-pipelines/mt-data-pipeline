69th Legislature 2025 HB 359.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. ISALY, P. STRAND, E. TILLEMAN, E. MATTHEWS, M. ROMANO, M. THANE, J. LYNCH
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING WORKING RETIREE PROVISIONS UNDER THE
5 TEACHERS' RETIREMENT SYSTEM; ALLOWING RETIRED TEACHERS TO MENTOR NEW TEACHERS;
6 AMENDING SECTION 19-20-734, MCA; AMENDING SECTION 6, CHAPTER 135, LAWS OF 2023; AND
7 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 19-20-734, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "19-20-734. (Temporary) Break-in-service requirements. (1) Except as provided in 19-20-732 and
13 subsection (2) of this section, a retired member who first applies for retirement benefits or applies for resumed
14 or recalculated retirement benefits pursuant to 19-20-733:
15 (a) based on a date of termination of January 1, 2014, through December 31, 2023, may not be
16 employed in a position reportable to the retirement system pursuant to 19-20-731 until the employee has a
17 break in service of 150 calendar days commencing on the first day following the member's date of termination;
18 or
19 (b) based on a date of termination of January 1, 2024, or later, may not be employed in a position
20 reportable to the retirement system pursuant to 19-20-731 until the employee has a break in service of 120
21 calendar days commencing on the first day following the member's date of termination.
22 (2) A retired member may be employed by an employer during the break-in-service period only if:
23 (a) the retired member:
24 (i) is employed as a substitute classroom teacher to carry on the duties of a regular, licensed
25 teacher who is temporarily absent or is employed to mentor a newly hired teacher;
26 (ii) performs the service after attaining retired member status; and
27 (iii) performs the service for no more than 45 days during the break-in-service period; or
28 (b) the retired member continues employment in a position in which the retired member was

1 appropriately reported to the public employees' retirement system prior to and at the time of retirement with the
2 teachers' retirement system.
3 (3) If a retired member is employed in a position reportable to the retirement system in violation of
4 this section:
5 (a) the retired member must be returned to active member status with the retirement system
6 retroactive to the member's date of retirement or the date of resumption of retirement benefits, whichever is
7 later, and the member's retirement benefits must be terminated;
8 (b) the member shall repay all retirement benefits received in violation of this section, plus interest
9 at the actuarially assumed rate; and
10 (c) the member and the employer shall pay to the retirement system contributions on all earned
11 compensation paid to the member for service performed during the break-in-service period, plus interest at the
12 actuarially assumed rate.
13 (4) For purposes of this section, the term "employed in a position reportable to the retirement
14 system" includes any work performed or service provided by a retired member to or on behalf of an employer,
15 including but not limited to work performed or service provided through a professional employer arrangement,
16 an employee leasing arrangement, as a temporary service contractor, or as an independent contractor.
17 (Terminates June 30, 2027 2029--sec. 6, Ch. 135, L. 2023.)
18 19-20-734. (Effective July 1, 2027 2029) Break-in-service requirements. (1) Except as provided in
19 19-20-732 and subsection (2) of this section, a retired member who first applies for retirement benefits or

23 the member's date of termination.
24 (2) A retired member may be employed by an employer during the break-in-service period only if:
25 (a) the retired member:
26 (i) is employed as a substitute classroom teacher to carry on the duties of a regular, licensed
27 teacher who is temporarily absent;
28 (ii) performs the service after attaining retired member status; and
