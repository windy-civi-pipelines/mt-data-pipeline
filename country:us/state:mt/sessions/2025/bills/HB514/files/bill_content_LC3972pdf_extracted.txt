4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO VIOLATING
5 PRIVACY IN COMMUNICATIONS; EXPANDING OFFENSES TO INCLUDE OBTAINING MONEY OR
6 VALUABLES FROM A PERSON; ADDING THE ACT OF POSSESSING AND THREATENING TO DISCLOSE
7 REAL OR DIGITALLY FABRICATED MEDIA AS A VIOLATION; PROVIDING PENALTIES; DEFINING
8 "DIGITALLY FABRICATED"; AND AMENDING SECTION 45-8-213, MCA.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 45-8-213, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "45-8-213. Privacy in communications. (1) Except as provided in 69-6-104, a person commits the
14 offense of violating privacy in communications if the person knowingly or purposely:
15 (a) with the purpose to terrify, intimidate, threaten, harass, or injure, communicates with a person
16 by electronic communication and threatens to inflict injury or physical harm to the person or property of the
17 person or makes repeated use of obscene, lewd, or profane language or repeated lewd or lascivious
18 suggestions;
19 (b) uses an electronic communication to attempt to extort money or any other thing of value from a
20 person or to disturb by repeated communications the peace, quiet, or right of privacy of a person at the place
21 where the communications are received;
22 (c) records or causes to be recorded a conversation by use of a hidden electronic or mechanical
23 device that reproduces a human conversation without the knowledge of all parties to the conversation; or
24 (d) with the purpose to terrify, intimidate, threaten, harass, or injure, or to obtain money or other
25 valuable consideration, publishes, or distributes, or discloses real or digitally fabricated printed or electronic
26 photographs, pictures, images, or films of an identifiable person without the consent of the person depicted that
27 show:
28 (i) the visible genitals, anus, buttocks, or female breast if the nipple is exposed; or
- 1 - LC 3972
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 3972
1 (ii) the person depicted engaged in a real or simulated sexual act. ; or
2 (e) with the purpose to obtain money or other valuable consideration from an identifiable person
3 without the consent of the person depicted, possesses and threatens to disclose real or digitally fabricated
4 images or videos that show:
5 (i) the visible genitals, anus, buttocks, or female breast if the nipple is exposed; or
6 (ii) the person depicted engaged in a real or simulated sexual act.
7 (2) (a) Subsection (1)(c) does not apply to:
8 (i) elected or appointed public officials or to public employees when the transcription or recording
9 is done in the performance of official duty;
10 (ii) persons speaking at public meetings;
11 (iii) persons given warning of the transcription or recording. If one person provides the warning,
12 either party may record.
13 (iv) a health care facility, as defined in 50-5-101, or a government agency that deals with health
14 care if the recording is of a health care emergency telephone communication made to the facility or agency.
15 (b) Subsection (1)(d) does not apply to:
16 (i) images involving the voluntary exposure of a person's genitals or intimate parts in public or
17 commercial settings;
18 (ii) disclosures made in the public interest, including but not limited to the reporting of unlawful
19 conduct;
20 (iii) disclosures made in the course of performing duties related to law enforcement, including
21 reporting to authorities, criminal or news reporting, legal proceedings, or medical treatment; or
22 (iv) disclosures concerning historic, artistic, scientific, or educational materials.
23 (3) Except as provided in 69-6-104, a person commits the offense of violating privacy in
24 communications if the person purposely intercepts an electronic communication. This subsection does not
25 apply to elected or appointed public officials or to public employees when the interception is done in the
26 performance of official duty or to persons given warning of the interception.
27 (4) (a) A person convicted of the offense of violating privacy in communications shall be fined an
28 amount not to exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term not to exceed 6 months, or both. A
- 2 - LC 3972
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 3972
1 person convicted under subsection (1)(d) or (1)(e) is guilty of a misdemeanor for a first offense.
2 (b) On a second conviction of subsection (1)(a), or (1)(b), or (1)(d), a person shall be imprisoned in
3 the county jail for a term not to exceed 1 year or be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000, or both.
4 (c) On a third or subsequent conviction of subsection (1)(a), or (1)(b), or (1)(d), a person shall be
5 imprisoned in the state prison for a term not to exceed 5 years or be fined an amount not to exceed $10,000, or
6 both.
7 (d) On a second or subsequent conviction of subsection (1)(d) or (1)(e), a person is guilty of a
8 felony and shall be imprisoned for a term not to exceed 5 years or be fined an amount not to exceed $25,000,
9 or both.
10 (5) Nothing in this section may be construed to impose liability on an interactive computer service
11 for content provided by another person.
12 (6) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
13 (a) "Digitally fabricated" means using technical means, such as artificial intelligence, to create
14 media that realistically misrepresents an identifiable individual as engaging in conduct in which the identifiable
15 individual did not engage.
16 (b) "Electronic communication" means any transfer between persons of signs, signals, writing,
17 images, sounds, data, or intelligence of any nature transmitted in whole or in part by a wire, radio,
18 electromagnetic, photoelectronic, or photo-optical system.
19 (c) "Interactive computer service" means any information service, system, or access software
20 provider that provides or enables computer access by multiple users to a computer server, including specifically
21 a service or system that provides access to the internet and this type of service or system as operated or
22 offered by a library or educational institution."
23 - END -
