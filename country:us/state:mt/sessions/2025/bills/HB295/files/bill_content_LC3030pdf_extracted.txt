4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO JUDICIAL TRANSPARENCY IN
5 JUDICIAL ELECTIONS; ALLOWING JUDICIAL CANDIDATES TO INDICATE A POLITICAL PARTY
6 AFFILIATION ON A DECLARATION FOR NOMINATION AND TO HAVE THAT AFFILIATION APPEAR ON
7 THE BALLOT; ALLOWING JUDICIAL CANDIDATES TO ANNOUNCE POLITICAL PARTY AFFILIATIONS
8 AND ENDORSEMENTS; AMENDING SECTIONS 13-10-209, 13-10-602, 13-12-203, 13-14-112, AND 13-14-
9 115, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 WHEREAS, the purpose of this bill is to allow judicial candidates to state their political party affiliation, if
12 desired, when filing for offices; and
13 WHEREAS, allowing candidates to share party affiliations will give voters additional information about
14 the people on the ballot.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Indication by judicial candidate of political party affiliation. A
19 candidate for judicial office may indicate the candidate's political party affiliation on the candidate's declaration
20 for nomination.
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Announcement by judicial candidate of political party affiliation and
23 endorsements. A candidate for judicial office may announce the candidate's political party affiliation or a
24 campaign endorsement that the candidate has received.
25
26 Section 3. Section 13-10-209, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "13-10-209. Arrangement and preparing of primary ballots. (1) (a) Ballots for a primary election
28 must be arranged and prepared in the same manner and number as provided in chapter 12 for general election

1 ballots, except that there must be separate ballots for each political party entitled to participate. The name of
2 the political party must appear at the top of the separate ballot for that party and need not appear with each
3 candidate's name.
4 (b) Nonpartisan offices and ballot issues may be prepared on separate ballots or may appear on
5 the same ballot as partisan offices if:
6 (i) each section is clearly identified as separate; and
7 (ii) the nonpartisan offices and ballot issues appear on each party's ballot.
8 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), an election administrator does not need to prepare a
9 primary ballot for a political party if:
10 (a) the party does not have candidates for more than half of the offices to appear on the ballot; and
11 (b) no more than one candidate files for nomination by that party for any of the offices to appear on
12 the ballot.
13 (3) Subsection (2) does not apply to elections for precinct committee offices. If more than one
14 candidate files for a precinct committee office from a party that will not have a primary ballot prepared, that
15 party shall select the candidate to fill the office.
16 (4) If, pursuant to subsection (2), in a primary election held in an even-numbered year a primary
17 ballot for a political party is not prepared, the secretary of state shall certify that a primary election is
18 unnecessary for that party and shall instruct the election administrator to certify the names of the candidates for
19 that party for the general election ballot only.
20 (5) The separate ballots for each party must have the same appearance. Each set of party ballots
21 must bear the same number. If prepared as a separate ballot, the nonpartisan ballot may have a different
22 appearance than the party ballots but must be numbered in the same order as the party ballots.
23 (6) Regardless of whether the election administrator has prepared separate ballots for nonpartisan
24 offices or has listed nonpartisan candidates on the same ballots as partisan candidates, if a candidate for
25 judicial office indicates the candidate's political party affiliation on a declaration for nomination, the candidate's
26 name must appear under the title of the office sought, with the name of the candidate's political party affiliation,
27 if any, in no more than three words appearing opposite or below the candidate's name. If a candidate for judicial
28 office did not indicate a political party affiliation as provided in 13-14-112, the name of the candidate must

4 election but must be numbered in the same order.
5 (7)(8) Each elector must receive a set of ballots that includes the party, nonpartisan, and ballot issue

8 Section 4. Section 13-10-602, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "13-10-602. Use of party name. (1) Except as provided in subsection (3), a political party and its
10 regularly nominated candidates, members, and officers have the sole and exclusive right to the use of the party
11 name. A Except as provided in [section 1], a candidate for office may not use any word of the name of any
12 other political party or organization other than that by which the candidate is nominated in a manner that
13 indicates or implies the individual is a candidate of the nonnominating party.
14 (2) An independent or nonpartisan candidate, except as provided in [section 1] or subsection (3) of
15 this section, may not use any word of the name of any existing political party or organization in the candidacy in
16 a manner that indicates or implies that the individual is a candidate of that party or organization.
17 (3) A candidate for an elective office under an elected county official government provided for in 7-
18 3-111 who is running in an election conducted after electors have approved a change pursuant to 7-3-103(4) in
19 the type of election held from partisan to nonpartisan may use a party name or symbol in the candidate's
20 campaign material."
21
22 Section 5. Section 13-12-203, MCA, is amended to read:
23 "13-12-203. Appearance of candidate's name and party designation on ballot. (1) Subject to 13-
24 12-202 and except as provided in 13-10-209 for nonpartisan offices and 13-10-303 for certain other candidates,
25 in partisan elections, candidates' names must appear under the title of the office sought, with the name of the
26 party in not more than three words appearing opposite or below the name.
27 (2) (a) Subject Except as provided in subsection (2)(b) and subject to 13-12-202, in nonpartisan
28 general elections, the candidates' names must appear under the title of the office sought, with no description or

1 designation appearing with the name unless partisan and nonpartisan offices appear on the same ballot. In
2 such a case, the names of nonpartisan candidates must appear with the word "Nonpartisan".
3 (b) In an election for judicial office, the candidates' names must appear under the title of the office
4 sought, with the name of the candidate's political party affiliation, if any, in no more than three words appearing
5 opposite or below the candidate's name. If a candidate for judicial office did not indicate a political party
6 affiliation as provided 13-14-112, the name of the candidate must appear with the word "Undisclosed".
7 (3) Except as otherwise provided by this section, information about the candidate other than the
8 candidate's name may not appear on the ballot, including a title, accomplishment, award, or degree."
9
10 Section 6. Section 13-14-112, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "13-14-112. Declarations for nomination -- fee -- filing. (1) Nonpartisan candidates shall file
12 declarations for nomination as required by the primary election laws in a form prescribed by the secretary of
13 state except as provided in 13-14-113. Except for a candidate covered under 7-1-205, a candidate may not file
14 for more than one public office.
15 (2) (a) DeclarationsExcept as provided as subsection (2)(b), declarations may not indicate political
16 affiliation. The candidate may not state in the declaration any principles or measures that the candidate
17 advocates or any slogans.
18 (b) A candidate for judicial office may indicate the candidate's political party affiliation on the
19 declaration. A candidate who does not indicate a political party affiliation on the declaration may list
20 "Undisclosed".
21 (3) Each individual filing a declaration shall pay the fee prescribed by law for the office that the
22 individual seeks.
23 (4) Declarations must be filed:
24 (a) in the office of the secretary of state or the appropriate election administrator as provided in 13-
25 10-201; and
26 (b) within the filing period provided in 13-10-201(7) for the office that the individual seeks."
27
28 Section 7. Section 13-14-115, MCA, is amended to read:

1 "13-14-115. Preparation and distribution of nonpartisan primary ballots -- determination on
2 conducting primary. (1) The election administrators shall arrange, prepare, and distribute primary ballots for
3 nonpartisan offices, designated "nonpartisan primary ballots". The ballots must be arranged and prepared as
4 provided in 13-10-209 and be without political designation, but a candidate for judicial office must be listed with
5 the candidate's political party affiliation, if any, as provided in 13-10-209 and 13-12-203.
6 (2) (a) Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), the election administrator of a political subdivision
7 may determine that a local nonpartisan portion of a primary election need not be held if:
8 (i) the number of candidates for an office exceeds three times the number to be elected to that
9 office in no more than one-half of the offices on the ballot; and
10 (ii) the number of candidates in excess of three times the number to be elected is not more than
11 one for any office on the ballot.
12 (b) The election administrator may determine that a primary election for a nonpartisan county office
13 need not be held if fewer than three candidates have filed for that office.
14 (c) If the election administrator determines that a primary election must be held pursuant to
15 subsection (2)(a) or (2)(b), the election administrator shall conduct the primary election only for the nonpartisan
16 offices that have a sufficient number of candidates that have filed to be elected to that office.
17 (d) If the election administrator determines that a primary election need not be held pursuant to
18 subsection (2)(a), (2)(b), or (2)(c) for a nonpartisan office, the administrator shall give notice to the governing
19 body that a primary election will not be held for that office.
20 (3) The governing body may require that a primary election be held for a nonpartisan office if it
21 passes a resolution not more than 10 days after the close of filing by candidates for election stating that a
22 primary election must be held for that office."
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Codification instruction. (1) [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
25 integral part of Title 13, chapter 14, part 2, and the provisions of Title 13, chapter 14, part 2, apply to [section 1].
26 (2) [Section 2] is intended to be codified as an integral part of Title 13, chapter 35, part 2, and the
27 provisions of Title 13, chapter 35, part 2, apply to [section 2].
