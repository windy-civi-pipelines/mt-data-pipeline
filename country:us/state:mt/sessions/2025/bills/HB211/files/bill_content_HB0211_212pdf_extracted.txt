15 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Delivery training program -- rulemaking. (1) The department shall
16 certify all delivery training programs that include the following:
17 (a) methods to identify and prevent sales to underage or intoxicated persons;
18 (b) strategies used by underage persons to obtain alcohol;
19 (c) acceptable forms of identification and procedures for checking identification;
20 (d) actions that can be taken to prevent an alcohol-related incident from occurring; and
21 (e) other requirements adopted by the department supported by statute or rule relating to the
22 delivery of alcoholic beverages.
23 (2) On receipt of a proposed delivery training program from the holder of a third-party alcohol
24 delivery license, the department has 30 business days to approve, deny, or request modifications to the
25 proposed training program.
26 (3) The department may adopt rules to implement this section.

2 "16-4-1008. Penalty. (1) A licensee found as a result of a routine check for compliance with 16-3-301,
3 16-6-304, or 16-6-305, or [section 1] to be out of compliance with 16-4-1005 shall pay a $50 penalty for a first
4 offense, a $200 penalty for a second offense, and a $350 penalty for a third offense in a 3-year period. The fine
5 must be paid to the department and deposited in the enterprise fund to the credit of the department for
6 administration of this part.
7 (2) The department shall consider the following as mitigating circumstances before taking an
8 action pursuant to 16-4-406 against a licensee who is not in compliance with the provisions of this part:
9 (a) the licensee's prior violation history;
10 (b) the licensee's good faith effort to prevent a violation;
11 (c) the existence of written policies governing employee conduct; and
12 (d) whether the evidence of a violation was based solely on the investigating authority creating an
13 opportunity for the violation rather than on complaints received or observed misconduct."
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Delivery training program -- rulemaking. (1) The department shall
16 certify all delivery training programs that include the following:
17 (a) methods to identify and prevent sales to underage or intoxicated persons;
18 (b) strategies used by underage persons to obtain alcohol;
19 (c) acceptable forms of identification and procedures for checking identification;
20 (d) actions that can be taken to prevent an alcohol-related incident from occurring; and
21 (e) other requirements adopted by the department supported by statute or rule relating to the
22 delivery of alcoholic beverages.
23 (2) On receipt of a proposed delivery training program from the holder of a third-party alcohol
24 delivery license, the department has 30 business days to approve, deny, or request modifications to the
25 proposed training program.
26 (3) The department may adopt rules to implement this section.
27
