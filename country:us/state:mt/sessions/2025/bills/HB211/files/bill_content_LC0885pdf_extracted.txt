4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ALLOWING OFF-PREMISES BEER AND WINE LICENSES TO
5 DELIVER BEER AND TABLE WINE; CREATING A THIRD-PARTY DELIVERY LICENSE FOR ALCOHOL;
6 ALLOWING AN OFF-PREMISES RETAIL LICENSEE TO HAVE A THIRD-PARTY DELIVERY LICENSEE
7 DELIVER BEER OR TABLE WINE; REQUIRING THE SALE OF ALCOHOL TO THE END CONSUMER;
8 PROVIDING LICENSURE REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING REQUIREMENTS FOR THE THIRD-PARTY
9 DELIVERY LICENSEE; REVISING LAWS RELATING TO RESPONSIBLE SERVER TRAINING; PROVIDING
10 FOR LICENSING BY THE DEPARTMENT OF REVENUE; PROVIDING FOR PAYMENT OF A FEE;
11 PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AMENDING SECTIONS 16-4-115, 16-
12 4-501, 16-4-1001, 16-4-1002, 16-4-1003, 16-4-1004, 16-4-1005, 16-4-1007, AND 16-4-1008, MCA; AND
13 PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

17 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Third-party delivery license -- definitions. (1) The off-premises retail
18 licensee shall sell the beer or table wine to the end consumer for personal use and not for resale. The third-
19 party delivery licensee may not sell beer or table wine to the end consumer. The off-premises retail licensee
20 must have the exclusive authority to determine which beer or table wine is available for delivery and set the
21 prices for these beverages.
22 (2) (a) The department may issue a third-party delivery license to a third-party delivery applicant if
23 the following requirements are met:
24 (i) the third-party delivery applicant meets the requirements for an off-premises retail licensee
25 under 16-4-401 to hold a license; and
26 (ii) the third-party delivery applicant submits an application and pays the initial license fee as
27 provided in 16-4-501.
28 (b) Third-party delivery licenses may be renewed annually by June 30 if:

1 (i) the third-party delivery licensee continues to meet the requirements for an off-premises retail
2 licensee under 16-4-401 to hold a license; and
3 (ii) the third-party delivery licensee submits a renewal form and the annual renewal fee as
4 provided in 16-4-501.
5 (3) A third-party delivery licensee may only deliver or facilitate the delivery of beer or table wine
6 through drivers who meet the following requirements:
7 (a) are an employee or an independent contractor of the third-party delivery licensee;
8 (b) are at least 21 years of age;
9 (c) have a valid driver's license or a state-issued identification;
10 (d) have not been convicted of a felony or a driving under the influence offense in the past 7 years;
11 and
12 (e) have completed a responsible server and sales training program or a delivery training program
13 under Title 16, chapter 4, part 10, prior to their first alcohol delivery.
14 (4) Drivers are subject to the penalties provided in Title 16, chapter 4, part 10.
15 (5) Any beer or table wine sold by an off-premises retail licensee for delivery by or through a third-
16 party delivery licensee must be removed from the off-premises retail licensee's licensed premises only during
17 the hours in which the off-premises retail licensee is permitted to sell alcoholic beverages. The delivery must be
18 completed within an hour after the time that the off-premises retail licensee is required to end sales of alcoholic
19 beverages.
20 (6) Any beer or table wine unable to be delivered for any reason must be returned to the licensed
21 premises of the off-premises retail licensee that sold the beer or table wine. The third-party delivery licensee
22 shall document and maintain a record of the delivery attempt. Drivers may not be compensated based on
23 whether a delivery of beer or table wine results in a completed delivery transaction.
24 (7) The person to whom the delivery is made must be at least 21 years of age. The driver shall use
25 an identification scanning software technology or an alternative approved by the department to verify the age of
26 the recipient at the time of delivery.
27 (8) Deliveries may not be made to anyone who is apparently or obviously intoxicated.
28 (9) The outer packaging containing the beer or table wine being delivered must clearly indicate

2 (a) the person delivering the order has personally retrieved and bagged or otherwise packaged the
3 beer or table wine for delivery; or
4 (b) the driver is notified through electronic or other means that the delivery order contains alcohol
5 and the recipient must be 21 years of age or older.
6 (10) Beer or table wine intended for delivery by a motor vehicle must be transported in the rear of
7 the motor vehicle and may not be within the immediate arm's length of the driver or within a separate cargo
8 area.
9 (11) Beer or table wine intended for delivery by bicycle must be in a closed compartment or
10 otherwise out of reach of the bicycle operator.
11 (12) A licensee under this section may not deliver or facilitate delivery of beer or table wine to the
12 following locations:
13 (a) a business licensed to sell alcoholic beverages at retail, with the exception of delivery to
14 individual guest rooms of a lodging facility that is also licensed to sell alcoholic beverages at retail;
15 (b) a residence hall located on the premises of an institution of higher education; or
16 (c) a property that does not have a permanent address that includes a street name and number.
17 (13) A licensee under this section that delivers or facilitates the delivery of alcoholic beverages shall
18 maintain books and records reflecting the date, time, address, and recipient of the alcohol delivery for each
19 delivery, the name and address of the person making the delivery, and the name and business address of the
20 off-premises retail licensee selling the alcoholic beverage. Records must be maintained either in hard copy or
21 electronic format for 3 years. Licensees shall make these books and records available to the department and its
22 investigators on reasonable notice from the department.
23 (14) An off-premises retail licensee may utilize the services of a third-party delivery licensee by
24 means of the telephone, the internet, a mobile application, or other electronic means to facilitate the ordering or
25 delivery of beer or table wine from inventory located at the off-premises retail licensee's licensed premises.
26 (15) An off-premises retail licensee is not subject to violations of this section that arise out of the
27 delivery of beer or table wine delivered by a third-party delivery licensee.

11 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Delivery training program -- rulemaking. (1) The department shall
12 certify all delivery training programs that include the following:
13 (a) methods to identify and prevent sales to underage or intoxicated persons;
14 (b) strategies used by underage persons to obtain alcohol;
15 (c) acceptable forms of identification and procedures for checking identification;
16 (d) actions that can be taken to prevent an alcohol-related incident from occurring; and
17 (e) other requirements adopted by the department supported by statute or rule relating to the
18 delivery of alcoholic beverages.
19 (2) On receipt of a proposed delivery training program from the holder of a third-party alcohol
20 delivery license, the department has 30 business days to approve, deny, or request modifications to the
21 proposed training program.
22 (3) The department may adopt rules to implement this section.
