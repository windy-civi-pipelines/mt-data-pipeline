4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING HUNTING LICENSING LAWS FOR
5 PERSONS UNDER 18 YEARS OF AGE; ALLOWING FOR THE ISSUING OF A FREE SPECIAL PERMIT
6 LICENSE FOR COMBINATION LICENSE OR AN ELK, DEER, OR ANTELOPE PERMIT TO A PERSON
7 UNDER 18 YEARS OF AGE WITH A LIFE-THREATENING ILLNESS WHO IS RECOMMENDED TO THE
8 DEPARTMENT OF FISH, WILDLIFE, AND PARKS BY A MONTANA CHARITY OR NONPROFIT
9 ORGANIZATION; SETTING REQUIREMENTS TO OBTAIN A LICENSE; SETTING REQUIREMENTS TO USE
10 A LICENSE; SETTING LICENSE CAPS; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING
11 SECTIONS 87-2-105 AND SECTION 87-2-805, MCA.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Section 87-2-105, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "87-2-105. Hunter safety and education required. (1) Except for a youth who qualifies for a license
17 pursuant to 87-2-805(4) or (5) or a person who has been issued an apprentice hunting certificate pursuant to
18 87-2-810, a hunting license may not be issued to a person born after January 1, 1985, unless the person
19 authorized to issue the license determines proof of completion of:
20 (a) a Montana hunter safety and education course established in subsection (4) or (6);
21 (b) a hunter safety course in any other state or province; or
22 (c) a Montana hunter safety and education course that qualifies the person for a provisional
23 certificate as provided in 87-2-126.
24 (2) A hunting license may not be issued to a member of the regular armed forces of the United
25 States or to a member of the armed forces of a foreign government attached to the armed forces of the United
26 States who is assigned to active duty in Montana and who is otherwise considered a resident under 87-2-
27 102(1) or to a member's spouse or dependent, as defined in 87-2-102, who resides in the member's household,
28 unless the person authorized to issue the license determines proof of completion of a hunter safety and

2 (3) A bow and arrow license may not be issued to a resident or nonresident unless the person
3 authorized to issue the license receives an archery license issued for a prior hunting season or determines
4 proof of completion of a bowhunter education course from the national bowhunter education foundation or any
5 other bowhunter education program approved by the department. Neither the department nor the license agent
6 is required to provide records of past archery license purchases. As part of the department's bow and arrow
7 licensing procedures, the department shall notify the public regarding bowhunter education requirements.
8 (4) The department shall provide for a hunter safety and education course that includes instruction
9 in the safe handling of firearms and for that purpose may cooperate with any reputable organization having as
10 one of its objectives the promotion of hunter safety and education. The department may designate as an
11 instructor any person it finds to be competent to give instructions in hunter safety and education, including the
12 handling of firearms. A person appointed shall give the course of instruction and shall issue a certificate of
13 completion from Montana's hunter safety and education course to a person successfully completing the course.
14 (5) The department shall provide for a course of instruction from the national bowhunter education
15 foundation or any other bowhunter education program approved by the department and for that purpose may
16 cooperate with any reputable organization having as one of its objectives the promotion of safety in the
17 handling of bow hunting tackle. The department may designate as an instructor any person it finds to be
18 competent to give bowhunter education instruction. A person appointed shall give the course of instruction and
19 shall issue a certificate of completion to a person successfully completing the course.
20 (6) The department may develop an adult hunter safety and education course.
21 (7) The department may adopt rules regarding how a person authorized to issue a license
22 determines proof of completion of a required course."

24 only one hunting season. As used in this subsection, "life-threatening illness" means any progressive,

20 youth prior to and during the hunt.

22 pursuant to this subsection (5) annually, but not more than one tag per species per hunting district annually.

24 to this subsection (5) do not count against the number of antelope licenses reserved for people with permanent
25 disabilities as provided in 87-2-706 or against the licenses reserved for youth with a life-threatening illness as
