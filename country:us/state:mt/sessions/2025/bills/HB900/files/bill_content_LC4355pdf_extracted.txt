4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING AN INCOME TAX CREDIT FOR A BUSINESS THAT
5 DIRECTS PAYMENT PROCESSING FEES TO A CHARITABLE ORGANIZATION; PROVIDING THAT THE
6 CREDIT MAY BE TAKEN AGAINST THE INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX OR THE CORPORATE INCOME TAX;
7 PROVIDING THAT THE CREDIT MAY BE CARRIED FORWARD 2 YEARS; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS;
8 AMENDING SECTION 15-30-2303, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Credit for business that directs payment processing fees to
13 charitable organization. (1) A taxpayer is allowed a credit against the tax imposed by Title 15, chapter 31, or
14 this chapter for payment processing fees directed to a charitable organization if the payment processor
15 reimburses the state for the credit amount in the first year the credit is claimed.
16 (2) The credit is equal to the amount of payment processing fees directed to a charitable
17 organization under a contract that allows the taxpayer to direct a portion of payment processing fees to a
18 charitable organization.
19 (3) The credit allowed by this section may not be refunded if the taxpayer has a tax liability less
20 than the amount of the credit. If the sum of credit carryovers from the credit, if any, and the amount of credit
21 allowed by this section for the tax year exceed the taxpayer's tax liability for the current tax year, the excess
22 attributable to the current tax year's credit is a credit carryover to succeeding tax years for a period not to
23 exceed 2 years from the tax year the credit was claimed.
24 (4) A credit is not allowed under this section with respect to any amount deducted by the taxpayer
25 for state tax purposes as a charitable contribution. This section does not prevent a taxpayer from:
26 (a) claiming a credit under this section instead of a deduction; or
27 (b) claiming an exclusion, deduction, or credit for a charitable contribution that exceeds the amount
28 for which the credit is allowed under this section.

1 (5) (a) If the credit allowed under this section is claimed by a small business corporation, a pass-
2 through entity, or a partnership, the credit must be attributed to shareholders, owners, or partners using the
3 same proportion as used to report the entity's income or loss.
4 (6) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
5 (a) "Charitable organization" means an organization for which a contribution qualifies as a
6 charitable contribution as defined in 26 U.S.C. 170(c).
7 (b) "Payment processing fee" means a fee paid by a business to a payment processor for the
8 purpose of processing electronic payment transactions, including but not limited to payments with a credit card,
9 debit card, or digital wallet.
10 (c) "Payment processor" means a company or service that facilitates electronic transactions
11 between businesses and their customers.
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Credit for business that directs payment processing fees to
14 charitable organization. There is a credit against tax liability under this chapter for payment processing fees
15 directed to a charitable organization in [section 1].
