3 SCHILLINGER
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A STUDY OF DEPARTMENT SUSPENSION OR
6 CLOSURE OF NEW GROUND WATER APPROPRIATIONS IN THE FOX HILLS-HELL CREEK AQUIFER;
7 PROVIDING FOR A FEASIBILITY STUDY OF GROUND WATER AVAILABILITY; PROVIDING REPORTING
8 REQUIREMENTS; APPROPRIATING FUNDS; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE AND A
9 TERMINATION DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Fox Hills-Hell Creek aquifer -- temporary suspension of action on
14 permits -- temporary suspension of action on permits -- groundwater GROUND WATER study. (1) In order to
15 balance the need for continued development of Montana's water and for protection of existing water rights in
16 the Fox Hills -Hell Creek aquifer, the department may SHALL suspend action on a class of applications or may
17 close an aquifer source and refuse to accept a class of applications, or both, for a permit to appropriate ground
18 water from that source pursuant to this part.
19 (2) Pursuant to subsection (1), the department may suspend or close permit applications to
20 appropriate ground water from the Fox Hills-Hell Creek aquifer within department administrative boundaries
21 39E, 39FJ, 39G, 39H, 40D, 40E, 40P, 40Q, 40R, 40S, 42A, 42C, 42J, 42K, 42KJ, 42L, and 42M if the proposed
22 appropriation is 3, 000 gallons a minute or more EXCEEDS THE PERMIT EXCEPTIONS PROVIDED IN 85-2-306.
23 (3) In order to balance the need for continued development of Montana's water and for protection
24 of existing water rights in the Fox Hills-Hell Creek aquifer, the department may suspend action on a class of
25 applications or may close an aquifer source and refuse to accept a class of applications, or both, for a permit to
26 appropriate ground water from that source pursuant to this part.
27 (2) Pursuant to subsection (1), the department shall suspend or close permit applications to

1 appropriate ground water from the Fox Hills-Hell Creek aquifer within department administrative boundaries
2 40P, 42M, and the portion of 40S east of the Redwater River if the proposed appropriation exceeds a permit
3 exemption provided by 85-2-306.
4 (3) The bureau of mines and geology shall complete a feasibility study on ground water availability
5 FROM THE FOX HILLS-HELL CREEK AQUIFER in the department administrative boundaries listed in subsection (2)
6 39E, 39FJ, 39G, 39H, 40D, 40E, 40P, 40Q, 40R, 40S, 42A, 42C, 42J, 42K, 42KJ, 42L, AND 42M. THE STUDY MAY
7 INCLUDE:
8 (A) THE COMPILATION AND UPDATE OF NEW GEOLOGIC AND HYDROGEOLOGIC DATA;
9 (B) A LIMITED FIELD COLLECTION OF WATER LEVELS AND PRESSURE FOR WELLS WITH HISTORICAL DATA;
10 (C) A PRELIMINARY WATER BUDGET FOR THE FOX HILLS-HELL CREEK AQUIFER; AND
11 (D) A PRELIMINARY MODEL TO EVALUATE THE EFFECTS OF PUMPING ON WATER LEVELS AND PRESSURE.
12 (4)(2)(4) THE BUREAU SHALL REPORT THE PROGRESS AND RESULTS OF ITS STUDY TO THE WATER
13 POLICY INTERIM COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED IN 5-5-231, IN ACCORDANCE WITH 5-11-210. THE COMMITTEE SHALL
14 CONSIDER THE RESULTS OF THE STUDY AND MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE LEGISLATURE FOR DEPARTMENT
15 SUSPENSION OR CLOSURE OF NEW GROUND WATER APPROPRIATIONS FROM THE FOX HILLS-HELL CREEK AQUIFER.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Transfer. By June 30, 2025, the state treasurer shall transfer $5,000
18 $50,000 FROM THE GENERAL FUND to the ground water assessment account in 85-2-905.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Appropriation. There is appropriated $5,000 $50,000 from the general
21 fund to the bureau of mines and geology for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, for a feasibility study
22 pursuant to subsection (3) of [section 1(1)(3)].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
25 integral part of Title 85, chapter 2, part 3, and the provisions of Title 85, chapter 2, part 3, apply to [section 1].
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
