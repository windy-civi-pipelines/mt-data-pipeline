4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING TARGETING PREGNANCY CENTERS FOR
5 REGULATION OR OVERSIGHT BECAUSE PREGNANCY CENTERS DO NOT PERFORM, REFER, OR
6 COUNSEL IN FAVOR OF ABORTION OR CONTRACEPTION; PROVIDING A PRIVACY POLICY;
7 REQUIRING CONSENT TO SHARE PERSONAL INFORMATION; CREATING A RIGHT TO REQUEST
8 DELETION OF DATA; PROVIDING DATA SECURITY; PROHIBITING THE SALE OF DATA WITHOUT
9 CONSENT; PROVIDING A PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING
10 REMEDIES; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers have a considerable and growing life-affirming impact on the women,
13 men, children, and communities they serve; and
14 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers serve women in Montana and across the United States with integrity
15 and compassion; and
16 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers provide comprehensive care to women and men facing unexpected
17 pregnancies, including resources to meet their physical, psychological, emotional, and spiritual needs; and
18 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers offer women free, confidential, and compassionate services, which can
19 include pregnancy tests, peer counseling, 24-hour telephone hotlines, childbirth and parenting classes, referrals
20 to community health care, adoption referrals, and other support services; and
21 WHEREAS, many medical pregnancy centers offer ultrasounds and other medical services; and
22 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers encourage women to make positive life choices by equipping them with
23 complete and accurate information regarding their pregnancy options and the development of their unborn
24 children; and
25 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers provide important support and resources for women who choose
26 childbirth over abortion; and
27 WHEREAS, pregnancy centers have faced unprecedented attacks since the Supreme Court's decision
28 in Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization, overturning Roe v. Wade and the federal constitutional right

4 decisions, while numerous cities and municipalities have considered similar ordinances.

6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

8 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 4 11 4], the following
9 definitions apply:
10 (1) (a) "Abortion" means the act of using or prescribing an instrument, medicine, or drug or a
11 substance, device, or means with the intent to terminate the clinically diagnosable pregnancy of a woman and
12 for the purpose of ensuring the death of her unborn child.
13 (b) The term does not include an act taken with the intent to:
14 (i) save the life or preserve the health of the unborn child;
15 (ii) remove a dead unborn child caused by miscarriage;
16 (iii) remove an ectopic pregnancy; or
17 (iv) perform a previability separation procedure when the procedure is, in reasonable medical
18 judgment, necessary to save or preserve the life of the pregnant woman.
19 (2) (a) "Abortion-inducing drug" means a medicine, drug, or any other substance prescribed or
20 dispensed with the intent to terminate the clinically diagnosable pregnancy of a woman and for the purpose of
21 ensuring the death of her unborn child.
22 (b) The term includes the off-label use of drugs known to have abortion-inducing properties that
23 are prescribed specifically with the intent to cause an abortion.
24 (c) The term does not include drugs that are known to cause an abortion but that are prescribed
25 for other medical indications.
26 (3) "CONSUMER" MEANS A NATURAL PERSON WHO IS A MONTANA RESIDENT OR WHOSE HEALTH OR
27 PERSONAL DATA WAS COLLECTED AT A MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER IN MONTANA
28 (4) "CONSUMER HEALTH DATA" MEANS PERSONAL INFORMATION THAT IS LINKED OR REASONABLY

4 (B) SOCIAL, PSYCHOLOGICAL, BEHAVIORAL, AND MEDICAL INTERVENTIONS;
5 (C) HEALTH-RELATED SURGERIES OR PROCEDURES;
6 (D) USE OR PURCHASE OF PRESCRIBED MEDICATION;
7 (E) BODILY FUNCTIONS, VITAL SIGNS, SYMPTOMS, OR MEASUREMENTS;
8 (F) DIAGNOSES OR DIAGNOSTIC TESTING, TREATMENT, OR MEDICATION;
9 (G) REPRODUCTIVE OR SEXUAL HEALTH INFORMATION;
10 (H) BIOMETRIC DATA; AND
11 (I) GENETIC DATA.
12 (3)(5) (3) "Contraception" means the use of any natural or artificial means to prevent the
13 fertilization of a human ovum.
14 (4)(6) (4) "Ectopic pregnancy" means the state of carrying an unborn child outside of the uterine
15 cavity.
16 (5)(7) (5) "Medical pregnancy center" means a pregnancy center that provides medical testing,
17 counseling, and pregnancy-related care.
18 (6)(8) (6) "Miscarriage" means the spontaneous loss of an unborn child.

24 ASSOCIATE A CONSUMER WITH THE DATA THAT IS DERIVED OR EXTRAPOLATED FROM NONHEALTH INFORMATION.

26 and alternatives to abortion and provides women, children, and families with resources, counseling, classes,

4 there is a reasonable likelihood of sustained survival of the unborn child outside the mother's body, with or
5 without artificial support.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Prohibition on interference with work of pregnancy center. The state
9 or a local government may not adopt or enact a law, rule, policy, or similar measure that:
10 (1) requires a pregnancy center to:
11 (a) offer or perform abortions;
12 (b) offer, provide, or distribute abortion-inducing drugs or contraception;
13 (c) refer a patient for an abortion, an abortion-inducing drug, or contraception;
14 (d) counsel a patient in favor of an abortion, an abortion-inducing drug, or contraception; or
15 (e) post an advertisement, sign, flyer, or similar material that promotes or provides information
16 about obtaining an abortion, abortion-inducing drugs, or contraception;
17 (2) prohibits a pregnancy center from:
18 (a) providing information, care, counseling, classes, or other services related to pregnancy,
19 childbirth, or parenting because the pregnancy center does not perform, refer, or counsel in favor of abortion,
20 abortion-inducing drugs, or contraception;
21 (b) providing prenatal and postnatal resources, such as diapers, baby clothes, baby furniture,
22 formula, and similar items, because the pregnancy center does not perform, refer, or counsel in favor of
23 abortion, abortion-inducing drugs, or contraception;
24 (c) providing medical testing, counseling, and care related to pregnancy or childbirth because the
25 pregnancy center does not perform, refer, or counsel in favor of abortion, abortion-inducing drugs, or
26 contraception; or
27 (d) counseling a woman on pregnancy-related care or treatment, including care or treatment that
28 may reverse the effects of abortion-inducing drugs; or

2 center to interview, hire, or continue to employ a person who does not affirm the center's mission statement or
3 agree to comply with the center's pro-life ethic and operating procedures.

5 NEW SECTION. SECTION 3. PRIVACY POLICY. ( 1) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS AND PREGNANCY
6 CENTERS SHALL MAINTAIN A CONSUMER HEALTH DATA AND PERSONAL DATA PRIVACY POLICY THAT CLEARLY AND
7 CONSPICUOUSLY DISCLOSES:
8 (A) THE CATEGORIES OF PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION AND MEDICAL DATA COLLECTED AND THE
9 PURPOSE FOR WHICH THE DATA IS COLLECTED, INCLUDING HOW THE DATA WILL BE USED;
10 (B) THE CATEGORIES OF SOURCES FROM WHICH THE DATA IS COLLECTED;
11 (C) THE CATEGORIES OF THIRD PARTIES AND SPECIFIC AFFILIATES WITH WHOM THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY
12 CENTER AND PREGNANCY CENTER SHARES THE CONSUMER HEALTH DATA; AND
13 (D) HOW A CONSUMER MAY EXERCISE THEIR RIGHTS TO PROTECT THIS INFORMATION.
14 (2) A MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR A PREGNANCY CENTER SHALL PROMINENTLY PUBLISH A LINK TO ITS
15 CONSUMER HEALTH DATA PRIVACY POLICY ON ITS HOMEPAGE.
16 (3) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS AND PREGNANCY CENTERS SHALL POST A CLEAR STATEMENT THAT
17 THEIR ENTITY IS NOT CONSIDERED A REGULATED ENTITY UNDER THE HEALTH INSURANCE PORTABILITY AND
18 ACCOUNTABILITY ACT.
19 (4) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS OR PREGNANCY CENTERS MAY NOT COLLECT, USE, OR SHARE
20 ADDITIONAL CATEGORIES OF CONSUMER HEALTH DATA NOT DISCLOSED IN THE POLICY WITHOUT FIRST DISCLOSING THE
21 ADDITIONAL CATEGORIES AND OBTAINING THE CONSUMER'S AFFIRMATIVE WRITTEN CONSENT PRIOR TO THE COLLECTION,
22 USE, OR SHARING OF THE CONSUMER HEALTH DATA.
23 (5) IT IS A VIOLATION OF THIS CHAPTER FOR A MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER TO
24 CONTRACT WITH A PROCESSOR TO PROCESS CONSUMER HEALTH DATA IN A MANNER THAT IS INCONSISTENT WITH THE
25 MEDICAL PRIVACY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER CONSUMER DATA PRIVACY POLICY.
26
27 NEW SECTION. SECTION 4. CONSENT TO COLLECT AND SHARE INFORMATION REQUIRED. (1) MEDICAL
28 PREGNANCY CENTERS AND PREGNANCY CENTERS MAY NOT COLLECT ANY CONSUMER HEALTH DATA EXCEPT:

3 REQUESTED FROM THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTERS;
4 (2) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS OR PREGNANCY CENTERS MAY NOT SHARE ANY CONSUMER HEALTH

6 (A) WITH CONSENT FROM THE CONSUMER FOR SHARING THAT IS SEPARATE AND DISTINCT FROM THE

8 (B) TO THE EXTENT NECESSARY TO PROVIDE A PRODUCT OR SERVICE THAT THE CONSUMER TO WHOM THE
9 DATA RELATES HAS REQUESTED.
10 (3) CONSENT MUST BE OBTAINED PRIOR TO THE COLLECTION OR SHARING OF ANY CONSUMER HEALTH
11 DATA, AND THE REQUEST FOR CONSENT MUST CLEARLY AND CONSPICUOUSLY DISCLOSE
12 (A) THE CATEGORIES OF CONSUMER HEALTH DATA COLLECTED OR SHARED;
13 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE COLLECTION OR SHARING OF THE CONSUMER HEALTH DATA, INCLUDING THE
14 SPECIFIC WAYS IN WHICH IT WILL BE USED;
15 (C) THE CATEGORIES OF ENTITIES WITH WHOM THE CONSUMER HEALTH DATA IS SHARED; AND
16 (D) HOW THE CONSUMER CAN WITHDRAW CONSENT FROM FUTURE COLLECTION OR SHARING OF THE
17 CONSUMER'S HEALTH DATA.
18
19 NEW SECTION. SECTION 5. RIGHT TO REQUEST DATA DELETION. (1) A CONSUMER HAS THE RIGHT TO
20 CONFIRM WHETHER THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER IS COLLECTING, SHARING, OR SELLING
21 DATA CONCERNING THE CONSUMER AND TO ACCESS THAT DATA, INCLUDING A LIST OF ALL THIRD PARTIES AND
22 AFFILIATES WITH WHOM THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER HAS SHARED OR SOLD THE
23 CONSUMER DATA AND AN ACTIVE E-MAIL ADDRESS OR OTHER ONLINE MECHANISM THAT THE CONSUMER MAY USE TO
24 CONTACT THIRD PARTIES.
25 (2) (A) A CONSUMER HAS THE RIGHT TO WITHDRAW CONSENT FROM THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR
26 PREGNANCY CENTER'S COLLECTION AND SHARING OF THE CONSUMER'S DATA.
27 (B) A MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER THAT RECEIVES THE REQUEST TO DELETE
28 ANY DATA SHALL:

1 (I) DELETE THE DATA FROM ITS RECORDS, INCLUDING FROM ALL PARTS OF THE CENTER'S NETWORK,
2 INCLUDING ARCHIVED OR BACKUP SYSTEMS; AND
3 (II) NOTIFY ALL AFFILIATES, PROCESSORS, CONTRACTORS, AND OTHER THIRD PARTIES WITH WHOM THE
4 CENTER HAS SHARED CONSUMER HEALTH DATA OF THE DELETION REQUEST.
5 (C) ALL AFFILIATES, PROCESSORS, CONTRACTORS, AND OTHER THIRD PARTIES WITH WHOM MEDICAL
6 PREGNANCY CENTERS OR PREGNANCY CENTERS HAVE SHARED DATA SHALL HONOR THE CONSUMER'S DELETION
7 REQUEST AND DELETE THE DATA FROM ITS RECORDS.
8 (D) A CONSUMER MAY EXERCISE THESE RIGHTS BY SUBMITTING A WRITTEN REQUEST TO THE MEDICAL
9 PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER. THE CENTER MAY NOT REQUIRE THE CONSUMER TO PAY TO DELETE THE
10 INFORMATION OR TO SET UP AN ONLINE ACCOUNT OR REQUEST ADDITIONAL PERSONAL INFORMATION TO GET THE
11 INFORMATION DELETED.
12 (E) THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER SHALL COMPLETE REMOVAL WITHIN 45
13 DAYS OF THE REQUEST.
14 (F) THE MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER MAY NOT UNLAWFULLY DISCRIMINATE
15 AGAINST A CONSUMER FOR EXERCISING ANY RIGHT TO REMOVE INFORMATION.
16
17 NEW SECTION. SECTION 6. DATA SECURITY. (1) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS OR PREGNANCY
18 CENTERS SHALL RESTRICT ACCESS TO CONSUMER HEALTH DATA BY THE EMPLOYEES, PROCESSORS, AND CONTRACTORS
19 OF THE REGULATED ENTITY TO ONLY THOSE NECESSARY TO SERVE THE CONSUMER.
20 (2) MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTERS OR PREGNANCY CENTERS SHALL ESTABLISH, IMPLEMENT, AND
21 MAINTAIN ADMINISTRATIVE, TECHNICAL, AND PHYSICAL DATA SECURITY PRACTICES THAT, AT A MINIMUM, SATISFY
22 REASONABLE STANDARDS OF CARE WITHIN THE INDUSTRY.
23
24 NEW SECTION. SECTION 7. SALE OF DATA WITHOUT CONSENT PROHIBITED. (1) IT IS UNLAWFUL FOR A
25 MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER TO SELL OR OFFER TO SELL CONSUMER HEALTH DATA OR
26 PERSONAL DATA WITHOUT VALID AUTHORIZATION FROM THE CONSUMER.
27 (2) A CONSUMER AUTHORIZATION FORM MUST BE WRITTEN IN PLAIN LANGUAGE AND MUST, AT MINIMUM,
28 INCLUDE:

2 (B) THE NAME AND CONTACT INFORMATION OF THE SELLER:
3 (C) THE NAME AND CONTACT INFORMATION OF THE PURCHASER; AND
4 (D) A DESCRIPTION OF THE PURPOSE OF THE SALE.

10 NEW SECTION. SECTION 9. RIGHT OF ACTION. A PERSON HARMED BY A VIOLATION OF [SECTIONS 1
11 THROUGH 11] MAY BRING A CAUSE OF ACTION AGAINST A MEDICAL PREGNANCY CENTER OR PREGNANCY CENTER FOR
12 INJUNCTIVE RELIEF, MONETARY DAMAGES, REASONABLE ATTORNEY FEES AND COSTS, AND ANY OTHER APPROPRIATE
13 RELIEF.
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Legal remedies -- damages. (1) A pregnancy center or a party
16 aggrieved by a violation of [section 2] may commence a civil action for damages, declaratory relief, injunctive
17 relief, and any other appropriate relief.
18 (2) On a finding of a violation of [section 2], the prevailing party is entitled to recover actual
19 damages or statutory damages of $50,000, whichever is greater, plus attorney fees and costs. If the person
20 acted with malice, the court may award treble actual damages or treble statutory damages, whichever is
21 greater.
22 (3) Damages awarded under this section are cumulative and are not limited by other remedies that
23 may be available under federal or state law.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Right of intervention. The legislature, by joint resolution, may appoint

27 capacity to intervene as a matter of right in a case in which the constitutionality of this part [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH
