20 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Approval of grants -- completion of biennial appropriation. (1) The

27 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Condition of grants -- disbursement of funds. (1) The disbursement of
28 grant funds for the projects specified in [section 1(2) and (5)] is subject to completion of the following conditions:

1 (a) The grant recipient shall document that other matching funds required for completion of the
2 project are firmly committed.
3 (b) The grant recipient must have a project management plan that is approved by the department
4 of commerce.
5 (c) The grant recipient must be in compliance with the auditing and reporting requirements
6 provided for in 2-7-503 and shall use a financial accounting system that the department of commerce can
7 reasonably ensure conforms to generally accepted accounting principles. Tribal governments shall comply with
8 auditing and reporting requirements provided for in 2 CFR 200.
9 (d) The grant recipient shall satisfactorily comply with all conditions described in the application’s
10 project summaries section of the Montana coal endowment program 2027 biennium project funding
11 recommendations to the 69th legislature.
12 (e) The grant recipient shall satisfy other specific requirements considered necessary by the
13 department of commerce to accomplish the purpose of the project as evidenced by the application to the
14 department.
15 (f) Pursuant to 90-6-710(6), the grant recipient shall comply with all Montana coal endowment
16 program requirements as determined by the department of commerce in order to qualify for reimbursement of
17 project expenses.
18 (g) The grant recipient shall execute a grant agreement with the department of commerce.
19 (2) With the exception of bridges, all projects must adhere to the design standards required by the
20 department of environmental quality. Recipients of Montana coal endowment program funds that are not
21 subject to the department of environmental quality design standards must adhere to generally accepted
22 industry standards, such as Recommended Standards for Wastewater Facilities or Recommended Standards
23 for Water Works, published by the Great Lakes-Upper Mississippi River Board of State and Provincial Public
24 Health and Environmental Managers, latest edition.
25 (3) Recipients of Montana coal endowment program funds are subject to the requirements of the
26 department of commerce as described in the most recent edition of the Montana Coal Endowment Program
27 Project Administration Manual adopted by the department through the administrative rulemaking process.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Other powers and duties of department. (1) The department of
2 commerce shall disburse grant funds on a reimbursement basis as grant recipients incur eligible project
3 expenses.
4 (2) If actual project expenses are lower than the projected expense of the project, the department
5 may, at its discretion:
6 (a) reduce the amount of grant funds to be provided to grant recipients in proportion to all other
7 project funding sources; or
8 (b) reduce the amount of grant funds to be provided so that the grant recipient’s projected average
9 residential user rates do not become lower than their target rate as determined by the department.
10 (3) If the grant recipient obtains a greater amount of grant funds than was contained in the
11 Montana coal endowment program application, the department may reduce the amount of the Montana coal
12 endowment program grant funds to be provided to ensure that the grant recipient continues to meet the
13 threshold requirements contained in the program guidelines for receiving the larger Montana coal endowment
14 program grant.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Appropriation from Montana coal endowment special revenue
17 account for emergency grants. There is appropriated $150,000 from the Montana coal endowment special
18 revenue account established in 17-5-703(3)(a) to the department of commerce for the biennium beginning July
19 1, 2025, for the purpose of providing local governments, as defined in 90-6-701, with emergency grants for
20 infrastructure projects, as defined in 90-6-701.
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Appropriation from Montana coal endowment special revenue
23 account for infrastructure planning grants. There is appropriated $1,500,000 from the Montana coal
24 endowment special revenue account established in 17-5-703(3)(a) to the department of commerce for the
25 biennium beginning July 1, 2025, for the purpose of providing local governments, as defined in 90-6-701, with
26 infrastructure planning grants for infrastructure projects, as defined in 90-6-701.
