21 through (9) is allocated according to the following schedule:

25 provided in 20-9-331, 20-9-333, and 20-9-360 ;

27 taxing jurisdictions in which production occurs, except a distribution may not be made for county and state
28 levies under 15-10-109, 20-9-331, 20-9-333, and 20-9-360 ; and

1 Section 2. Section 17-3-222, MCA, is amended to read:
2 "17-3-222. Apportionment of money to counties. (1) The state treasurer shall apportion the money
3 received under 17-3-221 to the appropriate counties and then allocate the money due each county as follows:
4 (a) 50% to the county treasurer for deposit in the county general fund; and

6 established in 20-9-336 to be used for the elementary BASE funding programs of the school districts in the
7 county.

9 statutorily appropriated as provided in 17-7-502."

11 Section 3. Section 20-9-331, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "20-9-331. Basic county tax for elementary equalization and other revenue for county
13 equalization of elementary BASE funding program. (1) Subject to 15-10-420, the county commissioners of
14 each county shall levy an annual basic county tax of 33 mills on the dollar of the taxable value of all taxable
15 property within the county, except for property subject to a tax or fee under 61-3-321(2) or (3), 61-3-529, 61-3-
16 537, 61-3-562, 61-3-570, and 67-3-204, for the purposes of elementary equalization and state BASE funding
17 program support. The revenue collected from this levy must be apportioned to the support of the elementary
18 BASE funding programs of the school districts in the county and to the school equalization and property tax
19 reduction account established in 20-9-336 in the following manner:
20 (a) In order to determine the amount of revenue raised by this levy that is retained by the county,
21 the sum of the estimated revenue identified in subsection (2) must be subtracted from the total of the BASE
22 funding programs of all elementary districts of the county.
23 (b) If the basic levy and other revenue prescribed by this section produce more revenue than is
24 required to repay a state advance for county equalization, the county treasurer shall remit the surplus funds to
25 the department of revenue, as provided in 15-1-504, for deposit to the state general fund school equalization
26 and property tax reduction account established in 20-9-336 immediately upon on occurrence of a surplus
27 balance and each subsequent month, with any final remittance due no later than June 20 of the fiscal year for
28 which the levy has been set.
