4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING HEALTH UTILIZATION REVIEW LAWS;
5 PROHIBITING RETROACTIVE DENIALS UNDER CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES; PROVIDING BIOLOGIC
6 PRESCRIPTIONS FOR MINORS; APPLYING TO CERTAIN ENTITIES; AMENDING SECTION 2-18-704,
7 MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Retroactive denials prohibited. When prior approval for a covered
12 service is required and obtained by or on behalf of a covered person and the covered person undergoes the
13 covered service, the approval is final and the health insurance issuer may not rescind approval after the
14 covered service has been provided. This section does not apply to cases of fraud, misrepresentation,
15 nonpayment of premium, exhaustion of benefits, if the covered person for whom the prior approval was granted
16 is not covered when the service was provided, or if the covered service provided was not the same covered
17 service that received prior approval, unless it was necessary and incidental.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Biologic prescriptions for minors. (1) Biologic therapies may be
20 prescribed by a treating provider for individuals under 18 years of age as provided in this section. The biologic
21 therapies must meet the following requirements:
22 (a) the biologic therapies must be approved by the U.S. food and drug administration for use on
23 adults, but need not be approved for use on persons under 18 years of age; and
24 (b) the biologic therapies must be medically necessary, covered benefits and supported by medical
25 literature in at least two peer-reviewed journals demonstrating that the off-label use is both safe and effective
26 for use on persons in the following comparable phases of life age ranges:
27 (i) infancy, between birth and 2 years of age;
28 (ii) childhood, from 2 to 12 years of age; and

1 (iii) adolescence, from 12 to 18 years of age.
2 (2) Upon receipt of the request for prior authorization and medical records supporting the request
3 and the literature described in section (1)(b), an entity covered under the provisions of this chapter performing a
4 utilization review must approve or deny a request for prior authorization for biologic therapies under subsection
5 (1) in the time frame set forth in this chapter.
6 (3) For the purposes of this section, the term "biologic therapies" means a treatment that uses
7 substances made from living organisms to treat disease. These substances may be made in a laboratory or
8 occur naturally in the body.
