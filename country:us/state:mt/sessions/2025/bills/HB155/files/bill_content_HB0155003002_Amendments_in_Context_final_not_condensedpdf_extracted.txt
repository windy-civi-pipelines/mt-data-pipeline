6 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 3 through 7] and 15-6-134, the
7 following definitions apply:
8 (1) "Homestead exemption" means the tax rate provided for in 15-6-134(3)(b).
9 (2) "Owner" includes a purchaser under contract for deed as defined in 70-20-115, a grantor of a
10 trust indenture as defined in 71-1-303, and the trustee of a grantor trust that is revocable as defined in 72-38-
11 103.
12 (3) (a) "Principal residence" means class four residential property:
13 (i) that is a single-family dwelling unit, unit of a multiple-unit dwelling, trailer, manufactured home,
14 or mobile home and the parcel on which the principal residence improvements are located but not including any
15 contiguous or adjacent parcels;
16 (ii) in which an owner can demonstrate the owner owned and lived for at least 7 months of the
17 year for which the homestead exemption for a principal residence is claimed;
18 (iii) that is the only residence for which the owner claims the homestead exemption for that year;
19 and
20 (iv) for which the owner made payment of the assessed Montana property taxes.
21 (b) An owner who cannot meet the requirements of subsection (3)(a)(ii) because the owner’s
22 principal residence changed during the tax year to another principal residence may still qualify for the
23 homestead exemption if the owner paid the Montana property taxes while residing in each principal residence
24 for a total of at least 7 consecutive months for each tax year.
25 (4) "Tax year 2025" means the period from January 1, 2025, through December 31, 2025.
26 (5) "Tax year 2026" means the period from January 1, 2026, through December 31, 2026.

12 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Homestead exemption -- improper approval -- penalty for false or
13 fraudulent application. (1) Except as provided in subsection (2), if the department determines that an
14 application for a homestead exemption was improperly approved, the department shall revise the assessment
15 for each year the homestead exemption was improperly granted subject to the assessment revision procedure
16 established in 15-8-601.
17 (2) (a) A person who files a false or fraudulent application for a homestead exemption provided for
18 in [section 4] is subject to criminal prosecution under the provisions of 45-7-202.
19 (3) (a) If a person is determined to have filed a false or fraudulent application, the department shall
20 revise the assessment of the property subject to the assessment revision procedure established in 15-8-601
21 and this section and assess a penalty as provided in this subsection (3). The penalty is equal to three times the
22 base penalty amount calculated under subsection (3)(b) plus interest at the rate provided in 15-16-102
23 calculated from the original due date of the taxes, until paid.
24 (b) The base penalty amount is equal to the property tax due for each year the homestead
25 exemption was improperly received, determined using the tax rate provided for in 15-6-134(3)(a), the appraised
26 value, and the mill levies in effect for the year, less the actual property taxes paid in the year.
27 (c) The revised assessment and penalty must be assessed against a person who filed a false or
28 fraudulent application even if the person no longer owns the property.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Appeal or denial of exemption. (1) (a) If the department denies an
14 application for a homestead exemption, the owner may request an informal review of the denial by submitting
15 an objection on written or electronic forms provided by the department for that purpose in a manner prescribed
16 by the department. The objection must be made no later than 30 days after the date of the denial notification
17 sent pursuant to [section 4(4)].
18 (b) The property owner may request that the department consider extenuating circumstances to
19 grant an application for the homestead exemption. Extenuating circumstances include but are not limited to
20 extraordinary, unusual, or infrequent events that are material in nature and of a character different from the
21 typical or customary, and that are not expected to recur.
22 (c) After the informal review, the department shall determine the correct status of the homestead
23 exemption and notify the taxpayer of its determination by mail or electronically. In the notification, the
24 department shall state its reasons for accepting or denying the application.
25 (2) If a property owner is aggrieved by the determination made by the department after the review
26 provided for in subsection (1), the property owner has the right to first appeal to the county tax appeal board
27 and then to the Montana tax appeal board, whose findings are final subject to the right of review in the courts.
