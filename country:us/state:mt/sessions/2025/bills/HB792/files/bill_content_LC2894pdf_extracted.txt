4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO MARIJUANA
5 LABELING AND EDUCATION; REQUIRING WARNING POSTERS AT POINTS OF MARIJUANA SALE;
6 PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 16-12-112 AND 16-12-215, MCA.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 Section 1. Section 16-12-112, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "16-12-112. Rulemaking authority -- fees. (1) The department may adopt rules to implement and
12 administer this chapter, including:
13 (a) the manner in which the department will consider applications for licenses, permits, and
14 endorsements and renewal of licenses, permits, and endorsements;
15 (b) the acceptable forms of proof of Montana residency;
16 (c) the procedures for obtaining fingerprints for the fingerprint-based and name-based background
17 checks required under 16-12-129;
18 (d) the security and operating requirements for licensees;
19 (e) the security and operating requirements for manufacturing, including but not limited to
20 requirements for:
21 (i) safety equipment;
22 (ii) extraction methods, including solvent-based and solvent-free extraction; and
23 (iii) post-processing procedures;
24 (f) notice and contested case hearing procedures for fines or license and endorsement
25 revocations, suspensions, or modifications;
26 (g) implementation of a system to allow the tracking of marijuana and marijuana products as
27 required by 16-12-105;
28 (h) labeling and packaging standards that protect public health by requiring the listing of

2 content, the THC and other cannabinoid amount in milligrams per serving, the number of servings per package,
3 and quantity limits per sale to comply with the allowable possession amount;
4 (i) investigating and making rules to limit, if necessary, the appropriate THC potency percentages
5 for marijuana and marijuana products;

7 have required warning labels as set forth in 16-12-215, and that marijuana and marijuana products be sold in
8 resealable, child-resistant exit packaging to protect public health as provided in 16-12-208;
9 (k) requirements for the warning posters that must be displayed at any point of sale pursuant to

12 to 16-12-215.
13 (k) (m) requirements and standards for the testing and retesting of marijuana and marijuana products,

18 accessories that are unsafe or contaminated;
19 (n) (p) the activities that constitute advertising in violation of 16-12-211 and requirements and

27 and other fees necessary to administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter. The fees established by the

4 (2) The department may not adopt any rule or regulation that is unduly burdensome or undermines

6 (3) The department may consult or contract with other public agencies in carrying out its duties
7 under this chapter."
8
9 Section 2. Section 16-12-215, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "16-12-215. Required warning labels and posters. (1) A person may not manufacture, package,
11 sell, or transfer any marijuana or marijuana product unless the package containing the marijuana or marijuana
12 product bears the following statements in a form required by the department a warning label. The text of the
13 warning label must be displayed in a legible font, such as times new roman, arial, or helvetica, and the
14 lowercase letter "o" must be at least one-sixteenth of an inch in height. The statement must be placed next to a
15 yellow triangle with an exclamation point and must read:
16 (1) (a) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana may cause anxiety, agitation, paranoia, psychosis, and
17 cannabinoid hyperemesis excessive vomiting."
18 (2) (b) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana by pregnant women may result in fetal injury and low
19 birth weight."
20 (3) (c) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana by nursing mothers may result in infant hyperactivity
21 and poor cognitive function."
22 (d) "WARNING: Driving while under the influence of marijuana is a crime."
23 (2) Each warning statement must be printed on all forms of marijuana exit packaging.
24 (3) In addition to the requirements of subsection (1), a package of marijuana product with a THC
25 content over 10% must include in red, bold print "WARNING: This product contains high-potency THC over
26 10%."
27 (4) A marijuana business shall display at any point of sale a poster that includes:
28 (a) graphic depictions of all text warnings; and
- 3 - LC 2894

1 (b) all text warnings pursuant to this section.
2 (5) Each label and poster must provide a website address or phone number for a resource that
3 provides assistance with overcoming marijuana addiction."
