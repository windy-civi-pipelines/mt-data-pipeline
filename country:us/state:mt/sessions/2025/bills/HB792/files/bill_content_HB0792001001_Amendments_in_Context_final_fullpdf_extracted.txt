5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO MARIJUANA
6 LABELING AND EDUCATION; REQUIRING WARNING POSTERS AT POINTS OF MARIJUANA SALE;
7 PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 16-12-112 AND 16-12-215, MCA;
8 AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 16-12-112, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "16-12-112. Rulemaking authority -- fees. (1) The department may adopt rules to implement and
14 administer this chapter, including:
15 (a) the manner in which the department will consider applications for licenses, permits, and
16 endorsements and renewal of licenses, permits, and endorsements;
17 (b) the acceptable forms of proof of Montana residency;
18 (c) the procedures for obtaining fingerprints for the fingerprint-based and name-based background
19 checks required under 16-12-129;
20 (d) the security and operating requirements for licensees;
21 (e) the security and operating requirements for manufacturing, including but not limited to
22 requirements for:
23 (i) safety equipment;
24 (ii) extraction methods, including solvent-based and solvent-free extraction; and
25 (iii) post-processing procedures;
26 (f) notice and contested case hearing procedures for fines or license and endorsement
27 revocations, suspensions, or modifications;

3 (h) labeling and packaging standards that protect public health by requiring the listing of

6 and quantity limits per sale to comply with the allowable possession amount;

11 resealable, child-resistant exit packaging to protect public health as provided in 16-12-208;
12 (k) requirements for the warning posters that must be displayed at any point of sale pursuant to
13 16-12-215;
14 (l) recommended resources to provide assistance with overcoming marijuana addiction pursuant
15 to 16-12-215.
16 (k) (m) requirements and standards for the testing and retesting of marijuana and marijuana products,
17 including testing of samples collected during the department's inspections of licensed premises;
18 (l) (n) the amount of variance allowable in the results of raw testing data that would warrant a
19 departmental investigation of inconsistent results as provided in 16-12-202;
20 (m) (o) requirements and standards to prohibit or limit marijuana, marijuana products, and marijuana
21 accessories that are unsafe or contaminated;

1 (q) (s) the fees for testing laboratories, the fingerprint-based and name-based background checks

3 and other fees necessary to administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter. The fees established by the

13 Section 2. Section 16-12-215, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "16-12-215. Required warning labels and posters. (1) A person may not manufacture, package,
15 sell, or transfer any marijuana or marijuana product unless the package containing the marijuana or marijuana
16 product bears the following statements in a form required by the department a warning label. The text of the
17 warning label must be displayed in a legible font, such as times new roman, arial, or helvetica, and the
18 lowercase letter "o" must be at least one-sixteenth of an inch in height. The statement must be placed next to a
19 yellow triangle with an exclamation point the universal warning symbol for marijuana and must read:
20 (1) (a) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana may cause anxiety, agitation, paranoia, psychosis, and
21 cannabinoid hyperemesis excessive vomiting."
22 (2) (b) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana by pregnant women may result in fetal injury and low
23 birth weight."
24 (3) (c) "WARNING: Consumption of marijuana by nursing mothers may result in infant hyperactivity
25 and poor cognitive function."
26 (d) "WARNING: Driving while under the influence of marijuana is a crime."
27 (2) Each warning statement must be printed on all forms of marijuana exit packaging.

1 (3) In addition to the requirements of subsection (1), a package of marijuana product with a THC
2 content over 10% must include in red, bold print "WARNING: This product contains high-potency THC over
3 10%."
4 (4)(3) A marijuana business shall display at any point of sale a poster that includes:
5 (a) graphic depictions of all text warnings; and
6 (b) all text warnings pursuant to this section.
7 (5)(4) Each label and poster must provide a website address or phone number for a resource that
8 provides assistance with overcoming marijuana addiction."

10 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective January 1, 2026.
