8 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO AQUATIC
9 INVASIVE SPECIES MANAGEMENT; REQUIRING AQUATIC INVASIVE SPECIES AWARENESS TRAINING;
10 REQUIRING ALL VESSEL OPERATORS OBTAIN AN AQUATIC INVASIVE SPECIES PREVENTION PASS;
11 REVISING PENALTIES RELATED TO TRANSPORTING AQUATIC INVASIVE SPECIES; PROVIDING
12 RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AMENDING SECTIONS 23-2-541 AND 80-7-1014, MCA; AND PROVIDING A
13 DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Aquatic invasive species awareness training -- requirements. (1) A
18 person may not be issued an aquatic invasive species prevention pass, as required pursuant to 23-2-541 or 87-
19 2-130, unless the person authorized to issue the pass determines proof of completion of a Montana aquatic
20 invasive species awareness and education course established in subsection (2).
21 (2) The department shall provide for an aquatic invasive species awareness and education course
22 that includes instruction on how to prevent the transfer of aquatic invasive species, Montana aquatic invasive
23 species regulations, and Montana inspection before launch requirements. The course may not take longer than
24 an average of 30 minutes to complete and must consist of:
25 (a) an educational video produced by the department; and
26 (b) a test on the contents of the video.
27 (3) The department may adopt rules regarding how a person authorized to issue a pass

4 Section 2. Section 23-2-541, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "23-2-541. Aquatic invasive species prevention pass for nonresident vessels -- rulemaking. (1)
6 In order for a motorized vessel exempt from registration in Montana pursuant to 61-3-321 or a nonmotorized
7 vessel owned by a nonresident to launch on the waters of this state, the operator must possess an aquatic
8 invasive species prevention pass purchased for the vessel, available for inspection either in physical form or as
9 an electronic copy at the request of a warden, another officer, or an employee of the department. The pass
10 must include a description of the vessel for which it was purchased.
11 (2) (a) The annual fee for an aquatic invasive species prevention pass purchased pursuant to this
12 section is:
13 (i) $10 for a nonmotorized vessel; and
14 (ii) $30 for a motorized vessel.
15 (b) The pass expires at the end of each calendar year and is not transferable between vessels.
16 (3) Fees collected pursuant to this section must be deposited in the invasive species account
17 established in 80-7-1004.
18 (4) The department may adopt rules to implement the provisions of this section.
19 (5) The provisions of this section do not apply to a motorized vessel owned or controlled by the
20 United States or any state, county, city, special district as defined in 18-8-202, or tribal government or to a
21 motorized vessel that meets the description of property exempt from taxation under 15-6-201(1)(d), (1)(n), or
22 (1)(o) or 15-6-215.
23 (6) For the purposes of this section, the term "nonmotorized vessel" includes canoes, catamarans,
24 drift boats, kayaks, rafts, and sailboats."
25
