10 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CAMPAIGN FINANCE LAWS; PROHIBITING A
11 FOREIGN NATIONAL FROM INTERFERING IN STATEWIDE BALLOT ISSUES; PROVIDING
12 INVESTIGATORY AUTHORITY TO THE COMMISSIONER OF POLITICAL PRACTICES OR AN
13 APPROPRIATE COUNTY ATTORNEY; CREATING A CAUSE OF ACTION; PROVIDING PENALTIES; AND

19 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Prohibition on foreign national interference in statewide ballot
20 issues. (1) A foreign national may not, directly or indirectly through any person or entity, do the following:
21 (a) make a contribution, expenditure, or independent expenditure in support of or in opposition to a
22 statewide ballot issue, regardless of whether the ballot issue has been certified to appear on the ballot;
23 (b) make a disbursement for the direct cost of producing or airing an election communication or an
24 electioneering communication; or
25 (c) make or solicit a contribution to a candidate, campaign committee, political action committee,
26 political contributing entity, legislative campaign fund, state candidate fund, political party, separate segregated
27 fund, or committee created to support or to oppose a ballot issue or, to the maximum extent permitted by law
28 and by the constitutions of the United States and Montana, to a continuing association; or

11 knows were received from a foreign national for any purpose described in this section.

16 sources within the 4-year period immediately preceding the date the contribution is made.

18 obligations under 13-37-207 and 13-37-208.

20 support or to oppose a ballot issue under 13-37-225 through 13-37-229 must be amended to include an
21 affirmation, submitted as an attachment to each finance report, that the committee has not knowingly received,
22 solicited, or accepted contributions or expenditures from a prohibited source.

24 opposition to a ballot issue shall submit an affirmation to the commissioner of political practices that it has not
25 knowingly accepted funds aggregating in excess of $100,000 from one or more prohibited sources within the 4-
26 year period immediately preceding the date on which the expenditure is made and that it will not do so through
27 the date of the election in which the ballot issue will appear on the ballot.

5 "13-37-201. Campaign treasurer. (1) Except as provided in 13-37-206, each candidate, each political
6 committee, and each joint fundraising committee shall appoint one campaign treasurer and certify the full name
7 and complete address of the campaign treasurer pursuant to this section.
8 (2) (a) A candidate shall file the certification within 5 days after becoming a candidate.
9 (b) Except as provided in subsection (2)(c), a political committee and a joint fundraising committee
10 shall file the certification, which must include an organizational statement and the name and address of all
11 officers, if any, within 5 days after it makes an expenditure or authorizes another person to make an
12 expenditure on its behalf, whichever occurs first. A joint fundraising committee shall also provide a list of
13 participants with the certification.
14 (c) A political committee that is seeking to place a ballot issue before the electors shall file the
15 certification, including the information required in subsection (2)(b), within 5 days after the proposed issue is
16 submitted to the secretary of state under 13-27-214.
17 (3) The certification of a candidate, political committee, or joint fundraising committee must be filed
18 with the commissioner.
19 (4) A person or entity filing the certification required under this section for a political committee
20 provided in subsection (2)(c) or for a political committee organized to support or oppose a ballot issue shall
21 include, at the time of filing, an accompanying certification that no preliminary activity was funded by prohibited
22 sources, whether directly or indirectly, as those terms are defined in [section 1]. Preliminary activity includes but
23 is not limited to conducting a poll, drafting ballot measure language, focus groups, telephone calls, and travel."

1 (2) "Foreign national" means:
2 (a) a government of a foreign country;
3 (b) a political party of a foreign country;
4 (c) an entity located outside of the United States unless that entity:
5 (i) is organized under or created under federal law, state law, or the law of another place subject

7 (ii) has its principal place of business within the United States;
8 (d) an entity that:
9 (i) is organized under the laws of a foreign country; or

18 (i) any contribution or expenditure it makes derives entirely from funds generated by the U.S.
19 entity’s U.S. operations; and
20 (ii) all decisions concerning the contribution or expenditure are made by individuals who are U.S.
21 citizens or permanent residents, except for setting overall budget amounts."
