5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO DOULAS;
6 PROVIDING FOR CERTIFICATION LICENSURE AND REGULATION OF DOULAS; ESTABLISHING
7 VOLUNTARY CERTIFICATION LICENSURE REQUIREMENTS; ALLOWING THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC
8 HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES TO PROVIDE MEDICAID COVERAGE OF STATE-CERTIFIED LICENSED
9 DOULA SERVICES; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AMENDING SECTIONS 37-1-401 AND 53-6-
10 101, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 4], the following definitions
15 apply:
16 (1) "Antepartum" means the period of pregnancy prior to labor and delivery THE END OF PREGNANCY.
17 (2) "Department" means the department of labor and industry provided for in Title 2, chapter 15,
18 part 17.
19 (3) (A) "Doula" means a trained, nonmedical professional who provides continuous physical,
20 emotional, and informational support to a pregnant person WOMAN during the antepartum, or intrapartum, period
21 or during the period up to 1 year postpartum PERIOD.
22 (B) THE TERM DOES NOT INCLUDE A TRADITIONAL HEALING PROVIDER OR A PERSON PROVIDING SERVICES
23 TO A FRIEND OR FAMILY MEMBER.
24 (4) "Intrapartum" means the period of pregnancy after the onset of labor through delivery.
25 (5) "Postpartum" means the 12-month period beginning on the last day of the pregnancy.
26 (6) "State-certified doula" means a doula who has been certified by the department.
27 (6) "TRADITIONAL HEALING PROVIDER" MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO PROVIDES TRADITIONAL HEALING
28 SERVICES IN A MANNER THAT IS RECOGNIZED BY AN AMERICAN INDIAN OR ALASKA NATIVE TRIBE AS BEING CONSISTENT

1 WITH THE TRIBE'S TRADITIONAL HEALING PRACTICES.
2 (7) "TRADITIONAL HEALING SERVICES" MEANS A SYSTEM OF CULTURALLY APPROPRIATE HEALING METHODS
3 FOR PHYSICAL, MENTAL, AND EMOTIONAL HEALING.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Certification LICENSE requirements -- coordination with doulas. (1) A
6 BEGINNING JANUARY 1, 2027, A person may not practice as a state-certified doula unless certified LICENSED
7 under Title 37, chapter 1, and [sections 1 through 4].
8 (2) A doula may voluntarily apply for certification from the department under this section.
9 (3)(2) The department shall certify LICENSE a person as a state-certified doula if the person pays the
10 fees prescribed by the department and:
11 (a) submits a completed application as required by the department;
12 (b) satisfactorily completes competencies that meet the requirements established by the
13 department by rule;
14 (c) has not engaged in unprofessional conduct as defined in 37-1-410; and
15 (d) is not currently subject to any disciplinary proceedings.
16 (3) THE DEPARTMENT MAY ISSUE A LIMITED LICENSE TO QUALIFIED INDIVIDUALS ENGAGED IN SUPERVISED
17 PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE AS DEFINED BY RULE.
18 (4) The department shall coordinate with doulas to establish certification requirements and oversee
19 the certification of state-certified doulas.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Voluntary certification -- practice as doula without certification
22 permitted. Nothing in [sections 1 through 4] prohibits a person from practicing as a doula without obtaining
23 certification under [section 2].
24
25 NEW SECTION. SECTION 3. EXEMPTIONS. [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 4] DO NOT PROHIBIT A QUALIFIED
26 MEMBER OF ANOTHER PROFESSION, BUSINESS, EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM, OR VOLUNTEER ORGANIZATION WHO IS NOT
27 LICENSED OR CERTIFIED OR FOR WHOM THERE IS NO APPLICABLE CODE OF ETHICS, INCLUDING PEER MENTORS,
28 ADVOCATES, AND COACHES, FROM PERFORMING DUTIES AND SERVICES CONSISTENT WITH THE PERSON'S TRAINING, AS

14 SECTION, AN INDIVIDUAL SHALL DEMONSTRATE THE EVIDENCE TO THE DEPARTMENT ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 31,
15 2027.
