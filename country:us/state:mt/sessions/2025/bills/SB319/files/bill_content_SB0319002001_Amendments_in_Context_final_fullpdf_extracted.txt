14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 4], the following definitions
15 apply:
16 (1) "Antepartum" means the period of pregnancy prior to labor and delivery the end of pregnancy.
17 (2) "Department" means the department of labor and industry provided for in Title 2, chapter 15,
18 part 17.
19 (3) (a) "Doula" means a trained, nonmedical professional who provides continuous physical,
20 emotional, and informational support to a pregnant person WOMAN during the antepartum, or intrapartum, period
21 or during the period up to 1 year postpartum period.
22 (b) The term does not include a traditional healing provider or a person providing services to a
23 friend or family member.
24 (4) "Intrapartum" means the period of pregnancy after the onset of labor through delivery.
25 (5) "Postpartum" means the 12-month period beginning on the last day of the pregnancy.
26 (6) "State-certified doula" means a doula who has been certified by the department.
27 (6) "Traditional healing provider" means an individual who provides traditional healing services in a
28 manner that is recognized by an American Indian or Alaska Native tribe as being consistent with the tribe's

12 licensure defined by department rule. To be eligible for licensure under this section, an individual shall
13 demonstrate the evidence to the department on or before December 31, 2027.
