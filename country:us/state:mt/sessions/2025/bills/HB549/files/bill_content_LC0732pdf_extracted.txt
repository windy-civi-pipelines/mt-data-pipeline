4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE LAWS;
5 ALLOWING A MONTANA DISTILLERY LICENSEE TO MARKET AND SELL INTERESTS IN AGING LIQUOR
6 THROUGH WAREHOUSE RECEIPTS; ALLOWING THE RESALE OF LIQUOR WAREHOUSE RECEIPTS;
7 ALLOWING MANAGEMENT AND BROKERING OF WAREHOUSE RECEIPT SALES; PROVIDING THAT
8 OWNERSHIP OF WAREHOUSE RECEIPTS FOR LIQUOR DOES NOT CONSTITUTE AN OWNERSHIP
9 INTEREST IN THE DISTILLERY BUSINESS OR LICENSEE; AMENDING SECTIONS 16-4-312 AND 16-4-
10 401, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 16-4-312, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "16-4-312. Domestic distillery. (1) A distillery located in Montana and licensed pursuant to 16-4-311
16 may:
17 (a) import necessary products in bulk;
18 (b) bottle, produce, blend, store, transport, or export liquor that it produces;
19 (c) perform those operations that are permitted for bonded distillery premises under applicable
20 regulations of the United States department of the treasury; and
21 (d) have a premises that includes more than one building for manufacturing purposes pursuant to
22 27 CFR 19.53 and is operated under a federal basic permit.
23 (2) (a) A distillery that is located in Montana and licensed pursuant to 16-4-311 shall sell liquor to
24 the department under this code, and the department shall include the distillery's liquor as a listed product.
25 (b) The distillery may use a common carrier for delivery of the liquor to the department.
26 (c) A distillery that produces liquor within the state under this subsection (2) shall maintain records
27 of all sales and shipments. The distillery shall furnish monthly and other reports concerning quantities and
28 prices of liquor that it ships to the department and other information that the department may determine to be

1 necessary to ensure that distribution of liquor within this state conforms to the requirements of this code.
2 (3) (a) A distillery that is located in Montana and licensed to manufacture distilled spirits may be
3 licensed by the department to own, lease, maintain, and operate anywhere in the state a storage depot for
4 receiving, handling, and storing distilled spirits in addition to distributing and selling distilled spirits from the
5 storage depot, subject to this code.
6 (b) To be licensed for a storage depot, a distillery shall pay an annual license fee as provided in
7 16-4-501 for each storage depot operated by the distillery, in addition to all other fees and taxes required to be
8 paid by the distillery, and must meet all applicable suitability requirements.
9 (4) A microdistillery may:
10 (a) provide, with or without charge, not more than 2 ounces of liquor that it produces at the
11 microdistillery to consumers for prepared servings:
12 (i) through curbside pickup between 10 a.m. and 8 p.m.; and
13 (ii) for on-premises consumption during the hours of operation that are identical to those allowed
14 for a brewery license provided for in 16-3-213(2)(b) and corresponding administrative rules relating to the
15 service, consumption, and possession of alcoholic beverages on the premises; or
16 (b) sell liquor in original packaging that it produces at retail at the distillery between the hours of 8
17 a.m. and 2 a.m. directly to the consumer, including curbside pickup, for off-premises consumption if:
18 (i) not more than 4.5 liters a day is sold to an individual; and
19 (ii) the minimum retail price as determined by the department is charged.
20 (5) Liquor samples provided pursuant to subsection (4)(a) are not permitted at more than one
21 manufacturing premises for each license.
22 (6) (a) A distillery located in the state and licensed pursuant to 16-4-311 may issue or sell
23 warehouse receipts to any person. The warehouse receipt is for liquor stored at the distillery's licensed
24 premises, including a licensed warehouse premises of the distillery, within the portion of the premises that is
25 bonded pursuant to federal law, as provided in Title 30, chapter 7, except that for the purposes of this
26 subsection (6), the term "distilled spirits" in 30-7-201(2) means "liquor" as defined in 16-1-106. The sale or
27 purchase of warehouse receipts as provided in this subsection (6) does not constitute a transfer of an
28 ownership interest in the distillery business or license.

1 (b) The department may not require any application or report to be filed in connection with the sale
2 or purchase of warehouse receipts, provided that the liquor to which the warehouse receipts apply must
3 physically remain within the distillery's licensed premises, including a licensed warehouse premises of the
4 distillery, until the liquor is physically transferred in bond to another federally bonded premises, or until the
5 liquor is otherwise transferred, sold, or disposed of in accordance with this code or applicable federal law.
6 (c) Warehouse receipts originally issued by a distillery as provided in this subsection (6) may be
7 resold by a holder of the warehouse receipts to any person. A distillery or any subsequent holder of warehouse
8 receipts may use an intermediary business to market, facilitate, manage, sell, and invest in warehouse receipts.

10 distilleries licensed under federal law, regardless of whether the distillery is located in the state. Nothing in this
11 section authorizes the holder or owner of a warehouse receipt to accept delivery of any liquor unless the person
12 is permitted under law to receive the liquor."

24 with all applicable laws of the state and local governments;
25 (ii) the applicant has not been convicted of a felony or, if the applicant has been convicted of a
26 felony, the applicant's rights have been restored; and
27 (iii) the applicant is not under 19 years of age;
