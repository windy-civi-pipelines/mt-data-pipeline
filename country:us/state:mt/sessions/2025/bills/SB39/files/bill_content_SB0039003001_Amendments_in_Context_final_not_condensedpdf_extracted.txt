1 SENATE BILL NO. 39
2 INTRODUCED BY G. HERTZ
3 BY REQUEST OF THE SENATE SELECT COMMITTEE ON JUDICIAL OVERSIGHT AND REFORM
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE AWARD OF ATTORNEY FEES IN CIVIL
6 LAWSUITS; REQUIRING THAT ALL CERTAIN AWARDS OF ATTORNEY FEES IN CASES CHALLENGING A
7 STATE STATUTE OR ADMINISTRATIVE RULE MUST BE REASONABLE; PROVIDING REQUIREMENTS
8 FOR REASONABLE ATTORNEY FEE AWARD REQUESTS; AMENDING SECTION 25-10-711, MCA; AND
9 PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Requirements for attorney fee award request -- awards against
14 state. (1) An award of attorney fees in cases in which the attorney fee claims are less than $10,000 IN CASES
15 CHALLENGING A STATE STATUTE OR ADMINISTRATIVE RULE must be reasonable. A reasonable claim for attorney
16 fees must include the following:
17 (a) the date a task is performed and the name of the timekeeper;
18 (b) a specific description of the task, including but not limited to the nature, purpose, or subject of
19 the work performed. Generic descriptors for a task and the work claimed are unreasonable. The description of
20 the task performed must be sufficient for a reasonable person to understand what work is being claimed.
21 (c) time that is billed in increments of tenths of an hour, including:
22 (i) a statement indicating whether the attorney has or has had a client who pays the hourly rate
23 requested by the attorney; and
24 (ii) a detailed list of time spent on each task. Block billing or aggregating of tasks may not be
25 claimed.
26 (d) a specific description of the work performed;
27 (e) an itemized list of expenses, including receipts from vendors;

1 (f) in-house copying expenses that may not exceed 10 cents a page; and
2 (g) an indication of whether a portion of the attorney fee has been paid and whether the client is
3 responsible for paying a portion of the bill if the attorney does not collect the award.
4 (2) There is a rebuttable presumption that the following are unreasonable requests for attorney
5 fees:
6 (a) sending more than one attorney to a deposition;
7 (b) sending more than two attorneys to an oral argument or trial;
8 (c) charging for THE COST OF telephone calls, faxes, library facilities, or electronic research
9 subscriptions; and
10 (d) charging an hourly rate greater than the statewide average if the attorney has not been
11 practicing for more than 10 years.
12 (3) In addition to the requirements imposed by subsections (1) and (2), when considering an award
13 of attorney fees against the state, the court shall take into consideration the hourly rate charged by the attorney
14 representing the state. If the court elects to grant a rate higher than the rate charged by the attorney
15 representing the state, the court shall provide an explanation as to why the rate charged by the state does not
16 adequately compensate the attorney for which attorney fees are requested.
17 (4) In determining the reasonableness of an award of attorney fees, a court may consider factors
18 other than those contained in this section.
19
20 Section 2. Section 25-10-711, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "25-10-711. Award of costs against governmental entity when suit or defense is frivolous or
22 pursued in bad faith. (1) In any civil action brought by or against the state, a political subdivision, or an agency
23 of the state or a political subdivision, the opposing party, whether plaintiff or defendant, is entitled to the costs
24 enumerated in 25-10-201 and reasonable attorney fees as determined by the court if:
25 (a) the opposing party prevails against the state, political subdivision, or agency; and
26 (b) the court finds that the claim or defense of the state, political subdivision, or agency that
27 brought or defended the action was frivolous or pursued in bad faith.

1 (2) Costs may be granted pursuant to subsection (1) notwithstanding any other provision of the law
2 to the contrary. The award of costs must be reasonable and considered under [section 1]."
3
4 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
5 integral part of Title 25, chapter 10, and the provisions of Title 25, chapter 10, apply to [section 1].
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Applicability. [This act] applies to awards of attorney fees in civil
8 lawsuits that are filed pending on or after [the effective date of this act].
