5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CLARIFYING CERTAIN BONDING REQUIREMENTS FOR WIND
6 AND SOLAR GENERATION FACILITIES; PROVIDING FOR A RECLAMATION LIEN; AMENDING SECTION
7 75-26-304, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 75-26-304, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "75-26-304. Bond -- penalty for failure to submit. (1) (a) Within 12 months of a wind generation
13 facility or solar facility commencing commercial operation, the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility
14 operating in Montana shall:
15 (i) notify the department in writing of the date that the facility began commercial operation;
16 (ii) subject to subsection (2), submit a plan for decommissioning the facility to the department,
17 including the scope of work to be completed and cost estimates for completion; and
18 (iii) provide the department with any other necessary information in accordance with this part and
19 rules adopted pursuant to this part in order for the department to determine bond requirements in accordance
20 with this section.
21 (b) Except as provided in subsection (1)(c), if a wind generation facility or solar facility commenced
22 commercial operation before May 7, 2019, the owner of the facility shall submit to the department the
23 information required in subsection (1)(a) on or before July 1, 2020.
24 (c) If a wind generation facility commenced commercial operation before May 7, 2019, and the
25 owner of the facility submitted information required by subsection (1)(a) on or before July 1, 2018, the owner is
26 not required to resubmit the information.
27 (2) If a property owner and the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility reach an

4 subsection (4) in the event a lease is terminated or not renewed by the facility owner or the landowner.
5 (7) If a wind generation facility or solar facility is repurposed, as determined by the department in
6 consultation with the owner, the owner is not required to provide a bond, and any existing bond must be
7 released until the repurposed facility reaches its 5th year of operation.
8 (8) An owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility is exempt from the requirements of
9 subsection (6) if:
10 (a) for that portion of a wind generation facility or solar facility, the owner posts has posted a
11 decommissioning bond with a federal agency, with the department of natural resources and conservation for
12 the lease of state land, or with a tribal, county, or local government;
13 (b) the owner furnishes documents to the department that prove the owner is responsible under
14 the terms and conditions of a lease agreement to provide private bonding. The parties shall agree that release
15 of the agreed upon on bond is subject to the approval of the department upon on completion of reclamation.
16 (c) the private landowner on whose land the wind generation facility or solar facility is located owns
17 a 10% or greater share of the wind generation facility or solar facility, as determined by the department; or
18 (d) the facility:

20 and has less than 25 megawatts in nameplate capacity; or
21 (ii) commenced commercial operation on or before January 1, 2020, is a solar facility, and has less
22 than 2 megawatts in nameplate capacity.
23 (9) (a) If the owner of the wind generation facility or solar facility fails to submit a decommissioning
24 bond acceptable to the department within the timeframe required by this section, the department shall provide
25 notice to the facility owner. If after 30 days the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility has not

1 decommissioning bond continues.
2 (b) The owner of the wind generation facility or solar facility may appeal the department's penalty
3 assessment to the board within 20 days after receipt of written notice of the penalty. The contested case
4 provisions of the Montana Administrative Procedure Act, Title 2, chapter 4, part 6, apply to a hearing before the
5 board under this subsection (9).
6 (10) If the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility transfers ownership of the facility to a
7 successor owner, the first owner's bond must be released after 90 days. The new owner shall submit any
8 necessary bond within 90 days after transfer of ownership or be subject to penalties in accordance with this
9 section.
10 (11) Once every 5 years after a facility is bonded, the owner of a wind generation facility or solar
11 facility may submit an amended plan for the department's approval. As part of the submission, the owner of a
12 wind generation facility or solar facility may also apply to the department for a reduction in the amount of the
13 decommissioning bond applicable to the wind energy facility or solar facility. The owner's application to the
14 department must include a detailed description of any material changes to information considered by the
15 department in setting the initial amount of the bond and may include an amended decommissioning plan for the
16 department's approval.
17 (12) Submitting a bond in accordance with this section does not absolve the owner of a wind
18 generation facility or solar facility from complying with applicable regulations and requirements for:
19 (a) areas subject to local zoning adopted under Title 76, chapter 2;
20 (b) military affected areas under Title 10, chapter 1, part 15; or
21 (c) airport affected areas under Title 67, chapter 7."
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Reclamation lien. The day before a wind generation facility or solar
24 facility files for bankruptcy, the state of Montana has a lien on generation assets of a wind generation facility or
25 solar facility for the amount of reclamation costs in a decommissioning plan filed as required by 75-26-304.
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.

2 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Section 2] is intended to be codified as an

5 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Applicability. [This act] applies to a wind generation facility or solar
6 facility finally constructed on or after October 1, 2025.
