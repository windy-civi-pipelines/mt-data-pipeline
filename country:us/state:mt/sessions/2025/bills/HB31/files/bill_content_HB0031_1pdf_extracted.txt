69th Legislature 2025 HB 31.1
2 INTRODUCED BY N. DURAM
3 BY REQUEST OF THE DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CLARIFYING CERTAIN BONDING REQUIREMENTS FOR WIND
6 AND SOLAR GENERATION FACILITIES; AMENDING SECTION 75-26-304, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
7 IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 75-26-304, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "75-26-304. Bond -- penalty for failure to submit. (1) (a) Within 12 months of a wind generation
13 facility or solar facility commencing commercial operation, the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility
14 operating in Montana shall:
15 (i) notify the department in writing of the date that the facility began commercial operation;
16 (ii) subject to subsection (2), submit a plan for decommissioning the facility to the department,
17 including the scope of work to be completed and cost estimates for completion; and
18 (iii) provide the department with any other necessary information in accordance with this part and
19 rules adopted pursuant to this part in order for the department to determine bond requirements in accordance
20 with this section.
21 (b) Except as provided in subsection (1)(c), if a wind generation facility or solar facility commenced
22 commercial operation before May 7, 2019, the owner of the facility shall submit to the department the
23 information required in subsection (1)(a) on or before July 1, 2020.
24 (c) If a wind generation facility commenced commercial operation before May 7, 2019, and the
25 owner of the facility submitted information required by subsection (1)(a) on or before July 1, 2018, the owner is
26 not required to resubmit the information.
27 (2) If a property owner and the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility reach an
28 agreement concerning alternative restoration of buildings, cabling, electrical components, roads, or any other
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 31
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 31.1
1 associated facilities, instead of removal, or alternative plans for reclamation of surface lands, or both,
2 decommissioning does not include removal, plans for reclamation, or both, as long as a copy of the agreement
3 is provided to the department.
4 (3) (a) If necessary, the department may modify a plan for decommissioning to determine bond
5 requirements in accordance with subsections (4) through (8).
6 (b) The department shall notify the owner of the facility of any modification. The owner of the wind
7 generation facility or solar facility may appeal a modification by the department of a plan for decommissioning to
8 the board within 60 days of receiving notice of the modification to the plan.
9 (4) In determining the amount of a bond required in accordance with subsection (6), the
10 department shall consider:
11 (a) the character and nature of the site where the wind generation facility or solar facility is located;
12 and
13 (b) the current market salvage value of the wind generation facility or solar facility, as determined
14 by an independent evaluator.
15 (5) Except as provided in subsections (7) and (8) and in accordance with subsection (6), the owner
16 of a wind generation facility or solar facility shall submit to the department a bond payable to the state of
17 Montana in a form acceptable by the department and in the sum determined by the department, conditioned on
18 the faithful decommissioning of the wind generation facility or solar facility.
19 (6) (a) Except as provided in subsections (7) and (8), if a wind generation facility or solar facility
20 commenced commercial operation on or before January 1, 2007, the operator shall submit the
21 decommissioning bond to the department prior to the conclusion of the 16th year of operation of the wind
22 generation facility or solar facility.
23 (b) Except as provided in subsections (7) and (8), if a wind generation facility or solar facility
24 commenced commercial operation after January 1, 2007, the operator shall submit the decommissioning bond
25 to the department prior to the conclusion of the 15th year of operation of the wind generation facility or solar
26 facility.
27 (7) If a wind generation facility or solar facility is repurposed, as determined by the department in
28 consultation with the owner, the owner is not required to provide a bond, and any existing bond must be
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 31

69th Legislature 2025 HB 31.1
1 released until the repurposed facility reaches its 5th year of operation.
2 (8) An owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility is exempt from the requirements of
3 subsection (6) if:
4 (a) for that portion of a wind generation facility or solar facility, the owner posts has posted a
5 decommissioning bond with a federal agency, with the department of natural resources and conservation for
6 the lease of state land, or with a tribal, county, or local government;
7 (b) the owner furnishes documents to the department that prove the owner is responsible under
8 the terms and conditions of a lease agreement to provide private bonding. The parties shall agree that release
9 of the agreed upon on bond is subject to the approval of the department upon on completion of reclamation.
10 (c) the private landowner on whose land the wind generation facility or solar facility is located owns
11 a 10% or greater share of the wind generation facility or solar facility, as determined by the department; or
12 (d) the facility:
13 (i) commenced commercial operation on or before January 1, 2018, is a wind generation facility,
14 and has less than 25 megawatts in nameplate capacity; or
15 (ii) commenced commercial operation on or before January 1, 2020, is a solar facility, and has less
16 than 2 megawatts in nameplate capacity.
17 (9) (a) If the owner of the wind generation facility or solar facility fails to submit a decommissioning
18 bond acceptable to the department within the timeframe required by this section, the department shall provide
19 notice to the facility owner. If after 30 days the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility has not
20 submitted a decommissioning bond, the department may assess an administrative penalty of not more than
21 $1,500 and an additional administrative penalty of not more than $1,500 for each day the failure to submit the
22 decommissioning bond continues.
23 (b) The owner of the wind generation facility or solar facility may appeal the department's penalty
24 assessment to the board within 20 days after receipt of written notice of the penalty. The contested case
25 provisions of the Montana Administrative Procedure Act, Title 2, chapter 4, part 6, apply to a hearing before the
26 board under this subsection (9).
27 (10) If the owner of a wind generation facility or solar facility transfers ownership of the facility to a
28 successor owner, the first owner's bond must be released after 90 days. The new owner shall submit any
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – HB 31
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 31.1
1 necessary bond within 90 days after transfer of ownership or be subject to penalties in accordance with this
2 section.
3 (11) Once every 5 years after a facility is bonded, the owner of a wind generation facility or solar
4 facility may submit an amended plan for the department's approval. As part of the submission, the owner of a
5 wind generation facility or solar facility may also apply to the department for a reduction in the amount of the
6 decommissioning bond applicable to the wind energy facility or solar facility. The owner's application to the
7 department must include a detailed description of any material changes to information considered by the
8 department in setting the initial amount of the bond and may include an amended decommissioning plan for the
9 department's approval.
10 (12) Submitting a bond in accordance with this section does not absolve the owner of a wind
11 generation facility or solar facility from complying with applicable regulations and requirements for:
12 (a) areas subject to local zoning adopted under Title 76, chapter 2;
13 (b) military affected areas under Title 10, chapter 1, part 15; or
14 (c) airport affected areas under Title 67, chapter 7."
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
17 - END -
