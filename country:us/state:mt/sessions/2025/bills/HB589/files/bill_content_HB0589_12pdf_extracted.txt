69th Legislature 2025 HB 589.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. GILLETTE, S. KELLY, B. USHER, D. BAUM
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING PEACE OFFICER EDUCATION
5 STANDARDS; PROVIDING THAT A QUALIFIED LAW ENFORCEMENT ACADEMY MAY PROVIDE THE
6 PEACE OFFICER BASIC COURSE EDUCATION REQUIREMENT; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY;
7 AMENDING SECTIONS 2-15-2029 AND 7-32-303, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE
8 DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 2-15-2029, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "2-15-2029. Montana public safety officer standards and training council -- administrative
14 attachment -- rulemaking. (1) (a) There is a Montana public safety officer standards and training council. The
15 council is a quasi-judicial board, as provided for in 2-15-124, and is allocated to the department of justice,
16 established in 2-15-2001, for administrative purposes only as provided in 2-15-121, except as provided in
17 subsection (1)(b) of this section.
18 (b) The council may hire its own personnel and independently administer the conduct of its
19 business, and 2-15-121(2)(a), (2)(d), and (3)(a) do not apply.
20 (2) The council may adopt rules to implement the provisions of 7-32-303 and Title 44, chapter 4,
21 part 4. Rules must be adopted pursuant to the Montana Administrative Procedure Act."
22
23 Section 2. Section 7-32-303, MCA, is amended to read:
24 "7-32-303. Peace officer employment, education, and certification standards -- suspension or
25 revocation -- penalty. (1) For the purposes of this section, unless the context clearly indicates otherwise, the
26 following definitions apply:
27 (a) "Council" means the Montana public safety officer standards and training council established in
28 2-15-2029.

1 (b) "peace officer" "Peace officer" means a deputy sheriff, undersheriff, police officer, highway
2 patrol officer, fish and game warden, park ranger, campus security officer, or airport police officer.
3 (2) A sheriff of a county, the mayor of a city, a board, a commission, or any other person
4 authorized by law to appoint peace officers in this state may not appoint a person as a peace officer who does
5 not meet the qualifications provided in this subsection (2) plus any additional qualifying standards for
6 employment promulgated by the Montana public safety officer standards and training council established in 2-
7 15-2029 council. A peace officer must:
8 (a) be a citizen of the United States;
9 (b) be at least 18 years of age;
10 (c) be fingerprinted and a search made of the local, state, and national fingerprint files to disclose
11 any criminal record;
12 (d) not have been convicted of a crime for which the person could have been imprisoned in a
13 federal or state penitentiary;
14 (e) be of good moral character, as determined by a thorough background investigation;
15 (f) be a high school graduate or have been issued a high school equivalency diploma by the
16 superintendent of public instruction or by an appropriate issuing agency of another state or of the federal
17 government;
18 (g) be free of any mental condition that might adversely affect performance of the duties of a
19 peace officer, as determined after:
20 (i) a mental health evaluation performed by a licensed physician or a mental health professional
21 who is licensed by the state under Title 37, who is acting within the scope of the person's licensure when
22 performing a mental health evaluation, who is not the applicant's personal physician or licensed mental health
23 professional, and who is selected by the employing authority; or
24 (ii) satisfactory completion of a standardized mental health evaluation instrument determined by
25 the employing authority to be sufficient to examine for any mental conditions within the meaning of this
26 subsection (2)(g), if the instrument is scored by a licensed physician or a mental health professional acting
27 within the scope of the person's licensure by a state;

1 council may consider in granting or denying the extension include but are not limited to illness of the peace
2 officer or a member of the peace officer's immediate family, absence of reasonable access to the basic
3 equivalency course, and an unreasonable shortage of personnel within the department. The council may not
4 grant an extension to exceed 180 days.
5 (10) A peace officer who has successfully met the training, employment, and educational standards
6 of this section, has successfully met the training and employment standards set by the council, and has
7 completed a 1-year probationary term of employment must be issued a peace officer basic certificate by the
8 council certifying that the peace officer has met all of the basic qualifying peace officer standards of this state.
9 (11) It is unlawful for a person whose basic certification as a peace officer has been revoked or
10 denied by the Montana public safety officer standards and training council for misconduct to act as a peace
11 officer. It is unlawful for a person whose peace officer basic certification has been suspended by the council to
12 act or be appointed or employed as a peace officer in Montana during the period in which the certification is
13 suspended. A person convicted of violating this subsection is guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a term of
14 imprisonment not to exceed 6 months in the county jail or by a fine not to exceed $500, or both.
15 (12) The council may adopt rules to implement the provisions of this section as provided in 2-15-
16 2029. The rules must be adopted pursuant to the Montana Administrative Procedure Act."
