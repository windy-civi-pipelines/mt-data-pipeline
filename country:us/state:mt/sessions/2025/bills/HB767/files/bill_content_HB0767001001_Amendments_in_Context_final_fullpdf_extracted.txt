1 HOUSE BILL NO. 767
2 INTRODUCED BY J. DARLING, E. TILLEMAN, W. MCKAMEY
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING COUNTY PREDATOR CONTROL LAWS TO INCLUDE
5 GOATS; AUTHORIZING COUNTY COMMISSIONERS TO ESTABLISH A PREDATORY ANIMAL CONTROL
6 PROGRAM FOR THE PROTECTION OF GOATS; AUTHORIZING A PER CAPITA LICENSE FEE ON GOATS;
7 REVISING COUNTY PREDATOR CONTROL LAWS TO ALIGN THE AGE THRESHOLD OF PER CAPITA
8 ANNUAL ASSESSMENT OF LIVESTOCK; PROVIDING FOR THE DEPOSIT OF PROCEEDS FROM THE
9 SALE OF PREDATORY ANIMAL SKINS TAKEN; PROVIDING FOR LOCAL GOAT OWNERS TO PETITION
10 FOR AND MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE PREDATORY ANIMAL CONTROL PROGRAM;
11 PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 81-7-303 AND 81-7-603, MCA.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 5], the following definitions
16 apply:
17 (1) "Owners" or "persons" include natural persons, partnerships, corporations, trusts, and estates.
18 (2) "Predatory animal" has the meaning provided in 81-7-101.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 2. County commissioners authorized to establish predatory animal
21 control program for protection of goats. On the recommendation of an organized association of goat
22 producers in the county, the board of county commissioners may, either alone or in conjunction with the county
23 commissioners of other counties, establish a predatory animal control program for the protection of goats in the
24 county or counties.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 3. County commissioners permitted to require per capita license fee
27 on goats. (1) To defray the expense of protection, the board of county commissioners may require all owners

1 or persons in possession of a goat 1 year 9 months of age or older in the county on the regular assessment
2 date of each year, as provided in 15-24-903, to pay a per capita license fee in an amount to be determined by
3 the board. All owners or persons in possession of a goat 1 year 9 months of age or older coming into the county
4 after the regular assessment date and subject to the per capita levy under the provisions of Title 15, chapter 24,
5 part 9, are subject to payment of the license fee.
6 (2) On the order of the board of county commissioners, the license fee may be imposed by
7 entering the name of the licensee on the assessment record of the county by the department of revenue. The
8 license fee is payable to and must be collected by the county treasurer. When levied, the fee is a lien on the
9 property, both real and personal, of the licensee. If the person against whom the license fee is levied does not
10 own real estate against which the license fee is or may become a lien, then the license fee is payable
11 immediately on its levy and the treasurer shall collect the fee in the manner provided by law for the collection of
12 personal property taxes that are not a lien on real estate.
13 (3) The fees must be placed in a predatory animal control fund, separate from the funds provided
14 for in 81-7-303 and 81-7-603. The money in the predatory animal control fund may be expended by the board
15 of county commissioners for predatory animal control, including bounties paid pursuant to Title 81, chapter 7,
16 part 2. Interest earned on money in the fund must be deposited in the fund.
17 (4) Money from any source may be deposited in the predatory animal control fund provided for in
18 this section to carry out the provisions of [sections 1 through 5].
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Proceeds of furs and skins taken. All furs, skins, and other portions of
21 predatory animals taken through the expenditure of license fees must be sold and the proceeds of the sale
22 deposited in the predatory animal control fund for use in carrying out the purpose of [sections 1 through 5].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Duty of county commissioners -- petition of goat owners -- license
25 fees. (1) In conducting a predatory animal control program, the board of county commissioners shall give
26 preference to recommendations for a program made by an organized association of goat producers in the
