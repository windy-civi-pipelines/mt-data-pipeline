16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Rules of conduct for child protection specialists. (1) A child
17 protection specialist or applicant for certification as a child protection specialist pursuant to 41-3-127 through
18 41-3-130 may not:
19 (a) threaten a person with criminal charges to deter the person from contesting any matter in a
20 child abuse and neglect proceeding; or
21 (b) prevent a person from becoming a foster placement or guardian for a child solely because that
22 person has:
23 (i) offered advice to a parent or foster parent in opposition to the department;
24 (ii) recommended that a person being investigated by the department obtain a lawyer; or
25 (iii) offered testimony to a public body, including a legislative interim committee or standing
26 committee.
27 (2) A child protection specialist or applicant for certification as a child protection specialist pursuant
28 to 41-3-127 through 41-3-130 shall, when coordinating services or evaluations for a parent or child, utilize
