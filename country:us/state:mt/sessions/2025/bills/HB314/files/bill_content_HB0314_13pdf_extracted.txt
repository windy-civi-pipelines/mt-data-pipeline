5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO THE GENERATION
6 OF ENERGY AND TRANSMISSION OF ELECTRICITY; ESTABLISHING A STATE ENERGY AUTHORITY;
7 PROVIDING THE AUTHORITY WITH CERTAIN POWERS; REQUIRING THAT STATE AGENCIES
8 COOPERATE WITH THE AUTHORITY; CREATING A SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT; STATUTORILY
9 APPROPRIATING MONEY FROM THE WHOLESALE ENERGY TRANSACTION TAX TO THE AUTHORITY
10 TO SUPPORT ITS ACTIVITIES; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING A STATUTORY APPROPRIATION;
11 AMENDING SECTIONS 15-72-106 AND 17-7-502, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”

15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 10] may be cited as the "Montana Energy
16 Authority Act".
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The legislature finds and declares that:
19 (1) Montana is endowed with significant and diverse energy resources. The development of
20 Montana's energy resources is essential to a healthy state economy.
21 (2) Montana's energy resources provide a tremendous opportunity to diversify the state's economy
22 and to stabilize and increase revenue to the state;
23 (3) energy generation and electric transmission constraints impede the development of the state's
24 energy resources;
25 (4) an essential function of government and its public purpose is to assist with the removal of
26 energy generation and electric transmission impediments to facilitate the development of Montana's energy
27 resources;
28 (5) development of Montana's energy resources promotes the public interest by increasing

1 employment, stimulating economic activity, augmenting sources of tax revenue, fostering economic stability,
2 and improving the state's economy; and
3 (6) it is in the public interest that the energy authority has the ability to facilitate the planning,
4 development, economic analysis, and coordination of energy generation and electric transmission.
5
6 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 10], unless the context
7 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
8 (1) "Authority" means the energy authority established in [section 4].
9 (2) "Department" means the department of commerce provided for in 2-15-1801.
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Energy authority -- composition -- allocation. (1) There is an energy
12 authority created to diversify and expand the state's economy by developing and advising in this state the
13 energy production facilities and the electric transmission facilities necessary to produce and transport energy to
14 markets within the state and regionally.
15 (2) The authority is allocated to the department of commerce for administrative purposes only as
16 prescribed in 2-15-121. However, the authority may hire its own executive director, and 2-15-121(2)(d) does not
17 apply.
18 (3) (a) The authority is composed of 14 members appointed by the governor in the same manner
19 as provided in 2-15-124, except as otherwise provided in this section and except that the requirement that at
20 least one member be an attorney does not apply. The authority must be representative of state and local
21 utilities, cooperatives, developers, and stakeholders engaged in the sustainment and development of the state's
22 energy future.
23 (4) (a) Except as provided in subsection (4)(b), appointed members serve staggered terms of 3
24 years.
25 (b) The initial appointments are as follows:
26 (i) four members appointed to 1-year terms;
27 (ii) four members appointed to 2-year terms; and
28 (iii) six members appointed to 3-year terms.

1 (5) In selecting the members, the governor shall:
2 (a) consider each prospective member's knowledge and understanding of the structural and
3 financial dimensions of the energy generation and electric transmission components of the state's economy;
4 (b) ensure that six members broadly represent, as evidenced by their background, experience,
5 and livelihood, the following categories of energy consumption:
6 (i) residential;
7 (ii) agricultural; and
8 (iii) commercial and industrial enterprise;
9 (c) choose two members with experience in electric transmission, distribution, and energy
10 generation issues; and
11 (d) choose two members with experience in renewable energy.
12 (6) The membership of the authority must include the following four members appointed by the
13 governor:
14 (a) a representative of the department of commerce;
15 (b) a representative of the department of environmental quality;
16 (c) a representative of the public service commission; and
17 (d) a representative of the governor's office.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Compensation -- per diem. Members of the authority may not receive a
20 salary but are entitled to compensation as provided in 2-15-122 for each day they are engaged in the
21 transaction of official business. Members must be reimbursed for travel expenses, as provided in 2-18-501
22 through 2-18-503, incurred while on official business.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Election of officers -- frequency of meetings -- quorum. (1) The
25 members of the authority shall annually elect a presiding officer and a vice presiding officer from among the
26 authority membership. The presiding officer and vice presiding officer shall each serve a 1-year term.
27 (2) The authority shall meet at least every 90 days and at other times as called by the presiding
28 officer or by any six members of the authority.

****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 314.1
1 (3) Eight members of the authority constitute a quorum for the transaction of all business, including
2 the election of officers.
3
4 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Authority powers. The state energy authority may:
5 (1) facilitate the planning, development, economic analysis, and coordination of facilities within and
6 outside of the state;
7 (2) employ officers, agents, and employees that the authority considers necessary in carrying out
8 the provisions of [sections 1 through 10];
9 (3) contract for legal, financial, engineering, and other professional services necessary in carrying
10 out the provisions of [sections 1 through 10];
11 (4) receive by gift, grant, donation, or otherwise money, aid, or assistance from the federal
12 government, the state, any political subdivision, or any other public or private entity;
13 (5) assist in investigating, planning, establishing, and coordinating corridors for electric
14 transmission through all energy generating resource types;
15 (6) participate in regional energy transmission organizations established in response to or in
16 compliance with an order of the federal energy regulatory commission or any regional electric transmission
17 initiatives;
18 (7) participate in a regional organization established for the purpose of planning, analyzing, and
19 coordinating energy generation projects, electric transmission projects and transmission infrastructure;
20 (8) report any findings and recommendations as requested by the governor or the legislature; and
21 (9) perform other functions necessary to carry out the provisions of [sections 1 through 10].
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Interagency cooperation. (1) State agencies shall cooperate with the
24 authority in planning, permitting, and constructing facilities.
25 (2) Within the limits of available resources, state agencies shall provide scientific, economic, and
26 other relevant data requested by the authority.
27
