- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 Drafter: Rebecca Power, HB0627.001.001
1 HOUSE BILL NO. 627
2 INTRODUCED BY B. MERCER
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING PROCUREMENT REPORTING REQUIREMENTS;
5 AND REQUIRING THE DEPARTMENT OF ADMINISTRATION TO PROVIDE CERTAIN REPORTS TO THE
6 LEGISLATIVE FINANCE COMMITTEE ON PROCUREMENT SOLICITATIONS, CONTRACTS, AND
7 CONTRACT MODIFICATIONS; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Report to interim committee. (1) By March 1, June 1, September 1,
12 and December 1 of each year, the department shall provide a written report to the legislative finance committee
13 in accordance with 5-11-210 containing the information listed in subsection (2) related to procurement
14 solicitations and contracts.
15 (2) The report must include:
16 (a) for a procurement solicitation for a contract that the department anticipates will cost at least
17 $250,000 $200,000 over the life of the contract:
18 (i) the name of each purchasing agency issuing the solicitation;
19 (ii) what the purchasing agency intends to procure through the solicitation;
20 (iii) the portion of the purchasing agency's appropriation that it intends to use to pay the vendor;
21 (iv) the closing date of the solicitation; and
22 (v) any other information the department finds relevant to the proposal;
23 (b) for a contract executed with or without a solicitation that is expected to cost $250,000 $200,000
24 or more over the life of the contract:
25 (i) the names of the purchasing agency; and
26 (ii) the contractor and the date of execution; and
27 (c) for a contract modification that causes the total value of a contract to exceed $250,000
28 $200,000 over the life of the contract:

1 (i) the names of the purchasing agency; and
2 (ii) the contractor and the date of execution.
3 (3) By February 15, May 15, August 15, and November 15 of each year, each governmental body
4 must submit the information listed in subsection (2) related to procurement solicitations and contracts to the
5 department using the information technology resources specified by the department.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
8 integral part of Title 18, chapter 4, part 1, and the provisions of Title 18, chapter 4, part 1, apply to [section 1].
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective January 1, 2026.
11 - END -
