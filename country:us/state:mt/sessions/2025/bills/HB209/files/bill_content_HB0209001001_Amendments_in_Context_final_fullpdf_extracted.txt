4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING A TAX CREDIT FOR NONPUBLIC SCHOOL

10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Tax credit for nonpublic school education expenses -- definitions.
12 As used in [section 2] and this section, the following definitions apply:
13 (1) "Additional expenses" means:
14 (a) educational therapies from a licensed or accredited practitioner or provider, including any
15 amount not covered by health insurance for a policy benefiting a qualifying student;
16 (b) a licensed or accredited paraprofessional or educational aide;
17 (c) fees for vocational and life skills education and programs approved by the superintendent;
18 (d) fees for educational and psychological evaluations, assistive technology rentals, and braille
19 translation goods and services programs approved by the superintendent;
20 (e) fees for a nationally standardized norm-referenced achievement test, an advanced placement
21 examination, or any exam related to college or university admission;
22 (f) fees for items charged by a public school, including individual classes and extracurricular
23 programs; or
24 (g) computer hardware and technological devices.
25 (2) (a) "Computer hardware and technological device" means a device used primarily for an
26 educational purpose, including calculators, personal computers, laptops, tablet devices, microscopes,
27 telescopes, and printers.
28 (b) The term does not include devices used for entertainment or other primarily noneducational

19 (c) all associated materials, equipment, and textbooks needed for the teaching of subjects that are
20 legally and commonly taught in public elementary and secondary schools; or
21 (d) private instruction, tutors, and special education instruction.

23 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Tax credit for nonpublic school education expenses -- rulemaking.
24 (1) A taxpayer is allowed a credit for nonpublic school education expenses as provided in this section.
25 (2) For each qualifying student, the credit is equal to the lesser of the nonpublic school education
26 expenses paid by the taxpayer in the tax year or 44.7% of the applicable per-ANB maximum rate established in
27 20-9-306. The credit may not exceed the taxpayer's tax liability and may not be carried forward or carried back.

1 member or legal guardian of the qualifying student may claim the nonpublic school education expenses credit
2 for a given qualifying student in a tax year. In cases involving separation or divorce for which each parent
3 resides in a different location, a noncustodial parent may claim the credit if:
4 (a) the child meets the definition of a qualifying student, except for the residency requirement

7 benefits associated with a child tax credit if one is authorized by law and the noncustodial parent attaches a
8 copy of the court order to their tax return; or
9 (ii) the custodial parent signs a written declaration that the custodial parent will not claim the credit

12 to implement this credit. The rules may not require evidence of a taxpayer’s compliance with the compulsory
13 attendance requirement in 20-5-109 beyond applicable evidentiary requirements otherwise found in state law
14 for proving compliance with the compulsory attendance requirement in 20-5-109.
15 (b) The superintendent of public instruction shall make rules establishing vocational and life skills
16 education and programs, educational and psychological evaluations, assistive technology rentals, and braille
17 translation goods and services programs eligible for the credit.
18 (5) Pursuant to 5-4-104, the legislature finds that the purpose of the tax credit provided for in this
19 section is to provide an income tax credit to reimburse expenses for nonpublic schooling, such as private
20 schools and home schooling.
