69th Legislature 2025 LC 0281
2 INTRODUCED BY _________________________________________________
(Primary Sponsor)
3 BY REQUEST OF THE DEPARTMENT OF MILITARY AFFAIRS
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ADOPTING THE MOST RECENT FEDERAL MILITARY LAWS,
6 REGULATIONS, AND CODES APPLICABLE TO THE GOVERNANCE OF THE MONTANA NATIONAL
7 GUARD; AMENDING SECTION 10-1-104, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 10-1-104, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "10-1-104. Federal regulations to govern. (1) Federal laws and regulations, forms, precedents, and
13 usages relating to and governing the armed forces of the United States and the national guard, as in effect on
14 October 1, 2023 2025, insofar as they are applicable and not inconsistent with the constitution and laws of this
15 state or with a rule or regulation adopted pursuant to 10-1-105, apply to and govern the national guard of this
16 state, including all members on active duty within the state as active duty guard/reserve (AGR) personnel under
17 Title 32 of the United States Code.
18 (2) The Uniform Code of Military Justice, as in effect on October 1, 2023 2025, including the
19 regulations, manuals, forms, precedents, and usages implementing, interpreting, and complementing the code,
20 is adopted for use by the national guard of this state and applies, insofar as the code is not otherwise
21 inconsistent with the constitution and laws of this state, including the regulations, manuals, forms, precedents,
22 and usages implementing, interpreting, and complementing the constitution and laws of this state, or with a rule
23 or regulation adopted pursuant to 10-1-105, to the greatest extent practicable to govern the national guard of
24 this state, including all members on active duty within the state as active duty guard/reserve (AGR) personnel
25 under Title 32 of the United States Code when the members are serving other than in a federal capacity under
26 Title 10 of the United States Code."
27
28 NEW SECTION. Applicability. [This act] applies to events that occur and proceedings begun on or
