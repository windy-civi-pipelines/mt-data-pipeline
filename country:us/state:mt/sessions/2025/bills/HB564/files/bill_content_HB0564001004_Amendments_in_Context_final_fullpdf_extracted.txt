69th Legislature 2025 Drafter: Maxwell Parson, HB0564.001.004
1 HOUSE BILL NO. 564
2 INTRODUCED BY M. MARLER
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REQUIRING HUNTERS TO REPORT HUNTING ACTIVITY FOR
5 DEER AND ELK TO THE DEPARTMENT OF FISH, WILDLIFE, AND PARKS; PROVIDING INCENTIVES FOR
6 REPORTING AND PENALTIES FOR NONREPORTING; ESTABLISHING DEADLINES; REQUIRING THE
7 DEPARTMENT TO DEVELOP A SYSTEM TO COLLECT REPORTING INFORMATION; PROVIDING
8 RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; DEFINING "HUNTING ACTIVITY"; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE
9 DATE.”
10
11 WHEREAS, phone survey efficacy has decreased due to the increased use of mobile phones and
12 reduced participation from individuals; and
13 WHEREAS, information regarding the total number of elk and deer harvested in a license year is
14 crucial information for biologists, hunters, and the Department of Fish, Wildlife, and Parks to make the
15 management decisions; and
16 WHEREAS, electronic reporting may increase hunter participation and increase the amount of
17 information available to interested parties.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Mandatory reporting Reporting of deer and elk hunting activity --
22 bonus point -- administrative fee -- rulemaking. (1) A person who holds a hunting license for deer or elk
23 shall report to the department their hunting activity for each animal during the license year.
24 (2) A person who completes reporting of their hunting activity by February 15 of the license year in
25 which the person purchased a deer or elk license may choose to receive one bonus point for either a deer or
26 elk upon completion and submission of the report.
27 (3)(2) A person who does not complete reporting of their hunting activity prior to the end of the

1 license year in which the person purchased a deer or elk license will be assessed an administrative fee of no
2 less than $5 and no more than $50, subject to determination by the department may not purchase a
3 conservation license for the coming license year until they report their hunting activity. The fee must be paid
4 prior to the purchase of or upon purchasing a base hunting license for the coming license year. A hunter may
5 not be required to pay more than one administrative fee for all reporting violations during a license year.
6 (4)(3) The validation of an electronic tag pursuant to 87-2-119 does not satisfy the reporting
7 requirements of this section.
8 (5)(4) The department shall:
9 (a) develop a system for collecting and retaining mandatory hunting activity reports;. The
10 department shall provide physical reporting forms, if applicable for carrying out the provisions of this section.
11 (b) make reporting forms available online and physically;
12 (c) notify hunters that the report is available electronically, if applicable; and
13 (d) allow a parent to complete the report for a youth under 18 years of age.
14 (5) The department may ask questions not related to hunting activity but may not require a person
15 to answer them.
16 (6) The department may make rules to implement this section.
17 (7) As used in this section, "hunting activity" includes harvest success, hunting district locations,
18 dates during which hunting occurred, and any other pertinent hunting information as determined by the
19 department currently collected by the department.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
22 integral part of Title 87, chapter 2, part 1, and the provisions of Title 87, chapter 2, part 1, apply to [section 1].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective March 1, 2026.
25 - END -
