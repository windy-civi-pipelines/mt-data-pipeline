4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CLARIFYING THE POINT OF TAXATION FOR GASOLINE AND
5 SPECIAL FUELS TAXES TO THE FIRST LICENSED DISTRIBUTOR WHO OWNS THE GASOLINE OR

12 Section 1. Section 15-70-401, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "15-70-401. Definitions. As used in this part, the following definitions apply:
14 (1) "Agricultural use" means use of gasoline or special fuel by a person who earns income while
15 engaging in the business of farming or ranching and who files farm or income reports for tax purposes as
16 required by the United States internal revenue service.
17 (2) "Aviation fuel" means gasoline or any other liquid fuel by whatever name the liquid fuel may be
18 known or sold, compounded for use in and sold for use in aircraft, including but not limited to any and all
19 gasoline or liquid fuel meeting or exceeding the minimum specifications prescribed by the United States for use
20 by its military forces in aircraft.
21 (3) (a) "Biodiesel" means a fuel produced from monoalkyl esters of long-chain fatty acids derived
22 from vegetable oils, renewable lipids, animal fats, or any combination of those ingredients. The fuel must meet
23 the requirements of ASTM D6751, also known as the Standard Specification for Biodiesel Fuel (B100) Blend
24 Stock for Distillate Fuels, as adopted by the American society for testing and materials.
25 (b) Biodiesel is also known as "B-100".
26 (4) "Bulk delivery" means placing gasoline or special fuel not intended for resale in storage or
27 containers. The term does not mean gasoline or special fuel delivered into the supply tank of a motor vehicle.
28 (5) "Cardtrol" or "keylock" means a unique device intended to allow access to a fuel dealer's

5 storage, other than by pipeline, by a licensed distributor for sale or use in this state, including.
6 (b) Gasoline or special fuel imported into this state, other than gasoline or special fuel placed in
7 storage at a refinery or pipeline terminal, is considered to be distributed after it has arrived in and is brought to
8 rest in this state.

18 rest in this state.

4 CFR, parts 20 and 21, and that meets the standards for ethanol adopted pursuant to 82-15-103.
5 (10) "Ethanol-blended gasoline" means gasoline blended with ethanol. The percentage of ethanol in
6 the blend is identified by the letter "E" followed by the percentage number. A blend that is 10% denatured
7 ethanol and 90% gasoline would be reflected as E-10. A blend that is 85% denatured ethanol and 15% gasoline

10 a motor vehicle, gasoline or special fuel received from a refinery or pipeline terminal within Montana.
11 (12) "Exporter" means a person who transports, other than in the fuel supply tank of a motor
12 vehicle, gasoline or special fuel received from a refinery or pipeline terminal in Montana to a destination outside
13 Montana for sale, use, or consumption outside Montana.
14 (13) (a) "Gasoline" includes:
15 (i) all petroleum products commonly or commercially known or sold as gasolines, including
16 casinghead gasoline, natural gasoline, aviation fuel, and all flammable liquids composed of a mixture of
17 selected hydrocarbons expressly manufactured and blended for the purpose of effectively and efficiently
18 operating internal combustion engines; and
19 (ii) any other type of additive when the additive is mixed or blended into gasoline, regardless of the
20 additive's classifications or uses.
21 (b) "Gasoline" does not include special fuels as defined in this section.
22 (14) "Import" means to first receive gasoline or special fuel into possession or custody after its
23 arrival and coming to rest at a destination within the state or to first receive any gasoline or special fuel shipped
24 or transported into this state from a point of origin outside this state other than in the fuel supply tank of a motor
25 vehicle.

27 special fuel into Montana for sale, use, or distribution.

12 Section 2. Section 15-70-402, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "15-70-402. License and security of distributors -- denial or disciplinary action. (1) (a) Prior to
14 doing business, each gasoline or special fuel distributor, including an exporter and importer, as those terms are
15 defined in 15-70-401, shall file:
16 (i) an application for a license with the department, on forms prescribed and furnished by the
17 department, setting forth the information that may be requested by the department; and
18 (ii) security with the department in an amount to be determined by the department.
19 (b) (i) Except as provided in subsection (1)(b)(ii), the required amount of security may not exceed
20 twice the estimated amount of gasoline or special fuel taxes the distributor will pay to this state each month.
21 (ii) The minimum required security for a distributor who imports or exports fuel is $25,000.
22 (c) Upon On approval of the application, the department shall issue to the distributor a
23 nonassignable license that is in force until surrendered, canceled, or revoked.
24 (2) The department may deny the issuance of a distributor license or take disciplinary action
25 against the distributor if it determines that the applicant or distributor:
26 (a) has violated any provision of this chapter or any rule of the department relating to gasoline or
27 special fuel, or both;
28 (b) fails to provide the security required by the department;

2 a 3-year period;
3 (d) is not in compliance with motor fuels laws in other jurisdictions; or
4 (e) fails to pay the gasoline or special fuel tax.
5 (3) Disciplinary action against a distributor may result in revocation of the license or issuance of a
6 probationary license. At its discretion, the department may issue a probationary license to a distributor who
7 repeatedly fails to report in the manner prescribed. The probationary license must be issued for a specified time
8 period and may require the distributor to attend motor fuel tax training conducted by the department. If a
9 distributor issued a probationary license fails to provide accurate reports by the end of the time period specified
10 by the probationary license, the department may revoke the distributor license.
11 (4) If an application for a distributor license is denied or revoked or if the distributor is under
12 disciplinary action, the applicant or distributor has the right to appeal the department's decision pursuant to Title
13 2, chapter 4, part 6.
14 (5) Only a licensed distributor may withdraw gasoline or special fuel from a refinery or terminal.
15 (6) Failure to obtain a distributor license as required in this section subjects the distributor to the
16 provisions of 15-70-419 allowing for the seizure, confiscation, and possible forfeiture of the fuel.

18 (a) a bond executed by a distributor as principal with a corporate surety qualified under the laws of

5 Section 3. Section 15-70-403, MCA, is amended to read:

9 (a) 33 cents for each gallon of gasoline distributed by the distributor within the state and on which
10 the gasoline tax has not been paid by any other distributor;

12 which the special fuel tax has not been paid by any other distributor; and
13 (c) 5 cents for each gallon of aviation fuel, other than fuel sold to the federal defense fuel supply
14 center, which is allocated to the department as provided by 67-1-301.

16 (a) the revenue from 22 cents of the tax less the allocations provided for in 60-3-201(1)(a) through

18 (b) the revenue from 4 cents of the tax less the allocations provided for in 60-3-201(1)(a) through
19 (1)(d) to the highway patrol administration state special revenue account established in 44-1-110; and
20 (c) the remaining revenue from the tax less the allocations provided for in 60-3-201(1)(a) through
21 (1)(d) to the local government road construction and maintenance restricted account provided for in 15-70-128.
22 (3) The special fuel tax provided for in subsection (1)(b) must be deposited as follows:
23 (a) the revenue from 24.6 cents of the tax to the highway restricted account provided for in 15-70-
24 126;
25 (b) the revenue from 4 cents of the tax to the highway patrol administration state special revenue
26 account established in 44-1-110; and
27 (c) the remaining revenue from the tax to the local government road construction and maintenance
28 restricted account provided for in 15-70-128.

16 Section 4. Section 15-70-404, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "15-70-404. Computation. (1) The tax imposed on the distributor under 15-70-403(1) may be
18 rounded to the nearest whole dollar amount.
19 (2) The tax imposed under 15-70-403(6)(9) on owners or operators of the motor vehicles operating
20 on the public roads and highways of this state must be computed, with respect to gasoline or special fuel for
21 which the tax has not been paid in this state and that has been consumed by the purchaser, by multiplying the
22 corresponding tax rate per gallon as provided in 15-70-403(1) by the number of gallons of gasoline or special
23 fuel consumed by the person in the operation of motor vehicles on the public roads and highways of this state."
