69th Legislature 2025 HB 841.1
2 INTRODUCED BY B. BARKER, E. ALBUS, D. BEDEY, M. BERTOGLIO, J. DARLING, S. ESSMANN, D.
3 FERN, G. HERTZ, L. JONES, S. KELLY, G. KMETZ, G. NIKOLAKAKOS, G. PARRY, K. WALSH, D. EMRICH,
4 J. KASSMIER, W. MCKAMEY, T. MCGILLVRAY, M. YAKAWICH
5
6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT SUBMITTING TO THE QUALIFIED ELECTORS OF MONTANA AN
7 AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VIII, SECTION 16, OF THE MONTANA CONSTITUTION TO PROVIDE THAT
8 REVENUE FROM A SALES TAX OR USE TAX MUST BE USED TO REDUCE PROPERTY TAXES THAT
9 FUND PUBLIC SCHOOLS AND THE MONTANA UNIVERSITY SYSTEM UNLESS APPROPRIATED FOR
10 OTHER PURPOSES BY A VOTE OF THREE-FOURTHS OF THE LEGISLATURE; AND PROVIDING AN
11 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Article VIII, section 16, of The Constitution of the State of Montana is amended to read:
16 "Section 16. Limitation on sales tax or use tax rates. The rate of a general statewide sales tax or
17 use tax may not exceed 4%. Sales tax or use tax revenue, less administrative costs, must be used solely to
18 reduce property taxes that fund public schools and the Montana university system unless appropriated for other
19 purposes by a vote of three-fourths of the members of each house of the legislature."
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Two-thirds vote required. Because [section 1] is a legislative proposal
22 to amend the constitution, Article XIV, section 8, of the Montana constitution requires an affirmative roll call vote
23 of two-thirds of all the members of the legislature, whether one or more bodies, for passage.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. If approved by the electorate, [this act] is effective July 1,
26 2027.
27
28 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Submission to electorate. [This act] shall be submitted to the qualified
