Section 1:
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 828.1
2 INTRODUCED BY Z. ZEPHYR, D. ZOLNIKOV
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CONSUMER RIGHTS LAWS RELATED TO POWERED
5 WHEELCHAIRS; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES OF POWERED
6 WHEELCHAIR MANUFACTURERS; PROVIDING ENFORCEMENT; PROVIDING LIMITATIONS; AND
7 PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 5] may be cited as the "Powered
12 Wheelchair Right to Repair Act".
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 5], the following definitions
15 apply:
16 (1) "Authorized repair provider" means:
17 (a) an original equipment manufacturer who offers the services of diagnosis, maintenance, or
18 repair of its own powered wheelchair; or
19 (b) an individual or business who offers the services of diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of a
20 powered wheelchair under the name of the original equipment manufacturer as part of an agreement with the
21 manufacturer.
22 (2) "Documentation" means a manual, diagram, reporting output, service code description,
23 schematic diagram, security code, password, or other guidance or information used in effecting the services of
24 diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of a powered wheelchair.
25 (3) "Independent repair provider" means an individual or business operating in the state that does
26 not have an arrangement described in subsection (1)(b) with an original equipment manufacturer and who is
27 engaged in the services of diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of a powered wheelchair.
28 (4) "Original equipment manufacturer" means a business that sells, leases, or otherwise supplies
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 828
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 828.1
1 new powered wheelchairs manufactured by or on behalf of itself.
2 (5) "Owner" means an individual or business who owns or leases a powered wheelchair purchased
3 or used in the state.
4 (6) "Part" means a new or used replacement part made available by an original equipment
5 manufacturer for the maintenance or repair of a powered wheelchair manufactured by or on behalf of or sold or
6 otherwise supplied by the original equipment manufacturer.
7 (7) "Powered wheelchair" means a motorized wheeled device designed for use by a person with a
8 physical disability.
9 (8) "Tools" means a software program, hardware implement, or other apparatus used for the
10 diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of a powered wheelchair, including software or other mechanisms that
11 provision, program, or pair a new part, calibrate functionality, access and reset electronic security locks and
12 other security-related functions, or perform any other function required to bring the product back to fully
13 functional condition, including installing updates.
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Responsibilities and duties of powered wheelchair manufacturers.
16 (1) The original equipment manufacturer shall provide an independent repair provider or owner with
17 documentation, parts, and tools in a way that enables the efficient and cost-effective diagnosis, maintenance,
18 and repair of powered wheelchairs that are sold or used in the state.
19 (2) For powered wheelchairs that contain electronic security locks or other security functions, the
20 original equipment manufacturer shall make available to an independent repair provider or owner
21 documentation, parts, and tools needed to access and reset the security lock or security function when disabled
22 to facilitate diagnosis, maintenance, or repair. The documentation, parts, and tools may be made available
23 through appropriate secure release systems.
24 (3) Documentation, tools, and parts must be made available either by the original equipment
25 manufacturer or through an authorized repair provider under the following fair and reasonable terms:
26 (a) There may be a charge for the actual cost of preparing and sending a physical printed copy of
27 documentation. Electronic copies of documentation must be made available free of charge.
28 (b) There may be a charge for the actual cost of preparing and sending a tool in physical form.
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 828
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 828.1
1 Otherwise, tools must be made available at no charge and without requiring authorization or internet access for
2 the use or operation of a tool.
3 (c) Original equipment manufacturers shall make parts available to an independent repair provider
4 or owner at costs and terms that are equivalent to the most favorable costs and terms that an original
5 equipment manufacturer offers the parts to an authorized repair provider and that:
6 (i) account for any discount, rebate, convenient and timely means of delivery, means of enabling
7 fully restored and updated functionality, rights of use, or other incentive or preference the original manufacturer
8 offers to an authorized repair provider;
9 (ii) account for any additional cost, burden, or impediment the original equipment manufacturer
10 imposes on an independent repair provider or owner;
11 (iii) do not impose a substantial obligation or restriction that is not reasonably necessary for the
12 diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of a powered wheelchair; and
13 (iv) are not conditioned on an arrangement with an authorized repair provider as defined in [section
14 2].
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Enforcement. A violation of any of the provisions of [sections 1 through
17 5] is an unlawful practice under the Montana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Act, Title 30,
18 chapter 14, part 1.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Limitations. (1) Nothing in [sections 1 through 5] may be construed to
21 require an original equipment manufacturer to divulge a trade secret to an independent repair provider or
22 owner.
23 (2) Nothing in [sections 1 through 5] may be construed to alter the terms of any arrangement
24 between an authorized repair provider and an original equipment manufacturer.
25 (3) An original equipment manufacturer or an authorized repair provider may not be liable for any
26 damage or injury caused to a powered wheelchair by an independent repair provider or owner that occurs
27 during the course of diagnosis, maintenance, or repair.
28

69th Legislature 2025 HB 828.1
1 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 5] are intended to be
2 codified as a new part in Title 30, chapter 14, and the provisions of Title 30, chapter 14, apply to [sections 1
3 through 5].
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Effective date. [This act] is effective January 1, 2026.
6 - END -
