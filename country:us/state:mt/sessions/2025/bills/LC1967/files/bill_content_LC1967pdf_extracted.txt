69th Legislature 2025 LC1967.1
2 INTRODUCED BY _________________________________________________
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING EMPLOYERS FROM OFFERING INCENTIVES TO
5 EMPLOYEES TO REGISTER TO VOTE; PROVIDING A PENALTY; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND
6 PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Voter registration incentives prohibited -- penalty. (1) (a) An
11 employer may not compensate, either directly or indirectly, an employee for the act of registering to vote or for
12 assisting another individual to register to vote.
13 (b) The restriction under subsection (1)(a) includes offering or providing any form of compensation,
14 including monetary payment, bonuses, or other benefits, to an employee in exchange for registering to vote.
15 (c) The restriction under subsection (1)(a) also includes offering or providing any form of
16 compensation, including monetary payment, bonuses, or other benefits, to an employee in exchange for the
17 employee helping another individual to register to vote.
18 (d) The restriction under subsection (1)(a) does not include situations in which an employer offers
19 paid time off or other leave time to employees to allow them to vote in an election.
20 (2) An employer violating the provisions of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and on
21 conviction shall be punished by a fine in any sum not exceeding $1,000 for each offense.
22 (3) Nothing in this section may be construed to prevent employees from freely exercising their right
23 to vote, registering to vote, or engaging in any other lawful activity related to the electoral process. Employees
24 shall retain the right to participate in political activities outside of the work environment and without interference
25 from their employer.
26 (4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
27 (a) "Employee" means a person who may be permitted, required, or directed by an employer as
28 defined in subsection (4)(b) in consideration of direct or indirect gain or profit to engage in any employment.

1 (b) "Employer" means an individual, partnership, association, corporation, legal representative,
2 trustee, receiver, and trustee in bankruptcy and any common carrier by rail, motor, water, air, or express
3 company doing business in or operating within the state.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
6 integral part of Title 39, chapter 2, part 3, and the provisions of Title 39, chapter 2, part 3, apply to [section 1].
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
9 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
10 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
