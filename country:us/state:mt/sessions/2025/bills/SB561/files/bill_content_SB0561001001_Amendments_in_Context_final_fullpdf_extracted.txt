10 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Qualifications for appointed commissioners. (1) Appointed
11 commissioners are meant to must be industry experts.
12 (2) An appointed commissioner must have a minimum of 4 years of separation from any
13 professional affiliation with an entity or affiliated company of an entity that is subject to regulation by the
14 commission.
15 (3) An appointed commissioner, spouse, or minor children may not have financial interests in any
16 entity or affiliated company of an entity that is subject to regulation by the commission.
17 (4) An appointed commissioner must be a citizen of the United States and must have resided in
18 the state of Montana for a minimum of 2 years immediately prior to appointment.
19 (5) (a) An appointed commissioner must have substantial experience or significant technical
20 qualifications relevant to the laws governing and administrating the public service commission.
21 (b) Experience and qualification include but are not limited to experience and qualifications:
22 (i) the fields of finance, law, or energy; or
23 (ii) fields within the utility sector that are subject to the provisions of Title 69.
24 (6) Prior to nomination by the governor pursuant to [section 6], a qualified commissioner shall
25 document their relevant qualifications and file the document with a $100 filing fee to the commissioner of
26 political practices.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Process for appointing commissioners. (1) As provided in 69-1-103,
2 commissioners are to must be appointed by the governor and confirmed by at least two-thirds of the senate.
3 (2) An entity that is subject to regulation by the commission may not put forward candidate
4 suggestions to the governor for consideration.
5 (3)(2) The governor shall ensure that candidates meet the requirements provided in [section 5] before
6 announcing their nomination.
7 (4)(3) (a) During the confirmation process, the senate shall confirm that the governor's assessment of
8 the candidates' qualifications is accurate. The senate may also explore the ethics and fitness for office of the
9 candidates.
10 (b) The senate may refuse an appointment based on criteria not listed here or in [section 5].
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Transition. (1) The commissioners in office on [the effective date of this
13 act] shall serve out the remainder of their respective terms.
14 (2) (a) On [the effective date of this act], the commissioner for the second district becomes the
15 commissioner for the eastern congressional district and the commissioner for the third district becomes the
16 commissioner for the western congressional district. These commission seats will remain election positions
17 pursuant to 69-1-103.
18 (b) A commissioner elected at the general election in 2028 and in subsequent elections serves a
19 6-year term as provided in 69-1-105 and pursuant to the qualifications in [section 8].
20 (3) No later than January 1, 2027, the governor shall appoint commissioners to fill the seats for the
21 current first and fifth districts when the current commissioners' terms end.
22 (4) No later than January 1, 2029, the governor shall appoint a commissioner to fill the seat for the
23 current fourth district when the current commissioner's term ends.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Qualifications for elected commissioners. (1) An elected
26 commissioner must have a minimum of 4 years of separation from any professional affiliation with an entity or
27 affiliated company of an entity that is subject to regulation by the commission.
