4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FUNDING FOR ADDITIONAL WILDLIFE
5 CROSSINGS IN MONTANA; AUTHORIZING THE FISH AND WILDLIFE COMMISSION TO ESTABLISH A
6 SPECIALTY LICENSE PLATE; ESTABLISHING A BIG GAME AND WILDLIFE HIGHWAY CROSSINGS AND
7 ACCOMMODATIONS ACCOUNT; PROVIDING A STATUTORY APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING FOR
8 REIMBURSEMENT OF FUNDS; AMENDING SECTION 17-7-502, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE
9 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 WHEREAS, per capita Montana has the second highest number of wildlife-vehicle collisions in the
12 nation with a 1-in-54 likelihood of hitting an animal and with a minimum of 6,000 big game carcasses collected
13 each year from Montana highways; and
14 WHEREAS, wildlife accommodations, such as underpasses and overpasses with fencing on public
15 roadways, effectively reduce vehicle collisions with wildlife, save lives, and help prevent the costs associated
16 with wildlife-vehicle collisions, including vehicle damage repairs, human injuries or fatalities, emergency
17 response, and other costs estimated at a minimum of $119,719,100 annually, as well as the costs of increased
18 insurance premiums; and
19 WHEREAS, protecting wildlife movement has been shown to improve the herd vitality of big game
20 species that are critical to Montana’s outdoor recreation economy and is essential for other wildlife species; and
21 WHEREAS, most Montanans from all walks of life support constructing more wildlife crossings, 77% of
22 Montanans support providing funding for additional wildlife crossings in Montana, according to a 2023 survey
23 done by Moore Information Group, and 70% of Montanans have been involved in a wildlife-vehicle collision in
24 the state; and
25 WHEREAS, the Montana Wildlife and Transportation Partnership was created in part to assemble data
26 and supporting information to define and identify areas of greatest need for wildlife accommodations based on
27 wildlife-vehicle conflict; and
28 WHEREAS, the effectiveness and cost-efficiency of all the existing efforts in the state would be

69th Legislature 2025 LC 2103
1 enhanced by a comprehensive and coordinated effort through funding for highway accommodation and
2 crossing projects.
3
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
5
6 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Big game and wildlife highway crossings and accommodations
7 license plates -- authorization. (1) An applicant for a generic specialty license plate that is sponsored by the
8 fish and wildlife commission shall make a donation of $20 to the department of fish, wildlife, and parks upon
9 initial issuance of the license plates and a donation of $20 for each annual renewal of the license plates.
10 (2) The commission shall establish a generic specialty license plate, as defined in 61-3-473, no
11 later than January 1, 2026.
12 (3) The donation provided for in subsection (1) must be paid to the county treasurer, who shall
13 remit the entire amount to the department of revenue for deposit in the big game and wildlife highway crossings
14 and accommodations state special revenue account established in [section 2].
15
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Big game and wildlife highway crossings and accommodations
18 account -- purpose -- funds invested -- statutory appropriation. (1) There is a big game and wildlife
19 highway crossings and accommodations state special revenue account within the state special revenue fund
20 established in 17-2-102 administered by the department.
21 (2) The purpose of the account is to provide money exclusively for the design, construction,
22 identification, maintenance, and conservation of fish and wildlife crossings and other related crossing
23 accommodations to improve fish and wildlife permeability in the state, which may include but is not limited to:
24 (a) matching any federal money for a project to design, construct, identify, maintain, or protect fish
25 and wildlife crossings and other related crossing accommodation features;
26 (b) conducting studies on fish and wildlife crossings and other related crossing accommodations;
27 (c) designing or constructing fish and wildlife crossings and other related crossing accommodation
28 features;
- 2 - LC 2103
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 2103
1 (d) planning related to fish and wildlife crossings and other related crossing accommodation
2 features, including assessing risk of wildlife disease transmission;
3 (e) staffing needs related to the design, construction, identification, maintenance, and conservation
4 of fish and wildlife crossings and other related crossing accommodation features; and
5 (f) any other needs the department identified in consultation with the department of transportation
6 related to wildlife roadway accommodations.
7 (3) There must be deposited into the account transfers, gifts, grants, donations, income from
8 investment of the fund, and any other money distributed or otherwise allocated to the fund.
9 (4) Money in the account is statutorily appropriated, as provided in 17-7-502, to the department for
10 the purposes of [sections 1 and 2].
11 (5) Money in the account may be used to reimburse the department of transportation for eligible
12 projects or programs as determined by the department of fish, wildlife, and parks. The department of fish,
13 wildlife, and parks shall consult with the department of transportation before reimbursing it for qualifying
14 projects in the state in accordance with subsection (2).
15 (6) Money that was not encumbered or expended from the account during the previous biennium
16 must remain in the account.
17 (7) Deposits to the account must be placed in short-term investments to accrue interest. The
18 interest must be deposited into the account.
