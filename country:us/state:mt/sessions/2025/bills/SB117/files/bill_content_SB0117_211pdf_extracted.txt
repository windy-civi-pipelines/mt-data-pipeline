4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING PROPERTY TAXES; REVISING
5 GOVERNMENTAL ENTITY LIMITS ON PROPERTY TAX INCREASES; INCREASING THE RATE OF
6 INFLATION LIMITATION IMPOSED ON GOVERNMENTAL ENTITIES FOR CALCULATING PROPERTY TAX
7 LEVIES; PROVIDING THAT A PORTION OF NEWLY TAXABLE PROPERTY IS SUBJECT TO THE MILL
8 LEVY LIMITATION CALCULATION; PROVIDING A FIXED LIMIT ON MILL LEVY AUTHORITY; PROVIDING
9 THAT A LOCAL GOVERNMENT MAY CREATE A LARGE TAXPAYER RESERVE ACCOUNT; AMENDING
10 SECTION 15-10-420, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. SECTION 1. LOCAL GOVERNMENT LARGE TAXPAYER RESERVE ACCOUNT -- EXPENDITURE
15 RESTRICTIONS. (1) THE GOVERNING BODY OF A CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY MAY ESTABLISH A
16 LARGE TAXPAYER RESERVE ACCOUNT.
17 (2) A CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY THAT ESTABLISHES AN ACCOUNT PURSUANT TO THIS
18 SECTION SHALL DEPOSIT ANNUALLY 10% OF REVENUE GENERATED FROM NEWLY TAXABLE PROPERTY IN CLASSES OTHER
19 THAN CLASS FOUR IN THE LARGE TAXPAYER RESERVE ACCOUNT.
20 (3) A CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY MAY USE A PORTION OF THE REVENUE FROM NEWLY
21 TAXABLE PROPERTY GENERATED UNDER SUBSECTION (2) TO OFFSET ANY REQUIRED PAYMENT TO THE DEPARTMENT FOR
22 TECHNOLOGY ENHANCING THE ASSESSMENT OF NEWLY TAXABLE PROPERTY.
23 (4) SUBJECT TO A PAYMENT MADE PURSUANT TO SUBSECTION (3), MONEY DEPOSITED IN THE ACCOUNT BY
24 THE CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY MUST REMAIN IN THE ACCOUNT AND MAY NOT BE APPROPRIATED BY
25 THE GOVERNING BODY UNTIL A LARGE TAXPAYER HAS PERMANENTLY CEASED OPERATIONS OR EXPERIENCED A
26 SIGNIFICANT DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE.
27 (5) IF THE CIRCUMSTANCES DESCRIBED IN SUBSECTION (4) OCCUR, THE GOVERNING BODY OF THE CITY,
28 COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY SHALL USE THE FUNDS IN THE ACCOUNT TO:
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 117
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0117.2
1 (A) PAY FOR OUTSTANDING CAPITAL PROJECT BONDS OR OTHER EXPENSES INCURRED PRIOR TO THE
2 CESSATION OF OPERATIONS OR A SIGNIFICANT DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE;
3 (B) FOR UP TO 10 YEARS, DECREASE MILL LEVIES OF THE CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY
4 THAT ARE DIRECTLY IMPACTED BY THE CESSATION OF OPERATIONS OR A SIGNIFICANT DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE;
5 (C) ATTRACT NEW INDUSTRY TO THE IMPACT AREA;
6 (D) PROVIDE CASH INCENTIVES FOR EXPANDING THE EMPLOYMENT BASE OF THE AREA IMPACTED BY THE
7 CESSATION OF OPERATIONS OR A SIGNIFICANT DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE; OR
8 (E) INVEST IN INFRASTRUCTURE DIRECTLY RELATED TO NEW DEVELOPMENT OR HOUSING.
9 (6) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (5)(B), MONEY HELD IN THE ACCOUNT MAY NOT BE CONSIDERED
10 AS CASH BALANCE FOR THE PURPOSE OF REDUCING MILL LEVIES.
11 (7) (A) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (7)(B), MONEY IN THE ACCOUNT MUST BE INVESTED AS
12 PROVIDED BY LAW. INTEREST AND INCOME FROM THE INVESTMENT OF FUNDS IN THE ACCOUNT MUST BE CREDITED TO
13 THE ACCOUNT.
14 (B) THE CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY MAY USE INVESTMENT EARNINGS ON THE
15 ACCOUNT NOT SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (3) FOR THE PURPOSES PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (5).
16 (8) AS USED IN THIS SECTION, THE FOLLOWING DEFINITIONS APPLY:
17 (A) "LARGE TAXPAYER" MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL OR ENTITY WITH TAXABLE VALUE FOR ALL PROPERTY OWNED
18 BY THE INDIVIDUAL OR ENTITY WITHIN THE CITY, COUNTY, OR CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY THAT PLACES THE INDIVIDUAL
19 OR ENTITY AMONG THE 20% OF TAXPAYERS WITH THE LARGEST TAXABLE VALUE IN THE CITY, COUNTY, OR
20 CONSOLIDATED CITY-COUNTY.
21 (B) "SIGNIFICANT DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE" MEANS A DECREASE IN TAXABLE VALUE FOR PROPERTY
22 CLASSES OTHER THAN CLASS FOUR PROPERTY THAT EQUALS 25% OR MORE OF THE PRIOR YEAR'S TAXABLE VALUE.
