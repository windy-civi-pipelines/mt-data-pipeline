4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING DISTRIBUTION OF CERTAIN OIL AND NATURAL GAS
5 PRODUCTION TAXES; REVISING DEPOSITS TO THE OIL AND GAS PRODUCTION DAMAGE MITIGATION

11 Section 1. Section 15-36-331, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "15-36-331. Distribution of taxes. (1) (a) For each calendar quarter, the department shall determine
13 the amount of tax, late payment interest, and penalties collected under this part.
14 (b) For the purposes of distribution of oil and natural gas production taxes to county and school
15 district taxing units under 15-36-332 and to the state, the department shall determine the amount of oil and
16 natural gas production taxes paid on production in the taxing unit.
17 (2) (a) The amount of oil and natural gas production taxes collected for the percentage of privilege
18 and license tax established by the board pursuant to 82-11-131 must be deposited, in accordance with the
19 provisions of 17-2-124, in the account in the state special revenue fund for the purpose of paying expenses of
20 the board, as provided in 82-11-135.
21 (b) After the allocation provided for in subsection (2)(a), up to 0.08% of the tax collected pursuant
22 to 15-36-304(7) must be deposited in the oil and gas natural resource distribution account established in 90-6-
23 1001(1) for distribution pursuant to 15-36-332(7).
24 (c) (i) Any Except as provided in subsection (2)(c)(ii), any funds remaining after the allocations
25 provided for in subsections (2)(a) and (2)(b) must be deposited in the oil and gas production damage mitigation
26 account as provided in 82-11-161.
27

3 Section 2. Section 82-11-161, MCA, is amended to read:
4 "82-11-161. (Temporary) Oil and gas production damage mitigation account -- statutory
5 appropriation. (1) There is an oil and gas production damage mitigation account within the state special
6 revenue fund established in 17-2-102. The oil and gas production damage mitigation account is controlled by
7 the board.
8 (2) (a) At the beginning of each biennium, there must be allocated to the oil and gas production
9 damage mitigation account $650,000 from the interest income of the resource indemnity trust fund, except that
10 if at the beginning of a biennium the unobligated cash balance in the oil and gas production damage mitigation
11 account:
12 (i) equals or exceeds $1 million, no allocation will be made; or
13 (ii) is less than $1 million, then an amount less than or equal to the difference between the
14 unobligated cash balance and $1 million, but not more than $650,000, must be allocated to the oil and gas
15 production damage mitigation account from the interest income of the resource indemnity trust fund.
16 (b) If $650,000 is not allocated pursuant to subsection (2)(a), the remainder must be deposited in
17 the natural resources projects state special revenue account established in 15-38-302 for the purpose of
18 making grants.
19 (3) In addition to the allocation provided in subsection (2), there must be deposited in the oil and
20 gas production damage mitigation account all funds received by the board pursuant to 15-36-331(2)(c) and 82-
21 11-136.
22 (4) If a sufficient balance exists in the account, funds are statutorily appropriated, as provided in
23 17-7-502, from the oil and gas production damage mitigation account, upon the authorization of the board, to
24 pay the reasonable costs of properly plugging a well and either reclaiming or restoring, or both, a drill site or
25 other drilling or producing area damaged by oil and gas operations if the board determines that the well, sump,
26 hole, drill site, or drilling or producing area has been abandoned and the responsible person cannot be
27 identified or located or if the responsible person fails or refuses to properly plug, reclaim, or restore the well,
28 sump, hole, drill site, or drilling or producing area within a reasonable time after demand by the board. The
- 4 - LC 2642
****

1 responsible person shall, however, pay costs to the extent of that person's available resources and is
2 subsequently liable to fully reimburse the account or is subject to a lien on property as provided in 82-11-164 for
3 costs expended from the account to properly plug, reclaim, or restore the well, sump, hole, drill site, or drilling or
4 producing area and to mitigate any damage for which the person is responsible.
5 (5) Interest from funds in the oil and gas production damage mitigation account accrues to that
6 account.
7 82-11-161. (Effective on occurrence of contingency) Oil and gas production damage mitigation
8 account -- statutory appropriation. (1) There is an oil and gas production damage mitigation account within
9 the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102. The oil and gas production damage mitigation account
10 is controlled by the board.
11 (2) (a) At the beginning of each biennium, there must be allocated to the oil and gas production
12 damage mitigation account $650,000 from the interest income of the resource indemnity trust fund, except that
13 if at the beginning of a biennium the unobligated cash balance in the oil and gas production damage mitigation
14 account:
15 (i) equals or exceeds $1 million, no allocation will be made; or
16 (ii) is less than $1 million, then an amount less than or equal to the difference between the
17 unobligated cash balance and $1 million, but not more than $650,000, must be allocated to the oil and gas
18 production damage mitigation account from the interest income of the resource indemnity trust fund.
19 (b) If $650,000 is not allocated pursuant to subsection (2)(a), the remainder must be deposited in
20 the natural resources projects state special revenue account established in 15-38-302 for the purpose of
21 making grants.
22 (3) In addition to the allocation provided in subsection (2), there must be deposited in the oil and
23 gas production damage mitigation account all funds received by the board pursuant to 15-36-331(2)(c) and 82-
24 11-136(1).
25 (4) If a sufficient balance exists in the account, funds are statutorily appropriated, as provided in
26 17-7-502, from the oil and gas production damage mitigation account, upon the authorization of the board, to
27 pay the reasonable costs of properly plugging a well and either reclaiming or restoring, or both, a drill site or
28 other drilling or producing area damaged by oil and gas operations if the board determines that the well, sump,
- 5 - LC 2642

2 identified or located or if the responsible person fails or refuses to properly plug, reclaim, or restore the well,

4 However, the responsible person shall pay costs to the extent of that person's available resources and is

7 producing area and to mitigate any damage for which the person is responsible.
8 (5) Interest from funds in the oil and gas production damage mitigation account accrues to that
