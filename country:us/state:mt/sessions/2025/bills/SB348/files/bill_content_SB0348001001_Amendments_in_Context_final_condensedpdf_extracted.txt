5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING PENALTIES FOR THE CRIME OF SEXUAL ASSAULT;
6 PROVIDING THAT OFFENDERS WHO COMMIT A SECOND OFFENSE OF SEXUAL ASSAULT MUST
7 REGISTER AS A SEXUAL OFFENDER; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 45-5-502 AND 46-23-502, MCA.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 45-5-502, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "45-5-502. Sexual assault. (1) A person who knowingly subjects another person to any sexual
13 contact without consent commits the offense of sexual assault.
14 (2) Except as provided in subsections (3) and (4):
15 (a) on a first conviction for sexual assault, the offender shall be fined an amount not to exceed
16 $500 $1,000 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term not to exceed 6 months 1 year, or both;
17 (b) on a second conviction for sexual assault, the offender shall be fined an amount not to exceed
18 $1,000 $5,000 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term not to exceed 1 year exceed 5 years, or both; and
19 (c) on a third and subsequent conviction for sexual assault, the offender shall be fined an amount
20 not to exceed $10,000 or be imprisoned for a term not to exceed 5 10 years, or both.
21 (3) If the victim is less than 16 years old and the offender is 3 or more years older than the victim
22 or if the offender inflicts bodily injury upon anyone in the course of committing sexual assault, the offender shall
23 be punished by life imprisonment or by imprisonment in the state prison for a term of not less than 4 years,
24 unless the judge makes a written finding that there is good cause to impose a term of less than 4 years and
25 imposes a term of less than 4 years, or more than 100 years and may be fined not more than $50,000.
26 (4) If the victim is a client receiving psychotherapy services and the offender is providing or
27 purporting to provide psychotherapy services to the victim, the offender shall be punished by life imprisonment
28 or by imprisonment in the state prison for a term of not less than 4 years, unless the judge makes a written

20 Section 2. Section 46-23-502, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "46-23-502. Definitions. As used in Title 45, chapter 5, part 3 and parts 5 through 7, 46-18-255, and
22 this part, the following definitions apply:
23 (1) "Department" means the department of corrections provided for in 2-15-2301.
24 (2) "Foreign offenses" means a conviction for a sexual offense involving any of the conduct listed
25 in this section that was obtained under the laws of Canada, the United Kingdom, Australia, or New Zealand, or
26 under the laws of any foreign country when the United States department of state, in its country reports on
27 human rights practices, has concluded that an independent judiciary generally or vigorously enforced the right
28 to a fair trial in that country during the year in which the conviction was obtained.
