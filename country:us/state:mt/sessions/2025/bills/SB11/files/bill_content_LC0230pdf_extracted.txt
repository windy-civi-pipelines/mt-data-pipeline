14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Purpose. The right of the people to exercise the rights of initiative and
15 referendum in a local government unit is guaranteed by Article XI, section 8, of the Montana constitution and
16 may be exercised through adherence to the procedures established in [sections 1 through 13].
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 13], unless the context
19 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
20 (1) "Local government" means any city, town, county, or consolidated city-county.
21 (2) "Local government's next election held in accordance with Title 13, chapter 1, part 4" means a
22 primary or general election that includes the regularly scheduled nomination or election of officers of that local
23 government unit.

6 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Procedure for election on local government petition. (1) Except as
7 provided in 7-5-131, the electors of a local government may, by petition, request an election on whether to
8 enact, repeal, or amend an ordinance.
9 (2) Except as provided in [section 9(3)], a petition under [sections 4 through 10] signed by at least
10 15% of the local government's qualified electors is sufficient to require an election. In order to determine the
11 number of signatures needed on a petition to meet the percentage requirements of this subsection, the number
12 of electors must be the number of individuals registered to vote at the preceding general election for the local
13 government.
14 (3) Before a petition may be circulated for signatures, a sample petition must be submitted in the
15 form in which it will be circulated to the county election administrator. On receiving the sample petition, the
16 county election administrator shall promptly refer a copy of the sample petition to the attorney for the relevant
17 local government unit.
18 (4) (a) The local government attorney shall review the sample petition for proper form and
19 compliance with 7-5-131 and [sections 1 through 13].
20 (b) If the sample petition is rejected as to form, within 21 days after submission of the sample
21 petition, the local government attorney shall send written notice and a statement of the reasons for rejection to
22 the person who submitted the sample petition and the county election administrator.
23 (c) If the sample petition is approved as to form, within 21 days after submission of the sample
24 petition, the local government attorney shall send written notice to the person who submitted the sample
25 petition and the county election administrator. This notice must include the statement of purpose and the
26 statements of implication prepared by the local government attorney pursuant to subsections (5) and (6) of this
27 section.

2 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Petition signatures. (1) A petition approved as to form may not be
3 circulated for the purpose of signature gathering more than 1 year prior to the final date for filing the signed
4 petition with the county election administrator.
5 (2) A local government petition may be signed only by a qualified elector of the local government.
6 (3) A person gathering signatures for a petition may not be paid anything of value based on the
7 number of signatures gathered.
8 (4) Signatures may be withdrawn from a petition up to the time of final submission of petition
9 sheets.
10 (5) Signatures must be verified in the manner provided in 13-27-103.
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Governing body review of petition. (1) The governing body may, within
13 60 days of receiving the petition, take the action called for in the petition. If the action is taken, the question
14 need not be submitted to the electors. If the county election administrator has already certified the ballot issue
15 pursuant to [section 9], the results of the election are void.
16 (2) If the governing body does not within 60 days take the proposed action and the ballot issue is
17 certified by the county election administrator, then the question must be submitted to the electors at the election
18 specified in the petition.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Submission of signatures -- certification -- election. (1) Signed
21 sheets or sections of petitions with original signatures must be:
22 (a) collected and filed with the official responsible for the registration of electors in the county in
23 which the signatures were obtained within 90 days of the date of the notice that the petition was approved as to
24 form pursuant to [section 4]; and
25 (b) submitted no later than 4 weeks before the certification date specified in subsection (2) of this
26 section.
27 (2) The county election administrator shall certify a local government ballot issue for a local
28 government election no later than 85 days before the election.

10 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Delay of ordinance's effective date -- suspension of emergency
11 ordinance. (1) If an approved petition containing sufficient signatures is filed prior to the ordinance's effective
12 date or within 60 days after the passage of the ordinance, whichever is later, a petition requesting an election
13 on whether to amend or repeal the ordinance delays the ordinance's effective date until the ordinance is ratified
14 by the electors.
15 (2) If an approved petition containing sufficient signatures is filed within 60 days after the effective
16 date of an emergency ordinance, the emergency ordinance is suspended until it is ratified by the electors.

18 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Ballot issue referred by governing body of local government. (1) A
19 governing body of a local government may refer an existing or proposed ordinance to a vote of the people by
20 resolution.
21 (2) Unless otherwise provided by law, an ordinance referred to a vote of the people by the
22 governing body of a local government must comply with the statement of purpose requirements of [section 4(5)]
23 and the statements of implication requirements provided by [section 4(6)].
24 (3) The governing body of the local government shall transmit a local government ballot issue
25 referred to a vote of the people according to the requirements of Title 13, chapter 1, part 4.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 13. Suit to determine validity and constitutionality of petition and
5 proposed action. (1) The governing body may direct that a suit be brought in district court by the local
6 government to determine whether the proposed action is valid and constitutional. The suit must be initiated
7 within 14 days of the date a petition is approved as to form under [section 4].
8 (2) An action brought under this section takes precedence over other cases and matters in the
9 district court. The court shall render a decision as soon as possible as to whether the proposed action is valid
10 and constitutional.
11 (3) If the defendant prevails, the defendant is entitled to be reimbursed by the local government for
12 costs and reasonable attorney fees incurred.
13 (4) The 90-day period during which petition signatures must be collected under [section 10] begins
14 on the date of the court order resolving the suit.
