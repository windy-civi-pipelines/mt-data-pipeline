15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Purpose. The right of the people to exercise the rights of initiative and
16 referendum in a local government unit is guaranteed by Article XI, section 8, of the Montana constitution and
17 may be exercised through adherence to the procedures established in [sections 1 through 13].
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 13], unless the context
20 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
21 (1) "Local government" means any city, town, county, or consolidated city-county.
22 (2) "Local government's next election held in accordance with Title 13, chapter 1, part 4" means a
23 primary or general election that includes the regularly scheduled nomination or election of officers of that local
24 government unit.

7 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Procedure for election on local government petition. (1) Except as
8 provided in 7-5-131, the electors of a local government may, by petition, request an election on whether to
9 enact, repeal, or amend an ordinance.
10 (2) Except as provided in [section 9(3)], a petition under [sections 4 through 10] signed by at least
11 15% of the local government's qualified electors is sufficient to require an election. In order to determine the
12 number of signatures needed on a petition to meet the percentage requirements of this subsection, the number
13 of electors must be the number of individuals registered to vote at the preceding general election for the local
14 government.
15 (3) (A) Before a petition may be circulated for signatures, a sample petition must be submitted in
16 the form in which it will be circulated to the county election administrator. On receiving the sample petition, the
17 county election administrator shall DESIGNATE A BALLOT ISSUE AND ITS NUMBER PURSUANT TO SUBSECTION (3)(B)
18 AND THEN promptly refer a copy of the sample petition to the attorney for the relevant local government unit.
19 (B) THE ELECTION ADMINISTRATOR SHALL SERIALLY NUMBER ALL SUBMITTED PETITIONS THAT ARE
20 APPROVED AS TO FORM CONTINUOUSLY FROM YEAR TO YEAR. THE NUMBERING SYSTEM MUST DISTINGUISH THE
21 DIFFERENT TYPES OF PETITIONS RECEIVED AND INCLUDE PROVISIONS FOR NUMBERING MEASURES REFERRED TO THE
22 PEOPLE BY A GOVERNING BODY. THE ELECTION ADMINISTRATOR MAY DISTINGUISH THE NUMBERING SYSTEM FOR EACH
23 LOCAL GOVERNMENT UNIT.
24 (4) (a) The local government attorney shall review the sample petition for proper form and
25 compliance with 7-5-131 and [sections 1 through 13].
26 (b) If the sample petition is rejected as to form, within 21 days after submission of the sample
27 petition, the local government attorney shall send written notice and a statement of the reasons for rejection to
28 the person who submitted the sample petition and the county election administrator.

7 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Form of petition. (1) A local government petition must be substantially in
8 the form provided by [sections 4 through 10]. Clerical or technical errors that do not interfere with the ability to
9 judge the sufficiency of signatures on the petition do not render a petition void.
10 (2) (a) Petition sheets may not exceed 8 1/2 x 14 inches in size. Separate sheets of a petition may
11 be fastened in sections of not more than 25 sheets.
12 (b) Near the top of each sheet containing signature lines must be printed the number of the
13 ordinance referred or the petition title. If signature lines are printed on both the front and back of a petition
14 sheet, the information required above must appear on both the front and back of the sheet.
15 (c) The complete text of the issue proposed or referred must be attached to or contained within
16 each signature sheet if sheets are circulated separately. The text of the issue must be in the format prescribed
17 by the governing body pursuant to 7-5-103(1) and, unless otherwise provided by the governing body of the local
18 government, use the style and language and form guide of the most recent edition of the bill drafting manual
19 furnished by the legislative services division. If sheets are circulated in sections, the complete text of the issue
20 must be attached to each section.
21 (3) An internet posting of petition language must include a statement that the petition language
22 and format may not be modified. An internet posting must include an affidavit in substantially the same form as
23 provided in [section 6].
24 (4) Unless otherwise provided by law, the following is substantially the form for a local government
25 petition calling for approval or rejection of an ordinance:

19 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Petition signatures. (1) A petition approved as to form may not be
20 circulated for the purpose of signature gathering more than 1 year prior to the final date for filing the signed
21 petition with the county election administrator.
22 (2) A local government petition may be signed only by a qualified elector of the local government.
23 (3) A person gathering signatures for a petition may not be paid anything of value based on the
24 number of signatures gathered.
25 (4) Signatures may be withdrawn from a petition up to the time of final submission of petition
26 sheets.
27 (5) Signatures must be verified in the manner provided in 13-27-103.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Governing body review of petition. (1) The governing body OF THE
2 LOCAL GOVERNMENT may, within 60 days of receiving the petition, take the action called for in the petition. If the
3 action is taken, the question need not be submitted to the electors. If the county election administrator has
4 already certified the ballot issue pursuant to [section 9], the results of the election are void.
5 (2) If the governing body does not within 60 days take the proposed action and the ballot issue is
6 certified by the county election administrator, then the question must be submitted to the electors at the election
7 specified in the petition.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Submission of signatures -- certification -- election. (1) Signed
10 sheets or sections of petitions with original signatures must be:
11 (a) collected and filed with the official responsible for the registration of electors in the county in
12 which the signatures were obtained within 90 days of the date of the notice that the petition was approved as to
13 form pursuant to [section 4]; and
14 (b) submitted no later than 4 weeks before the certification date specified in subsection (2) of this
15 section.
16 (2) The county election administrator shall certify a local government ballot issue for a local
17 government election no later than 85 days before the election.
18 (3) Subject to [section 7(1)] and subsections (1) and (2) of this section, an election on an approved
19 petition containing sufficient signatures held pursuant to [sections 1 through 13] must be conducted in
20 conjunction with the local government's next election held in accordance with Title 13, chapter 1, part 4.
21 However, if the petition requests a special election, is signed by at least 25% of the qualified electors, and
22 otherwise complies with the requirements of subsections (1) and (2) of this section, a special election must be
23 held on the date specified in the petition. In order to determine the number of signatures needed on a petition to
24 meet the percentage requirements of this subsection (3), the number of electors must be calculated as provided
25 in [section 4(2)].

20 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Effective date of local government ballot issue. If a majority of
21 people voting on the question of a local government ballot issue conducted pursuant to [sections 1 through 13]
22 approve the proposal, the local government ballot issue becomes effective when the election results are
23 officially declared, unless otherwise stated in the proposal.
