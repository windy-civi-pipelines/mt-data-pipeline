4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A LEGISLATIVE STUDY OF THE IMPACT OF
5 FEDERAL DIRECTIVES ON MONTANA'S ECONOMY BY THE LEGISLATIVE FINANCE COMMITTEE;
6 PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING FOR CONTINGENT VOIDNESS; AND PROVIDING AN
7 EFFECTIVE DATE AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
8
9 WHEREAS, President Donald Trump was inaugurated as President of the United States on January
10 20, 2025; and
11 WHEREAS, since the day of his inauguration, President Trump and his executive appointees have
12 issued many directives through various means, including executive orders and the newly created Department of
13 Government Efficiency; and
14 WHEREAS, these directives have and will continue to have significant impacts on Montana's people,
15 businesses, governments, and landscapes; and
16 WHEREAS, these impacts may necessitate significant actions by Montana's executive and legislative
17 branches, as well as local governments, tribes, the Board of Regents, school districts, and universities,
18 including the replacement of lost funding, the development or modification of programs or structures that are
19 being reduced or eliminated through federal directives, the adjustment of budgets, and the provision of
20 assistance to the many Montanans who have lost their jobs as federal employees.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Legislative finance committee study of economic impacts related to
25 federal directives. (1) For the 2025-2026 interim, the legislative finance committee provided for in 5-12-201
26 shall direct a study of federal directives made since January 20, 2025, and their impact on Montana citizens,
27 local, tribal, and state governments, institutions, and businesses.
28 (2) The legislative finance committee may establish a subcommittee to complete its work in

1 accordance with this section. The presiding officer of the legislative finance committee shall appoint the
2 members of a subcommittee with equal representation from the majority and minority parties. The presiding
3 officer may also appoint ex officio members to a subcommittee provided they are equally represented from the
4 majority and minority parties.
5 (3) Any vacancies on a subcommittee must be filled in accordance with subsection (2).
6 (4) The committee may create additional bipartisan subcommittees to carry out the study or
7 request that an interim committee assist with the study, provided that the presiding officer of that interim
8 committee consents and ensures that the interim committee completes its assigned role in time to include its
9 findings with the reporting required by this section.
10 (5) The committee shall complete its preliminary work and issue a written report of its findings and
11 recommendations by January 1, 2026. Copies of the report must be given to the governor, all state legislators,
12 each of the federally recognized tribal governments of the state, and to the members of Montana's
13 congressional delegation.
14 (6) Additional meetings, research, and updates to the report required in subsection (5) may take
15 place after January 1, 2026. The final results of the study, including an updated report if applicable, must be
16 presented to the legislative finance committee before September 1, 2026. Copies of the final report must be
17 given to the governor, all state legislators, each of the federally recognized tribal governments of the state, and
18 to the members of Montana's congressional delegation.
19 (7) The legislative fiscal division shall provide administrative staff support and fiscal analysis.
