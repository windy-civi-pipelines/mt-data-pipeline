4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO THE OFFICE OF
5 THE CHILD AND FAMILY OMBUDSMAN; PROVIDING FOR ADDITIONAL PERSONNEL; REVISING THE
6 POWERS AND DUTIES OF THE OMBUDSMAN TO INCLUDE PROVIDING TIMELY RESPONSES AND
7 ACTIVE EFFORTS TO ASSIST INDIVIDUALS WHO FILE REQUESTS FOR ASSISTANCE AND TO
8 FORWARD REPORTS OF MISCONDUCT TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE; AMENDING SECTIONS 41-
9 3-1208, 41-3-1211, AND 41-3-1215, MCA; AND REPEALING SECTION 41-3-1213, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 Section 1. Section 41-3-1208, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "41-3-1208. Office of child and family ombudsman established -- personnel. (1) There is an
15 office of the child and family ombudsman within the department of justice provided for in 2-15-2001.
16 (2) (a) The attorney general shall appoint a person who is a resident of this state and is qualified
17 by training and experience to perform the duties of the ombudsman.
18 (b) The ombudsman shall hire and supervise other personnel necessary to perform the function of
19 the office of the child and family ombudsman, including:
20 (i) two intake and outreach specialists;
21 (ii) two associate ombudsmen; and
22 (iii) two research and data analysts."
23
24 Section 2. Section 41-3-1211, MCA, is amended to read:
25 "41-3-1211. Powers and duties. The powers and duties of the ombudsman are:
26 (1) to respond to requests for assistance regarding administrative acts and to investigate
27 administrative acts;

4 Section 3. Section 41-3-1215, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "41-3-1215. Systemic oversight of child protective services activities. (1) The office of the child
6 and family ombudsman shall provide oversight of the child protective services provided by the department to
7 identify and report on trends in the handling of the cases and make recommendations on ways to improve the
8 child protective services system.
9 (2) The office shall analyze information received, reviewed, and compiled by the ombudsman,
10 including but not limited to:
11 (a) the reports provided pursuant to 41-3-209;
12 (b) the requests for assistance received by the office;
13 (c) policies and procedures used by the department in responding to and investigating reports of
14 child abuse and neglect;
15 (d) findings relating to ombudsman investigations; and
16 (e) best practices for the handling of child abuse and neglect cases and the degree to which the
17 department is using those practices.
18 (3) Based on the analysis, the ombudsman shall provide systemic and trend recommendations
19 twice each calendar year to the department. The department shall respond to the recommendations within 60
20 days of receiving the report unless the department has requested in writing and received an extension of the
21 deadline for response. The response must include a description of how it will implement the recommendations
22 or justification as to why the department is not implementing the recommendations at that time.
23 (4) If the department fails to respond to the recommendations as required or provides justification
24 as to why it is not implementing the recommendations, the ombudsman shall send the report to the governor,
25 the director of the department, and the children, families, health, and human services interim committee.
26 (5) The ombudsman may carry out the oversight duties provided for in this section independently
27 or in conjunction with other governmental bodies or nongovernmental research organizations, consistent with
