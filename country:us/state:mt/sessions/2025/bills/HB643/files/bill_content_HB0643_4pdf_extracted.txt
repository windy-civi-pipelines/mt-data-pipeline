2 INTRODUCED BY T. SHARP, M. VINTON, S. KELLY, S. GIST, G. KMETZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING PAYMENT OF COSTS FOR USE OF A DETENTION
5 CENTER; REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "ACTUAL COSTS"; REQUIRING THE ARRESTING AGENCY,
6 THE DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS, AND THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN
7 SERVICES TO PAY THE ACTUAL COSTS PLUS 10% OF HOLDING A PERSON IN CONFINEMENT IN A
8 DETENTION CENTER UNDER CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES; REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "ACTUAL
9 COSTS"; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AND AMENDING SECTION 7-32-2242, MCA; AND PROVIDING
10 A TERMINATION DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 7-32-2242, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "7-32-2242. Use of detention center -- payment of costs. (1) Local government, state, and federal
16 law enforcement and correctional agencies may use any detention center for the confinement of arrested
17 persons and the punishment of offenders, under conditions imposed by law and with the consent of the
18 governing body responsible for the detention center.
19 (2) (a) Except as provided in 7-32-2245, if a person is confined in a detention center by an
20 arresting agency not responsible for the operation of the detention center, the actual costs plus 10% of holding
21 the person in confinement must be paid by the arresting agency unless otherwise agreed to by the arresting
22 agency and the operator of the detention center.
23 (b) If a city or town commits a person to the detention center of the county in which the city or town
24 is located for a reason other than detention pending trial for or detention for service of a sentence for violating
25 an ordinance of that city or town, the costs must be paid by the county, except as provided in 7-32-2245. If the
26 department of corrections is the arresting agency and the inmate is a probation violator, the costs must be paid
27 by the county in which the district court that retains jurisdiction over the inmate is located, except as provided in
28 7-32-2245.

- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0643.4
1 (c) The department of corrections is responsible to pay for paying the actual costs plus 10% for
2 defendants following the pronouncement of sentence pursuant to 46-19-101.
3 (d) The department of public health and human services is responsible for paying the actual costs
4 plus 10% of holding in confinement in a detention center a person who has been committed to the custody of
5 the director of public health and human services and is awaiting placement in an appropriate facility pursuant to
6 46-14-221, 46-14-301, 46-14-302, 46-14-304, 46-14-312, or 46-19-101.
7 (d)(e)(D) Payments must be made to the government unit responsible for the detention center or
8 to the administrator operating a private detention center under an agreement provided for in 7-32-2201 on
9 presentation of a claim to the arresting agency or the department of public health and human services pursuant
10 to subsection (2) (d).
11 (e)(f)(E)For the purposes of this section, "actual costs" of a detention center is defined as the greater
12 of:
13 (i) the daily per inmate provider rate for crossroads correctional facility less 10%; or
14 (ii) $82 means the costs of housing an inmate as documented by the detention center in which the
15 inmate is held OF A DETENTION CENTER IS DEFINED AS THE GREATER OF:
16 (I) THE DAILY PER INMATE PROVIDER RATE FOR A CROSSROADS CORRECTIONAL FACILITY AN OPERATOR
17 OF A PRIVATE CORRECTIONAL FACILITY IN MONTANA WITH A POPULATION OF MORE THAN 500 STATE INMATES THAT
18 CONTRACTS WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS LESS 10%; OR
19 (II) $82.
20 (3) If a person is a fugitive from justice from an out-of-state jurisdiction, the costs, including medical
21 expenses, of holding the person in a detention center pending extradition must be paid by the out-of-state
22 jurisdiction."
23
24 NEW SECTION. SECTION 2. APPROPRIATION. (1) THERE IS APPROPRIATED $6 MILLION FROM THE STATE
25 SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT CREATED IN 50-1-119 TO THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES FOR
26 THE BIENNIUM BEGINNING JULY 1, 2025. THE FUNDS MAY BE USED ONLY TO REIMBURSE COUNTIES FOR THE COSTS OF
27 HOLDING IN CONFINEMENT IN DETENTION CENTERS INDIVIDUALS WHO ARE AWAITING EVALUATION OR EXAMINATION
28 UNDER 46-14-101, 46-14-202, 46-14-221, OR 46-14-311, OR WHO HAVE BEEN COMMITTED TO THE CUSTODY OF THE
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 643
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0643.4
1 DIRECTOR OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES AND ARE AWAITING PLACEMENT IN APPROPRIATE FACILITIES
2 PURSUANT TO 46-14-221, 46-14-301, 46-14-302, 46-14-304, 46-14-312, OR 46-19-101.
3 (2) THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES SHALL REIMBURSE COUNTIES AT A RATE
4 OF 150% 135% OF THE DAILY PER INMATE PROVIDER RATE OF AN OPERATOR OF A PRIVATE CORRECTIONAL FACILITY IN
5 MONTANA WITH A POPULATION OF MORE THAN 500 STATE INMATES THAT CONTRACTS WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF
6 CORRECTIONS.
7 (2) (3) THE LEGISLATURE INTENDS THAT THIS IS A ONE-TIME-ONLY APPROPRIATION.
8
9 NEW SECTION. SECTION 3. TERMINATION. [THIS ACT] TERMINATES JUNE 30, 2027.
10 - END -
