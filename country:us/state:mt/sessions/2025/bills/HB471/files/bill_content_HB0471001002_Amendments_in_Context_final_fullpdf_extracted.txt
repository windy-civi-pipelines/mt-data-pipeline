3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING SCHOOL LAWS RELATED TO HUMAN SEXUALITY
5 AND IDENTITY INSTRUCTION TO INCREASE THE TRANSPARENCY OF CURRICULUM AND
6 STRENGTHEN THE RIGHTS OF PARENTS TO CONTROL THEIR CHILD'S INSTRUCTION BY REQUIRING
7 PARENT OR GUARDIAN PERMISSION FOR A CHILD TO RECEIVE IDENTITY INSTRUCTION; REVISING
8 REQUIREMENTS FOR SCHOOLS TO PROVIDE NOTICE TO PARENTS AND GUARDIANS PRIOR TO THE
9 PROVISION OF HUMAN SEXUALITY INSTRUCTION; PROVIDING AND REVISING DEFINITIONS;
10 CLARIFYING AVAILABLE REMEDIES AND REQUIRING TRUSTEES TO REPORT VIOLATIONS TO THE
11 BOARD OF PUBLIC EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 20-7-120, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
12 DATE.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 Section 1. Section 20-7-120, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "20-7-120. Excused absences from curriculum requirements Human sexuality instruction --
18 notice to parents -- excused absences -- prohibited activities -- remedies. (1) (a) A parent, guardian, or
19 other person who is responsible for the care of a child may refuse to allow the child to attend or withdraw the
20 child from a course of instruction, a class period, an assembly, an organized school function, or instruction
21 provided by the district through its staff or guests invited at the request of the district regarding human sexuality
22 instruction. The withdrawal or refusal to attend is an excused absence pursuant to 20-5-103.
23 (b) A school may not allow a child to attend identity instruction unless the child's parent or guardian
24 agrees in writing to allow the child to attend. Pursuant to 20-5-103, a child is excused from participating in
25 identity instruction unless the child fails to attend after the parent or guardian has agreed in writing to allow the
26 child's attendance. A school may allow a parent or guardian the opportunity to provide the agreement under this
27 subsection (1)(b) at the beginning of a school year or semester as part of the annual notification under

11 course that includes units or lessons on human sexuality instruction or identity instruction in the district or
12 school in advance of the instruction of:
13 (a) the basic content of the district's or school's human sexuality instruction or identity instruction

15 human sexuality instruction or identity instruction in the course pursuant to subsection (5); and
16 (b) the parent's or guardian's right to withdraw the student from the district's or school's human
17 sexuality instruction; and
18 (c) the requirement that the parent or guardian provide written permission to allow the child to
19 attend identity instruction.

25 (b) a calendar of events or assemblies at which human sexuality instruction or identity instruction

3 (b) A school district shall prepare a summary of the district's health enhancement curriculum and
4 shall review and prepare any updates to the summary each year. The summary is public information and is

6 (5)(6) A school district or its personnel or agents may not permit a person, entity, or any affiliate or
7 agent of the person or entity to offer, sponsor, or furnish in any manner any course materials or instruction
8 relating to human sexuality or sexually transmitted diseases to its students or personnel if the person, entity, or
9 any affiliate or agent of the person or entity is a provider of abortion services.
10 (7) (a) Remedies for the violation of this section include but are not limited to those under 20-4-
11 110.
12 (b) If, after investigating a violation under this section, the trustees of a district find that an
13 individual has knowingly or repeatedly violated this section, the trustees shall report the findings to the board of
14 public education pursuant to 20-4-110.
15 (c) Nothing in this subsection (7) precludes an individual from filing a suit asserting a violation of
16 this section or any other claim otherwise allowed by law, and nothing in this subsection (7) requires exhaustion
17 of the other remedies prior to filing a suit.

23 abstinence, contraception, or reproductive rights and responsibilities.
24 (ii) "Identity instruction" means instruction that has the goal or purpose of studying, exploring, or
25 informing students about gender identity, or gender expression, or sexual orientation.
26 (iii) "Instruction" means the conduct of organized learning activities, including the provision of
