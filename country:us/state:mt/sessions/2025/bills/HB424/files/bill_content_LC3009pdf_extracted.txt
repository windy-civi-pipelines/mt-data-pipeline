14 subsections (2)(a) through (2)(i), class thirteen property includes:
15 (a) electrical generation facilities, except wind generation facilities, biomass generation facilities,
16 and energy storage facilities classified under 15-6-157, of a centrally assessed electric power company;
17 (b) electrical generation facilities, except wind generation facilities, biomass generation facilities,
18 and energy storage facilities classified under 15-6-157, owned or operated by an exempt wholesale generator
19 or an entity certified as an exempt wholesale generator pursuant to 42 U.S.C. 16451;
20 (c) noncentrally assessed electrical generation facilities, except wind generation facilities, biomass
21 generation facilities, and energy storage facilities classified under 15-6-157, owned or operated by any
22 electrical energy producer;
23 (d) allocations of centrally assessed telecommunications services companies; and
24 (e) dedicated communications infrastructure described in 15-6-162(5) for which construction
25 commenced after June 30, 2027 2037, or for which the 15-year 25-year period provided for in 15-6-162(5)(c)
26 has expired.
27 (2) Class thirteen property does not include:
28 (a) property owned by cooperative rural electric cooperative associations classified under 15-6-

13 (a) (i) "Electrical generation facilities" means any combination of a physically connected generator
14 or generators, associated prime movers, and other associated property, including appurtenant land and
15 improvements and personal property, that are normally operated together to produce electric power. The term
16 includes but is not limited to generating facilities that produce electricity from coal-fired steam turbines, oil or
17 gas turbines, turbine generators that are driven by falling water, or solar panel systems.
18 (ii) The term does not include electrical generation facilities used for noncommercial purposes or
19 exclusively for agricultural purposes.
20 (iii) (A) The term also does not include a qualifying facility certified by the federal energy regulatory
21 commission.
22 (B) To qualify for consideration of an abatement as allowed in 15-24-1402, the requesting entity
23 must disclose, in writing, its intent to request certification as a qualifying facility to the governing body.
24 (C) If the intent is not disclosed and an abatement granted, abatement may be rescinded by the
25 governing body.
26 (D) Certified qualifying facilities are classified under 15-6-134 and 15-6-138.
27 (iv) The term also does not include a facility that is owned and operated by a person not primarily
28 engaged in the generation or sale of electricity other than power from a small power production facility and

1 classified under 15-6-134 and 15-6-138.
2 (b) (i) "Fiber optic or coaxial cable" means any fiber optic or coaxial cable, including all capitalized
3 costs associated with installing and placing in service the fiber optic or coaxial cable, and other property that is
4 normally operated when installing and placing in service fiber optic or coaxial cable to deliver digital
5 communication and access to the internet.
6 (ii) The term does not include routers, head-end equipment, central office equipment and other
7 electronics, or hardware or software not directly associated with installing and placing in service fiber optic or
8 coaxial cable or the buildings used to house equipment.
9 (4) (a) Except as provided in subsection (4)(b), class thirteen property is taxed at 6% of its market
10 value.
11 (b) (i) Except as provided in subsection (4)(b)(ii), fiber optic or coaxial cable installed and placed in
12 service on or after July 1, 2021, is exempt from taxation for a period of 5 years starting from the date the fiber
13 optic or coaxial cable was placed in service, after which the property exemption is phased out at a rate of 20%
14 a year, with the property being assessed at 100% of its taxable value after a 10-year period. In order to
15 maintain the exemption, the owner of fiber optic or coaxial cable shall reinvest the tax savings from the
16 exemption by installing and placing in service new fiber optic or coaxial cable in Montana within 2 years from
17 the date the owner first claimed the exemption provided for in this subsection (4)(b) without charging those
18 costs to the consumer. The cost of installing or placing into service fiber optic or coaxial cable with the
19 reinvested tax savings without charging those costs to the consumer must be equal to or greater than the value
20 of the tax savings received from the tax incentive.
21 (ii) Fiber optic or coaxial cable installed using federal funds received pursuant to Section 9901 of
22 the American Rescue Plan Act is not eligible for exemption from taxation under this section.
23 (iii) An entity that claims a tax exemption under this subsection (4)(b) shall maintain adequate
24 books and records demonstrating the investment the owner made when installing and placing in service fiber
25 optic or coaxial cable in Montana. The property owners shall make those records available to the department
26 for inspection upon request.
27 (5) (a) The property taxes exempted from taxation by subsection (4)(b) are subject to termination
28 or recapture if the department determines that the owner failed to install and place in service new coaxial or

2 (b) Upon notice from the department that the owner's exemption has terminated, any local
3 governing body may recapture taxes previously exempted in that jurisdiction, plus interest and penalties for
4 nonpayment of property taxes as provided in 15-16-102, during any tax year in which an exemption under the
5 provisions of this section was improper. Any recapture must occur within 10 years after the end of the calendar
6 year in which the exemption was first claimed.
7 (c) The recapture of abated taxes may be cancelled, in whole or in part, if the local governing body
8 determines that the taxpayer's failure to meet the requirements is a result of circumstances beyond the control
9 of the taxpayer."

11 Section 2. Section 15-6-162, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "15-6-162. Class seventeen property -- description -- taxable percentage. (1) Class seventeen
13 property includes the land, improvements, furniture, fixtures, equipment, tools that are not exempt under 15-6-
14 219, and supplies except those included in class five under 15-6-135 of a qualified data center.
15 (2) (a) "Qualified data center" means the land, improvements, and personal property of a facility
16 designed or modified to house networked computers or equipment supporting computing, networking, or data
17 storage that is composed of one or more buildings under single ownership, provided that a single ownership
18 entity includes a wholly owned subsidiary or a parent company with 100% ownership interest, on contiguous
19 parcels of land that consist of at least:
20 (i) 300,000 square feet, where the total cost of land, improvements, personal property, and
21 software is at least $150 million with construction commencing after June 30, 2017; or
22 (ii) 25,000 square feet of new or expanded area, where the total cost of land, improvements,
23 personal property, and software is at least $50 million invested during a 48-month period with construction
24 commencing after January 1, 2019.
25 (b) The term includes but is not limited to:
26 (i) cooling systems, cooling towers, and other temperature infrastructure;
27 (ii) power infrastructure for transformation, distribution, or management of electricity used for the
28 maintenance and operation of the facility, such as including:

3 (C) electrical generation and storage systems located on the facility side of the utility meter and
4 primarily used by a qualified data center for onsite power; and
5 (iii) any other equipment necessary for the maintenance and operation of the facility.
6 (3) During construction, property not meeting the requirements of subsection (2) must be classified
7 as class seventeen property if, prior to March 1 of the first tax year for which the classification will be applied,
8 the taxpayer certifies to the department that the facility under construction will meet the requirements of
9 subsection (2) within 2 years of the date of the certification.
10 (4) The Except as provided in subsection (5), the taxable property of a qualified data center must
11 be locally assessed.
12 (5) (a) Class seventeen property includes centrally assessed interstate or intrastate dedicated
13 communications infrastructure that is owned or leased by the owner of a qualified data center and is composed
14 of telecommunication or data lines, equipment, and services, including but not limited to copper or fiber optic
15 lines or microwave, satellite, or other wireless communication systems.
16 (b) To qualify under this subsection (5), construction of the owned or leased interstate or intrastate
17 communications infrastructure must commence after June 30, 2017, and before July 1, 2027 2037, and must
18 satisfy the criteria of this section.
19 (c) Dedicated communications infrastructure provided for in this subsection (5) is taxed at the rate
20 provided for in subsection (6) for a period of 15 25 years from the time that construction commences. After the
21 15-year 25-year period, the dedicated communications infrastructure is taxed as class thirteen property at the
22 rate provided in 15-6-156.
23 (6) (a) Except as provided in subsection (6)(b), electrical generation and storage systems are
24 considered primarily used onsite if used at least 80% for onsite consumption as measured on an annualized
25 kilowatt hour basis as certified annually to the department.
26 (b) If the governor declares an electrical generation emergency, the 80% requirement in
27 subsection (6)(a) does not apply to a qualified data center that relies on backup power generation systems and
28 makes available electricity generated on the facility side of the utility meter to service residential and business
