69th Legislature 2025 HB0137.2
2 INTRODUCED BY S. FITZPATRICK
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING SECURITIES REGISTRATION FEES TO ELIMINATE
5 THE DISTINCTION BETWEEN IN-STATE AND OUT-OF-STATE SALESPERSONS OR INVESTMENT
6 ADVISER REPRESENTATIVES; AMENDING SECTION 30-10-209, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE
7 EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 30-10-209, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "30-10-209. Fees. The following fees must be paid in advance under the provisions of parts 1 through
13 3 of this chapter:
14 (1) (a) For the registration of securities by notification, coordination, or qualification or for notice
15 filing of a federal covered security, there must be paid to the commissioner for the initial year of registration or
16 notice filing a fee of $200 for the first $100,000 of initial issue or portion of the first $100,000 in this state, based
17 on offering price, plus 1/10 of 1% for any excess over $100,000, with a maximum fee of $1,000.
18 (b) Each succeeding year, a registration of securities or a notice filing of a federal covered security
19 may be renewed, prior to its termination date, for an additional year upon consent of the commissioner and
20 payment of a renewal fee to be computed at 1/10 of 1% of the aggregate offering price of the securities that are
21 to be offered in this state during that year. The renewal fee may not be less than $200 or more than $1,000.
22 The registration or the notice filing may be amended to increase the amount of securities to be offered.
23 (c) If a registrant or issuer of federal covered securities sells securities in excess of the aggregate
24 amount registered for sale in this state or for which a notice filing has been submitted, the registrant or issuer
25 may file an amendment to the registration statement or notice filing to include the excess sales. If the registrant
26 or issuer of a federal covered security fails to file an amendment before the expiration date of the registration
27 order or notice, the registrant or issuer shall pay a filing fee for the excess sales of three times the amount
28 calculated in the manner specified in subsection (1)(b). Registration or notice of the excess securities is
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 137
- 2025

1 effective retroactively to the date of the existing registration or notice.
2 (d) Each series, portfolio, or other subdivision of an investment company or similar issuer is treated
3 as a separate issuer of securities. The issuer shall pay a notice filing fee to be calculated as provided in
4 subsections (1)(a) through (1)(c). The notice filing fee collected by the commissioner must be deposited in the
5 state special revenue account provided for in 30-10-115. The issuer shall pay a fee of $50 for each filing made
6 for the purpose of changing the name of a series, portfolio, or other subdivision of an investment company or
7 similar issuer.
8 (2) (a) For registration of a broker-dealer or investment adviser, the fee is $400 for original
9 registration and $400 for each annual renewal.
10 (b) (i) For registration of a salesperson or investment adviser representative, the fee:
11 (A) for an out-of-state salesperson or investment adviser representative is $100 for original
12 registration with each employer, $100 for each annual renewal, and $100 for each transfer; and
13 (B) for an in-state salesperson or investment adviser representative is $50 for original registration
14 with each employer, $50 for each annual renewal, and $50 for each transfer.
15 (ii) A salesperson who is registered as an investment adviser representative with a broker-dealer
16 registered as an investment adviser is not required to pay the fee in subsection (2)(b)(i)(A) or (2)(b)(i)(B) to
17 register as an investment adviser representative.
18 (c) For a federal covered adviser, the fee is $400 for the initial notice filing and $400 for each
19 annual renewal.
20 (3) For certified or uncertified copies of any documents filed with the commissioner, the fee is the
21 cost to the department.
22 (4) For a request for an exemption under 30-10-105(15), the fee must be established by the
23 commissioner by rule. For a request for any other exemption or an exception to the provisions of parts 1
24 through 3 of this chapter, the fee is $100.
25 (5) All fees are considered fully earned when received. In the event of overpayment, only those
26 amounts in excess of $10 may be refunded.
27 (6) (a) Except as provided in subsection (6)(b), all fees, miscellaneous charges, fines, and
28 penalties collected by the commissioner pursuant to parts 1 through 3 of this chapter and the rules adopted

1 under parts 1 through 3 of this chapter must be deposited in the general fund.
2 (b) From March 7, 2013, through June 30, 2027, the fees collected under subsection (1)(b), the
3 notice filing fees provided for in subsection (1)(d), and the amounts collected for examination costs under 30-
4 10-210 are subject to deposit as provided in 30-10-115(2). On or after July 1, 2027, the notice filing fees
5 provided for in subsection (1)(d) and the amounts collected for examination costs under 30-10-210 are subject
6 to deposit as provided in 30-10-115(2)."
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Applicability. [This act] applies to registration fees paid to the
11 commissioner of insurance and securities on or after [the effective date of this act].
