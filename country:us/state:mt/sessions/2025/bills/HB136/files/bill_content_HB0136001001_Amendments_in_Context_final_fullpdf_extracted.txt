1 HOUSE BILL NO. 136
2 INTRODUCED BY S. FITZPATRICK
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: "AN ACT REVISING INSURANCE LAWS TO ALLOW INSURERS TO
5 PROVIDE A BENEFIT OR PREMIUM REDUCTION TO A POLICYHOLDER FOR MEASURES TAKEN TO
6 REDUCE THE RISK OF DAMAGE TO PROPERTY FROM FIRE, WIND, AND HAIL; PROVIDING THAT A
7 BENEFIT OR PREMIUM REDUCTION DOES NOT CONSTITUTE A MIDTERM PREMIUM INCREASE;
8 REQUIRING AN INSURER TO DISCLOSE THE BENEFIT OR PREMIUM REDUCTION; AND AMENDING
9 SECTIONS 33-15-1108, 33-16-203, AND 33-18-210, MCA."
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Risk preventative measures for homeowners -- premium reduction -
14 - fire, wind, and hail. (1) An insurer may provide a benefit or a premium reduction to a homeowner's insurance
15 policyholder for preventative measures taken by the policyholder. Preventative measures include but are not
16 limited to actions that reduce the risk of damage to property from fire, wind, or hail.
17 (a) Fire preventative measures include but are not limited to any of the following:
18 (i) using ignition-resistant, fire-resistive, or noncombustible building materials in construction,
19 including noncombustible roofing or coating, exterior glazing for fire protection, and interior building materials
20 designed to stop flame spread and prevent building collapse upon occupants and emergency service
21 personnel;
22 (ii) maintaining an adequate outside water source;
23 (iii) providing annual confirmation, if requested by the insurer, that the insured has employed
24 defensible space landscaping around the insured's structures;
25 (iv) keeping a border of at least 5 feet of nonflammable landscaping around the insured property;
26 and
27 (v) complying with any local, state, or federal fire prevention or community preparedness

2 (vi) installing fire alarm or emergency alarm systems;
3 (vii) complying with the requirements of the International Wildland-Urban Interface Code if a
4 building is located in a wildland-urban interface area; and
5 (viii) providing sufficient access to the property for firefighting equipment and authorized emergency
6 vehicles as defined in 61-8-102.
7 (b) Wind and hail preventative measures include but are not limited to the following:
8 (i) using an industry-approved resistant roof shingle;
9 (ii) anchoring outdoor furniture;
10 (iii) pruning tree branches or otherwise providing landscaping that reduces potential damage to the
11 insured property; and
12 (iv) installing protective coverings to avoid additional damage to the insured property.
13 (2) An insurer who provides a benefit or premium reduction as provided in this section must
14 include the benefit or premium reduction in writing to the policyholder. A benefit or premium reduction provided
15 by an insurer under this section does not constitute an illegal rebate under 33-18-210.
16 (3) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
17 (a) "Benefit" means a rebate, discount, abatement, credit, reduction of premium, or other
18 advantage to the policyholder provided under the terms of a preventative measures agreement.
19 (b) "Policyholder" means a person who is the named insured in a homeowner's insurance policy.

21 Section 2. Section 33-15-1108, MCA, is amended to read:
22 "33-15-1108. Limitation on midterm premium increases. (1) In any case involving property or
23 casualty insurance that is subject to this part, the insurer may not unilaterally increase the rate charged or
24 decrease the coverage provided for the contract period unless:
25 (a) there is a change in risk during that period because of the addition or removal of persons or
26 property that was included in the rate at last renewal;
27 (b) the risk was misrepresented by the insured; or

7 Section 3. Section 33-16-203, MCA, is amended to read:
8 "33-16-203. Rates filed. (1) Every insurer, rating organization, or advisory organization shall file with
9 the commissioner all rates intended for use within this state, together with supporting data sufficient to
10 substantiate the filing. The filing required by this subsection may be made by rating organizations on behalf of
11 their members and subscribers. However, this provision does not prohibit a member or subscriber from filing
12 rates on its own behalf. Any deviations from a rating organization's rates by a member or subscriber must be
13 filed with the commissioner and must be accompanied by supporting data.
14 (2) Rates filed must provide for a premium reduction to:
15 (a) qualified insured operators who are 55 years of age or older in accordance with 33-16-222; or
16 (b) qualified members of the Montana national guard in accordance with 33-16-230.
17 (3) Rates filed may provide a benefit or premium reduction for preventative measures taken by the
18 insured for homeowner's insurance under [section 1]."

20 Section 4. Section 33-18-210, MCA, is amended to read:

22 insurance -- exceptions -- limitations. (1) Except as provided in subsections (3), (4), and (11)(a), a title,
23 property, casualty, or surety insurer or an employee, representative, or insurance producer of an insurer may
24 not, as an inducement to purchase insurance or after insurance has been effected, pay, allow, or give or offer to
25 pay, allow, or give, directly or indirectly, a:
26 (a) rebate, discount, abatement, credit, or reduction of the premium named in the insurance policy;
27 (b) special favor or advantage in the dividends or other benefits to accrue on the policy; or

15 insurance, in the dividends or other benefits payable on insurance, or in any other of the terms and conditions
16 of the insurance either between insureds or property having like insuring or risk characteristics or between
17 insureds because of race, color, creed, religion, or national origin.
18 (6) This section may not be construed as prohibiting the payment of commissions or other
19 compensation to licensed insurance producers or as prohibiting an insurer from allowing or returning lawful
20 dividends, savings, or unabsorbed premium deposits to its participating policyholders, members, or subscribers.
21 (7) An insurer may not make or permit unfair discrimination between individuals or risks of the
22 same class and of essentially the same hazards by refusing to issue, refusing to renew, canceling, or limiting
23 the amount of insurance coverage on a property or casualty risk because of the geographic location of the risk,
24 unless:
25 (a) the refusal, cancellation, or limitation is for a business purpose that is not a mere pretext for
26 unfair discrimination; or
27 (b) the refusal, cancellation, or limitation is required by law or regulatory mandate.
