4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CREATING A FIREFIGHTING TRAINING FACILITY GRANT
5 PROGRAM TO FUND CAPITAL CONSTRUCTION, MAINTENANCE AND REPAIR PROJECTS, AND
6 EQUIPMENT PURCHASES; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; ALLOWING
7 APPROPRIATIONS TO CONTINUE INTO THE 2027 AND 2029 BIENNIUMS; AND PROVIDING AN
8 EFFECTIVE DATE AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. For the purposes of [sections 1 through 6], unless otherwise
13 provided, the following definitions apply:
14 (1) "Department" means the department of military affairs established in 2-15-1201.
15 (2) "Local government firefighting authority" means a governmental fire agency organized under
16 Title 7, chapter 33.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Firefighting training facility infrastructure account -- use. (1) There is
19 a firefighting training facility infrastructure account within the state special revenue fund provided for in 17-2-102
20 to provide grant funding to local government firefighting authorities that provide firefighting services and for
21 administrative costs related to administering the grants. The department shall administer the account.
22 (2) Up to 3% of the funds appropriated in [section 8] may be allocated for the department's
23 administrative costs.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Firefighting training facility infrastructure grants authorization. (1)
26 The department is authorized to make up to $5 million in grants to local government firefighting authorities for
27 property acquisition, construction, space acquisition, remodeling, repairs, and the purchase of appliances and
28 equipment. The grants authorized in this section are subject to the conditions set forth in [section 5].
- 1 - LC 4344
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 4344
1 (2) The department shall receive proposals from local government firefighting authorities for
2 firefighting training facility infrastructure projects.
3 (3) Funding for projects may be provided only as long as there are sufficient funds available from
4 the amount that was deposited or transferred in the firefighting training facility infrastructure account for grants
5 established in [section 2]. Funding for these projects must be made available in the order that the grant
6 recipients satisfy the conditions described in [section 5].
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Eligibility -- submission deadline -- priority -- guidelines. (1) A local
9 government firefighting authority may apply to the department for firefighting training facility infrastructure grants
10 under [section 3].
11 (2) Local government firefighting authorities shall submit grant applications to the department in
12 order to be eligible for funding under [section 3].
13 (3) The department is authorized to adopt guidelines necessary to implement [sections 3 through
14 5].
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Condition of grants -- disbursement of funds. (1) The disbursement of
17 grant funds for the projects chosen by the department pursuant to [section 3] is subject to the following
18 conditions:
19 (a) for grants in an amount of $25,000 or more, the department may require a grant recipient to
20 provide a 1% match in cash or donated services to be eligible to receive a grant;
21 (b) the grant recipient shall execute a grant agreement with the department that includes a project
22 management plan and reporting requirements to track the outcomes of allocated grants; and
23 (c) the grant recipient shall satisfy other specific requirements considered necessary by the
24 department to accomplish the purpose of the project as evidenced by the application to the department.
25 (2) Projects must adhere to the design standards required by applicable regulations. Recipients of
26 program funds for projects that are not subject to any design standards shall comply with generally accepted
27 industry standards.
28 (3) If actual project expenses are lower than the projected expense of the project, the department
- 2 - LC 4344
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 4344
1 shall reduce the amount of grant funds to be provided to grant recipients.
2
3 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Maximum state funding available -- for each project -- for each
4 county. (1) The amount of state funding allocated to entities within an individual county under [section 3
5 through 5] may not exceed $500,000. No more than two applications may be funded in any one county.
6 (2) The amount of a grant to a single project may not exceed $250,000.
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Transfer of funds. By July 1, 2025, the state treasurer shall transfer $5
9 million from the general fund to the firefighting training facility infrastructure account established in [section 2].
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Appropriation. There is appropriated $5 million from the firefighting
12 training facility infrastructure account established in [section 2] to the department of military affairs for the
13 biennium beginning July 1, 2025, for grants authorized in [sections 3 through 5]. Appropriations are authorized
14 to continue through the biennium beginning July 1, 2029.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Termination. [This act] terminates June 30, 2031.
19 - END -
