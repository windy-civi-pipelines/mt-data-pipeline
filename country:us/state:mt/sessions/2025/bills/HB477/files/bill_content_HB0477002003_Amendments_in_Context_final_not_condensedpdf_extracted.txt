1 HOUSE BILL NO. 477
2 INTRODUCED BY M. MARLER, M. YAKAWICH
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ELIMINATING THE USE PROHIBITING THE REGULATION OF
5 STYROFOAM IN FOOD-RELATED BUSINESSES; PROVIDING EXEMPTIONS; REQUIRING FEES FOR
6 EXEMPTION APPLICATIONS; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AUTHORIZING USE OF
7 ACCOUNT; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS A DEFINITION; AMENDING SECTION 7-1-111,75-1-110, MCA; AND
8 PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 WHEREAS, expanded polystyrene foam, commonly known as styrofoam, is a material that causes
11 environmental harm and poses a threat to fish and wildlife; and
12 WHEREAS, styrofoam takes up space in landfills and contributes to the need for costly expansions
13 borne by taxpayers; and
14 WHEREAS, styrofoam threatens the right of each Montanan to a clean and healthful environment.
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
17
18 (Refer to Introduced Bill)
19 Strike everything after the enacting clause and insert:
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Banning of polystyrene prohibited. (1) (a) The state or a political
22 subdivision may not regulate or prohibit the use of polystyrene foam containers or packaging materials.
23 (b) Local legislation adopted or continued in violation of this section is void and unenforceable.
24 (2) For the purposes of this section, "polystyrene foam" means blown polystyrene and expanded
25 and extruded foams that are thermoplastic petrochemical materials using styrene.
26
