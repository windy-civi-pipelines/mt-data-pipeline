8 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM LAWS;
9 REQUIRING THE USE OF A CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM FOR CHILDREN UNDER CERTAIN AGE
10 THRESHOLDS 6 YEARS OF AGE CERTAIN AGE THRESHOLDS; PROVIDING EXEMPTIONS FOR
11 CERTAIN VEHICLES AND CIRCUMSTANCES FROM CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM LAWS;
12 PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 61-9-419, 61-9-420, AND 61-9-421, MCA.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 Section 1. Section 61-9-419, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "61-9-419. "Properly restrained" defined Definitions. (1) As used in 61-9-420 through 61-9-423
18 and this section, unless the context requires otherwise, the following definitions apply:
19 (a) "Booster seat" means a child safety restraint system that meets applicable federal motor
20 vehicle safety standards and is designed to provide belt-positioning that elevates a child to be properly seated
21 with a safety belt.
22 (b) "Child safety restraint system" means a device that:
23 (i) meets applicable federal motor vehicle safety standards; and
24 (ii) is appropriate to the age of the child being restrained, as provided in 61-9-420.
25 (c) (i) "properly restrained" "Properly restrained" means fastened in a manner prescribed by the
26 manufacturer of the child safety restraint system that permits the child safety restraint system to act as a body
27 restraint, but.
28 (ii) The term does not mean a system in which the only body restraint is a safety belt of the type

1 required by 61-9-409.
2 (d) "Secured with a safety belt" means restrained or secured by a seat belt that:
3 (i) meets applicable federal motor vehicle safety standards; and
4 (ii) is properly adjusted and fastened WITH THE LAP STRAP ACROSS THE HIPS AND THE SHOULDER
5 STRAP ACROSS THE CHEST, including both the shoulder and lap straps,, INCLUDING BOTH THE SHOULDER AND LAP
6 STRAPS, when equipped in the vehicle."
7
8 Section 2. Section 61-9-420, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "61-9-420. Child safety restraint systems -- standards -- exemptions. (1) EACH MOTOR VEHICLE
10 PASSENGER WHO IS UNDER 6 YEARS OF AGE AND WEIGHS LESS THAN 60 POUNDS MUST BE TRANSPORTED AND
11 PROPERLY RESTRAINED IN A CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM. THE CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM MUST BE
12 APPROPRIATE FOR THE HEIGHT AND WEIGHT OF THE CHILD, AS INDICATED BY MANUFACTURER STANDARDS, AND COMPLY
13 WITH FEDERAL MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY STANDARDS.Each motor vehicle passenger who is under 6 years of age
14 and weighs less than 60 pounds must be transported and properly restrained in a child safety restraint. The
15 child safety restraint must be appropriate for the height and weight of the child as indicated by manufacturer
16 standards. A MOTOR VEHICLE PASSENGER WHO IS UNDER 2 YEARS OF AGE MUST BE PROPERLY RESTRAINED IN A REAR-
17 FACING CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM THAT COMPLIES WITH FEDERAL MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY STANDARDS. A
18 motor vehicle passenger who is under 2 years of age must be properly restrained in a rear-facing child safety
19 restraint system that complies with federal motor vehicle safety standards.
20 (2) A motor vehicle passenger who is between 2 years of age and up to 4 years of age must be
21 properly restrained in a rear-facing or forward-facing child safety restraint system with an internal harness that
22 complies with federal motor vehicle safety standards.
23 (3) A motor vehicle passenger who is between 4 years of age and up to 8 years of age must be
24 properly restrained in a forward-facing child safety restraint system with an internal harness or a child booster
25 seat that is secured with a vehicle lap-shoulder seat belt, whichever is applicable, that complies with federal
26 motor vehicle safety standards.
27 (4) A motor vehicle passenger who is at least 9 years of age or has outgrown the height or weight
28 limits of a child booster seat as set by the manufacturer, whichever comes first, must be secured with a motor

1 vehicle adult safety belt.
2 (2) A MOTOR VEHICLE PASSENGER WHO IS BETWEEN 2 YEARS OF AGE AND UP TO 4 YEARS OF AGE MUST
3 BE PROPERLY RESTRAINED IN A REAR-FACING OR FORWARD-FACING CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM WITH AN
4 INTERNAL HARNESS THAT COMPLIES WITH FEDERAL MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY STANDARDS.
5 (3) A MOTOR VEHICLE PASSENGER WHO IS BETWEEN 4 YEARS OF AGE AND UP TO 8 YEARS OF AGE MUST
6 BE PROPERLY RESTRAINED IN A FORWARD-FACING CHILD SAFETY RESTRAINT SYSTEM WITH AN INTERNAL HARNESS OR A
7 CHILD BOOSTER SEAT THAT IS SECURED WITH A VEHICLE LAP-SHOULDER SEAT BELT, WHICHEVER IS APPLICABLE, THAT
8 COMPLIES WITH FEDERAL MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY STANDARDS.
9 (4) A MOTOR VEHICLE PASSENGER WHO IS AT LEAST 9 YEARS OF AGE OR HAS OUTGROWN THE HEIGHT OR
10 WEIGHT LIMITS OF A CHILD BOOSTER SEAT AS SET BY THE MANUFACTURER, WHICHEVER COMES FIRST, MUST BE
11 SECURED WITH A MOTOR VEHICLE ADULT SAFETY BELT.
12 (5)(2)(5) A child safety restraint system pursuant to 61-9-419 through 61-9-423 must be
13 installed, adjusted, and used according to the manufacturer instructions.
14 (2) The department shall by rule establish standards in compliance with 61-9-419 through 61-9-
15 423 and applicable federal standards for approved types of child safety restraint systems.
16 (3)(6)(3)(6) The department may by rule exempt from the requirements of subsection (1) this
17 section a child who because of a physical or medical condition or body size cannot be placed in a child safety
18 restraint system."
19
20 Section 3. Section 61-9-421, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "61-9-421. Certain vehicles exempt. Section 61-9-420 is not applicable to a vehicle that as follows:
22 (1) if a vehicle is a motorbus MOTOR HOME, schoolbus SCHOOL BUS, taxicab, FARM TRACTOR, farm
23 tractor, bicycle, moped, quadricycle, or motorcycle, or motor-driven cycle or is not required to be equipped with
24 safety belts under 49 CFR 571 as it reads on January 1, 1984 January 3, 2025;
25 (2) if a vehicle is an ambulance or other emergency vehicle designated or authorized by the
26 department; or
27 (2) has a seating capacity as designated by the manufacturer of two persons and there are two
28 persons 4 years of age or older in the vehicle.

1 (3) if an emergency exists that threatens the life of a person operating a motor vehicle to whom
2 this section would otherwise apply or the life of a child who would otherwise be required to be properly
3 restrained as provided in 61-9-420."
