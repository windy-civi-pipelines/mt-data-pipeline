4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO WAGE
5 TRANSPARENCY; CREATING THE WAGE OPPORTUNITY AND TRANSPARENCY ACT; PROHIBITING
6 WAGE DISCRIMINATION BY EMPLOYERS; PROVIDING EXCEPTIONS; REQUIRING THE LISTING OF
7 WAGE INFORMATION IN JOB POSTINGS; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND PROVIDING RULEMAKING
8 AUTHORITY.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 8] may be cited as the "Wage
13 Opportunity and Transparency Act".
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 8], unless the context clearly
16 indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
17 (1) "Commissioner" has the same meaning as provided in 39-1-101.
18 (2) "Employee" means a person employed by an employer.
19 (3) "Employer" means the state or any political subdivision, commission, department, institution, or
20 school district, and every other person employing a person in the state.
21 (4) "Liquidated damages" means damages to compensate an employee for the delay in receiving
22 amounts due because of an employer's violation of [sections 1 through 8]. The term does not constitute a
23 penalty to the employer.
24 (5) "Posting" means any solicitation intended to recruit job applicants for a specific available
25 position that includes qualifications for desired applicants, including recruitment done directly by an employer or
26 indirectly through a third party, and includes any postings done electronically or with a printed hard copy.
27 (6) "Sex" means an employee's gender identity.
28 (7) "Wage rate" means:

1 (a) for an employee paid on an hourly basis, the hourly compensation paid to the employee plus
2 the value per hour of all other compensation and benefits received by the employee from the employer; and
3 (b) for an employee paid on a salary basis, the total of all compensation and benefits received by
4 the employee from the employer.
5
6 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Wage discrimination prohibited. (1) An employer may not discriminate
7 between employees on the basis of sex, or on the basis of sex in combination with another protected status as
8 described in 49-2-303(1)(a), by paying an employee of one sex a wage rate less than the wage rate paid to an
9 employee of a different sex for substantially similar work, regardless of job title, based on a composite of skill;
10 effort, which may include consideration of shift work; and responsibility, except if the employer demonstrates
11 each of the following:
12 (a) that the wage rate differential is based on:
13 (i) a seniority system;
14 (ii) a merit system;
15 (iii) a system that measures earnings by quantity or quality of production;
16 (iv) the geographic location where the work is performed;
17 (v) education, training, or experience to the extent that they are reasonably related to the work in
18 question; or
19 (vi) travel, if the travel is a regular and necessary condition of the work performed;
20 (b) that each factor relied on in subsection (1)(a) is applied reasonably;
21 (c) that the factors relied on in subsection (1)(a) account for the entire wage rate differential; and
22 (d) that prior wage rate history was not relied on to justify a disparity in current wage rates.
23 (2) An employer may not:
24 (a) seek the wage rate or salary history of an applicant for employment from the applicant or a
25 current or former employer;
26 (b) require that an applicant's prior wage rate or salary history meet certain criteria, except as
27 provided in subsection (3);
28 (c) discriminate or retaliate against a prospective employee for failing to disclose the prospective

****

1 employee's wage rate history;
2 (d) discharge, or in any manner discriminate or retaliate against, an employee for invoking this
3 section on behalf of anyone or assisting in the enforcement of this subsection (2);
4 (e) discharge, discipline, discriminate against, coerce, intimidate, threaten, or interfere with an
5 employee or other person because the employee or person inquired about, disclosed, compared, or otherwise
6 discussed the employee's wage rate;
7 (f) prohibit, as a condition of employment, an employee from disclosing the employee's wage rate;
8 or
9 (g) require an employee to sign a waiver or other document that:
10 (i) prohibits the employee from disclosing wage rate information; or
11 (ii) purports to deny the employee the right to disclose the employee's wage rate information.
12 (3) An employer may confirm an applicant's wage rate or salary history:
13 (a) if the applicant has voluntarily disclosed the applicant's wage rate or salary history; or
14 (b) after the employer has negotiated and made an offer of employment with compensation to the
15 applicant.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Enforcement -- complaints -- civil action -- rulemaking. (1) The
18 commissioner is authorized to create and administer a process to accept and mediate complaints and to
19 provide legal resources concerning alleged violations of [section 3]. The commissioner may adopt
20 administrative rules as necessary for this purpose. The process created and administered by the commissioner
21 does not affect or prevent the right of an aggrieved person from commencing a civil action pursuant to
22 subsection (2).
23 (2) A person aggrieved by a violation of [section 3] may commence a civil action in district court no
24 later than 2 years after the violation occurs. A violation of [section 3(1)] occurs on each occasion that a person
25 is affected by wage discrimination, including each occasion that a discriminatory wage rate is paid.
26 (3) A person aggrieved by a violation of [section 3] may obtain relief for back pay for the entire time
27 the violation continues, not to exceed 3 years.
28 (4) If a civil action is commenced under this section, any party to the civil action may demand a

1 trial by jury.
2 (5) Nothing in this section prevents an aggrieved person from filing a charge with the commission
3 for human rights under 2-15-1706.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Employer liability -- awards -- suits. (1) (a) An employer who violates
6 [section 3(1)] is liable for economic damages in an amount equal to the difference between the amount that the
7 employer paid to the complaining employee and the amount that the employee would have received had there
8 been no violation plus liquidated damages in an amount equal to the employee's economic damages, except as
9 provided in subsection (1)(b).
10 (b) (i) If the employer demonstrates that the act or omission giving rise to the violation was in good
11 faith and that the employer has reasonable grounds for believing that the employer did not violate [section 3(1)],
12 the court may not award liquidated damages.
13 (ii) In determining whether the employer's violation was in good faith, the fact finder may consider
14 evidence that within 2 years prior to the date of the commencement of a civil action pursuant to [section 4], the
15 employer completed a thorough and comprehensive pay audit of its workforce, with the specific goal of
16 identifying and remedying unlawful pay disparities.
17 (2) An employer who violates any provision of [section 3] is liable for:
18 (a) legal and equitable relief, which may include employment, reinstatement, promotion, pay
19 increase, payment of lost wage rates, and liquidated damages; and
20 (b) the employee's reasonable costs, including attorney fees.
21 (3) Nothing in this section precludes an employee from asserting any other available statutory or
22 common-law claims.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Disclosure of wage or salary range by employer -- when required --
25 opportunities for promotion or advancement. (1) An employer shall disclose in each posting for each job
26 opening the hourly or salary compensation, or a range of the hourly or salary compensation, and a general
27 description of all the benefits and other compensation to be offered to the hired applicant.
28 (2) An employer shall make reasonable efforts to announce, post, or otherwise make known all
- 4 - LC 120
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 0120
1 opportunities for promotion to all current employees on the same calendar day and prior to making a promotion
2 decision.
3 (3) Upon request of an employee offered an internal transfer to a new position or promotion, the
4 employer shall provide the wage scale or salary range for the employee's new position.
5 (4) Subsections (1) and (3) apply only to employers with 15 or more employees.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Recordkeeping. An employer shall keep records of job descriptions and
8 wage rate history for each employee for the duration of the employment plus 2 years after the end of
9 employment to determine if there is a pattern of wage discrepancy.
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Enforcement -- rulemaking. (1) The commissioner has the power to
12 administer, carry out, and enforce all the provisions of [sections 1 through 8] and may adopt administrative rules
13 for that purpose. The commissioner shall provide written copies of adopted rules implementing this section to all
14 employees and employers upon written request.
15 (2) (a) A person who claims to be aggrieved by a violation of [section 6] or [section 7] may file a
16 written complaint with the commissioner within 1 year after the date that the person learned of the violation. The
17 written complaint must state the name and address of the employer and a detailed account of the alleged
18 violation.
19 (b) An employer's failure to comply with [section 6(1)] for one job opening is considered one
20 violation regardless of the number of postings that list the job opening.
21 (c) An employer's failure to comply with [section 6(2)] for one promotional opportunity is
22 considered one violation.
23 (3) The commissioner shall investigate complaints of violations of subsection (2) and the
24 commissioner shall adopt administrative rules necessary to govern the investigations.
25 (4) Upon finding that an employer has violated [section 6] or [section 7], the commissioner may
26 order the employer to pay a fine of no less than $500 and no more than $10,000 per violation.
27 (5) If an employee suing for a violation of [section 3] demonstrates a violation of [section 6] or
28 [section 7], and the court finds a violation of [section 6] or [section 7], the court may order appropriate relief,

1 including a rebuttable presumption that records not kept by the employer in violation of [section 7] contained
2 information favorable to the employee's claim and an instruction to the jury that failure to keep records can be
3 considered evidence that the violation was not made in good faith.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 8] are intended to be
6 codified as a new part in Title 39, chapter 3, and the provisions of Title 39, chapter 3, apply to [sections 1
7 through 8].
