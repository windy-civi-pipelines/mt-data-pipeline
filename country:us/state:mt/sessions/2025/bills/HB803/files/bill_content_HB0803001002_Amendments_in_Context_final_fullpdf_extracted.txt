1 HOUSE BILL NO. 803
2 INTRODUCED BY G. OVERSTREET
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATING TO ASBESTOS
5 CLAIMS; ESTABLISHING THE ASBESTOS TRUST CLAIMS TRANSPARENCY ACT; ESTABLISHING
6 DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS; ESTABLISHING REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOVERY OF ASBESTOS
7 TRUSTS AND USE OF TRUST CLAIMS MATERIALS; PROVIDING FOR SETOFFS; ESTABLISHING THE
8 ASBESTOS ACTION OVER-NAMING REFORM ACT; ESTABLISHING DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS;
9 ESTABLISHING REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOVERY; PROVIDING FOR DISMISSAL WITHOUT PREJUDICE
10 FOR NONCOMPLIANCE OR LACK OF EVIDENCE OF EXPOSURE; AND PROVIDING DEFINITIONS.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 6] may be cited as the "Asbestos Trust
15 Claims Transparency Act".
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 6], unless the context clearly
18 indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
19 (1) "Asbestos action" means a civil action arising out of, based on, or related to the health effects
20 of exposure to asbestos and a derivative claim made by or on behalf of a person exposed to asbestos or a
21 representative, spouse, parent, child, or other relative of that person. An asbestos action as provided under
22 [sections 1 through 6] is intended to be a separate action from the asbestos claims court in Title 3, chapter 20
23 does not include an asbestos-related claim as defined in 3-20-101, nor does it include asbestos-related claims
24 brought by any Montana citizen based on a mesothelioma diagnosis.
25 (2) "Asbestos trust" means a government-approved or court-approved trust, qualified settlement
26 fund, compensation fund, or claims facility created as a result of an administrative or legal action, or a court-
27 approved bankruptcy created under 11 U.S.C. 524(g), or 11 U.S.C. 1121(a), or other applicable provision of

1 law, that is intended to provide compensation to claimants arising out of, based on, or related to the health
2 effects of exposure to asbestos.
3 (3) "Asbestos trust claim" means a claim for compensation by an exposed person or the exposed
4 person’s representative against an asbestos trust.
5 (4) "Claimant" means a person bringing an asbestos action, including a personal representative if
6 the asbestos action is brought by an estate, or a conservator or next friend if the asbestos action is brought on
7 behalf of a minor or incapacitated person, as defined in sections 41-1-101 and 72-5-101.
8 (5) "Exposed person" means a person whose exposure to asbestos or to asbestos-containing
9 products is the basis for an asbestos action.
10 (6) "Trust claim materials" means a final executed proof of claim and documents and information
11 submitted to or received from an asbestos trust by a claimant or a claimant’s representative, including claim
12 forms and supplementary materials, proofs of claim, affidavits, depositions and trial testimony of the claimant
13 and others knowledgeable about the claimant’s exposure history, work history, exposure allegations, medical
14 and health records, documents that reflect the status of a claim against an asbestos trust, and if the trust claim
15 has settled, documents relating to the settlement of the trust claim.
16 (7) "Trust governance document" means a document that relates to trust claim eligibility and
17 payment levels, including claims payment matrices, trust distribution procedures, or plans for reorganization for
18 an asbestos trust.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Required disclosures by claimant. (1) Within 30 days after an
21 asbestos action is filed, the claimant shall do all of the following:
22 (a) provide the court and parties with a sworn statement signed by the claimant and claimant’s
23 counsel indicating that an investigation has been conducted and that all asbestos trust claims that can be made
24 by the claimant have been completed and filed. The sworn statement shall indicate whether there has been a
25 request to defer, delay, suspend, toll, withdraw, or otherwise alter the standing of an asbestos trust claim and
26 provide the status and disposition of each asbestos trust claim. A deferral or placeholder claim that is missing
27 necessary documentation for the trust to pay the claim does not meet the requirements of this section.

1 (b) provide all parties with all trust claim materials from all law firms connected to the claimant in
2 relation to exposure to asbestos, including trust claim materials that relate to conditions other than those that
3 are the basis for the asbestos action; and
4 (c) produce all available trust claims filed by an individual other than the claimant if the claimant’s
5 asbestos action is based on exposure to asbestos through that other individual and the materials are available
6 to the claimant or claimant’s counsel.
7 (2) A claimant shall supplement the statement and materials required to be provided pursuant to
8 this section when the claimant files an additional asbestos trust claim, supplements an existing asbestos trust
9 claim, or receives additional trust claim materials related to an asbestos trust claim made against an asbestos
10 trust.
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Identification of asbestos trust claims by defendant. (1) Not less than
13 60 days before trial in an asbestos action, if a defendant believes the claimant has not filed all asbestos trust
14 claims as required in [section 3], the defendant may move the court for an order to require the claimant to file
15 additional trust claims specified by the defendant.
16 (2) If the court determines that there is a sufficient basis for the claimant to file an asbestos trust
17 claim identified in the defendant’s motion, the court shall order the claimant to file the asbestos trust claim and
18 produce all related trust claim materials. An asbestos action may not proceed to trial until at least 90 days after
19 the claimant complies with the court’s order.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Asbestos trust discovery -- use of materials. (1) A defendant in an
22 asbestos action may seek discovery against an asbestos trust. The claimant may not claim privilege or
23 confidentiality to bar discovery. The claimant shall provide consent or any other expression of permission that
24 may be required by the asbestos trust to release information and materials sought by the defendant.
25 (2) Trust claim materials and trust governance documents are presumed to be relevant and
26 authentic and are admissible in evidence in an asbestos action. Claims of privilege may not apply to trust claim
27 materials or trust governance documents.

1 (3) A claimant’s submission of an asbestos trust claim and related trust claims materials may be
2 considered by the trier of fact to determine liability and apportion fault in an asbestos action and is sufficient to
3 support a finding that the claimant was exposed to products for which the trust was established to provide
4 compensation and that the exposure was a substantial contributing factor in causing the claimant’s injury that is
5 at issue in the asbestos action.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Setoff distribution. In an asbestos action in which damages are
8 awarded and setoffs are permitted under applicable law, a defendant is entitled to a setoff in the amount of the
9 valuation established under the applicable trust governance documents, including payment percentages, for
10 asbestos trust claims pending at trial and any amount the claimant has been awarded from an asbestos trust
11 claim that has been identified at the time of trial. If multiple defendants are found liable for damages, the court
12 shall distribute the amount of setoff proportionally between the defendants, according to the liability of each
13 defendant.
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Short title. [Sections 7 through 10] may be cited as the "Asbestos Action
16 Over-Naming Reform Act".
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Definitions. As used in [sections 7 through 10], unless the context
19 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
20 (1) "Asbestos action" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2];
21 (2) "Claimant" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2];
22 (3) "Exposed person" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Required disclosures by claimant. (1) Within 30 days after an
25 asbestos action is filed, the claimant shall file a sworn information form specifying the evidence that provides
26 the basis for each claim against each defendant. The sworn information form shall include all of the following:
27 (a) the name, date of birth, marital status, occupation, smoking history, current and past

3 (b) the name, address, and relationship to the exposed person for each person who is

18 (i) a certification that all supporting documentation relating to the information required in this
19 section has been provided to the parties.
20 (2) A claimant shall supplement the information required by this section when the claimant
21 receives information that is required to be disclosed or becomes aware that a prior disclosure was inaccurate or
22 incomplete.
23 (3) Discovery may not commence against a defendant in an asbestos action until the defendant’s
24 product or premises is identified in the disclosures required by this section.
25

27 evidence. (1) The court, on a motion by a defendant, shall dismiss the asbestos action without prejudice as to
