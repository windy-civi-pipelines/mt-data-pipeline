1 HOUSE BILL NO. 803
2 INTRODUCED BY G. OVERSTREET
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATING TO ASBESTOS
5 CLAIMS; ESTABLISHING THE ASBESTOS TRUST CLAIMS TRANSPARENCY ACT; ESTABLISHING
6 DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS; ESTABLISHING REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOVERY OF ASBESTOS
7 TRUSTS AND USE OF TRUST CLAIMS MATERIALS; PROVIDING FOR SETOFFS; ESTABLISHING THE
8 ASBESTOS ACTION OVER-NAMING REFORM ACT; ESTABLISHING DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS;
9 ESTABLISHING REQUIREMENTS FOR DISCOVERY; PROVIDING FOR DISMISSAL WITHOUT PREJUDICE
10 FOR NONCOMPLIANCE OR LACK OF EVIDENCE OF EXPOSURE; AND PROVIDING DEFINITIONS.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 6] may be cited as the "Asbestos Trust
15 Claims Transparency Act".
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 6], unless the context clearly
18 indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
19 (1) "Asbestos action" means a civil action arising out of, based on, or related to the health effects
20 of exposure to asbestos and a derivative claim made by or on behalf of a person exposed to asbestos or a
21 representative, spouse, parent, child, or other relative of that person. An asbestos action as provided under
22 [sections 1 through 6] is intended to be a separate action from the asbestos claims court in Title 3, chapter 20
23 does not include an asbestos-related claim as defined in 3-20-101.
24 (2) "Asbestos trust" means a government-approved or court-approved trust, qualified settlement
25 fund, compensation fund, or claims facility created as a result of an administrative or legal action, or a court-
26 approved bankruptcy created under 11 U.S.C. 524(g), or 11 U.S.C. 1121(a), or other applicable provision of
27 law, that is intended to provide compensation to claimants arising out of, based on, or related to the health
28 effects of exposure to asbestos.

1 (3) "Asbestos trust claim" means a claim for compensation by an exposed person or the exposed
2 person’s representative against an asbestos trust.
3 (4) "Claimant" means a person bringing an asbestos action, including a personal representative if
4 the asbestos action is brought by an estate, or a conservator or next friend if the asbestos action is brought on
5 behalf of a minor or incapacitated person, as defined in sections 41-1-101 and 72-5-101.
6 (5) "Exposed person" means a person whose exposure to asbestos or to asbestos-containing
7 products is the basis for an asbestos action.
8 (6) "Trust claim materials" means a final executed proof of claim and documents and information
9 submitted to or received from an asbestos trust by a claimant or a claimant’s representative, including claim
10 forms and supplementary materials, proofs of claim, affidavits, depositions and trial testimony of the claimant
11 and others knowledgeable about the claimant’s exposure history, work history, exposure allegations, medical
12 and health records, documents that reflect the status of a claim against an asbestos trust, and if the trust claim
13 has settled, documents relating to the settlement of the trust claim.
14 (7) "Trust governance document" means a document that relates to trust claim eligibility and
15 payment levels, including claims payment matrices, trust distribution procedures, or plans for reorganization for
16 an asbestos trust.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Required disclosures by claimant. (1) Within 30 days after an
19 asbestos action is filed, the claimant shall do all of the following:
20 (a) provide the court and parties with a sworn statement signed by the claimant and claimant’s
21 counsel indicating that an investigation has been conducted and that all asbestos trust claims that can be made
22 by the claimant have been completed and filed. The sworn statement shall indicate whether there has been a
23 request to defer, delay, suspend, toll, withdraw, or otherwise alter the standing of an asbestos trust claim and
24 provide the status and disposition of each asbestos trust claim. A deferral or placeholder claim that is missing
25 necessary documentation for the trust to pay the claim does not meet the requirements of this section.
26 (b) provide all parties with all trust claim materials from all law firms connected to the claimant in
27 relation to exposure to asbestos, including trust claim materials that relate to conditions other than those that
28 are the basis for the asbestos action; and

4 (2) A claimant shall supplement the statement and materials required to be provided pursuant to

7 trust.

9 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Identification of asbestos trust claims by defendant. (1) Not less than
10 60 days before trial in an asbestos action, if a defendant believes the claimant has not filed all asbestos trust
11 claims as required in [section 3], the defendant may move the court for an order to require the claimant to file
12 additional trust claims specified by the defendant.
13 (2) If the court determines that there is a sufficient basis for the claimant to file an asbestos trust
14 claim identified in the defendant’s motion, the court shall order the claimant to file the asbestos trust claim and
15 produce all related trust claim materials. An asbestos action may not proceed to trial until at least 90 days after
16 the claimant complies with the court’s order.
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Asbestos trust discovery -- use of materials. (1) A defendant in an
19 asbestos action may seek discovery against an asbestos trust. The claimant may not claim privilege or
20 confidentiality to bar discovery. The claimant shall provide consent or any other expression of permission that
21 may be required by the asbestos trust to release information and materials sought by the defendant.
22 (2) Trust claim materials and trust governance documents are presumed to be relevant and
23 authentic and are admissible in evidence in an asbestos action. Claims of privilege may not apply to trust claim
24 materials or trust governance documents.
25 (3) A claimant’s submission of an asbestos trust claim and related trust claims materials may be
26 considered by the trier of fact to determine liability and apportion fault in an asbestos action and is sufficient to
27 support a finding that the claimant was exposed to products for which the trust was established to provide
28 compensation and that the exposure was a substantial contributing factor in causing the claimant’s injury that is

3 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Setoff distribution. In an asbestos action in which damages are
4 awarded and setoffs are permitted under applicable law, a defendant is entitled to a setoff in the amount of the
5 valuation established under the applicable trust governance documents, including payment percentages, for
6 asbestos trust claims pending at trial and any amount the claimant has been awarded from an asbestos trust
7 claim that has been identified at the time of trial. If multiple defendants are found liable for damages, the court
8 shall distribute the amount of setoff proportionally between the defendants, according to the liability of each
9 defendant.

11 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Short title. [Sections 7 through 10] may be cited as the "Asbestos Action
12 Over-Naming Reform Act".
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Definitions. As used in [sections 7 through 10], unless the context
15 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
16 (1) "Asbestos action" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2];
17 (2) "Claimant" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2];
18 (3) "Exposed person" has the same meaning as provided in [section 2].
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Required disclosures by claimant. (1) Within 30 days after an
21 asbestos action is filed, the claimant shall file a sworn information form specifying the evidence that provides
22 the basis for each claim against each defendant. The sworn information form shall include all of the following:
23 (a) the name, date of birth, marital status, occupation, smoking history, current and past
24 residences and worksites, and current and past employers of the exposed person, and any person through
25 whom the exposed person was exposed to asbestos;
26 (b) the name, address, and relationship to the exposed person for each person who is
27 knowledgeable regarding the exposed person’s exposures to asbestos;
28 (c) the manufacturer or seller and brand and trade name of each asbestos-containing product to

19 product or premises is identified in the disclosures required by this section.

21 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Dismissal without prejudice for noncompliance or lack of exposure
22 evidence. (1) The court, on a motion by a defendant, shall dismiss the asbestos action without prejudice as to
23 any defendant whose product or premises is not identified in the disclosures required by [section 9].
24 (2) The court, on motion by a defendant, shall dismiss the asbestos action without prejudice as to
25 the moving defendant or as to all defendants, as applicable, if the claimant fails to comply in whole or in part
26 with [section 9].
