- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 Drafter: Chanan Brown, SB0216.001.001
1 SENATE BILL NO. 216
2 INTRODUCED BY D. LENZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CREATING THE MONTANA PHYSICIANS' WORKFORCE
5 INVESTMENT ACT; CREATING A GRANT PROGRAM TO SUPPORT GRADUATE MEDICAL EDUCATION
6 IN MONTANA; ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM; REQUIRING THE
7 DEPARTMENT OF LABOR AND INDUSTRY TO STAFF THE TASK FORCE; ESTABLISHING REPORTING
8 REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING AN ALLOCATION; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 5] may be cited as the "Montana
13 Physicians' Workforce Investment Act".
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purposes of [sections 1 through 5] are to address
16 Montana's physician workforce needs by:
17 (1) creating a graduate medical education task force; and
18 (2) establishing a start-up grant program to support the creation of graduate medical education
19 programs in the state.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 5], the following definitions
22 apply:
23 (1) "Graduate medical education" means residency and fellowship programs that provide
24 additional training after medical school that is required for medical graduates to obtain full medical licensure
25 and become eligible for specialty board certification.
26 (2) "Health sector facility" means a for-profit or nonprofit place of employment whose primary
27 function is the delivery of health care services.

7 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Montana graduate medical education task force -- membership --
8 duties -- reporting. (1) There is a Montana graduate medical education task force. The task force consists of
9 12 six members:
10 (a) the director of the department of public health and human services or the director's designee;
11 (b) the commissioner of higher education or the commissioner's designee a designee of the
12 commissioner with experience in graduate medical education;
13 (c) the director of the department of labor and industry or a designee of the director with
14 experience in physician workforce issues;
15 (c)(d) the following individuals appointed by the governor:
16 (i) a representative of the department of labor and industry with experience in physician workforce
17 issues;
18 (ii)(i) a representative of an organization that represents Montana physicians;
19 (iii) one representative from each of the three medical school programs in the state;
20 (iv)(ii) two representatives one representative from a hospital or other health sector facility with a
21 graduate medical education program in the state; and
22 (v)(iii) one patient advocate; and.
23 (d) two nonvoting members who are legislators:
24 (i) one house of representatives member selected by the speaker of the house; and
25 (ii) one senate member selected by the president of the senate.
26 (2) The task force shall elect a presiding officer and vice presiding officer.

2 (4) By September 1 of each year, the task force shall submit an annual report to the governor, the
3 economic affairs interim committee, and the children, families, health, and human services interim committee in
4 accordance with 5-11-210. The report must include:
5 (a) a description of the task force's proceedings since the previous report;
6 (b) an itemized expenditure report; and

11 (iv) the number of positions for medical graduates created in each graduate medical education
12 program and how many of these positions were filled; and
13 (v) the accreditation status of each graduate medical education program.
14 (5) The department of labor and industry shall provide staff services to the task force.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Graduate medical education grant program -- eligibility. (1) (a)
17 There is a graduate medical education grant program administered by the Montana graduate medical education
18 task force established in [section 4]. The purpose of the program is to address a shortage of physicians in the
19 state by developing and expanding graduate medical education programs in the state.
20 (b) The task force shall develop policies and procedures to receive and evaluate applications and
21 to administer grant funding.
22 (2) An application for a grant under this program must include:
23 (a) the specialty area for each program;
24 (b) proof of program accreditation or steps to obtain accreditation;
25 (c) a 2-year financial cost projection for the program; and
26 (d) a 5-year sustainability plan to ensure the program remains operational after the grant ends.
27 (3) A recipient of a grant under this program shall:

10 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Allocation. There is allocated to the department of labor and industry $6

18 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
