69th Legislature 2025 HB 316.1
2 INTRODUCED BY L. DEMING, L. SCHUBERT, M. NIKOLAKAKOS, S. KLAKKEN, G. OVERSTREET, T.
3 SHARP, E. TILLEMAN, T. MANZELLA, C. HINKLE, R. MARSHALL, A. REGIER, S. VINTON, B. LER, R.
4 MINER, J. ETCHART, F. NAVE, B. USHER, J. GILLETTE, L. BREWSTER, N. DURAM, G. KMETZ, F.
5 MANDEVILLE, K. SEEKINS-CROWE, Z. WIRTH, J. HINKLE, B. PHALEN
6
7 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT SUBMITTING TO THE QUALIFIED ELECTORS OF MONTANA AN
8 AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE II, SECTION 17, OF THE MONTANA CONSTITUTION DEFINING "PERSON" TO
9 INCLUDE ALL MEMBERS OF MANKIND AT ANY STAGE OF DEVELOPMENT; AND PROVIDING AN
10 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Article II, section 17, of The Constitution of the State of Montana is amended to read:
15 "Section 17. Due process of law. (1) No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without
16 due process of law.
17 (2) As used in this section, the word "person" applies to all members of mankind at any stage of
18 development, beginning at the stage of fertilization or conception, regardless of age, health, level of functioning,
19 or condition of dependency.
20 (3) No cause of action may arise as a consequence of harm caused to an unborn baby by an
21 unintentional act of its mother.
22 (4) The legislature shall implement this section by appropriate legislation."
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
25 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
26 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
27
28 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Two-thirds vote required. Because [section 1] is a legislative proposal
