4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ELIMINATING REVISING THE DEADLINE FOR THE
5 DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY TO REVIEW DATA FOR WATER QUALITY
6 ASSESSMENTS; AND AMENDING SECTION 75-5-702, MCA.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 Section 1. Section 75-5-702, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "75-5-702. Monitoring -- water quality assessment listing -- costs payable by department --
12 statewide advisory group. (1) The department shall monitor state waters to assess the quality of those waters
13 and to identify surface water bodies or segments of surface water bodies that are threatened or impaired. The
14 department shall use the monitoring results to revise the list of water bodies that are identified as threatened or
15 impaired and to establish a priority ranking for TMDL development for those waters in accordance with
16 subsections (4) and (7).
17 (2) In revising the list prepared pursuant to this section, the department shall use all currently
18 available data, including information or data obtained from federal, state, and local agencies, private entities, or
19 individuals with an interest in water quality protection. Except as provided in subsection (6), the department
20 may modify the list only if there is sufficient credible data to support the modification. Prior to publishing a final
21 list, the department shall provide public notice and allow 60 days for public comment on the draft list. The
22 department shall make available for public review, upon request, documentation used in the determination to
23 list or delist a particular water body, including, at a minimum, a description of the information, data, and
24 methodology used. The department may charge a reasonable fee for the documentation, commensurate with
25 the cost of providing the documentation to the requestor.
26 (3) A person may request that the department add or remove a water body or reprioritize a water
27 body on a draft or published list by providing the data or information necessary to support the request. The

3 request, the department shall provide public notice of its intended action and allow 60 days for public comment
4 prior to taking action on the request. A person aggrieved by the department's decision to grant or deny the
5 request may appeal the department's decision to the board.
6 (4) The department shall, in consultation with local conservation districts and watershed advisory
7 groups pursuant to 75-5-704, review and revise the list and priority rankings of water bodies identified as
8 threatened or impaired. The department shall review and revise the list at intervals not to exceed 5 years. The
9 department shall make available for public review the data and information used in making any changes in its
10 list of threatened or impaired water bodies that is developed and maintained pursuant to this section.
11 (5) By October 1, 1999, and in consultation with the statewide TMDL advisory group established
12 pursuant to subsection (10), the department shall develop and maintain a data management system that can be
13 used to assess the validity and reliability of the data used in the listing and priority ranking process. The
14 department shall make available to the public, upon request, data from its data management system. The
15 department may charge a reasonable fee for the data, commensurate with its cost of providing the data to the
16 requestor.
17 (6) By October 1, 1999, and in consultation with the statewide TMDL advisory group, the
18 department shall use the data management system developed and maintained pursuant to subsection (5) to
19 revise the list and to remove any water body that lacks sufficient credible data to support its listing. If the
20 department removes a water body because there is a lack of sufficient credible data to support its listing, the
21 department shall monitor and assess that water body during the next field season or as soon as possible
22 thereafter to determine whether it is a threatened water body or an impaired water body.

24 department shall, in consultation with the statewide TMDL advisory group, take into consideration the following:
25 (a) the beneficial uses established for a water body;
26 (b) the extent that natural factors over which humans have no control are contributing to any

2 application, complete development of the TMDL pursuant to 75-5-703.
3 (b) If the department is not able to complete development of the TMDL in accordance with
4 subsection (9)(a)(ii), the department shall, within 30 days of the department's receipt of the application, specify
5 in writing to the applicant why the department is not able to complete development of a TMDL in accordance
6 with subsection (9)(a)(ii). The department and the applicant shall make reasonable efforts to mutually agree in
7 writing to a timeframe in which the department shall complete development of the TMDL. If the department
8 specifies a lack of resources as a reason why the department cannot complete development of the TMDL in
9 accordance with subsection (9)(a)(ii), the department shall clearly explain in its written specification what
10 resources are not available, why those resources are not available, and when those resources will be available.
11 (c) If the department and the applicant cannot mutually agree to a timeframe in accordance with
12 subsection (9)(b), the department shall, within 60 days of the department's receipt of the application, specify in
13 writing to the applicant the timeframe in which the TMDL will be completed by the department and the reasons
14 why that timeframe is appropriate. If the department specifies a lack of resources as a reason why the
15 department's timeframe is appropriate, the department may request the applicant provide funding for the
16 development of the TMDL in order to accelerate the completion of the TMDL.
17 (d) The applicant may, within 15 days of the department's written specification provided in
18 accordance with subsection (9)(c), request in writing a hearing before the board for the purpose of petitioning
19 the board to reverse or modify the department's decision. The contested case provisions of the Montana
20 Administrative Procedure Act, Title 2, chapter 4, part 6, apply to a hearing before the board under this
21 subsection. If the parties to the contested case waive a formal proceeding pursuant to 2-4-603, the informal
22 proceeding must be conducted within 30 days after the board's receipt of the written request. After the hearing
23 and in a reasonable time, the board shall affirm, modify, or reverse the action of the department, and the board
24 shall make findings and conclusions that explain its decision. Pending the board's decision, the department
25 shall develop the TMDL in accordance with the timeframe specified in subsection (9)(a)(ii).
26 (e) The department may not declare an application incomplete or deficient because a TMDL has
27 not been prepared.
