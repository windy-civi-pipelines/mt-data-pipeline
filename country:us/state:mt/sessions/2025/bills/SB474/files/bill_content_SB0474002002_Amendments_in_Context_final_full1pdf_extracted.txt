4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REQUIRING ACCEPTANCE OF RELIGIOUS EXEMPTIONS AND
5 PERSONAL MEDICAL INFORMED CONSENT EXEMPTIONS TO REQUIRED IMMUNIZATIONS;
6 PROVIDING EXCEPTIONS FOR CERTAIN PUBLIC SCHOOLS; PROVIDING RELIEF FOR VIOLATIONS OF
7 RELIGIOUS EXEMPTIONS; PROVIDING A DEFINITION; AMENDING SECTION SECTIONS 20-5-405, AND
8 49-2-312, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Acceptance of religious exemptions to immunizations required --
13 immunity -- definition. (1) For the purposes of this chapter, discrimination on the basis of religion includes
14 denials of requests for an exemption from an otherwise required immunization as defined in 20-5-402 based on
15 the individual's religion.
16 (2) A person that accepts an individual's religious exemption from an immunization is not liable for
17 civil damages from an injury arising out of the individual's nonimmunized status.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Acceptance of religious exemption from immunization. For the
20 purposes of this chapter, discrimination on the basis of religion includes denials of requests for an exemption
21 from an otherwise required immunization based on the individual's religion.
22

14 reservation and with a majority of its enrolled pupils being American Indian, determined using the number of
15 American Indian students under 20-9-330, may, after consultation with the applicable tribal government, adopt
16 policy to suspend or disallow the religious and informed consent exemptions under subsections (1) and (3) of
17 this section. The policy may apply to one or more of the immunizations required under 20-5-403 as determined
18 by the trustees."

20 SECTION 4. SECTION 49-2-312, MCA, IS AMENDED TO READ:
21 "49-2-312. Discrimination based on vaccination status or possession of immunity passport
22 prohibited -- definitions. (1) Except as provided in subsection (2), it is an unlawful discriminatory practice for:
23 (a) a person or a governmental entity to refuse, withhold from, or deny to a person any local or
24 state services, goods, facilities, advantages, privileges, licensing, educational opportunities, health care access,
25 or employment opportunities based on the person's vaccination status or whether the person has an immunity
26 passport;
27 (b) an employer to refuse employment to a person, to bar a person from employment, or to

1 discriminate against a person in compensation or in a term, condition, or privilege of employment based on the
2 person's vaccination status or whether the person has an immunity passport; or
3 (c) a public accommodation to exclude, limit, segregate, refuse to serve, or otherwise discriminate
4 against a person based on the person's vaccination status or whether the person has an immunity passport.
5 (2) This section does not apply to vaccination requirements set forth for schools pursuant to Title
6 20, chapter 5, part 4, or day-care facilities pursuant to Title 52, chapter 2, part 7.
7 (3)(2) (a) A person, governmental government entity, or an employer does not unlawfully discriminate
8 under this section if they recommend that an employee receive a vaccine.
9 (b) A health care facility, as defined in 50-5-101, does not unlawfully discriminate under this
10 section if it complies with both of the following:
11 (i) asks an employee to volunteer the employee's vaccination or immunization status for the
12 purpose of determining whether the health care facility should implement reasonable accommodation measures
13 to protect the safety and health of employees, patients, visitors, and other persons from communicable
14 diseases. A health care facility may consider an employee to be nonvaccinated or nonimmune if the employee
15 declines to provide the employee's vaccination or immunization status to the health care facility for purposes of
16 determining whether reasonable accommodation measures should be implemented.
17 (ii) implements reasonable accommodation measures for employees, patients, visitors, and other
18 persons who are not vaccinated or not immune to protect the safety and health of employees, patients, visitors,
19 and other persons from communicable diseases.
20 (4)(3) An individual may not be required to receive any vaccine whose use is allowed under an
21 emergency use authorization or any vaccine undergoing safety trials.
22 (5)(4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
23 (a) "Immunity passport" means a document, digital record, or software application indicating that a
24 person is immune to a disease, either through vaccination or infection and recovery.
25 (b) "Vaccination status" means an indication of whether a person has received one or more doses
26 of a vaccine."
