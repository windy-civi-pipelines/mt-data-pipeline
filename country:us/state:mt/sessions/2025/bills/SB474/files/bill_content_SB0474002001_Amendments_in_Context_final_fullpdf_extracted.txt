1 SENATE BILL NO. 474
2 INTRODUCED BY D. EMRICH, V. RICCI
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REQUIRING ACCEPTANCE OF RELIGIOUS EXEMPTIONS AND
5 PERSONAL MEDICAL INFORMED CONSENT EXEMPTIONS TO REQUIRED IMMUNIZATIONS;
6 PROVIDING RELIEF FOR VIOLATIONS OF RELIGIOUS EXEMPTIONS; PROVIDING A DEFINITION;
7 PROVIDING THAT THE ACCEPTANCE OF AN EXEMPTION TO REQUIRED IMMUNIZATIONS DOES NOT
8 CREATE A CIVIL LIABILITY FOR CERTAIN AUTHORITIES; AMENDING SECTION SECTIONS 20-5-405,
9 AND 49-2-312, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Acceptance of religious exemptions to immunizations required --
14 immunity -- definition. (1) For the purposes of this chapter, discrimination on the basis of religion includes
15 denials of requests for an exemption from an otherwise required immunization as defined in 20-5-402 based on
16 the individual's religion.
17 (2) A person that accepts an individual's religious exemption from an immunization is not liable for
18 civil damages from an injury arising out of the individual's nonimmunized status.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Acceptance of religious exemption from immunization. For the
21 purposes of this chapter, discrimination on the basis of religion includes denials of requests for an exemption
22 from an otherwise required immunization based on the individual's religion.
23

25 requirements that are more burdensome or otherwise in excess of the requirements described in this section. A
26 form prescribed by the department that contains requirements not expressly described in this section is void to
27 the extent that it purports to impose requirements not included in this section.

11 officer or the department until the excluding authority is satisfied that the person no longer risks contracting or
12 transmitting that disease.
13 (6) (a) A person or governing authority that accepts an individual's exemption from an
14 immunization is not liable for civil damages from an injury arising out of the individual's immunization status.
15 (b) Subsection (6)(a) does not apply to a government entity."

17 Section 2. SECTION 49-2-312, MCA, IS AMENDED TO READ:
18 "49-2-312. Discrimination based on vaccination status or possession of immunity passport
19 prohibited -- definitions. (1) Except as provided in subsection (2), it It is an unlawful discriminatory practice
20 for:
21 (a) a person or a governmental government entity to refuse, withhold from, or deny to a person any
22 local or state services, goods, facilities, advantages, privileges, licensing, educational opportunities, health care
23 access, or employment opportunities based on the person's vaccination status or whether the person has an
24 immunity passport;
25 (b) an employer to refuse employment to a person, to bar a person from employment, or to
26 discriminate against a person in compensation or in a term, condition, or privilege of employment based on the
27 person's vaccination status or whether the person has an immunity passport; or

1 (c) a public accommodation to exclude, limit, segregate, refuse to serve, or otherwise discriminate
2 against a person based on the person's vaccination status or whether the person has an immunity passport.
3 (2) This section does not apply to vaccination requirements set forth for schools pursuant to Title
4 20, chapter 5, part 4, or day-care facilities pursuant to Title 52, chapter 2, part 7.
5 (3)(2) (a) A person, governmental a government entity, or an employer does not unlawfully
6 discriminate under this section if they recommend that an employee receive a vaccine.
7 (b) A health care facility, as defined in 50-5-101, does not unlawfully discriminate under this
8 section if it complies with both of the following:
9 (i) asks an employee to volunteer the employee's vaccination or immunization status for the
10 purpose of determining whether the health care facility should implement reasonable accommodation measures
11 to protect the safety and health of employees, patients, visitors, and other persons from communicable
12 diseases. A health care facility may consider an employee to be nonvaccinated or nonimmune if the employee
13 declines to provide the employee's vaccination or immunization status to the health care facility for purposes of
14 determining whether reasonable accommodation measures should be implemented.
15 (ii) implements reasonable accommodation measures for employees, patients, visitors, and other
16 persons who are not vaccinated or not immune to protect the safety and health of employees, patients, visitors,
17 and other persons from communicable diseases.
18 (4)(3) An individual may not be required to receive any vaccine whose use is allowed under an
19 emergency use authorization or any vaccine undergoing safety trials.
20 (5)(4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
21 (a) "Immunity passport" means a document, digital record, or software application indicating that a
22 person is immune to a disease, either through vaccination or infection and recovery.
23 (b) "Vaccination status" means an indication of whether a person has received one or more doses
24 of a vaccine."
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Codification instruction. (1) [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
27 integral part of Title 49, chapter 2, part 3, and the provisions of Title 49, chapter 2, part 3, apply to [section 1].
