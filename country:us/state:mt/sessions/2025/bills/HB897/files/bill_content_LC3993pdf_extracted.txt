4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING A NONRESIDENT WILDLIFE MANAGEMENT
5 AREA SHED HUNTING LICENSE; IMPLEMENTING A WAITING PERIOD FOR NONRESIDENTS;
6 ESTABLISHING VIOLATIONS AND ESTABLISHING PENALTIES; DEFINING "SHED HUNT" AND "SHED
7 HUNTING"; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 87-1-304, 87-2-101, 87-
8 2-201, AND 87-2-202, MCA.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Class F--nonresident wildlife management area shed hunting
13 license. (1) A person who is not a resident as defined in 87-2-102, on payment of a $50 fee, is entitled to
14 receive a Class F license that authorizes the holder of the license to shed hunt as defined in 87-1-201 in a
15 wildlife management area.
16 (2) A person found in violation of this section:
17 (a) for a first offense, must be given a warning;
18 (b) for a second offense, is guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be fined not more than twice the cost
19 of a Class F license. In addition, the person, on conviction or forfeiture of bond or bail, may be subject to
20 forfeiture of any current shed hunting license and the privilege to shed hunt in a wildlife management area for a
21 period of time set by the court.
22 (c) for a third offense, is guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be fined not more than $500. In
23 addition, the person, on conviction or forfeiture of bond or bail, shall forfeit any current shed hunting license and
24 the privilege to shed hunt in a wildlife management area for a period of time set by the court.
25

27 "87-1-304. Fixing of seasons and bag and possession limits. (1) Subject to the provisions of 87-5-
28 302 and subsections (7) through (9) of this section, the commission may:

3 animal as defined by 87-2-101;

5 bear, wild buffalo or bison, and wolf by persons holding an archery stamp and the required license, permit, or
6 tag and designate times when only bows and arrows may be used to hunt deer, antelope, elk, moose, sheep,

16 commission by the owner of that property. In determining to whom special licenses must be issued, the
17 commission may, when more applications are received than the number of animals to be killed, award permits
18 to those chosen under a drawing system. The procedures used for awarding the permits from the drawing
19 system must be determined by the commission.

24 state. The commission may declare a closed season for hunting, fishing, or trapping in any of those districts
25 and later may open those districts to hunting, fishing, or trapping.
26 (4) The commission may declare a closed season on any species of game, fish, game birds, or fur-
27 bearing animals threatened with undue depletion from any cause. The commission may close any area or
28 district of any stream, public lake, or public water or portions thereof to hunting, trapping, or fishing for limited

23 percussion mechanism using a percussion or musket cap. The muzzleloading rifle must be a minimum of 45
24 caliber and may not have more than two barrels.

26 commission shall allow a resident as defined in 87-2-102 access to the area 7 days in advance of a
27 nonresident. A nonresident who violates this subsection (10) is subject to the penalties provided for in [section
28 1]."
