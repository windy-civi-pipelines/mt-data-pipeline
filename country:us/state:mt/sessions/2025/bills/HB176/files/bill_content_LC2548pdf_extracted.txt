4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING WOLF HUNTING LAWS; REQUIRING AN UNLIMITED
5 GRAY WOLF HUNTING QUOTA WHEN THE WOLF POPULATION IS 450 WOLVES OR HIGHER;
6 REQUIRING THE FISH AND WILDLIFE COMMISSION TO AUTHORIZE DURING THE UNLIMITED QUOTA
7 THE ISSUANCE OF MULTIPLE WOLF LICENSES TO APPLICANTS, THE HARVEST OF AN UNLIMITED
8 NUMBER OF WOLVES BY A SINGLE INDIVIDUAL, THE USE OF BAIT FOR WOLF HUNTING OR
9 TRAPPING, AND THE HUNTING OF WOLVES ON PRIVATE LAND OUTSIDE OF DAYLIGHT HOURS WITH
10 THE USE OF ARTIFICIAL LIGHT OR NIGHT VISION SCOPES; REQUIRING DATA CONSISTENCY IN
11 DETERMINING WOLF POPULATIONS USING INTEGRATED PATCH OCCUPANCY MODELS; AND
12 AMENDING SECTION 87-1-901, MCA.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 Section 1. Section 87-1-901, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "87-1-901. Gray wolf management -- rulemaking -- reporting. (1) Except as provided in subsection
18 (3), the commission shall establish by rule hunting and trapping seasons for wolves with the intent to reduce the
19 wolf population in this the state to a sustainable level, but not less than the number of wolves necessary to
20 support at least 15 breeding pairs 450 wolves, which is the sustainable number of wolves that allows the state
21 to retain a delisted status for wolves. Trapping seasons must allow for the use of snares by the holder of a
22 trapping license.
23 (2) For game management purposes, the commission may apply different management
24 techniques depending on the conditions in each administrative region with the most liberal harvest regulations
25 applied in regions with the greatest number of wolves shall institute an unlimited statewide quota when the total
26 population in the state equals or exceeds 450 wolves. In doing so, the commission may shall authorize:
27 (a) the issuance of more than one Class E-1 or Class E-2 wolf hunting license to an applicant;
28 (b) the trapping or snaring of more than one wolf by the holder of a trapping license;
- 1 - LC 2548
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 2548
1 (c) the harvest of an unlimited number of wolves by the holder of a single wolf hunting or wolf
2 trapping license;
3 (d) during the wolf trapping season, the use of bait while hunting or trapping wolves as long as no
4 trap or snare trap is set within 30 feet of exposed bait visible from above; and
5 (e) the hunting of wolves on private lands outside of daylight hours with the use of artificial light or
6 night vision scopes.
7 (3) The commission shall adopt rules to allow a landowner or the landowner's agent to take a wolf
8 on the landowner's property at any time without the purchase of a Class E-1 or Class E-2 wolf license when the
9 wolf is a potential threat to human safety, livestock, or dogs. The rules must:
10 (a) be consistent with the Montana gray wolf conservation and management plan and the adaptive
11 management principles of the commission and the department for the Montana gray wolf population;
12 (b) require a landowner or the landowner's agent who takes a wolf pursuant to this subsection (3)
13 to promptly report the taking to the department and to preserve the carcass of the wolf;
14 (c) establish a quota each year for the total number of wolves that may be taken pursuant to this
15 subsection (3); and
16 (d) allow the commission to issue a moratorium on the taking of wolves pursuant to this subsection
17 (3) before a quota is reached if the commission determines that circumstances require a limitation of the total
18 number of wolves taken.
19 (4) Public land permittees who have experienced livestock depredation must obtain a special kill
20 permit authorized in 87-5-131(3)(b) to take a wolf on public land without the purchase of a Class E-1 or Class
21 E-2 license.
22 (5) When determining wolf populations using integrated patch occupancy model estimates, the
23 department shall use the same modeling criteria for estimates made for subsequent years to ensure the data
24 results are able to be interpreted consistently.
25 (6) The department shall report annually to the environmental quality council in accordance with 5-
26 11-210 regarding the implementation of 87-5-131, 87-5-132, and this section.
27 "
