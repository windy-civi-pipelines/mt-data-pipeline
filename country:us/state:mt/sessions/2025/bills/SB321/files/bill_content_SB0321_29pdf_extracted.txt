7 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING TAX CREDITS FOR CHILDREN AND CHILD CARE;
8 PROVIDING FOR A CHILD TAX CREDIT; PROVIDING FOR AN INCOME TAX CREDIT FOR CHILD-CARE
9 WORKERS; PROVIDING FOR AN EMPLOYER TAX CREDIT FOR DEPENDENT CARE ASSISTANCE;
10 PROVIDING THAT THE CREDITS ARE ADJUSTED FOR INFLATION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS;
11 PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AMENDING SECTION 15-30-2303, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
12 APPLICABILITY DATE.”
13
14 WHEREAS, nearly 60% of Montana's counties are classified as child-care deserts; and
15 WHEREAS, Montana's total child care capacity meets only 44% of the estimated demand, and infant
16 capacity meets only 34% of the estimated demand; and
17 WHEREAS, the average cost of child care for children under 5 years of age in Montana is
18 approximately $20,000 a year; and
19 WHEREAS, Montana's child care crisis is preventing thousands of Montanans from entering or
20 returning to the workforce.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Child tax credit. (1) Except as provided in subsection (3), a resident
25 taxpayer who is permitted a child tax credit under section 24 of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 24, is
26 allowed a credit against the taxes imposed by this chapter for each qualifying child of the taxpayer.
27 (2) Subject to subsection (5), the amount of the credit is $1,200 $600 for each qualifying child.
28 (3) To claim the credit, a taxpayer must have:
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 321
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0321.2
1 (a) proof of earned income; AND
2 (b) investment of income of less than $10,300; and
3 (c)(B) a valid social security number for each child claimed.
4 (4) The taxpayer is entitled to a refund equal to the amount by which the credit exceeds the
5 taxpayer's tax liability or, if the taxpayer has no tax liability under this chapter, a refund equal to the amount of
6 the credit. The credit may be claimed by filing a Montana income tax return.
7 (5) The credit in subsection (2) is reduced at a rate of $50 for each $1,000 of the taxpayer's federal
8 adjusted gross income in excess of:
9 (a) $40,000 for a single or head of household taxpayer OR A MARRIED INDIVIDUAL WHO DOES NOT
10 MAKE A JOINT RETURN; and
11 (b) $80,000 for a married taxpayer filing jointly OR A SURVIVING SPOUSE.
12 (6) The department shall annually adjust the credit amount in subsection (2) and the adjusted
13 gross income amounts in subsections (5)(a) and (5)(b) by the inflation factor for the following tax year and
14 round to the nearest $100.
15 (7) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
16 (a) "Earned income" means earned income as defined in section 32 of the Internal Revenue Code,
17 26 U.S.C. 32.
18 (b) "Inflation factor" means a number determined for each tax year by dividing the consumer price
19 index for June of the previous tax year by the consumer price index for June 2025.
20 (c) "Investment income" means "disqualified income" as defined in section 32 of the Internal
21 Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 32.
22 (d) (C) "Qualifying child" means a child of the taxpayer who is 5 years of age or younger as of the
23 close of the calendar year in which the taxpayer's tax year begins.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Credit for child-care workers -- rulemaking. (1) A resident taxpayer is
26 allowed a credit against the tax imposed by this chapter for employment as a child-care worker.
27 (2) The amount of the credit is equal to $1,600.
28 (3) To be eligible for the credit under this section, a child-care worker must have been employed
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – SB 321
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0321.2
1 for at least 6 months of the tax year and worked a minimum of 20 hours a week.
2 (4) The taxpayer is entitled to a refund equal to the amount by which the credit exceeds the
3 taxpayer's liability or, if the taxpayer has no tax liability under this chapter, a refund equal to the amount of the
4 credit. The credit may be claimed by filing a Montana income tax return.
5 (5) The department may adopt rules to administer this credit.
6 (6) The department shall annually adjust the credit provided for in this section by the inflation factor
7 for the following tax year and round to the nearest $100.
8 (7) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
9 (a) "Child-care worker" means a person listed in the department of public health and human
10 services' child care under the big sky system LICENSING DATA SYSTEM, A TRIBAL CHILD CARE DEVELOPMENT FUND
11 ADMINISTERED PROGRAM, OR A HEAD START OR EARLY HEAD START PROGRAM who:
12 (i) owns or is employed in a licensed, TRIBALLY LICENSED, or registered day-care center, day-care
13 facility, family day-care home, or group day-care home as those terms are defined in 52-2-703; or
14 (II) OWNS OR IS EMPLOYED BY A HEAD START OR EARLY START PROGRAM; OR
15 (ii)(III) works as an early childhood teacher, early childhood assistant, school-age teacher, or director
16 or owner of a center or facility provided for in subsection (7)(a)(i) OR (7)(A)(II).
17 (b) "Inflation factor" means a number determined for each tax year by dividing the consumer price
18 index for June of the previous tax year by the consumer price index for June 2025.
19
20 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Business-supported dependent care credit. (1) There is a credit
21 against the taxes otherwise due under Title 15, chapter 31, and this chapter for an employer for amounts paid
22 or incurred during the tax year by the employer for dependent care assistance actually provided to or on behalf
23 of an employee.
24 (2) (a) The amount of the credit allowed under subsection (1) is equal to the amount paid or
25 incurred by the employer during the tax year, but the credit may not exceed $5,000 of dependent care
26 assistance actually provided to or on behalf of the employee.
27 (b) In the case of an onsite facility, the amount on which the credit allowed under subsection (1) is
28 based, with respect to a dependent, must be based on utilization and the value of the services provided.

1 (3) An amount paid or incurred by an employer to provide dependent care assistance to or on
2 behalf of an employee does not qualify for the credit allowed under subsection (1):
3 (a) to the extent the amount is paid or incurred pursuant to a salary reduction plan; or
4 (b) if the amount is paid or incurred for services not performed within this state.
5 (4) If the credit allowed under subsection (1) is claimed, the amount of a deduction allowed or
6 allowable under this chapter for the amount that qualifies for the credit or on which the credit is based must be
7 reduced by the dollar amount of the credit allowed. The election to claim a credit allowed under this section
8 must be made at the time of filing the tax return.
9 (5) The amount on which the credit allowed under subsection (1) is based may not be included in
10 the gross income of the employee to whom the dependent care assistance is provided. However, the amount
11 excluded from the income of an employee under this section may not exceed the limitations provided in section
12 129(b) of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 129(b). For the purposes of Title 15, chapter 30, part 25, with
13 respect to an employee to whom dependent care assistance is provided, "wages" does not include an amount
14 excluded under this subsection.
15 (6) The credit allowed by this section may be refunded if the taxpayer has a tax liability less than
16 the amount of the credit.
17 (7) If the taxpayer is an S. corporation, as defined in section 1361 of the Internal Revenue Code,
18 26 U.S.C. 1361, OR A PARTNERSHIP and the taxpayer elects to take tax credit relief, the election may be made on
19 behalf of the corporation's shareholders OR THE PARTNERS. A shareholder's OR PARTNER'S credit must be
20 computed using the shareholder's pro rata share of the corporation's costs that qualify for the credit. In all other
21 respects, the effect of the tax credit applies to the corporation OR PARTNERSHIP as otherwise provided by law.
22 (8) The department shall annually adjust the credit provided for in this section by the inflation factor
23 for the following tax year and round to the nearest $100.
24 (9) For the purposes of the credit allowed under subsection (1), the definitions and special rules
25 contained in section 129(e) of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 129(e), apply to the extent applicable.
26 (10) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
27 (a) "Employer" means an employer carrying on a business, trade, occupation, or profession in this
28 state.

2 index for June of the previous tax year by the consumer price index for June 2025.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Business-supported dependent care credit. There is a credit against
5 the taxes otherwise due under this chapter for an employer for amounts paid or incurred during the tax year by
6 the employer for dependent care assistance actually provided to or on behalf of an employee as provided in
7 [section 3].
