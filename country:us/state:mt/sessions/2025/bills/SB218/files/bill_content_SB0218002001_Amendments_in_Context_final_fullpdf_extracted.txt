1 SENATE BILL NO. 218
2 INTRODUCED BY J. FULLER, G. HERTZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A PRIVATE CAUSE OF ACTION FOR
5 INJURIES CAUSED BY CERTAIN MEDICAL INTERVENTIONS AS TREATMENT OF GENDER DYSPHORIA;
6 PROVIDING A STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS; AMENDING SECTION 27-2-205, MCA; REPEALING SECTION
7 50-4-1005, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Private cause of action for injuries caused by treatment of gender
12 dysphoria -- statute of limitations -- definitions. (1) Any health care professional or physician who provides
13 the medical treatments listed in subsection (4) is strictly liable to a minor, may be subject to a civil action if the
14 medical treatment or the after-effects of the medical treatment result results in any injury, including physical,
15 psychological, emotional, or physiological harms, within 25 years from the date of the last treatment that is
16 proximately caused by a deviation from the applicable medical standard of care of the health care professional
17 or physician as established by qualified expert testimony.
18 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), a person who suffers an injury described in subsection
19 (1) or the person's legal guardian or estate may bring a civil action with respect to the injury within 25 years
20 from AT ANY TIME UNTIL 2 YEARS AFTER THE PERSON REACHES THE AGE OF 30 OR UNTIL within 2 YEARS AFTER from
21 the time of discovery by the injured party of both the injury and the causal relationship between the medical
22 treatment and the injury, WHICHEVER IS LATER, against the offending health care professional or physician in a
23 court of competent jurisdiction for:
24 (a) declaratory or injunctive relief;
25 (b) compensatory damages, including but not limited to pain and suffering, loss of reputation, loss
26 of income, and loss of consortium, including the loss of expectation of sharing parenthood; and
27 (c) punitive damages;

26 address the patient's perception that the patient's gender or sex is not male:

7 under subsection (1) only when knowingly provided to address a minor female patient's perception that the

15 chromosomes with undervirilization, or an individual having both ovarian and testicular tissue; and
16 (ii) a person whom a physician has otherwise diagnosed with a disorder of sexual development in
17 which the physician has determined through genetic or biochemical testing that the person does not have
18 normal sex chromosome structure, sex steroid hormone production, or sex steroid hormone action for a male or
19 female; and

21 a medical treatment listed in subsection (4)(a) or (4)(b), whether or not the medical treatment was performed in
22 accordance with state and federal law and whether or not funding for the medical treatment is permissible
23 under state and federal law; or
24 (c) any other clinically appropriate or evidence-based basis for the treatment.

2 life cycle and has a reproductive and endocrine system oriented around the production of those gametes. An
3 individual who would otherwise fall within this definition, but for a biological or genetic condition, is female for
4 the purposes of this section.

6 female.
7 (c) "Health care professional" means a person who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized
8 by the laws of this state to administer health care in the ordinary course of the practice of the person's
9 profession.
10 (d) "Male" means a member of the human species who, under normal development, has XY
11 chromosomes and produces or would produce small, mobile gametes, or sperm, during his life cycle and has a
12 reproductive and endocrine system oriented around the production of those gametes. An individual who would
13 otherwise fall within this definition, but for a biological or genetic condition, is male for the purposes of this
14 section.
15 (e) "Physician" means a person who is licensed to practice medicine in this state.
16 (f) "Sex" means the organization of body parts and gametes for reproduction in human beings and
17 other organisms. In human beings, there are exactly two sexes, male and female, with two corresponding types
18 of gametes. The sexes are determined by the biological and genetic indication of male or female, including sex
19 chromosomes, naturally occurring sex chromosomes, gonads, and nonambiguous internal and external
20 genitalia present at birth, without regard to an individual's psychological, behavioral, social, cultural, chosen, or
21 subjective experience of gender.

1 technologist, pharmacist, veterinarian, a licensed hospital or long-term care facility, or licensed medical
2 professional corporation, based upon alleged professional negligence or for rendering professional services
3 without consent or for an act, error, or omission, must, except as provided in subsection subsections (2) and
4 (3), be commenced within 2 years after the date of injury or within 2 years after the plaintiff discovers or through
5 the use of reasonable diligence should have discovered the injury, whichever occurs last, but in no case may
6 an action be commenced after 5 years from the date of injury. However, this time limitation is tolled for any
7 period during which there has been a failure to disclose any act, error, or omission upon which an action is
8 based and that is known to the defendant or through the use of reasonable diligence subsequent to the act,
9 error, or omission would have been known to the defendant.
10 (2) Notwithstanding the provisions of 27-2-401, in an action for death or injury of a minor who was
11 under the age of 4 on the date of the minor's injury, the period of limitations in subsection (1) begins to run
12 when the minor reaches the minor's eighth birthday or dies, whichever occurs first, and the time for
13 commencement of the action is tolled during any period during which the minor does not reside with a parent or
14 guardian.
15 (3) (a) In an action brought for an injury described in [section 1], the time limitation in subsection
16 (1) is tolled until the plaintiff reaches the age of 30 or until the plaintiff discovers both the injury and the causal
17 relationship between the medical treatment and the injury, whichever is later.
18 (b) The time limitations in this section and in [section 1] for an injury described in [section 1] do not
19 prohibit a plaintiff from commencing an action before the plaintiff reaches the age of 30."
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Repealer. The following section of the Montana Code Annotated is
22 repealed:
23 50-4-1005. Private cause of action for subsequent harm.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as a new
26 part in Title 50, chapter 4, and the provisions of Title 50, chapter 4, apply to [section 1].
