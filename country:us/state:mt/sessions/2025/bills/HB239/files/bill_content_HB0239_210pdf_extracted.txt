15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Purpose. The purpose of [sections 1 through 8] is to protect the public
16 health, safety, and welfare of the public through the regulation of construction contractors.

28 contractor’s property, employs members of more than one trade on a single job or under a single building

8 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Construction contractor license required -- application. (1) An
9 individual or business entity may not engage in business as a construction contractor without a current license
10 from the department. The license must include the following information:
11 (a) the applicant’s social security number;
12 (b) proof of compliance with workers' compensation laws;
13 (c) the internal revenue service employer identification number, if any; and
14 (d) the name and address of:
15 (i) each partner if the applicant is a firm or partnership;
16 (ii) the owner if the applicant is an individual partnership;
17 (iii) the corporate officers and registered agent if the applicant is a corporation; or
18 (iv) the manager of a manager-managed limited liability company or the members of a member-
19 managed limited liability company and the registered agent if the applicant is a limited liability company.
20 (2) Section 37-1-137 applies to all persons listed in subsection (1)(d) of this section.

25 unemployment insurance coverage, or for wages and fringe benefits for:

4 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Fees -- education fund -- fines. (1) Each applicant for a construction
5 contractor license shall pay an application fee established by the department.
6 (2) The fees collected under [sections 1 through 8] in accordance with 37-1-134 must be deposited
7 in a special revenue account for administration and enforcement of [sections 1 through 8].

9 educate consumers and the building industry about construction contractor licensing requirements.
10 (4) A fine collected from a construction contractor under 37-1-109 or 37-1-406 must be deposited
11 in the uninsured employers' fund established in 39-71-503.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Unprofessional conduct. (1) The following is unprofessional conduct for
14 a construction contractor licensee or license applicant:
15 (a) subject to Title 37, chapter 1, part 2, having a conviction, including a conviction following a plea
16 of nolo contendere of a crime relating to or committed during practice of the profession or occupation or
17 involving violence, the use or sale of drugs, fraud, deceit, or theft, whether or not an appeal is pending;
18 (b) engaging in conduct defined as a sexual crime in Title 45, chapter 5, part 5, regardless of
19 whether the conduct occurred during or is related to the licensee’s profession or occupation;
20 (c) engaging in conduct in the profession or occupation found to be a violation of the Montana
21 Human Rights Act, Title 49;
22 (d) permitting aiding, abetting, or conspiring with a person to violate or circumvent a law relating to
23 licensure or certification;
24 (e) engaging in fraud, misrepresentation, deception, or concealment of a material fact in
25 applying for or assisting in securing a license or license renewal or in taking an examination required for
26 licensure;
27 (f) signing or issuing, in the licensee’s professional capacity, a document or statement that the
28 licensee knows or reasonably ought to know contains a false or misleading statement;
