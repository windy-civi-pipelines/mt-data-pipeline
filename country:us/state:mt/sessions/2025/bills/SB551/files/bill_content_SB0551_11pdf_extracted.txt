4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING GAMBLING LAWS TO PROVIDE FOR FANTASY

6 CONTESTS; PROVIDING POWERS TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE TO REGULATE INTERNET

8 CONDUCT INTERNET FANTASY SPORTS CONTESTS; PROVIDING FOR FEES; PROVIDING FOR TAXES
9 ON INTERNET FANTASY SPORTS CONTESTS; PROVIDING FOR LICENSEE RECORDS REPORTS AND
10 RECORDS RETENTION; PROVIDING FOR AUDITS OF LICENSEES; PROVIDING PENALTIES; PROVIDING

16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 10], unless the context
17 otherwise requires, the following terms apply:
18 (1) "Applicant" means an internet fantasy sports contest service provider that is applying for a
19 license to conduct internet fantasy sports contests under [sections 1 through 10].
20 (2) "Department" means the department of justice.
21 (3) "Fantasy sports contest" means a fantasy or simulated game or contest composed of no fewer
22 than two individuals who are fantasy sports contest players and in which:
23 (a) the fantasy sports contest operator is not a player in the game or contest;
24 (b) the value of all prizes and awards offered to winning players is established and made known to
25 the players in advance of the contest;
26 (c) all winning outcomes reflect the relative knowledge and skill of the players and are determined
27 by accumulated statistical results of the performance of individuals, including athletes in the case of sporting
28 events; and

1 (d) no winning outcome is solely based on the score, point spread, or one or more performances of
2 a single real-world team or solely on a single performance of an individual athlete or real-world player in a
3 single real-world event.
4 (4) "Internet fantasy sports contest" means a method of entering a fantasy sports contest by which
5 a person may establish an account with an internet fantasy sports contest service provider, deposit money into
6 the account, and use the account balance for entering a fantasy sports contest by utilizing electronic
7 communication.
8 (5) "Internet fantasy sports contest adjusted revenues" means, for each internet fantasy sports
9 contest, the amount equal to the total charges and fees collected from all players entering the internet fantasy
10 sports contest less winnings paid to players in the contest, multiplied by the location percentage.
11 (6) "Internet fantasy sports contest player" means an individual who is at least 18 years of age and
12 participates in an internet fantasy sports contest operated by an internet fantasy sports contest service provider.
13 (7) "Internet fantasy sports contest service provider" means a person, including a licensee under
14 [sections 1 through 10], who conducts an internet fantasy sports contest as authorized by [sections 1 through
15 10].
16 (8) "Licensee" means a person licensed under [section 5] to conduct internet fantasy sports
17 contests.
18 (9) "Location percentage" means, for each internet fantasy sports contest, the percentage,
19 rounded to the nearest tenth of a percent, equal to the total charges and fees collected from all internet fantasy
20 sports contest players located in this state divided by the total charges and fees collected from all players in the
21 internet fantasy sports contest.
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Internet fantasy sports contest allowed. The system of entering an
24 internet fantasy sports contest as provided in [sections 1 through 10] is legal when conducted by a licensed
25 internet fantasy sports contest service provider as provided in [sections 1 through 10].

19 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Requirements of applicant -- fee. (1) An applicant for a license to
20 conduct internet fantasy sports contests shall complete and sign an application on the form prescribed and
21 published by the department. The application must include information of the applicant that the department
22 deems necessary for the purposes of issuing a license pursuant to [sections 1 through 10].
23 (2) An applicant shall submit fingerprints and information that the department deems necessary to
24 the department and the division of criminal investigation in the manner prescribed on the application forms. The
25 fingerprints may be submitted to the federal bureau of investigation by the department or the division of criminal
26 investigation for the purpose of a national criminal background check. The results of a criminal background
27 check conducted pursuant to this subsection must be considered a confidential record.

21 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Internet fantasy sports contest tax -- rate -- allocation. (1) A tax is
22 imposed on internet fantasy sports contest adjusted revenues received each fiscal year by an internet fantasy

1 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Internet fantasy sports contests -- age restriction. A person under 18
2 years of age may not enter an internet fantasy sports contest. A person who violates this section with respect to
3 entering an internet fantasy sports contest commits a criminal offense under 23-5-161.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Licensees -- records -- reports -- confidentiality. (1) An internet
6 fantasy sports contest service provider shall keep its books and records in a way that clearly shows the internet
7 fantasy sports contest adjusted revenues for each internet fantasy sports contest subject to tax in the state.
8 (2) (a) The licensee shall furnish to the department any reports and information required by the
9 department with respect to the licensee's activities.
10 (b) (i) A licensee shall promptly report to the department:
11 (A) any criminal or disciplinary proceedings commenced against the licensee or its employees in
12 connection with the licensee conducting an internet fantasy sports contest;
13 (B) any abnormal contest activity or patterns that may indicate a concern about the integrity of an
14 internet fantasy sports contest; and
15 (C) any other conduct with the potential to corrupt an outcome of an internet fantasy sports contest
16 for the purposes of financial gain, including but not limited to match fixing and suspicious or illegal internet
17 fantasy sports contest activities, such as the use of funds derived from illegal activity, deposits of money to
18 enter an internet fantasy sports contest to conceal or launder funds derived from illegal activity, the use of
19 agents to enter an internet fantasy sports contest, or the use of false identification.
20 (ii) The department shall share any information received pursuant to this section with the division
21 of criminal investigation, any other law enforcement entity on request, or any regulatory agency the department
22 deems appropriate.
23 (iii) The department shall promptly report any information received pursuant to this section with any
24 sports team or sports governing body as the department deems appropriate but may not share any information
25 that would interfere with an ongoing criminal investigation.
26 (3) (a) Except as provided in subsection (3)(b), the books and records kept by a licensee as
27 provided by this section are public records and the examination, publication, and dissemination of the books
28 and records are governed by the provisions of [sections 1 through 10].

18 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Annual audit of licensee operations. Within 180 days after the end of
19 the licensee's fiscal year, the licensee shall transmit to the department an audit of the licensee's total internet
20 fantasy sports contest operations, including an itemization of all expenses and subsidies. Each audit must be
21 conducted by an accountant licensed under Title 37 who is selected by the licensee and approved by the
22 department.

24 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Civil penalty. A person who willfully fails to comply with the
25 requirements of [sections 1 through 10] and the rules adopted pursuant to this chapter is liable for a civil penalty
26 of not more than $1,000 for each violation, not to exceed $10,000 for violations arising out of the same
27 transaction or occurrence, which must accrue to the state and may be recovered in a civil action.
