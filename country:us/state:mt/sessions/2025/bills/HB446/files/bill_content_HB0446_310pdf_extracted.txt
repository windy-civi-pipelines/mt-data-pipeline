6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING THE EXPOSURE OF A PERSON'S INTIMATE
7 PARTS IN A PUBLIC PLACE TO MEMBERS OF THE OPPOSITE SEX OR THE OPPOSITE SEX ASSIGNED
8 OBSERVED AT BIRTH TO THE PERSON WITH THE INTENT TO VIOLATE THE DIGNITY OF A PERSON
9 CONSTITUTES INDECENT EXPOSURE; PROVIDING A DEFINITION PROVIDING AN EXCEPTION;
10 AMENDING SECTIONS SECTION 45-5-504 AND 46-23-502, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 45-5-504, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "45-5-504. Indecent exposure. (1) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure if the person
16 knowingly or purposely exposes the person's genitals or intimate parts by any means, including electronic
17 communication as defined in 45-5-625(5)(a), under circumstances in which the person knows the conduct is
18 likely to cause affront or alarm in order to:
19 (a) abuse, humiliate, harass, violate the dignity of, or degrade another; or
20 (b) arouse or gratify the person's own sexual response or desire or the sexual response or desire
21 of any person.
22 (2) (a) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
23 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
24 (b) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
25 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
26 (c) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed
27 $10,000 or be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not more than 10 years, or both.
28 (3) (a) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure to a minor if the person commits an

1 offense under subsection (1) and the person knows the conduct will be observed by a person who is under 16
2 years of age and the offender is more than 4 years older than the victim.
3 (b) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure to a minor shall be fined an amount not
4 to exceed $50,000 or be imprisoned in the state prison for a term of not less than 4 years, unless the judge
5 makes a written finding that there is good cause to impose a term of less than 4 years and imposes a term of
6 less than 4 years, or more than 100 years, or both.
7 (4) (a) Except when a minor is accompanied by a parent or guardian, or when an individual with
8 developmental disabilities is accompanied by a parent, guardian, or other caretake r or health care provider
9 designated by the parent or guardian, a person commits the offense of indecent exposure in a public place
10 when the person knowingly or purposely, UNDER CIRCUMSTANCES IN WHICH THE PERSON KNOWS THE CONDUCT IS
11 LIKELY TO CAUSE AFFRONT OR ALARM, exposes their genitals or intimate parts to members of the opposite
12 biological sex or opposite the person's assigned sex OBSERVED at birth. A MINOR BEING ASSISTED BY A PARENT OR
13 GUARDIAN, OR A DEVELOPMENTALLY DISABLED PERSON, MAY NOT BE CHARGED UNDER THIS SECTION.
14 (b) (i) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
15 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
16 (ii) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
17 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
18 (iii) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed
19 $10,000 or be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not more than 10 years, or both.
20 (c) As used in this subsection (4), " p ublic place" means a place to which the public or a group of
21 persons has access and includes but is not limited to transportation facilities, restrooms, locker rooms, dressing
22 rooms, and showers that are designated for multiperson, single-sex use, schools, places of amusement, parks,
23 places of business, playgrounds, and hallways, lobbies, and other portions of apartment houses, multifamily
24 dwellings, and hotels, except for rooms or apartments designed for actual residence."
25
26 Section 2. Section 46-23-502, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "46-23-502. Definitions. As used in Title 45, chapter 5, part 3 and parts 5 through 7, 46-18-255, and
28 this part, the following definitions apply:

1 (1) "Department" means the department of corrections provided for in 2-15-2301.
2 (2) "Foreign offenses" means a conviction for a sexual offense involving any of the conduct listed
3 in this section that was obtained under the laws of Canada, the United Kingdom, Australia, or New Zealand, or
4 under the laws of any foreign country when the United States department of state, in its country reports on
5 human rights practices, has concluded that an independent judiciary generally or vigorously enforced the right
6 to a fair trial in that country during the year in which the conviction was obtained.
7 (3) "Mental abnormality" means a congenital or acquired condition that affects the mental,
8 emotional, or volitional capacity of a person in a manner that predisposes the person to the commission of one
9 or more sexual offenses to a degree that makes the person a menace to the health and safety of other persons.
10 (4) "Municipality" means an entity that has incorporated as a city or town.
11 (5) "Personality disorder" means a personality disorder as defined in the fourth edition of the
12 Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders adopted by the American psychiatric association.
13 (6) "Predatory sexual offense" means a sexual offense committed against a stranger or against a
14 person with whom a relationship has been established or furthered for the primary purpose of victimization.
15 (7) "Registration agency" means:
16 (a) if the offender resides in a municipality, the police department of that municipality; or
17 (b) if the offender resides in a place other than a municipality, the sheriff's office of the county in
18 which the offender resides.
19 (8) (a) "Residence" means the location at which a person regularly resides, regardless of the
20 number of days or nights spent at that location, that can be located by a street address, including a house,
21 apartment building, motel, hotel, or recreational or other vehicle.
22 (b) The term does not mean a homeless shelter.
23 (9) "Sexual offender evaluator" means a person qualified under rules established by the
24 department to conduct psychosexual evaluations of sexual offenders and sexually violent predators.
25 (10) (a) "Sexual offense" means any violation, attempt, solicitation, or conspiracy to commit a
26 violation, or flight after the attempt or commission of the following:
27 (i) 45-5-301, unlawful restraint, if the victim is less than 18 years of age and the offender is not a
28 parent of the victim;

6 (v) 45-5-503, sexual intercourse without consent;

18 (xi) 45-5-401, robbery;
19 (xii) 45-6-103, arson; or
20 (xiii) 45-9-132, operation of unlawful clandestine laboratory; or
21 (b) any violation of a law of another state, a tribal government, the federal government, or the
22 military or a foreign entity reasonably equivalent to a violation listed in subsection (14)(a)."

24 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
25 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
26 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
27
28 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
