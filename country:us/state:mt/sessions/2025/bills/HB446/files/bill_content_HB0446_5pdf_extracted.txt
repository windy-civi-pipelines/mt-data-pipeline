16 Section 1. Section 45-5-504, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "45-5-504. Indecent exposure. (1) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure if the person
18 knowingly or purposely exposes the person's genitals or intimate parts by any means, including electronic
19 communication as defined in 45-5-625(5)(a), under circumstances in which the person knows the conduct is
20 likely to cause affront or alarm in order to:
21 (a) abuse, humiliate, harass, violate the dignity of, or degrade another; or
22 (b) arouse or gratify the person's own sexual response or desire or the sexual response or desire
23 of any person.
24 (2) (a) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
25 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
26 (b) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
27 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
28 (c) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed

2 (3) (a) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure to a minor if the person commits an
3 offense under subsection (1) and the person knows the conduct will be observed by a person who is under 16
4 years of age and the offender is more than 4 years older than the victim.
5 (b) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure to a minor shall be fined an amount not
6 to exceed $50,000 or be imprisoned in the state prison for a term of not less than 4 years, unless the judge
7 makes a written finding that there is good cause to impose a term of less than 4 years and imposes a term of
8 less than 4 years, or more than 100 years, or both.
9 (4) (a) Except when a minor is accompanied by a parent or guardian, or when an individual with
10 developmental disabilities is accompanied by a parent, guardian, or other caretake r or health care provider
11 designated by the parent or guardian, a person commits the offense of indecent exposure in a public place
12 when the person knowingly or purposely, UNDER CIRCUMSTANCES IN WHICH THE PERSON KNOWS THE CONDUCT IS
13 LIKELY TO CAUSE AFFRONT OR ALARM, exposes their genitals or intimate parts to members of the opposite
14 biological sex or opposite the person's assigned sex OBSERVED at birth. A MINOR BEING ASSISTED BY A PARENT OR
15 GUARDIAN, OR A DEVELOPMENTALLY DISABLED PERSON, MAY NOT BE CHARGED UNDER THIS SECTION.
16 (b) (i) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
17 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
18 (ii) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
19 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
20 (iii) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed
21 $10,000 or be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not more than 10 years, or both.
22 (c) As used in this subsection (4), "public place" means a place to which the public or a group of
23 persons has access and includes but is not limited to transportation facilities, restrooms, locker rooms, dressing
24 rooms, and showers that are designated for multiperson, single-sex use, schools, places of amusement, parks,
25 places of business, playgrounds, and hallways, lobbies, and other portions of apartment houses, multifamily
26 dwellings, and hotels, except for rooms or apartments designed for actual residence.
