6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING THE EXPOSURE OF A PERSON'S INTIMATE
7 PARTS IN A PUBLIC PLACE TO MEMBERS OF THE OPPOSITE SEX OR THE OPPOSITE SEX ASSIGNED
8 AT BIRTH TO THE PERSON CONSTITUTES INDECENT EXPOSURE; PROVIDING A DEFINITION;
9 AMENDING SECTIONS 45-5-504 AND 46-23-502, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 Section 1. Section 45-5-504, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "45-5-504. Indecent exposure. (1) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure if the person
15 knowingly or purposely exposes the person's genitals or intimate parts by any means, including electronic
16 communication as defined in 45-5-625(5)(a), under circumstances in which the person knows the conduct is
17 likely to cause affront or alarm in order to:
18 (a) abuse, humiliate, harass, violate the dignity of, or degrade another; or
19 (b) arouse or gratify the person's own sexual response or desire or the sexual response or desire
20 of any person.
21 (2) (a) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
22 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
23 (b) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
24 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
25 (c) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed
26 $10,000 or be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not more than 10 years, or both.
27 (3) (a) A person commits the offense of indecent exposure to a minor if the person commits an
28 offense under subsection (1) and the person knows the conduct will be observed by a person who is under 16

1 years of age and the offender is more than 4 years older than the victim.
2 (b) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure to a minor shall be fined an amount not
3 to exceed $50,000 or be imprisoned in the state prison for a term of not less than 4 years, unless the judge
4 makes a written finding that there is good cause to impose a term of less than 4 years and imposes a term of
5 less than 4 years, or more than 100 years, or both.
6 (4) (a) Except when a minor is accompanied by a parent or guardian, or when an individual with
7 developmental disabilities is accompanied by a parent, guardian, or other caretaker or health care provider
8 designated by the parent or guardian, a person commits the offense of indecent exposure in a public place
9 when the person knowingly or purposely exposes their genitals or intimate parts to members of the opposite
10 biological sex or opposite the person's assigned sex at birth.
11 (b) (i) A person convicted of the offense of indecent exposure shall be fined an amount not to
12 exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 6 months, or both.
13 (ii) On a second conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed $1,000 or be
14 imprisoned in the county jail for a term of not more than 1 year, or both.
15 (iii) On a third or subsequent conviction, the person shall be fined an amount not to exceed
16 $10,000 or be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not more than 10 years, or both.
17 (c) As used in this subsection (4), "public place" means a place to which the public or a group of
18 persons has access and includes but is not limited to transportation facilities, restrooms, locker rooms, dressing
19 rooms, and showers that are designated for multiperson, single-sex use, schools, places of amusement, parks,
20 places of business, playgrounds, and hallways, lobbies, and other portions of apartment houses, multifamily
21 dwellings, and hotels, except for rooms or apartments designed for actual residence."
22
23 Section 2. Section 46-23-502, MCA, is amended to read:
24 "46-23-502. Definitions. As used in Title 45, chapter 5, part 3 and parts 5 through 7, 46-18-255, and
25 this part, the following definitions apply:
26 (1) "Department" means the department of corrections provided for in 2-15-2301.
27 (2) "Foreign offenses" means a conviction for a sexual offense involving any of the conduct listed
28 in this section that was obtained under the laws of Canada, the United Kingdom, Australia, or New Zealand, or

2 human rights practices, has concluded that an independent judiciary generally or vigorously enforced the right
3 to a fair trial in that country during the year in which the conviction was obtained.
4 (3) "Mental abnormality" means a congenital or acquired condition that affects the mental,
5 emotional, or volitional capacity of a person in a manner that predisposes the person to the commission of one
6 or more sexual offenses to a degree that makes the person a menace to the health and safety of other persons.

9 Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders adopted by the American psychiatric association.
