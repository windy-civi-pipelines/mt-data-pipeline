69th Legislature 2025 SB 467.1
2 INTRODUCED BY D. LENZ

4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING CHILD CARE LAWS; PROVIDING
5 LEGISLATIVE INTENT; PROVIDING THAT INFORMATION ABOUT CHILDREN MAINTAINED BY A CHILD
6 CARE LICENSEE OR REGISTRANT BE KEPT CONFIDENTIAL; REMOVING REQUIREMENTS FOR A
7 LOCAL PUBLIC HEALTH AUTHORITY TO PERFORM CERTAIN INSPECTIONS AND PROVIDE CERTAIN
8 TRAININGS; AMENDING SECTIONS 52-2-702, 52-2-703, 52-2-722, 52-2-723, 52-2-725, 52-2-731, 52-2-732,
9 52-2-735, AND 52-2-737, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 Section 1. Section 52-2-702, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "52-2-702. Purpose -- findings -- legislative intent. (1) The purpose of this part is to assure ensure
15 that children requiring day care be provided such the food, shelter, security and safety, guidance and direction,
16 nurture and comfort, and learning experiences commensurate to their ages and capabilities so as to safeguard
17 the growth and development of such the children, thereby facilitating their proper physical and emotional
18 maturation.
19 (2) (a) The legislature finds that the number of children living in homes where both parents work or
20 in homes with a single parent who works has increased dramatically over the last decade.
21 (b) The legislature finds that the availability of quality child care is critical to the self-sufficiency and
22 independence of Montana families, including the growing number of mothers who have young children and who
23 work out of economic necessity.
24 (c) The legislature further finds that the number of quality child-care arrangements falls far short of
25 the number required for children in need of child-care services.
26 (d) It is the intent of the legislature that the state promote day care for the purposes of:
27 (i) improving the quality of, and coordination among, child-care programs and providing additional
28 resources for child-care services;
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 467
****
69th Legislature 2025 SB 467.1
1 (ii) promoting the availability and diversity of quality child-care services for all children and families
2 that need such services;
3 (iii) providing assistance to families whose financial resources are not sufficient to enable them to
4 pay the full costs of necessary child-care services;
5 (iv) ensuring that parents are not forced by lack of available programs or financial resources to
6 place a child in an unsafe or unhealthy child-care facility; and
7 (v) assisting people in finding and maintaining employment by lessening the stress related to the
8 lack of adequate child care.
9 (e) It is the intent of the legislature that nothing in this part may be construed or applied to usurp or
10 infringe on the moral and legal rights and responsibilities of parents."
11
12 Section 2. Section 52-2-703, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "52-2-703. Definitions. In this part, the following definitions apply:
14 (1) "Child" means a person under 13 years of age or a person with special needs, as defined by
15 the department, who is under 18 years of age or is 18 years of age and a full-time student expected to complete
16 an educational program by who is under 19 years of age and is physically or mentally incapable of independent
17 self-care.
18 (2) "Day care" or "child care" means care for children provided by an adult, other than a parent of
19 the children or other person living with the children as a parent, on a regular or irregular basis, as applicable, for
20 daily periods of less than 24 hours, whether that care is for daytime or nighttime hours.
21 (3) (a) "Day-care center" means an out-of-home place in which day care is provided to 16 or more
22 children on a regular or irregular basis.
23 (b) The term does not include a place where day care is provided if a parent of a child for whom
24 day care is provided remains on the premises.
25 (4) "Day-care facility" means a person, association, or place, incorporated or unincorporated, that
26 provides day care on a regular basis or a place licensed or registered to provide day care on an irregular basis,
27 as provided for in subsection (3)(a), or for children suffering from illness. The term includes a family day-care
28 home, a day-care center, a group day-care home, or a facility providing care in a child's home for the purpose

7 who are 3 years of age or older.

9 2201.

11 eight children on a regular basis.

13 no more than six children from separate families or all children from a sibling group and who opts to be
14 registered.

18 complied with this part and the applicable standards and rules for day-care centers.

21 Section 3. Section 52-2-722, MCA, is amended to read:
22 "52-2-722. Application for a license or registration certificate. (1) Application for a license or

24 prescribed by the department.
25 (2) Applications for a license or registration certificate by Indians residing on Indian reservations
26 shall must be made through the tribal governing body on forms prescribed by the department. Applications
27 made through a tribal governing body shall must be accompanied by a request by the tribal governing body that
28 the department investigate to determine whether a license or registration certificate should be granted.

4 Section 4. Section 52-2-723, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "52-2-723. Requirements for licensure. (1) The department shall include in the minimum standards
6 for day-care centers the following requirements:
7 (a) The applicant, the applicant's employees, and all those persons who will come in direct contact
8 with the children are of good character and shall provide satisfactory results of a background check.
9 (b) The staff of the day-care facility is sufficient in number as provided by rule to provide adequate
10 supervision and care of the children in the facility.
11 (c) Essential programs and practices carried on by the facility staff are developed and carried out
12 with due regard for the protection of the health, safety, development, and well-being of the children.
13 (d) Applicant and staff are qualified by practical experience or education or training to give good
14 care and treatment to the children.
15 (e) Intake records are kept on each child admitted for care.
16 (f) The applicant and staff limit admissions to the maximum number indicated on the current
17 license.
18 (g) The applicant will arrange for the necessary precautions to guard against communicable
19 diseases.
20 (h) Public liability insurance and fire insurance are currently in force for the protection of the
21 operator, the staff, and the facility.
22 (i) The ages and numbers of children that may be cared for in a day-care facility are specified.
23 (2) It is the duty of the department or its authorized representative to assist applicants in meeting
24 the minimum requirements."
25

4 Section 6. Section 52-2-731, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "52-2-731. Standards for day care. (1) In developing standards, the department shall seek the
6 advice and assistance of the legislature, the superintendent of public instruction, representatives of day-care
7 facilities, specialists in child care, and representatives of parent groups who use the services of day-care
8 facilities. The standards may pertain to:
9 (1) (a) character, suitability, and qualifications of an applicant and other persons directly responsible
10 for the care of children;
11 (2) (b) the number of individuals or staff required for adequate supervision and care of children in day-
12 care facilities;
13 (3) (c) child-care programs and practices necessary to ensure the health, safety, safety in
14 transportation, development, and well-being of children;
15 (4) (d) adequate and appropriate admission policies;
16 (5) (e) adequacy of physical facilities and equipment;
17 (6) (f) general financial ability and competence of an applicant to provide necessary care for children
18 and maintain prescribed standards;
19 (7) (g) the ages and numbers of children that may be cared for in a day-care facility.
20 (2) Administrative rules adopted by the department pursuant to the rulemaking authority granted
21 under this part must comply with the provisions of this part and may not be more stringent than the
22 requirements provided by law in this part."

9 Section 8. Section 52-2-735, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "52-2-735. Health protection -- certification required rulemaking. (1) The department shall adopt
11 rules for the protection of children in day-care centers from the health hazards of inadequate food preparation,
12 poor nutrition, and communicable diseases. Rules adopted by the department must include rules requiring
13 children under 5 years of age to be immunized against Haemophilus influenza type "b" before being admitted
14 for care in the facility unless an exemption has been claimed as provided in 20-5-405.
15 (2) Local public health authorities shall arrange to provide training to day-care center providers and
16 employees regarding health hazards. Upon successful completion of the training the local public health
17 authorities shall issue certificates to the providers and employees.
18 (3) In lieu of training, local public health authorities may elect to inspect facilities and issue
19 certificates of approval to child-care center providers.
20 (4) Each applicant for a license to operate a day-care center shall submit to the department a
21 certificate issued pursuant to subsection (2) or (3) before the department will issue a license.
22 (5) The local public health authority may charge the applicant a reasonable fee, not to exceed $25,
23 for any inspection necessary to issue a certificate of approval, or a fee not to exceed the documented cost for
24 training it provides under this section."
25
