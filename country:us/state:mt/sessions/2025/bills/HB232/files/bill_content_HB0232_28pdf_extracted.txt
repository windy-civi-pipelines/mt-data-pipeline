4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO HOMEOWNERS'
5 ASSOCIATIONS; REQUIRING A CERTAIN HOMEOWNERS' ASSOCIATION ASSOCIATIONS TO PRODUCE
6 ANNUAL BUDGET AND FINANCIAL REPORTS; ESTABLISHING REQUIREMENTS BEFORE A CERTAIN
7 HOMEOWNERS' ASSOCIATION ASSOCIATIONS MAY INCREASE FEES, DUES, OR OTHER CHARGES;
8 PROVIDING A DEFINITION; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 35-2-114 AND 35-2-525, MCA.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Homeowners' association -- annual budget and financial report
13 requirements -- EXCEPTION. (1) (a) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (5), A A homeowners' association shall
14 produce an annual budget report by the end of the homeowners' association's fiscal year.
15 (b) The annual budget report must include an operating budget for the next fiscal year showing
16 estimated revenue and expenses, including the sources of revenue and anticipated itemized expenses.
17 (2) (a) A homeowners' association shall produce an annual financial report not less than 30 days
18 after the end of the homeowners' association's fiscal year.
19 (b) The annual financial report must include:
20 (i) FOR EACH CATEGORY OF REVENUE AND EXPENDITURE TOTALING AT LEAST $50 ANNUALLY, a summary
21 REPORT of revenue and expenditures of the fiscal year ending; and
22 (ii) THE ENDING BALANCE OF EACH FUND; AND
23 (III) any unreserved balance remaining at the end of the fiscal year from fees, dues, or other
24 charges collected.
25 (3) The annual budget report and the annual financial report required under this section must:
26 (a) be sent by mail and electronic mail to the address or last-known address of all members who
27 own property in a homeowners' association; and
28 (b) be postmarked or sent no later than 30 15 days after the required production date provided in
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 232
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0232.2
1 subsection (1)(a) or (2)(a).
2 (4) Nothing in this section prohibits a homeowners' association from adopting more stringent
3 standards than the provisions provided in this section.
4 (5) A HOMEOWNERS' ASSOCIATION THAT CONSISTS OF 10 OR FEWER PARCELS IS EXEMPT FROM THE
5 REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Homeowners' association -- allowable fee increases -- EXCEPTION. (1)
8 EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (5), A A homeowners' association may only increase annual or monthly fees,
9 dues, or other charges:
10 (a) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (2)(B), once annually; and
11 (b) in an amount not to exceed 15% OR $100, WHICHEVER IS GREATER, of fees, dues, or other
12 charges assessed in the prior year pursuant to subsection (2)(a) and except as provided in subsection (2)(b).
13 (2) For an increase in annual or monthly fees, dues, or other charges:
14 (a) not to exceed 15% of the amount assessed in the prior year, a majority of the property owners
15 assessed shall approve of the increase in writing; or
16 (b) OCCURRING MORE THAN ONCE ANNUALLY OR in excess of 15% of the amount assessed in the prior
17 year, 2/3 3/5 of the property owners assessed shall approve of the increase in writing.
18 (3) The homeowners' association shall include the amount of a proposed increase for the next
19 fiscal year in the annual budget report mailed and sent to all property owners pursuant to [section 1].
20 (4) A property owner may approve an increase allowed under subsection (2) by sending an
21 approval in writing by mail or electronic mail to the homeowners' association, which must:
22 (a) provide the legal name or names of the property owners;
23 (b) provide the legal address of the property that is within the homeowners' association for which
24 the approval is being sent; and
25 (c) clearly indicate that the property owner approves or disapproves of the increase for the next
26 fiscal year.
27 (5) A HOMEOWNERS' ASSOCIATION THAT CONSISTS OF 10 OR FEWER PARCELS IS EXEMPT FROM THE
28 REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.

3 "35-2-114. Definitions. As used in this chapter, the following definitions apply:
4 (1) "Approved by the members" means approved and ratified by the affirmative vote:
5 (a) of a majority of the votes represented and voting:
6 (i) at a meeting at which a quorum is present and the affirmative votes constitute a majority of the
7 required quorum;
8 (ii) by a written ballot or written consent in conformity with this chapter; or
9 (iii) by the affirmative vote, written ballot, or written consent of the majority; and
10 (b) that includes the votes of all the members of any class, unit, or grouping that may be required
11 by the articles, bylaws, or this chapter for any specified member action.
12 (2) "Articles of incorporation" or "articles" include amended and restated articles of incorporation
13 and articles of merger.
14 (3) "Authenticated electronic identification" includes any e-mail address or other electronic
15 identification designated by a user, including a corporation, for electronic communications.
16 (4) "Board" or "board of directors" means the board of directors except that a person or group of
17 persons is not the board of directors because of powers delegated to that person or group pursuant to 35-2-
18 414.
19 (5) "Bylaws" means the code, codes, or rules, other than the articles, adopted pursuant to this
20 chapter for the regulation or management of the affairs of the corporation, regardless of the name or names by
21 which the code, codes, or rules are designated.
22 (6) "Class" refers to a group of memberships that have the same rights with respect to voting,
23 dissolution, redemption, and transfer. For the purpose of this section, rights must be considered the same if
24 they are determined by a formula applied uniformly.
25 (7) "Corporation" means a public benefit corporation, mutual benefit corporation, or religious
26 corporation.
27 (8) "Delegates" means those persons elected or appointed to vote in a representative assembly for
28 the election of a director or directors or on other matters.
