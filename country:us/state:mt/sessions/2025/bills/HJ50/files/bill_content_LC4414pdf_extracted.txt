4 A JOINT RESOLUTION OF THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF
5 MONTANA REQUESTING AN INTERIM STUDY OF THE UNIFORM COMMON INTEREST OWNERSHIP
6 ACT; AND REQUESTING THAT THE FINAL RESULTS OF THE STUDY BE REPORTED TO THE 70TH
7 LEGISLATURE.
8
9 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature commonly hears and considers legislation each session
10 regarding owners' associations; and
11 WHEREAS, the joint Local Government Interim Committee has studied owners' associations for the
12 past two legislative cycles, including learning about the makeup of owners' associations, studying our current
13 legal structure that applies to owners' associations, talking to stakeholders that work with owners' associations
14 about how to address owners' associations in Montana law, and discussing the implementation of the Uniform
15 Common Interest Ownership Act to address concerns about owners' associations; and
16 WHEREAS, during the interim committee’s work, it was discussed that the Uniform Common Interest
17 Ownership Act also addresses the Unit Ownership Act in Title 70, chapter 23, MCA, which governs
18 condominiums in Montana and has not been thoroughly amended since its adoption in 1965; and
19 WHEREAS, the laws in banking, insurance, title, real estate, and development industries have
20 significantly changed since 1965; and
21 WHEREAS, it was identified in the last Local Government Interim Committee that the Uniform Common
22 Interest Ownership Act was specifically drafted by the Uniform Law Commission to address the issues the
23 committee identified regarding owners' associations; and
24 WHEREAS, the committee asked members of the Montana Bar Association to review and draft, with
25 input from stakeholders, the Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act, which the Local Government Interim
26 Committee would consider for possible introduction to the 69th Legislature; and
27 WHEREAS, the draft of the Uniform Common Ownership Act was completed after the final meeting of
28 the Local Government Interim Committee and therefore could not be considered and introduced as a committee
- 1 - LC 4414
69th Legislature LC 4414
1 bill; and
2 WHEREAS, the Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act was introduced in the 69th Legislature as
3 House Bill No. 619 and was co-sponsored by all sitting legislators that sat on the Local Government Interim
4 Committee; and
5 WHEREAS, the House Judiciary Committee tabled House Bill No. 619 after committee members
6 argued that the bill was too large and complex to understand after one hearing and needed more review; and
7 WHEREAS, it is in the best interests of Montanans to have comprehensive and updated laws
8 governing owners' associations and condominiums.
9
10 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF
11 THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12 That the Legislative Council be requested to designate an appropriate interim committee or statutory
13 committee, pursuant to section 5-5-217, MCA, or direct sufficient staff resources to:
14 (1) review and analyze the Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act, as introduced in the 69th
15 Legislature as House Bill NO. 619, for potential implementation in Montana;
16 (2) review and analyze the current structure of Montana law, including laws governing nonprofit
17 organizations in Title 35, chapter 2, MCA, the Unit Ownership Act in Title 70, chapter 23, MCA, and other laws
18 that specifically address owners' associations; and
19 (3) determine if legislative changes are warranted to address owners' associations in Montana.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the study include representatives from interested parties, including
21 but not limited to the Montana Bankers Association, Montana Land Title Association, Montana Association of
22 Realtors, and other industry representatives as considered appropriate.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that if the study is assigned to staff, any findings or conclusions be
24 presented to and reviewed by an appropriate committee designated by the Legislative Council.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that all aspects of the study, including presentation and review
26 requirements, be concluded prior to September 15, 2026.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the final results of the study, including any findings, conclusions,
28 comments, or recommendations of the appropriate committee, be reported to the 70th Legislature.
