17 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Tort actions -- gender transition treatment for minors. (1) An action
18 brought by a person for damages for injuries suffered from gender transition treatment the person received as a
19 minor must be commenced within 25 years from the date the person reaches 18 years of age or within 4 years
20 from the time of discovery by the person of both the injury and the causal relationship between the gender
21 transition treatment and the injury, whichever occurs later.
22 (2) For the purposes of this section, "gender transition treatment" means the following medical
23 treatments provided:
24 (a) to a female minor to address the minor's perception that her gender or sex is not female:
25 (i) surgical procedures, including a vaginectomy, hysterectomy, oophorectomy, ovariectomy,
26 reconstruction of the urethra, metoidioplasty, phalloplasty, scrotoplasty, implantation of erection or testicular
27 protheses, subcutaneous mastectomy, voice surgery, and pectoral implants;
28 (ii) supraphysiologic doses of testosterone or other androgens; or

11 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Reciprocity in gender transition treatment coverage required. (1) A
12 group or individual insurance policy that includes coverage for gender transition treatment or subsequent
13 treatment directly related to the provision of gender transition treatment must also include coverage for
14 detransition treatment under equivalent cost-sharing policies.

16 policy is not required to provide equivalent coverage for detransition treatment, except that the policy must
17 provide equivalent coverage to insureds who were enrolled when gender transition treatment coverage was
18 provided and received benefits under that coverage.
19 (3) For the purposes of this section "gender transition treatment" means the following medical
20 treatments provided:
21 (a) to a female to address her perception that her gender or sex is not female:

23 reconstruction of the urethra, metoidioplasty, phalloplasty, scrotoplasty, implantation of erection or testicular

25 (ii) supraphysiologic doses of testosterone or other androgens; or
26 (iii) puberty blockers, such as GnRH agonists or other synthetic drugs that suppress the production
27 of estrogen and progesterone to delay or suppress pubertal development in female minors; and
