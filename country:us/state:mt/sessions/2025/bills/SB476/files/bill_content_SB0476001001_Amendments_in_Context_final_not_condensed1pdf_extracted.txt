1 SENATE BILL NO. 476
2 INTRODUCED BY D. EMRICH, V. RICCI
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING AND CODIFYING LAWS REGARDING
5 THE SEPARATION OF POWERS DOCTRINE; DEFINING THE POWERS OF THE LEGISLATIVE BRANCH;
6 DEFINING THE POWERS OF THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH; DEFINING THE POWERS OF THE JUDICIAL
7 BRANCH; DEFINING THE POWERS OF THE BOARD OF REGENTS OF HIGHER EDUCATION AND THE
8 BOARD OF PUBLIC EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTIONS 2-15-1505 AND 2-15-1507, MCA; AND
9 PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Separation of powers doctrine -- government powers generally --
14 limits. (1) The powers of the legislative branch, the executive branch, and the judicial branch are limited to
15 those enumerated in the Montana constitution and granted by law.
16 (2) The legislature may not enact a law that is contrary to the constitution or exercise executive
17 branch powers provided for in [section 3] or judicial branch powers provided for in [section 4].
18 (3) The executive branch may not exercise legislative branch powers provided for in [section 2] or
19 judicial branch powers provided for in [section 4].
20 (4) The judicial branch may not:
21 (a) exercise legislative branch powers provided for in [section 2];
22 (b) amend the effective date of an act of the legislature before the law becomes effective;
23 (c) declare a law unenforceable as applied toward anyone other than the parties involved in a case
24 or controversy; or
25 (d) exercise executive branch powers provided for in [section 3].
26 (5) The power of the board of regents is defined as provided in [section 5].
27 (6) The power of the board of public education is limited as provided in [section 5].

2 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Legislative branch power. Article V of the Montana constitution vests
3 the legislature with power that cannot be exercised by other branches of government. This power is exercised
4 through a legislature consisting of a senate and a house of representatives.
5
6 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Executive branch power. Article VI of the Montana constitution vests
7 the executive branch with power that cannot be exercised by other branches of government. This power is
8 exercised through a governor, lieutenant governor, secretary of state, attorney general, superintendent of public
9 instruction, auditor, board of regents, and board of public education. The executive power includes:
10 (1) the authority of the board of regents to perform duties provided by law and [section 7]; and
11 (2) the authority of the board of public education to perform duties provided by law and [section 7].
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Judicial branch power. Article VII of the Montana constitution vests the
14 judicial branch with power that cannot be exercised by other branches of government. This power is exercised
15 through a supreme court, district courts, justice courts, and other courts as may be provided by state statute.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Executive branch power of board of regents and board of public
18 education -- definitions. (1) The board of regents and the board of public education are agencies of the
19 executive branch of state government. As such, they are required to enforce, within their respective spheres,
20 laws adopted by the legislature or by the people.
21 (2) The oversight of the Montana university system entrusted by the state constitution to the board
22 of regents refers to the kind of oversight customarily exercised by executive branch officers over operations
23 assigned to them and does not extend to overriding state statutes. The board of regents' power regarding the
24 government, control, supervision, coordination, and management of the university system and other public
25 educational institutions is assigned by law.
26 (3) The duties entrusted by the state constitution to the board of public education are limited to
27 executive branch functions and do not extend to overriding state statutes. The board of public education

3 (4) The rights of the people protected in the United States and Montana constitutions extend fully
4 to geographical areas under the control of the board of regents and the board of public education. The boards

6 (5) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
7 (a) "Board of public education" means the board of public education created in Article X, section

9 (b) "Board of regents" means the board of regents of higher education established by Article X,

17 basis.
18 (g) "Supervision" means regular monitoring of the administrative, clinical, or clerical work

20 authority to give direction and require change.
