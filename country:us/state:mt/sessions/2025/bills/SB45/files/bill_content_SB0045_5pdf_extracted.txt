5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “ESTABLISHING A MONTANA JUDICIAL PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
6 SYSTEM; CREATING A JUDICIAL PERFORMANCE EVALUATION COMMISSION AND PROVIDING FOR
7 DUTIES AND MEMBERS; ALLOCATING THE COMMISSION TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE FOR
8 ADMINISTRATIVE PURPOSES; REQUIRING THE COMMISSION TO PERFORM EVALUATIONS FOR
9 DISTRICT COURT JUDGES AND SUPREME COURT JUSTICES; REQUIRING THE COMMISSION TO
10 CONTRACT FOR A SURVEY; REQUIRING THE SECRETARY OF STATE TO PUBLISH A INCLUDE THE
11 JUDICIAL EVALUATION INFORMATION IN THE VOTER INFORMATION PAMPHLET; PROVIDING THE
12 COMMISSION AND THE SECRETARY OF STATE RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING RULEMAKING
13 AUTHORITY; PROVIDING A TRANSITION; PROVIDING A ONE-TIME-ONLY APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING
14 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 3-1-1124, 13-13-214, 13-27-401, AND 13-27-
15 410, MCA; AND PROVIDING A TERMINATION DATE.”
16
17 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature finds that it is necessary to provide a comprehensive evaluation
18 system of judicial performance to provide Montana citizens with fair, responsible, and useful information about
19 the judicial performance of supreme court justices and district court judges; and
20 WHEREAS, providing information to the people of Montana regarding the performance of judges and
21 justices is a matter of public interest and statewide concern; and
22 WHEREAS, the information provided by a comprehensive evaluation system of judicial performance
23 will provide judges and justices with useful information about their own performances; and
24 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has decided to establish an independent office on judicial
25 performance evaluation with authority to implement an evaluation process and conduct and publicize
26 evaluations.
27
28 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

2 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Judicial performance evaluation commission -- appointment --
3 members -- rulemaking. (1) There is a judicial performance evaluation commission. The commission consists
4 of 11 members as follows:
5 (a) three TWO members ONE MEMBER appointed by the president of the senate, including one
6 retired district court judge but no other member currently or formerly licensed to practice law in Montana AND

11 (c) three FOUR members appointed by the governor, including only one member currently or
12 formerly licensed to practice law in Montana; and
13 (d) two THREE members appointed by the members of the CHIEF JUSTICE OF THE supreme court,
14 including only one member currently or formerly licensed to practice law in Montana.

17 COMMISSION MEMBERS MUST BE STAGGERED.

20 unexpired term by the same appointing authority that appointed the member whose departure created the

23 (5) The commission shall provide recommendations to the legislature regarding the commission's
24 budget and if the judicial performance evaluation program should be expanded to other courts.

23 JUDGE.

26 (a) consider opposing views and alternative solutions permitted by law for resolving cases;

5 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Judicial performance evaluations. (1) Beginning in 2026, the
6 commission shall prepare:
7 (a) a midterm performance evaluation for each district court judge during the third year of the
8 judge’s term;
9 (b) a preelection performance evaluation for each district court judge during the fifth year of the
10 judge's term;
11 (c) a midterm performance evaluation for each justice of the Montana supreme court during the
12 third year of the justice’s term;
13 (d) a preelection performance evaluation for each supreme court justice during the seventh year of
14 the justice's term; and
15 (e) a preelection performance evaluation for each judge appointed to a vacancy during the year
16 prior to the last year of the judge's current term.
17 (2) The performance evaluation for a judge under subsection (1) may consider only the information
18 listed in subsections (3) and (4). The performance evaluation must give primary emphasis to information
19 gathered since the judge's election or appointment to the judge's current term in office.
20 (3) The information a performance evaluation must consider is as follows:
21 (a) the results of the judge’s most recent judicial performance survey conducted in accordance
22 with [section 5];
23 (b) information acquired by observation of the judge's conduct in the courtroom;
24 (c) the judge’s judicial disciplinary record, if any;
25 (d) public comment directed toward SOLICITED BY the commission whether solicited or unsolicited;
26 (e) information from any earlier judicial performance evaluation of the judge, except that the
27 commission shall rely primarily on information gathered subsequent to the last judicial election; and
28 (f) any other factor the commission considers relevant to evaluating the judge’s performance and

18 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Judicial performance survey. (1) A third party under contract with the
19 commission shall conduct a judicial performance survey. The third party may not be affiliated with a legal firm or
20 a legal professional.
21 (2) (a) Each judicial performance survey must survey respondents in each of the following
22 classifications:
23 (i) attorneys who have appeared before the judge as counsel either pro hac vice or while licensed
24 to practice law in Montana;
25 (ii) jurors who have served in one or more cases before the judge; and
26 (iii) court staff who have worked with the judge.
27 (b) Only a respondent under subsection (2)(a)(i) who is admitted to practice law in the state and in
28 good standing with the state bar of Montana may evaluate a judge’s legal ability under subsection (7)(a).

17 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Midterm reports. (1) The commission shall compile a midterm report for
18 each judge relying on that judge's midterm evaluation. The midterm report may also be based on further
19 information the commission considers useful for purposes of judicial evaluation or self-improvement.
20 (2) The commission shall provide the midterm report to the evaluated judge, the Montana supreme
21 court, and the court administrator.
22 (3) (a) The commission may publish a partial midterm report for a judge whose appointment date
23 precludes the collection of complete midterm evaluation data. For a newly appointed judge, a midterm report is
24 considered partial when the midterm evaluation is missing a respondent group.
25 (b) The commission's webpage must identify each judge receiving a partial midterm report.
26 (c) Prior to a partial midterm report, the commission shall inform the court administrator of judges
27 who will receive a partial report.

17 (b) Each report, together with the information collected for the report, must remain confidential
18 unless the judge who is the subject of the report files a declaration of candidacy in the ensuing election. In that
19 event, the report and the information collected for it become public record on the day following the last day on

22 (a) a summary of the results of the judicial performance survey and tabulations of the responses to
23 each question;
24 (b) information concerning any public discipline that a judge has received and is not subject to
25 restrictions on disclosure under Title 3, chapter 1, part 11;
26 (c) a narrative describing the judge’s performance with, at the option of the commission, an overall
27 score on a numerical scale from one to five;

17 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature finds that it is necessary to provide a comprehensive evaluation
18 system of judicial performance to provide Montana citizens with fair, responsible, and useful information about
19 the judicial performance of supreme court justices and district court judges; and
20 WHEREAS, providing information to the people of Montana regarding the performance of judges and
21 justices is a matter of public interest and statewide concern; and
22 WHEREAS, the information provided by a comprehensive evaluation system of judicial performance
23 will provide judges and justices with useful information about their own performances; and
24 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has decided to establish an independent office on judicial
25 performance evaluation with authority to implement an evaluation process and conduct and publicize
26 evaluations.

2 NEW SECTION. SECTION 15. APPROPRIATION. (1) THE FOLLOWING AMOUNTS ARE APPROPRIATED FROM
3 THE GENERAL FUND TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE FOR IMPLEMENTING THE PROVISIONS OF [THIS ACT]:
4 (A) $500,000 FOR THE FISCAL YEAR BEGINNING JULY 1, 2025; AND
5 (B) $500,000 FOR THE FISCAL YEAR BEGINNING JULY 1, 2026.
6 (2) THE LEGISLATURE INTENDS THAT THESE ARE ONE-TIME-ONLY APPROPRIATIONS.
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 16. Codification instruction. (1) [Sections 1 and 2] are intended to be
9 codified as an integral part of Title 2, chapter 15, part 20, and the provisions of Title 2, chapter 15, part 20,
10 apply to [sections 1 and 2].
11 (2) [Sections 3 through 8] are intended to be codified as a new part in Title 3, chapter 1, and the
12 provisions of Title 3, chapter 1, apply to [sections 3 through 8].
13
14 NEW SECTION. SECTION 17. TERMINATION. [THIS ACT] TERMINATES JUNE 30, 2035.
