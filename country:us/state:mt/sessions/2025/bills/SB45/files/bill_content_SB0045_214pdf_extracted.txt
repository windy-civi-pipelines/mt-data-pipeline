15 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature finds that it is necessary to provide a comprehensive evaluation
16 system of judicial performance to provide Montana citizens with fair, responsible, and useful information about
17 the judicial performance of supreme court justices and district court judges; and
18 WHEREAS, providing information to the people of Montana regarding the performance of judges and
19 justices is a matter of public interest and statewide concern; and
20 WHEREAS, the information provided by a comprehensive evaluation system of judicial performance
21 will provide judges and justices with useful information about their own performances; and
22 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has decided to establish an independent office on judicial
23 performance evaluation with authority to implement an evaluation process and conduct and publicize
24 evaluations.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

3 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. As used in [sections 3 through 8], unless the context
4 requires otherwise, the following definitions apply:
5 (1) "Bias" means prejudice for or against a party or issue arising for reasons other than the facts of
6 a case or the law governing a case. Bias in a judge may be inferred from comments, facial expressions, prior
7 activity, distortion of the law to obtain a particular result, or a conflict of interest DISPLAYED BY USING EPITHETS,
8 SLURS, DEMEANING NICKNAMES, OR THREATENING, INTIMIDATING, OR HOSTILE ACTS OR BODY LANGUAGE THAT CREATES
9 AN APPEARANCE OF BIAS OR PREJUDICE.
10 (2) "Commission" means the judicial performance evaluation commission established in [section
11 1].
12 (3) A "conflict of interest" arises when a judge has a financial, political, or other interest
13 that arguably creates bias.
14 (4)(3) "Court administrator" means the position established in 3-1-701.
15 (5)(4) "Impartiality" means the practice of making judicial decisions in accordance with the law, fairly,
16 and without evidence of bias ABSENCE OF PREJUDICE IN FAVOR OF OR AGAINST PARTICULAR PARTIES OR CLASSES OF
17 PARTIES AS WELL AS THE MAINTENANCE OF AN OPEN MIND WHEN CONSIDERING ISSUES THAT MAY COME BEFORE A
18 JUDGE.
19 (6)(5) "Judge" means a district court judge or a supreme court justice.
20 (7)(6) "Open-mindedness" means a willingness to:
21 (a) consider opposing views and alternative solutions permitted by law for resolving cases;
22 (b) remain open to persuasion despite a judge's existing views;
23 (c) concede that there is an appearance of one's own bias; and
24 (d) treat each case in accordance with the facts presented and the governing law.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Judicial performance survey. (1) A third party under contract with the
9 commission shall conduct a judicial performance survey. The third party may not be affiliated with a legal firm or
10 a legal professional.

12 classifications:
13 (i) attorneys who have appeared before the judge as counsel either pro hac vice or while licensed
14 to practice law in Montana;
15 (ii) jurors who have served in one or more cases before the judge; and
16 (iii) court staff who have worked with the judge.
17 (b) Only a respondent under subsection (2)(a)(i) who is admitted to practice law in the state and in
18 good standing with the state bar of Montana may evaluate a judge’s legal ability under subsection (7)(a).
19 (3) The commission may establish by rule additional classifications that the commission considers
20 helpful to voters.
21 (4) All survey responses are confidential, including added comments.
22 (5) If the commission provides information to a judge or the court administrator, it must do so in a
23 manner that protects the anonymity of survey respondents.
24 (6) A survey must be provided to a juror respondent no more than 30 days after the completion of
25 the case in which the juror served.
26 (7) Surveys must include questions inquiring into the judge’s:
27 (a) legal ability, including the following:

3 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Publication of judicial performance reports. (1) (a) The commission
4 shall provide each judge with the judge's reports in a timely manner. If a report is based on a preelection
5 evaluation, it must be provided at least 45 days before the last day on which the judge may file a declaration of

7 (b) Each report, together with the information collected for the report, must remain confidential
8 unless the judge who is the subject of the report files a declaration of candidacy in the ensuing election. In that
9 event, the report and the information collected for it become public record on the day following the last day on
10 which the judge may file a declaration of candidacy.
11 (2) The report must include:
12 (a) a summary of the results of the judicial performance survey and tabulations of the responses to
13 each question;
14 (b) information concerning any public discipline that a judge has received and is not subject to

16 (c) a narrative describing the judge’s performance with, at the option of the commission, an overall
17 score on a numerical scale from one to five;
18 (d) at the option of the judge evaluated, a written statement not to exceed 200 words submitted by
19 the judge within 15 days of receiving a copy of the report; and
20 (e) any other information the commission considers necessary to include in the report, including
21 reliably reported improprieties in the judge's conduct of the judge's office.
