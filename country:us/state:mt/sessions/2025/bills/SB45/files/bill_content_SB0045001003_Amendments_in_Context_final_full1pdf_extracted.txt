1 SENATE BILL NO. 45
2 INTRODUCED BY T. MCGILLVRAY
3 BY REQUEST OF THE SENATE SELECT COMMITTEE ON JUDICIAL OVERSIGHT AND REFORM
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “ESTABLISHING A MONTANA JUDICIAL PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
6 SYSTEM; CREATING A JUDICIAL PERFORMANCE EVALUATION COMMISSION AND PROVIDING FOR
7 DUTIES AND MEMBERS; ALLOCATING THE COMMISSION TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
8 ADMINISTRATION FOR ADMINISTRATIVE PURPOSES; REQUIRING THE COMMISSION TO PERFORM
9 EVALUATIONS FOR DISTRICT COURT JUDGES AND SUPREME COURT JUSTICES; REQUIRING THE
10 COMMISSION TO CONTRACT FOR A SURVEY; REQUIRING THE SECRETARY OF STATE TO PUBLISH A
11 JUDICIAL EVALUATION INFORMATION PAMPHLET; PROVIDING THE COMMISSION AND THE
12 SECRETARY OF STATE RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND
13 AMENDING SECTIONS 3-1-1124, 13-13-214, 13-27-401, AND 13-27-410, MCA.”
14
15 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature finds that it is necessary to provide a comprehensive evaluation
16 system of judicial performance to provide Montana citizens with fair, responsible, and useful information about
17 the judicial performance of supreme court justices and district court judges; and
18 WHEREAS, providing information to the people of Montana regarding the performance of judges and
19 justices is a matter of public interest and statewide concern; and
20 WHEREAS, the information provided by a comprehensive evaluation system of judicial performance
21 will provide judges and justices with useful information about their own performances; and
22 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has decided to establish an independent office on judicial
23 performance evaluation with authority to implement an evaluation process and conduct and publicize
24 evaluations.
25
26 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
27

5 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Publication of judicial performance reports. (1) (a) The commission
6 shall provide each judge with the judge's reports in a timely manner. If a report is based on a preelection
7 evaluation, it must be provided at least 45 days before the last day on which the judge may file a declaration of
8 the judge's candidacy in the next election.
9 (b) Each report, together with the information collected for the report, must remain confidential
10 unless the judge who is the subject of the report files a declaration of candidacy in the ensuing election. In that
11 event, the report and the information collected for it become public record on the day following the last day on
12 which the judge may file a declaration of candidacy.
13 (2) The report must include:
14 (a) a summary of the results of the judicial performance survey and tabulations of the responses to
15 each question;
16 (b) information concerning any public discipline that a judge has received following a judicial
17 standards commission finding of good cause for a hearing and is not subject to restrictions on disclosure under
18 Title 3, chapter 1, part 11;
19 (c) a narrative describing the judge’s performance with, at the option of the commission, an overall
20 score on a numerical scale from one to five; and
21 (d) at the option of the judge evaluated, a written statement not to exceed 200 1,000 words
22 submitted by the judge within 15 days of receiving a copy of the report; and.
23 (e) any other information the commission considers necessary to include in the report, including
24 reliably reported improprieties in the judge's conduct of the judge's office.
25 (3) The report may refer to information from a judge's earlier judicial performance report only if
26 necessary to provide context for the current reporting period.
27 (4) The commission shall make each report publicly available on the internet and may make
28 reports publicly available by other means consistent with budgetary constraints.

15 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature finds that it is necessary to provide a comprehensive evaluation
16 system of judicial performance to provide Montana citizens with fair, responsible, and useful information about
17 the judicial performance of supreme court justices and district court judges; and
18 WHEREAS, providing information to the people of Montana regarding the performance of judges and
19 justices is a matter of public interest and statewide concern; and
20 WHEREAS, the information provided by a comprehensive evaluation system of judicial performance
21 will provide judges and justices with useful information about their own performances; and
22 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has decided to establish an independent office on judicial
23 performance evaluation with authority to implement an evaluation process and conduct and publicize
24 evaluations.

3 requisition for the printing and delivery of a voter information pamphlet for all statewide ballot issues and a
4 judicial evaluation information pamphlet. The requisition must include a delivery list providing for shipment of
5 the required number of pamphlets to each county and to the secretary of state.
6 (2) The secretary of state shall estimate the number of copies necessary to furnish one copy to
7 each voter in each county, except that two or more voters with the same mailing address and the same last
8 name may be counted as one voter. The secretary of state shall provide for an extra supply of the pamphlets in
9 determining the number of voter pamphlets to be ordered in the requisition.
10 (3) The department of administration shall call for bids and contract with the lowest bidder for the
11 printing and delivery of the voter information pamphlet and judicial evaluation information pamphlet. The
12 contract must require completion of printing and shipment, as specified on the delivery list, of the voter
13 information pamphlets and judicial evaluation information pamphlets by not later than 45 days before the
14 election at which the statewide ballot issues and judges will be voted on by the people.
15 (4) The county official responsible for voter registration in each county shall mail one copy of the
16 voter information pamphlet and one copy of the judicial evaluation information pamphlet to each registered
17 voter in the county who is on the active voter list, except that two or more voters with the same mailing address
18 and the same last name may be counted as one voter. The mailing label may include an address line that
19 addresses the voter or the current resident. The mailing must take place no later than 30 days before the
20 election.
21 (5) Ten copies of the voter information pamphlet and ten copies of the judicial evaluation
22 information pamphlet must be available at each precinct for use by any voter wishing to read the explanatory
23 information and complete text before voting on the statewide ballot issues or judges."
