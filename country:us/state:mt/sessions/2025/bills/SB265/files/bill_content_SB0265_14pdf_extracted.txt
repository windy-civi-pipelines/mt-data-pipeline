4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CRYPTOCURRENCY LAWS; ESTABLISHING THE

11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 9] may be cited as the "Financial
14 Freedom and Innovation Act".
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Prohibitions on use and testing of central bank digital currency. (1)
17 A governing authority may not accept or require a payment using central bank digital currency.
18 (2) A governing authority may not participate in a test of central bank digital currency by the board
19 of governors of the federal reserve system or a branch or agency of the federal government.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Permitted uses of digital assets. A governing authority may not
22 prohibit, restrict, or otherwise impair the ability of an individual or business to:
23 (1) accept digital assets as a method of payment for legal goods and services; or
24 (2) take custody of digital assets using a self-hosted wallet or hardware wallet.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Access to blockchain protocols and transfer of digital assets. A
27 business or individual in the state may:
28 (1) operate a node for the purpose of connecting to a blockchain protocol and participating in the

6 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Securities exemptions. (1) A business offering to provide digital asset
7 mining as a service or staking as a service for individuals or to other businesses may not be considered as
8 offering a security or investment contract pursuant to state securities laws.
9 (2) Nothing in [sections 1 through 9] may preclude the attorney general from instituting appropriate
10 criminal charges against a business or individual that falsely claims to be offering digital asset mining as a
11 service or staking as a service.

19 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Sales limit on network tokens. (1) In order to receive an exemption
20 under 30-10-105, the aggregate of network tokens that may be sold by a network token issuer to accredited or
21 nonaccredited investors in a 12-month period may not exceed $250 million as the amount is adjusted annually

23 published by the bureau of labor statistics of the department of labor. This does not include programmatic and

25 accordance with the rules and code of the network.

27 of securities and insurance determines that the exemption is necessary or in the public interest and is
28 consistent with the protection of investors.

2 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 9], the following definitions
3 apply:
4 (1) "Affiliate persons" means a person who owns more than 10% of a digital asset or network
5 token from the current available supply.
6 (2) "Blockchain" means data that is:
7 (a) shared across a peer-to-peer network to create a ledger of verified transactions or information
8 among network participants linked together using cryptography to maintain the integrity of the ledger and to
9 execute other functions; and
10 (b) distributed among network participants in an automated fashion to concurrently update network
11 participants on the state of the ledger and any other functions.
12 (3) "Blockchain protocol" means any executable software deployed to a blockchain, typically
13 referred to as smart contracts, including an additional standardized set of rules that uses a previously existing
14 blockchain as a base, which facilitates the transfer of data and electronic records and allows that data to be
15 broadcast to nodes. The executable software is governed by a set of predefined rules that execute
16 autonomously without human intervention. The rules may be altered by a predetermined mechanism.
17 (4) (a) "Central bank digital currency" means a digital currency, a digital medium of exchange, or a
18 digital monetary unit of account issued by the United States federal reserve system or a federal agency that is
19 made directly available to institutions or consumers by the entities, or which is processed or validated directly
20 by the entities.
21 (b) The term does not include a digital asset backed by legal tender or government treasuries and
22 issued by a private entity.
