4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING VOTER IDENTIFICATION LAWS;
5 REVISING IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS FOR VOTING AND PROVISIONAL VOTING; REMOVING
6 THE OPTION TO EXECUTE A DECLARATION OF REASONABLE IMPEDIMENT TO MEETING THE
7 IDENTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS FOR PROVISIONAL BALLOTS; AMENDING SECTIONS 13-13-114, AND
8 13-13-602, AND 13-15-107, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 13-13-114, MCA, is amended to read:

18 military identification card, tribal photo identification card, United States passport, or Montana concealed carry

20 (ii) (A) a current utility bill, bank statement, paycheck, government check, or other government
21 document that shows the elector's name and current address; and
22 (B) photo identification that shows the elector's name, including but not limited to a school district
23 or postsecondary education photo identification.
24 (b) An elector who provides the information listed in subsection (1)(a) may sign the precinct
25 register and must be provided with a regular ballot to vote.
26 (c) If the information provided in subsection (1)(a) differs from information in the precinct register
27 but an election judge determines that the information provided is sufficient to verify the voter's identity and
28 eligibility to vote pursuant to 13-2-512, the elector may sign the precinct register, complete a new registration

16 provide sufficient identification as required pursuant to 13-13-114(1)(a):

20 designated pursuant to 13-1-116 on the affirmation required under 13-13-601 to the signature on the

22 administrator shall handle the ballot as provided in subsection (7)(5). If the signatures do not match and the
23 individual or the individual's agent fails to provide valid identification information by the deadline, the ballot must
24 be rejected and handled as provided in 13-15-108.

14 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Saving clause. [This act] does not affect rights and duties that matured,
15 penalties that were incurred, or proceedings that were begun before [the effective date of this act].
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
18 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
19 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
