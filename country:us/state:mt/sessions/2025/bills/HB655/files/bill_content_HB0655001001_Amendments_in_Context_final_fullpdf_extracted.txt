5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING DISCRIMINATORY ACTION AGAINST A PERSON
6 PROVIDING ADOPTION OR FOSTER CARE SERVICES BASED ON THE PERSON'S RELIGIOUS BELIEF;
7 PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING REMEDIES; AND PROVIDING A STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS.”
8
9 WHEREAS, the State of Montana seeks to place a child in a safe, loving, and supportive home
10 whenever the state is required to place a child with an adoptive or foster family; and
11 WHEREAS, adoption and foster care agencies in Montana assist families with adoption and foster
12 parent placements of children; and
13 WHEREAS, the adoption agencies and foster care providers of this state represent diverse
14 organizations and groups, some of which are faith-based and some of which are nonfaith-based; and
15 WHEREAS, children in need of placement services benefit from having as many adoption agencies
16 and foster care providers as possible because the more agencies and providers that take part in placement
17 services, the greater the likelihood that a child will find a permanent placement; and
18 WHEREAS, the United States Supreme Court in Fulton v. City of Philadelphia, 141 S. Ct. 1868 (2021),
19 recognized the benefits of having more and not fewer adoption agencies and foster care providers, stating that
20 "[m]aximizing the number of foster families and minimizing liability are important goals, but the City fails to show
21 that granting [Catholic Social Services] an exception will put those goals at risk. If anything, including CSS in
22 the program seems likely to increase, not reduce, the number of available foster parents"; and
23 WHEREAS, children and families benefit greatly from the adoption and foster care services provided by
24 faith-based and nonfaith-based child placing agencies; and
25 WHEREAS, faith-based organizations and groups have a lengthy and distinguished history of providing
26 adoption and foster care services in Montana; and
27 WHEREAS, private child-placing agencies and individuals, including faith-based child-placing agencies

1 and individuals, have the right to free exercise of religion under both the state and federal constitutions, which,
2 under well-settled principles of constitutional law, includes the freedom to abstain from conduct that conflicts
3 with an agency's sincerely held religious beliefs; and
4 WHEREAS, ensuring that faith-based child-placing agencies can continue to provide adoption and
5 foster care services will benefit the children and families who receive those services; and
6 WHEREAS, the United States Constitution allows all adoption and foster care providers agencies to
7 operate according to their beliefs without fear of unjust government punishment; and
8 WHEREAS, in the unanimous decision in Fulton v. City of Philadelphia, the U.S. Supreme Court made
9 clear that state governments violate the requirements of religious neutrality when they undermine religious
10 beliefs or practices and that the "[g]overnment fails to act neutrally when it proceeds in a manner intolerant of
11 religious beliefs or restricts practices because of their religious nature"; and
12 WHEREAS, the U.S. Supreme Court in Espinoza v. Montana Dept. of Revenue, 140 S. Ct. 2246, 2276
13 (2020) (Gorsuch, J., concurring), stated that "[t]he Constitution forbids laws that prohibit the free exercise of
14 religion. That guarantee protects not just the right to be a religious person, holding beliefs inwardly and secretly;
15 it also protects the right to act on those beliefs outwardly and publicly [italics removed]"; and
16 WHEREAS, the Espinoza Court noted that the U.S. Supreme Court has "long recognized the
17 importance of protecting religious actions, not just religious status" and, quoting Cantwell v. Connecticut, 310
18 U.S. 296, 3030 (1940), state that "the First Amendment protects the 'freedom to act' as well as the 'freedom to
19 believe'"; and
20 WHEREAS, in Thomas v. Review Bd. of Ind. Employment Security Div., 450 U.S. 707, 717-718 (1981),
21 the U.S. Supreme Court held that the government violates the Free Exercise Clause whenever it "conditions
22 receipt of an important benefit upon conduct prescribed by a religious faith, or... denies such a benefit because
23 of conduct mandated by a religious belief, thereby putting substantial pressure on an adherent to modify his
24 behavior and to violate his beliefs."
25

1 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 4] the following definitions

3 (1) "Adoption or foster care" or "adoption or foster care service" means social services provided to
4 or on behalf of children, including:
5 (a) promoting foster parenting;
6 (b) providing foster homes, residential care, group homes, or temporary group shelters for children;
7 (c) recruiting foster parents;
8 (d) placing children in foster homes;
9 (e) licensing or certifying foster homes;
10 (f)(a) promoting adoption or recruiting adoptive parents;
11 (g)(b) assisting adoptions or supporting adoptive families;
12 (h)(c) performing or assisting home studies;
13 (i) assisting kinship guardianships or kinship caregivers;
14 (j)(d) providing family preservation services; and
15 (k)(e) providing family support services; and
16 (l) providing temporary family reunification services.
17 (2) "Discriminatory action" means an action taken by the state government to:
18 (a) alter in any way the tax treatment of, cause any tax, penalty, or payment to be assessed
19 against, or deny, delay, revoke, or otherwise make unavailable an exemption from taxation to a person;
20 (b) disallow, deny, or otherwise make unavailable a deduction for state tax purposes of any
21 charitable contribution made to or by a person;
22 (c) withhold, reduce, exclude, terminate, materially alter the terms or conditions of, or otherwise
23 make unavailable or deny:
24 (i) any state grant, contract, subcontract, cooperative agreement, guarantee, loan, scholarship, or
25 other similar benefit from or to a person;
26 (ii) any entitlement or benefit under a state benefit program from or to a person; or
27 (iii) any license, certification, accreditation, custody award or agreement, diploma, grade,

3 (d) a person acting under color of state law; or
4 (e) a private person suing under or attempting to enforce a law, rule, or regulation adopted by the
5 state or a political subdivision of the state.

7 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Discriminatory acts prohibited in adoption or foster care services.
8 (1) The state government may not take a discriminatory action against a person that advertises, provides, or
9 facilitates adoption or foster care services wholly or partially on the basis that the person:
10 (a) has provided or declines to provide an adoption or foster care service or related service based
11 on or in a manner consistent with the person's sincerely held religious belief; or
12 (b) maintains policies and procedures in accordance with the person's sincerely held religious
13 belief.
14 (2) (a) Except as provided, in subsection (2)(b), the state government may not take a
15 discriminatory action against a person whom the state grants custody of a foster or adoptive child or who seeks
16 from the state the custody of a foster or adoptive child wholly or partially on the basis that the person guides,
17 instructs, or raises a child or intends to guide, instruct, or raise a child based on or in a manner consistent with
18 the person's sincerely held religious belief.
19 (b)(2) The state government may shall consider whether a person shares the same religion or faith
20 tradition as a foster or an adoptive child when considering placement of the child in order to prioritize placement
21 with a person of the same religion or faith tradition.
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Remedies -- statute of limitations. (1) A person may assert a violation
24 of [section 2] as a claim against the state government in a judicial or administrative proceeding or as a defense
25 in a judicial or administrative proceeding without regard to whether the proceeding is brought by or in the name
26 of the state government, a private person, or another party.
27 (2) An action alleging a violation of [section 2]:

11 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Construction. (1) [Sections 1 through 4] must be construed in favor of a
12 broad protection of free exercise of religious beliefs to the maximum extent permitted by the state and federal
13 constitutions.
14 (2) The protection of free exercise of religious beliefs afforded by [sections 1 through 4] are in
15 addition to the protections provided under federal law, state law, and the state and federal constitutions.
16 Nothing in [sections 1 through 4] may be construed to:
17 (a) preempt or repeal any state or local law that is equally or more protective of free exercise of
18 religious beliefs;
19 (b) narrow the meaning or application of any state or local law protecting free exercise of religious
20 beliefs; or
21 (c) prevent the state government from providing, either directly or through an individual or entity
22 not seeking protection under [sections 1 through 4] any benefit or service authorized under state law.

24 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 4] are intended to be
25 codified as a new part in Title 42, chapter 7, and the provisions of Title 42, chapter 7, apply to [sections 1
26 through 4].
