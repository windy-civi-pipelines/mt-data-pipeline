69th Legislature 2025 HB0084.2
2 INTRODUCED BY S. GIST
3 BY REQUEST OF THE DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING FIRE MANAGEMENT LAWS; ESTABLISHING THE
6 PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION AND LIABILITY ACT; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS;
7 PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; LIMITING THE LIABILITY OF CERTIFIED PRESCRIBED FIRE
8 MANAGERS; PROVIDING FOR APPLICABILITY AND ENFORCEMENT OF THE ACT; CREATING A FUND;
9 REQUIRING A PERMIT BEFORE A PRESCRIBED FIRE MAY BE IGNITED OR SET; AMENDING SECTIONS
10 50-63-102, 50-63-103, 76-13-121, AND 76-13-122, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE
11 DATES.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 6 8] may be cited as the "Prescribed Fire
16 Manager Certification and Liability Act".
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purposes of [sections 1 through 6 8] are to establish a
19 prescribed fire manager certification program and to establish a contingent liability standard.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Definitions. For the purposes of [sections 1 through 6 8], the following
22 definitions apply:
23 (1) "Certified prescribed fire manager" means a person who has successfully completed a
24 prescribed fire manager certification program approved by the department and who is in good standing with the
25 department.
26 (2) "Department" means the department of natural resources and conservation provided for in 2-
27 15-3301.
28 (3) "Prescribed fire" means an intentionally set fire that meets specific predefined land

- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0084.2
1 management objectives and that is done under specific weather conditions, in accordance with applicable laws,
2 rules, and policies.
3
4 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Prescribed fire manager certification program -- rulemaking. (1) The
5 department may create a prescribed fire manager certification program. The program must include training on
6 all relevant aspects of prescribed fire in the state, including but not limited to the following:
7 (a) applicable laws and rules;
8 (b) safety planning and management;
9 (c) weather;
10 (d) prescribed fire behavior, complexity analysis, and techniques;
11 (e) smoke management;
12 (f) prescribed fire burn plan requirements and standards;
13 (g) public relations;
14 (h) prescribed fire burn permitting; and
15 (i) contingencies.
16 (2) The department may adopt rules to establish training requirements, fees, and standards for the
17 program IMPLEMENT THE PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION PROGRAM, INCLUDING:
18 (A) TRAINING REQUIREMENTS;
19 (B) CERTIFICATION STANDARDS;
20 (C) RECIPROCITY REQUIREMENTS FOR PRESCRIBED FIRE CERTIFICATIONS FROM OTHER STATES AND
21 ORGANIZATIONS; AND
22 (D) FEES.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Liability. A certified prescribed fire manager or a landowner or
25 landowner's agent using a certified prescribed fire manager who conducts a prescribed fire in compliance with
26 [sections 1 through 6 8] and the rules adopted by the department is not liable for injury to or destruction of
27 property arising from a wildfire, except to the extent evidence demonstrates that:
28 (1) an action or omission of the certified prescribed fire manager, landowner, or landowner's agent
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 84
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0084.2
1 constituted negligence or a higher degree of fault; and
2 (2) the action or omission caused or contributed to the cause of the wildfire or caused or
3 contributed to the wildfire spreading.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Applicability -- enforcement. (1) [Sections 1 through 6 8] may not be
6 construed as requiring certification as a prescribed fire manager to conduct burning operations on one's own
7 property or on the property of another person with the person's permission if the person complies with
8 applicable laws and rules related to prescribed fire and burning.
9 (2) The department may revoke a person's prescribed fire manager certification if the person
10 violates the standards established in [sections 1 through 6 8] or rules adopted by the department.
11
12 NEW SECTION. SECTION 7. PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT. (1)
13 THERE IS A PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT WITHIN THE STATE SPECIAL
14 REVENUE FUND ESTABLISHED IN 17-2-102.
15 (2) THERE MUST BE DEPOSITED IN THE ACCOUNT MONEY RECEIVED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN THE FORM OF
16 GRANTS, GIFTS, TRANSFERS, BEQUESTS, DONATIONS, AND APPROPRIATIONS FROM ANY SOURCE INTENDED TO BE USED
17 FOR THE PURPOSES OF [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 8].
18 (3) MONEY DEPOSITED IN THE ACCOUNT MAY NOT BE USED FOR ANY PURPOSES OTHER THAN FOR THE
19 DEVELOPMENT AND ADMINISTRATION OF THE PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION PROGRAM.
20
21 NEW SECTION. SECTION 8. DEPARTMENT STUDY OF PRESCRIBED FIRE LIABILITY. (1) THE DEPARTMENT
22 SHALL CONDUCT A STUDY FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF A PRESCRIBED FIRE CLAIMS FUND.
23 (2) THE STUDY MUST EXAMINE:
24 (A) THE POTENTIAL USE OF THE FUND TO SUPPORT COVERAGE FOR PROPERTY DAMAGE FROM AN ESCAPED
25 PRESCRIBED FIRE CONDUCTED BY A CERTIFIED PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER; AND
26 (B) ECONOMIC DAMAGE FROM AN ESCAPED PRESCRIBED FIRE CONDUCTED BY A CERTIFIED PRESCRIBED
27 FIRE MANAGER, INCLUDING LOSS OF TIME, RESOURCES, CROPS, FENCING, AND AGRICULTURAL IMPROVEMENTS.
28

69th Legislature 2025 HB0084.2
1 Section 9. Section 50-63-102, MCA, is amended to read:
2 "50-63-102. Civil penalty for setting or leaving fire causing damage. A Except as provided in
3 [section 5], a person who sets or leaves a fire that spreads and damages or destroys property of any kind not
4 belonging to the person is subject to a civil penalty of not less than $50 or more than $500."
5
6 Section 10. Section 50-63-103, MCA, is amended to read:
7 "50-63-103. Liability of offender for damages and costs. Except as provided in 50-63-104 and
8 [section 5], a person who sets or leaves a fire that spreads and damages or destroys property of any kind not
9 belonging to the person is liable for all damages caused by the fire, and an owner of property damaged or
10 destroyed by the fire may maintain a civil suit for the purpose of recovering damages. A person who sets or
11 leaves a fire that threatens to spread and damage or destroy property is liable for all costs and expenses
12 incurred, including but not limited to expenses incurred in investigation of the fire and administration of fire
13 suppression, by the state of Montana, by any forestry association, or by any person extinguishing or preventing
14 the spread of the fire."
15
16 Section 11. Section 76-13-121, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "76-13-121. Permit for burning required. (1) (a) A person may not conduct a prescribed fire, as
18 defined in [section 3], without an official written permit to ignite or set the prescribed fire from the recognized
19 agency for that protection area.
20 (b) During the wildfire season or an expansion of the wildfire season, a person may not ignite or
21 set a fire, including a slash-burning fire, land-clearing fire, debris-burning fire, or, except as provided in
22 subsection (2), an open fire without an official written permit to ignite or set the fire from the recognized agency
23 for that protection area.
24 (2) (a) If no restrictions are in place, a permit is not needed for recreational fires measuring less
25 than 48 inches in diameter that are surrounded by a nonflammable area or structure and for which a suitable
26 source of extinguishing the fire is available.
27 (b) A recreational fire may not be ignited if special restrictions prohibiting recreational fires have
28 been established by an authority having jurisdiction."
- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 84
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0084.2
1
2 Section 12. Section 76-13-122, MCA, is amended to read:
3 "76-13-122. Failure to comply with permit. A person to whom a written permit is issued to set or
4 ignite a fire shall comply strictly with the permit. A Except as provided in [section 5], a person who fails to
5 comply with the permit, leaves the fire unattended, leaves the fire before it is totally extinguished, or negligently
6 allows the fire to spread from or beyond the burning area defined by the permit is subject to the penalty
7 provided in 50-63-102 and is subject to the provisions of 50-63-103. The department shall prescribe the form
8 and substance of the permit."
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 13. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 6 8] are intended to be
11 codified as a new part in Title 76, chapter 13, and the provisions of Title 76, chapter 13, apply to [sections 1
12 through 6 8].
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 14. Effective date -- CONTINGENCY. (1) [This act] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN
15 SUBSECTION (2), [THIS ACT] is effective January 1, 2026.
16 (2) [SECTION 5] IS EFFECTIVE ON THE LATER OF JANUARY 1, 2026, OR THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF A
17 LEGISLATIVE APPROPRIATION OF FUNDS TO THE PRESCRIBED FIRE MANAGER CERTIFICATION SPECIAL REVENUE
18 ACCOUNT IN [SECTION 7]. THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SUBMIT CERTIFICATION OF THE APPROPRIATION TO THE CODE
19 COMMISSIONER WITHIN 15 DAYS OF THE OCCURRENCE OF THE CONTINGENCY.
20 - END -
