69th Legislature 2025 HB 350.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. HINKLE, L. SCHUBERT, M. NIKOLAKAKOS, M. VINTON, S. KLAKKEN, G.
3 OVERSTREET, T. SHARP, E. BYRNE, L. BENNETT, C. SCHOMER, C. COCHRAN, M. THIEL, T. MILLETT,
4 T. MANZELLA, D. ZOLNIKOV, D. EMRICH, J. FULLER, C. HINKLE, L. JONES, G. LAMMERS, M. REGIER, S.
5 FITZPATRICK, C. GLIMM, R. MINER, J. SCHILLINGER, G. OBLANDER, J. ETCHART, L. DEMING, F. NAVE,
6 J. GILLETTE, L. BREWSTER, T. FALK, P. FIELDER, G. HERTZ, G. KMETZ, F. MANDEVILLE, B. MERCER,
7 B. MITCHELL, M. NOLAND, G. PARRY, J. TREBAS, Z. WIRTH, K. BOGNER, B. PHALEN
8
9 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING THE ENFORCEMENT OF POLICIES ISSUED BY
10 CERTAIN INTERGOVERNMENTAL ENTITIES IN THE STATE; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE
11 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Prohibition against enforcement of or reliance on requirements or
16 mandates issued by certain intergovernmental entities. (1) The world health organization, the united
17 nations, and the world economic forum have no jurisdiction in this state. The state and its political subdivisions,
18 including but not limited to any county, city, municipal corporation, consolidated city-county, school district,
19 special improvement or taxing district, or other political subdivision or public corporation, or any entity created
20 by agreement between two or more political subdivisions, may not be compelled to engage in the enforcement
21 of, or any collaboration with the enforcement of, any requirements or mandates issued by the world health
22 organization, the united nations, or the world economic forum.
23 (2) The state and its political subdivisions may not expend public funds or allocate public resources
24 for the enforcement of any requirement or mandates issued by the world health organization, the united
25 nations, or the world economic forum.
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as a new
28 part of Title 2, chapter 1, and the provisions of Title 2, chapter 1, apply to [section 1].
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 350

2 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
3 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
4 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
5
6 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
