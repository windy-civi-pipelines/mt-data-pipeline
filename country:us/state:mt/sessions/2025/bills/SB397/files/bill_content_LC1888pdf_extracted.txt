69th Legislature 2025 LC 1888
2 INTRODUCED BY _________________________________________________
(Primary Sponsor)
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE APPRAISAL PROCESS FOR CENTRALLY
5 ASSESSED PROPERTY; REQUIRING A QUALIFIED INDEPENDENT APPRAISER WHEN THE
6 DEPARTMENT AND THE PROPERTY OWNER DO NOT AGREE ON HIRING A PARTICULAR
7 INDEPENDENT APPRAISER; AMENDING SECTION 15-23-102, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED
8 EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 15-23-102, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "15-23-102. Independent appraisal option. (1) The department of revenue may have property
14 subject to the provisions of this chapter assessed by a qualified independent appraiser when both the
15 department and the owner of the property subject to the assessment agree in writing:
16 (a) on a particular independent appraiser to do an appraisal;
17 (b) to share the costs of the independent appraisal; and
18 (c) to accept the results of the appraisal.
19 (2) If the department and the owner of the property subject to the assessment do not agree in
20 writing to have a qualified independent appraiser assess the property, the department shall select a qualified
21 independent appraiser and pay the costs of the appraisal. The department shall consider the results of the
22 independent appraisal and may accept the results or modify the results. If the department modifies the results,
23 a copy of the appraisal before the modification must be provided to the owner of the property.
24 (2) (3) Appeals from the decision of the department are subject to mediation under 15-1-212 and may
25 be taken to the Montana tax appeal board."
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Effective date. [This act] is effective January 1, 2026.
28

****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 1888
1 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Applicability. [This act] applies to property tax years beginning after
2 December 31, 2025.
