6 A JOINT RESOLUTION OF THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF
7 MONTANA REQUESTING THE FEDERAL GOVERNMENT TO INCREASE THE HOURS OF SERVICE AT
8 MONTANA-CANADA CUSTOMS PORTS OF ENTRY.
9
10 WHEREAS, the U.S. Customs and Border Protection Agency has reduced the hours of service at
11 several Montana-Canada customs ports of entry; and
12 WHEREAS, the hours of service reductions have impeded commerce and trade between Montana and
13 Canada as well as the free flow of cross-border travel for business, leisure, competitive sports, and the
14 maintenance of family ties; and
15 WHEREAS, Montana and Canada have a trading relationship that enhances the economic viability of
16 communities on both sides of the border, including $1.2 billion in goods and services exported from Montana to
17 Canada annually; and
18 WHEREAS, Canada is Montana's number one export market, and Montana sells more goods to
19 Canada than to its next 20 largest foreign markets combined; and
20 WHEREAS, Canada has been America's most trusted ally and its friendliest and largest trading partner,
21 with bilateral trade of $2.7 billion a day and over 300,000 people crossing the border each day.
22
23 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF
24 THE STATE OF MONTANA:
25 That the 69th Montana Legislature, representing all citizens of Montana, requests the federal
26 government increase the hours of service at Montana-Canada customs ports of entry to enhance trade and
27 allow for increased cross-border travel.
28 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Secretary of State send copies of this resolution to the

1 President of the United States and to the Montana Congressional Delegation.
