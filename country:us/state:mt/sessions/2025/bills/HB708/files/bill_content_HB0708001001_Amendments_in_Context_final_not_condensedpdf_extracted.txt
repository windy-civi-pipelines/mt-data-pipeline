4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ALLOWING ALL PERSONS SERVING IN A RESERVE
5 COMPONENT TO BE ELIGIBLE FOR CERTAIN COMMERCIAL DRIVER'S LICENSE WAIVERS; AND
6 AMENDING SECTION 61-5-123, MCA.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 Section 1. Section 61-5-123, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "61-5-123. Waiver of skills test or knowledge test related to military commercial motor vehicles
12 experience -- definition. (1) As used in this section, "current or former military service member" means a
13 person:
14 (a) honorably discharged from the armed forces of the United States;
15 (b) currently serving in the armed forces of the United States;
16 (c) serving full-time in a reserve component, as defined in 37-1-138; or
17 (d) honorably discharged from the reserve component after serving full-time in the reserve
18 component.
19 (2) The department may waive the skills test, knowledge test, or both, required for a commercial
20 driver's license if an applicant is a current or former military service member and meets the conditions in
21 subsection (3), (4), or (5).
22 (3) A current or former military service member applying for waiver of the skills test shall:
23 (a) certify and provide evidence that the member:
24 (i) is or was regularly employed within the last year in a military position requiring operation of a
25 commercial motor vehicle;
26 (ii) was exempted from the commercial driver's license requirements in 61-8-803; and
27 (iii) was operating, for at least 2 years immediately preceding separation from the military, a vehicle

2 (b) certify that during the 2-year period immediately prior to applying for a commercial driver's
3 license, the member:
4 (i) has not simultaneously held more than one civilian license;
5 (ii) has not had any license suspended, revoked, or cancelled;
6 (iii) has not had any convictions for any type of motor vehicle for the disqualifying offenses
7 contained in 61-8-802;

9 motor vehicle traffic control, other than a parking violation, arising in connection with any traffic accident; and
10 (v) has no record of an accident in which the current or former military service member was at
11 fault.
12 (4) A current or former military service member applying for waiver of the knowledge test shall
13 certify and provide evidence that during the 1-year period immediately prior to the application, the member:
14 (a) is or was regularly employed and designated as a:
15 (i) motor transport operator--88M (Army);
16 (ii) PATRIOT launching station operator--14T (Army);
17 (iii) fueler--92F (Army);
18 (iv) vehicle operator--2T1 (Air Force);
19 (v) fueler--2F0 (Air Force);
20 (vi) pavement and construction equipment operator--3E2 (Air Force);
21 (vii) motor vehicle operator--3531 (Marine Corps); or
22 (viii) equipment operator--E.O. (Navy);
23 (b) is operating a vehicle representative of the commercial motor vehicle type the driver applicant
24 expects to operate on separation from the military or operated a similar vehicle type immediately preceding
25 separation from the military;
26 (c) has not simultaneously held more than one civilian license;
27 (d) has not had any license suspended, revoked, or cancelled;

1 (e) has not had any convictions for any type of motor vehicle for the disqualifying offenses

4 contained in 61-8-803;

7 (h) has no record of an accident in which the current or former military service member was at
8 fault.
9 (5) A current or former military service member applying for waiver of the applicable skills and
10 knowledge tests for a passenger, tank vehicle, or hazardous materials endorsement shall certify and provide
11 evidence that during the 1-year period immediately prior to the application, the member:
12 (a) is or was regularly employed in a military position requiring:
13 (i) operation of a passenger commercial motor vehicle if requesting waiver of the skills and
14 knowledge test for a passenger endorsement;
15 (ii) operation of a tank vehicle if requesting waiver of the skills and knowledge test for a tank
16 vehicle endorsement; or
17 (iii) transportation of hazardous materials if requesting waiver of the skills and knowledge test for a
18 hazardous materials endorsement;
19 (b) has not simultaneously held more than one civilian license;
20 (c) has not had any convictions for any type of motor vehicle for the disqualifying offenses
21 contained in 61-8-802;
22 (d) has not had more than one conviction for any type of motor vehicle for serious traffic violations
23 contained in 61-8-803;
24 (e) has not had any convictions for a violation of federal, military, state, or local law relating to
25 motor vehicle traffic control, other than a parking violation, arising in connection with any traffic accident; and
26 (f) has no record of an accident in which the current or former military service member was at
27 fault."
