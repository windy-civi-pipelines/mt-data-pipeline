27 9, 2020, and accepted for filing.

11 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Notice of changes. A vapor product manufacturer required to submit a

18 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Directory -- publication -- updates. (1) Starting January 1, 2026, the
19 attorney general shall maintain and make publicly available on the attorney general's official website a directory
20 that lists all vapor product manufacturers and all vapor products containing nicotine, such as brand names,
21 product names, categories, including but not limited to a disposable vapor product, e-liquid, e-liquid cartridge, e-

23 attorney general.

25 to add or remove a vapor product manufacturer or vapor product containing nicotine to keep the directory in
26 conformity with the requirements of this chapter. The attorney general shall establish a process to provide
27 licensed retailers, wholesalers, importers, and other relevant parties notice of the initial publication of the
28 directory and changes made to the directory in the prior month.

25 makes the directory available for inspection on the attorney general's public website to remove those vapor
26 products containing nicotine intended for retail sale in the state from the retailer, wholesaler, or importer's

7 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Penalties. The following penalties apply to violations of this chapter:
8 (1) A retailer, wholesaler, or importer who sells or offers for sale a vapor product containing
9 nicotine for retail sale in this state or to a consumer in this state that is not included in the directory shall be
10 subject to a civil penalty of $250 for each individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in
11 violation of this chapter.
12 (a) For a second violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is $500 for each
13 individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a licensee's license
14 must be suspended for 1 month.
15 (b) For a third violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is $750 for each
16 individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a licensee's license
17 must be suspended for 3 months.
18 (c) For a fourth or subsequent violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is
19 $1,000 for each individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a
20 licensee's license must be suspended for 1 year.
21 (2) A vapor product manufacturer whose vapor products containing nicotine are not listed in the
22 directory and who causes the vapor products containing nicotine that are not listed to be sold for retail sale in
23 this state or to a consumer in this state, whether directly or through a retailer, wholesaler, importer, or similar
24 intermediary or intermediaries, is subject to a civil penalty of $10,000 for each individual vapor product
25 containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter. In addition, any vapor product manufacturer that
26 falsely represents any information required by a certification form is guilty of a misdemeanor for each false
27 representation.
28 (3) In an action to enforce this chapter, the state is entitled to recover costs, including the costs of

5 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Agent for service of process. (1) A nonresident or foreign vapor
6 product manufacturer that has not registered to do business in the state as a foreign corporation or business
7 entity shall, as a condition precedent to having its vapor products containing nicotine on the directory, appoint,
8 and continually engage without interruption the services of an agent in this state to act as an agent for the
9 service of process on whom all process and any action or proceeding against it concerning or arising out of the
10 enforcement of this chapter may be served in any manner authorized by law. The service constitutes legal and
11 valid service of process on the vapor product manufacturer. The vapor product manufacturer shall provide the
12 name, address, phone number, and proof of the appointment and availability of the agent to the satisfaction of
13 the attorney general.
14 (2) The vapor product manufacturer shall provide notice to the attorney general at least 30 days
15 prior to termination of the authority of an agent and shall further provide proof to the satisfaction of the attorney
16 general of the appointment of a new agent no less than 5 days prior to the termination of an existing agent
17 appointment. If an agent terminates an agency appointment, the vapor product manufacturer shall notify the
18 attorney general of the termination within 5 days and include proof to the satisfaction of the attorney general of
19 the appointment of a new agent.
