6 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Penalties. The following penalties apply to violations of this chapter:
7 (1) A retailer, wholesaler, or importer who sells or offers for sale a vapor product containing
8 nicotine for retail sale in this state or to a consumer in this state that is not included in the directory shall be
9 subject to a civil penalty of $250 for each individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in
10 violation of this chapter.
11 (a) For a second violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is $500 for each
12 individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a licensee's license
13 must be suspended for 1 month.
14 (b) For a third violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is $750 for each
15 individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a licensee's license
16 must be suspended for 3 months.
17 (c) For a fourth or subsequent violation of this type within a 12-month period, the civil penalty is
18 $1,000 for each individual vapor product containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter and a
19 licensee's license must be suspended for 1 year.
20 (2) A vapor product manufacturer whose vapor products containing nicotine are not listed in the
21 directory and who causes the vapor products containing nicotine that are not listed to be sold for retail sale in
22 this state or to a consumer in this state, whether directly or through a retailer, wholesaler, importer, or similar
23 intermediary or intermediaries, is subject to a civil penalty of $10,000 for each individual vapor product
24 containing nicotine offered for sale in violation of this chapter. In addition, any vapor product manufacturer that
25 falsely represents any information required by a certification form is guilty of a misdemeanor for each false
26 representation.
27 (3) In an action to enforce this chapter, the state is entitled to recover costs, including the costs of
28 investigation, expert witness fees, and reasonable attorney fees.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Agent for service of process. (1) A nonresident or foreign vapor
5 product manufacturer that has not registered to do business in the state as a foreign corporation or business
6 entity shall, as a condition precedent to having its vapor products containing nicotine on the directory, appoint,
7 and continually engage without interruption the services of an agent in this state to act as an agent for the
8 service of process on whom all process and any action or proceeding against it concerning or arising out of the
9 enforcement of this chapter may be served in any manner authorized by law. The service constitutes legal and
10 valid service of process on the vapor product manufacturer. The vapor product manufacturer shall provide the
11 name, address, phone number, and proof of the appointment and availability of the agent to the satisfaction of
12 the attorney general.
13 (2) The vapor product manufacturer shall provide notice to the attorney general at least 30 days
14 prior to termination of the authority of an agent and shall further provide proof to the satisfaction of the attorney
15 general of the appointment of a new agent no less than 5 days prior to the termination of an existing agent
16 appointment. If an agent terminates an agency appointment, the vapor product manufacturer shall notify the
17 attorney general of the termination within 5 days and include proof to the satisfaction of the attorney general of
18 the appointment of a new agent.
