4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING WIND AND SOLAR DECOMMISSIONING
5 AND BONDING LAWS; REQUIRING DECOMMISSIONING BONDING PRIOR TO THE CONSTRUCTION
6 AND COMMENCEMENT OF COMMERCIAL OPERATION; REVISING PLAN AND BOND REQUIREMENTS
7 AND TIMELINES; ESTABLISHING PENALTIES; AMENDING SECTIONS 75-26-301, 75-26-304, 75-26-308,
8 AND 75-26-310, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”

12 Section 1. Section 75-26-301, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "75-26-301. Definitions. As used in this part, unless the context requires otherwise, the following
14 definitions apply:
15 (1) "Board" means the board of environmental review provided for in 2-15-3502.
16 (2) "Commission" means the public service commission provided for in 2-15-2602.
17 (2)(3) "Decommission" or "decommissioning" means:
18 (a) except as provided in 75-26-304(2) [section 2(4)], the removal of buildings, cabling, electrical
19 components, roads, or any other facilities associated with a wind generation or solar facility;
20 (b) except as provided in 75-26-304(2) [section 2(4)], reclamation of surface lands to the previous
21 grade and to comparable productivity in order to prevent adverse hydrologic effects; and
22 (c) (i) the removal of the solar facility after the end of the facility's useful life or abandonment; or
23 (ii) the removal of an aboveground wind turbine tower after the end of a wind generation facility's
24 useful life or abandonment.
25 (3)(4) "Department" means the department of environmental quality provided for in 2-15-3501.
26 (4)(5) "Owner" means a person who owns a wind generation or solar facility used for the generation
27 of electricity.
28 (5)(6) "Person" means any individual, firm, partnership, company, association, corporation, city, town,

1 or local governmental government entity or any other state, federal, or private entity, whether organized for
2 profit or not.
3 (6)(7) "Repurposed" means having made a significant investment in an existing wind generation or
4 solar facility to extend the useful life of the facility by more than 5 years.
5 (7)(8) "Solar facility" means an installation or combination of solar panels or plates, including a
6 canopy or array, that captures and converts solar radiation to produce electricity and includes flat plate,
7 focusing solar collectors, or photovoltaic solar cells that:
8 (a) has a nameplate capacity greater than or equal to 2 megawatts; and
9 (b) produces electricity that is not consumed on the premises of the solar facility or on land
10 immediately adjacent to the premises of the solar facility.
11 (8)(9) "Wind generation facility" means any combination of a physically connected wind turbine or
12 turbines, associated prime movers, and other associated property, including appurtenant land and
13 improvements and personal property, that are normally operated together to produce electric power from wind
14 and that have a nameplate capacity greater than or equal to 25 megawatts."

16 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Decommissioning period -- requirements. (1) The owner of a wind
17 generation or solar facility shall begin decommissioning within 12 months after abandonment or the end of its
18 useful life. Decommissioning must be completed within 24 months after abandonment or the end of its useful
19 life unless the department approves a plan specifying the steps and schedules to return the facility to operation.
20 (2) Decommissioning the facility includes but is not limited to:
21 (a) dismantling, removal, and disposal of all panels, plates, towers, turbine generators,
22 transformers, fencing, overhead cables, inverters, substations and any other associated equipment;
23 (b) removal of all underground cabling;
24 (c) removal of all cement, foundations, buildings, and ancillary equipment; and
25 (d) site restoration and reclamation to the approximate original topography that existed prior to the
26 construction of the facility with topsoil respread over the disturbed areas at a depth similar to that in existence
27 prior to the disturbance.
28 (3) If a decommissioning plan includes in-state disposal, the disposal of materials is subject to the

5 reclamation of surface lands, or both, decommissioning does not include removal, plans for reclamation, or
