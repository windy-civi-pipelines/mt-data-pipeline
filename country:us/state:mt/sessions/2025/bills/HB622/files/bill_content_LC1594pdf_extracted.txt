4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING PROTECTION ORDER LAWS;
5 PROVIDING A PROCEDURE FOR OBTAINING AN EXTREME RISK ORDER OF PROTECTION AND EX
6 PARTE EXTREME RISK ORDER OF PROTECTION; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING THAT
7 FIREARMS IN THE POSSESSION OF A PERSON AGAINST WHOM AN EXTREME RISK PROTECTIVE
8 ORDER IS ISSUED MUST BE SURRENDERED AND A PERMIT FOR CARRYING A CONCEALED WEAPON
9 MUST BE REVOKED; PROVIDING PENALTIES; PROVIDING CIRCUMSTANCES IN WHICH FIREARMS
10 ARE RETURNED; PROVIDING FOR DEVELOPMENT OF EDUCATIONAL MATERIALS; AND REQUIRING
11 ANNUAL REPORTS.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 12], unless the context
16 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
17 (1) "Crime of domestic violence" means any felony offense involving the use or attempted use of
18 physical force or the use or threatened use of a weapon as defined in 45-2-101 that is committed against a
19 partner or family member or any misdemeanor offense that has as an element of the use or attempted use of
20 physical force or the threatened use of a weapon that is committed against a partner or family member.
21 (2) "Extreme risk order of protection" means an ex parte temporary order or a final order granted
22 under [sections 1 through 12].
23 (3) "Family member" has the same meaning as provided in 45-5-206.
24 (4) "Partner" has the same meaning as provided in 45-5-206.
25 (5) "Petitioner" means a person who petitions for an order under [sections 1 through 12].
26 (6) "Respondent" means a person who is the subject of and identified as the named respondent in
27 a petition filed under [sections 1 through 12].

1 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Extreme risk order of protection established -- petition for extreme
2 risk order of protection. (1) An extreme risk order of protection is established for the purpose of providing a
3 court procedure by which law enforcement officers and partners or family members may obtain a court order
4 that prohibits a person from having control or custody of, purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm
5 because the person poses a risk of causing personal injury to self or others.
6 (2) A petitioner may seek an extreme risk order of protection from a district court.
7 (3) A petition for an extreme risk order of protection may be filed by:
8 (a) a partner or family member of the respondent; or
9 (b) a law enforcement officer or agency.
10 (4) An action under [sections 1 through 12] must be filed in the county where the petitioner resides
11 or the county where the respondent resides.
12 (5) A petition must:
13 (a) allege that the respondent poses a significant risk of causing personal injury to the
14 respondent's self or others by having custody or control of, purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm and
15 must be accompanied by an affidavit made under oath stating the specific statements or actions made by the
16 respondent or facts that give rise to a reasonable fear of future acts of violence by the respondent;
17 (b) identify the number, type, and location of any firearms the petitioner believes to be in the
18 respondent's current ownership, possession, custody, or control;
19 (c) identify whether there is a known existing order of protection governing the respondent under
20 Title 40, chapter 15, part 2; and
21 (d) identify whether there is a pending lawsuit, complaint, petition, or other action between the
22 parties to the petition under the laws of the state.
23 (6) The clerk of district court of the judicial district in which the petition is filed or the office of court
24 administrator shall verify the terms of any existing order governing the parties. The court in which the petition is
25 filed may not delay granting relief because of the existence of a pending action between the parties or the
26 necessity of verifying the terms of an existing order. A petition for an extreme risk order of protection may be
27 granted whether or not there is a pending action between the parties.
28 (7) If the petitioner is a law enforcement officer or agency, the petitioner shall make a good faith

24 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Hearings -- issuance of extreme risk protection order. (1) (a) Upon
25 receipt of the petition, the court shall order a hearing to be held no later than 14 days from the date of the order
26 and shall issue a notice of hearing to the parties.
27 (b) The court may schedule a hearing by telephone or videoconference to reasonably
28 accommodate a disability, or in exceptional circumstances to protect a petitioner from potential harm. The court

1 shall require assurances of the petitioner's identity before conducting a hearing by telephone or

3 (c) The clerk of court shall provide a copy of the notice of hearing and petition within 48 hours to
4 the appropriate law enforcement agency for service on the respondent under [section 5].
5 (d) (i) The court may, as provided in [section 4], issue an ex parte extreme risk order of protection
6 before the hearing ordered under this section.
7 (ii) An ex parte order must be served concurrently with the notice of hearing and petition.
8 (iii) Upon hearing the matter, if the court finds good cause to believe that the respondent poses a
9 significant risk of causing personal injury to the respondent's self or others by having custody or control of,
10 purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm, the court shall issue an extreme risk order of protection for a
11 period of 1 year.
12 (2) In determining whether grounds for an extreme risk order of protection exist, the court may
13 consider any relevant evidence, including but not limited to any of the following:
14 (a) a recent act or threat of violence by the respondent against the respondent's self or others,
15 whether or not the violence or threat of violence involved a firearm;
16 (b) a pattern of acts or threats of violence by the respondent within the past 12 months, including
17 but not limited to acts or threats of violence by the respondent against the respondent's self or others;
18 (c) any serious mental health issues of the respondent;
19 (d) a violation by the respondent of an order of protection issued under 40-15-202;
20 (e) a previous or existing extreme risk order of protection issued against the respondent;
21 (f) a violation of a previous or existing extreme risk order of protection issued against the
22 respondent;
23 (g) a conviction of the respondent for a crime that constitutes a crime of domestic violence as
24 defined in [section 1];
25 (h) the respondent's ownership of, access to, or intent to possess a firearm;
26 (i) the unlawful or reckless use, display, or brandishing of a firearm by the respondent;
27 (j) the history of use, attempted use, or threatened use of physical force by the respondent
28 against another person or the respondent's history of stalking another person;

7 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Ex parte extreme risk order of protection. (1) A petitioner may request
8 that an ex parte extreme risk order of protection be issued before a hearing for an extreme risk order of
9 protection, without notice to the respondent, by including in the petition detailed allegations based on personal
10 knowledge that the respondent poses a significant risk of causing personal injury to the respondent's self or
11 others in the near future by having custody or control of, purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm.
12 (2) In considering whether to issue an ex parte extreme risk order of protection under this section,
13 the court shall consider all relevant evidence, including the evidence described in [section 3].
14 (3) If a court finds there is good cause to believe that the respondent poses a significant risk of
15 causing personal injury to the respondent's self or others by having custody or control of, purchasing,
16 possessing, or receiving a firearm if the court does not act immediately, the court shall issue an ex parte
17 extreme risk order of protection.
18 (4) The court shall hold an ex parte extreme risk order of protection hearing in person or by
19 telephone or videoconference on the day the petition is filed or within 48 hours of the filing of the petition.
20 (5) In accordance with [section 3], the court shall schedule a hearing within 14 days after the
21 issuance of an ex parte extreme risk order of protection to determine if a 1-year extreme risk order of protection
22 should be issued under [sections 1 through 12].

24 (a) a statement of the grounds asserted for the order;
25 (b) the date and time the order was issued;
26 (c) the date and time the order expires;
27 (d) the address of the court in which any responsive pleading should be filed;

8 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Termination and renewal of orders. (1) (a) The respondent may submit
9 one written request for a hearing to terminate an extreme risk order of protection issued under [sections 1
10 through 12] during every 12-month period that the order is in effect, starting from the date of the order and
11 continuing through any renewals.
12 (b) Upon receipt of the request for a hearing to terminate an extreme risk order of protection, the
13 court shall set a date for a hearing. Notice of the request must be served on the petitioner in the same manner
14 as provided in [section 5]. The hearing must occur no sooner than 14 days and no later than 30 days from the
15 date of service of the request upon the petitioner.
16 (c) The respondent has the burden of proving that there is no longer good cause to believe that
17 the respondent poses a significant risk of causing personal injury to the respondent's self or others by having
18 custody or control of, purchasing, possessing, or receiving a firearm. The court may consider any relevant
19 evidence, including evidence of the considerations listed in [section 3].
20 (d) If the court finds after the hearing that the respondent has met the burden, the court shall
21 terminate the order.
22 (2) The court shall notify the petitioner of the impending expiration of an extreme risk order of
23 protection. Notice must be received by the petitioner at least 60 days before the date the order expires.
24 (3) (a) A partner or family member of a respondent or a law enforcement officer or agency may by
25 motion request a renewal of an extreme risk order of protection at any time within 60 days before the expiration
26 of the order.
27 (b) (i) Upon receipt of the motion to renew, the court shall order that a hearing be held within 14
28 days.

1 (ii) The court may schedule a hearing by telephone in the manner provided in [section 3].
2 (iii) The respondent must be personally served in the manner provided in [section 5].
3 (c) In determining whether to renew an extreme risk order of protection, the court shall consider all
4 relevant evidence presented by the petitioner and follow the same procedure as provided in [section 3].
5 (d) If the court finds there is continued good cause to believe that there is danger of harm if the

10 (e) An extreme risk order of protection may be renewed for 1 year, subject to termination as
11 provided in subsection (1), or further renewal by order of the court.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Surrender of firearms. (1) Upon issuance of an extreme risk order of
14 protection, including an ex parte extreme risk order of protection, the court shall order the respondent to
15 surrender to the local law enforcement agency or a federally licensed firearms dealer all firearms in the
16 respondent's custody, control, or possession and any concealed weapons permit issued under 45-8-321.
17 (2) (a) The law enforcement officer serving an extreme risk order of protection under [sections 1
18 through 12], including an ex parte extreme risk order of protection, shall request that the respondent
19 immediately surrender all firearms in the respondent's custody, control, or possession and any concealed
20 weapons permit issued under 45-8-321. The law enforcement officer may obtain a search warrant and conduct
21 any search permitted by law for the firearms.
22 (b) The law enforcement officer shall take possession of all firearms belonging to the respondent
23 that are surrendered, in plain view, or discovered pursuant to a lawful search.
24 (c) Alternatively, if personal service by a law enforcement officer is not possible, or not required
25 because the respondent was present at the extreme risk order of protection hearing, the respondent shall
26 surrender the firearms in a safe manner to the control of the local law enforcement agency within 48 hours of
27 being served with the order by alternate service or within 48 hours of the hearing at which the respondent was
28 present.

12 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Reporting of orders. (1) The clerk of court shall enter any extreme risk

25 justice may only expunge orders from the systems that have expired or terminated.
26 (d) Entry into the computer-based criminal intelligence information system constitutes notice to all
27 law enforcement agencies of the existence of the order.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Penalties. (1) A person who files a petition under [sections 1 through
14 12] knowing the information in the petition to be materially false, or with intent to harass the respondent, is guilty
15 of a misdemeanor and shall be fined an amount not to exceed $500 or be imprisoned in the county jail for a
16 term not to exceed 6 months, or both.
17 (2) A person who has custody or control of, purchases, possesses, or receives a firearm with the
18 knowledge that the person is prohibited from doing so by an order issued under [sections 1 through 12] is guilty
19 of a felony and shall be imprisoned in a state prison for not more than 10 years and fined not more than
20 $50,000, or both.
