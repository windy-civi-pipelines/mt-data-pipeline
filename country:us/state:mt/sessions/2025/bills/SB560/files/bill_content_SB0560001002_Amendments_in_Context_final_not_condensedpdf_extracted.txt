4 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Community benefit to equal potential property tax liability -- fee. (1)
5 (a) Each nonprofit hospital shall provide a total annual community benefit that exceeds the prior year’s potential
6 property tax liability the nonprofit hospital would have incurred during the hospital's tax year prior to the year in
7 which the community benefit is provided.
8 (b) The community benefit must be calculated using the net community benefit expenses, net
9 community building expenses, and total bad debt and medicare shortfall amounts listed by the nonprofit
10 hospital on schedule H of internal revenue service form 990.
11 (2) No later than March 31Within 30 days of filing the required forms with the internal revenue
12 service each year, each nonprofit hospital shall provide the department of public health and human services
13 with a copy of schedule H for internal revenue service form 990 filed for the most recent tax year. The
14 department of public health and human services shall determine whether the amount of community benefit
15 reported on schedule H exceeds the statement of potential tax liability provided by the department of revenue
16 pursuant to [section 4].
17 (3) If the amount of community benefit spending does not exceed the amount of the nonprofit
18 hospital's potential tax liability for the prior year, the department of public health and human services shall
19 assess the nonprofit hospital a fee equal to the difference between the potential tax liability for the prior year
20 and the amount of reported community benefit spending.
21 (4) The department of public health and human services shall deposit money assessed pursuant
22 to this section in the special revenue account provided for in [section 3].

3 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Statement of potential tax liability -- rulemaking authority. (1) The
4 department of revenue shall prepare and provide to the department of public health and human services a
5 statement of potential tax liabilityindicating the amount of property taxes a nonprofit hospital would have owed
6 on its real property in the prior tax year if the nonprofit hospital had not been exempt from taxation under 15-6-
7 201.
8 (2) (a) For Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), for the purposes of determining potential tax
9 liability, the department of revenue shall treat nonprofit hospital property as class four commercial property
10 pursuant to 15-6-134. The department of revenue shall calculate the property tax using the prior year estimated
11 taxable value and the prior year mills levied by each taxing jurisdiction in which the nonprofit hospital is located.
12 (b) The department of revenue may not include a property owned by a nonprofit hospital in its
13 calculation to determine potential tax liability if the hospital pays property taxes on that property.
14 (3) If a nonprofit hospital owns real property in multiple counties, the department of revenue shall
15 prepare a single statement that reflects the total potential tax liability of all the nonprofit hospital's real property.
16 (4) The department of revenue shall adopt rules providing a process for a nonprofit hospital to
17 dispute the determination of assessed or taxable value of nonprofit hospital property used to establish the
18 potential tax liability.
19 (5) For the purposes of this section, “potential tax liability” means the amount of property taxes a
20 nonprofit hospital would have owed on its real property in the prior tax year if the nonprofit hospital had not
21 been exempt from taxation under 15-6-201.

10 hospital on schedule H of internal revenue service form 990.

12 service each year, each nonprofit hospital shall provide the department of public health and human services
13 with a copy of schedule H for internal revenue service form 990 filed for the most recent tax year. The
14 department of public health and human services shall determine whether the amount of community benefit
15 reported on schedule H exceeds the statement of potential tax liability provided by the department of revenue
16 pursuant to [section 4].

18 hospital's potential tax liability for the prior year, the department of public health and human services shall

20 and the amount of reported community benefit spending.
