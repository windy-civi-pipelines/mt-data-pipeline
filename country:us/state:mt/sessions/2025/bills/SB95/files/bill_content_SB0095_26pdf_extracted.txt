Section 1:
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0095.2
2 INTRODUCED BY M. YAKAWICH
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ALLOCATING MONEY FOR SUICIDE PREVENTION EFFORTS
5 FOR VETERANS AND SERVICE MEMBERS; ESTABLISHING PURPOSES FOR USE OF THE MONEY; AND
6 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Allocation. (1) There is allocated $500,000 $300,000 from the state
11 special revenue account established in 17-6-603 to the department of public health and human services for the
12 biennium beginning July 1, 2025, for grants made pursuant to 53-21-1101 and 53-21-1111 for suicide
13 prevention activities.
14 (2) The allocation must be used to:
15 (a) implement strategies that are developed, with assistance from the U.S. department of veterans
16 affairs and the federal substance abuse and mental health services administration, as part of the mayor's
17 challenge or the governor's challenge MONTANA DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES' CAMPAIGN
18 to prevent suicide among service members, veterans, and their families;
19 (b) identify and screen service members, veterans, and their families for suicide risk by:
20 (i) establishing screening standards for the identification of suicide risk among service members,
21 veterans, and their families;
22 (ii) educating providers and peers involved in the intake process; and
23 (iii) establishing a review process for identification of service members, veterans, and their families
24 in mortality and data review;
25 (c) promote connectedness and improve care transitions for service members, veterans, and their
26 families by:
27 (i) increasing peers who are trained in suicide prevention to work in connecting those individuals
28 to services; and
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 95

69th Legislature 2025 SB0095.2
1 (ii) developing collaborative policies and protocols across military and civilian agencies to improve
2 continuity of care; and
3 (d) increase lethal means safety and safety planning by:
4 (i) implementing a safe storage campaign statewide;
5 (ii) conducting statewide lethal means safety training events; and
6 (iii) convening a working group to support adoption of clinical practice guidelines for safety
7 planning.
8 (3) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL REPORT ON THE RESULTS OF THE ACTIVITIES DESCRIBED IN THIS SECTION AS
9 PART OF ITS ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REPORT REQUIRED UNDER 2-12-105.
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a
12 copy of [this act] to each federally recognized tribal government in Montana.
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
15
16 NEW SECTION. SECTION 4. TERMINATION. [THIS ACT] TERMINATES JUNE 30, 2027.
17 - END -
