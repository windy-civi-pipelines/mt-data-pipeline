1 SENATE BILL NO. 50
2 INTRODUCED BY D. EMRICH
3 BY REQUEST OF THE CHILDREN, FAMILIES, HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES INTERIM COMMITTEE
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO THE REMOVAL OF A CHILD
6 FROM THE CHILD'S HOME; REQUIRING A WARRANT TO REMOVE A CHILD FROM THE CHILD'S HOME
7 EXCEPT IN EXIGENT CIRCUMSTANCES; REQUIRING AN ABUSE AND NEGLECT PETITION TO BE FILED
8 WITHIN 72 HOURS 5 WORKING DAYS OF THE EMERGENCY REMOVAL OF A CHILD; AND AMENDING
9 SECTIONS 41-3-101, 41-3-102, 41-3-301, AND 41-3-423, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE
10 DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Warrant to remove child. (1) A child protection specialist of the
15 department, a peace officer, or a county attorney may apply, in writing, by telephone, or electronically, on oath
16 or affirmation, to a district court within the state for the issuance of a warrant to remove a child from the child's
17 home and place the child in a protective facility if necessary to prevent the child from being abused or
18 neglected.
19 (2) A warrant may be issued in writing, by telephone, or electronically.
20 (3) If the court finds from the application that there is probable cause that removal is necessary to
21 prevent the child from being abused or neglected, the court shall issue a warrant to remove the child. The
22 warrant must:
23 (a) identify the child to be removed and the person responsible for removing the child;
24 (b) recite the facts on which the conclusion that the child is being abused or neglected or is in
25 danger of being abused or neglected is based; and
26 (c) provide for the placement of the child, pending an emergency protective services hearing.
27 (4) The provisions of 46-5-222 apply when an application for a warrant is made by telephone or
28 electronically or when a warrant is issued by telephone or electronically.

12 Section 6. Section 41-3-423, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "41-3-423. Reasonable efforts required to prevent removal of child or to return -- exemption --
14 findings -- permanency plan. (1) (a) The department shall make reasonable efforts to prevent the necessity of
15 removal of a child from the child's home and to reunify families that have been separated by the state. The
16 application for a warrant to remove a child from the child's home pursuant to [section 1] does not absolve the
17 department from the duty to make reasonable efforts to prevent the necessity of removal.
18 (b) For the purposes of this subsection (1), the term "reasonable efforts" means the department
19 shall in good faith:
20 (i) conduct a comprehensive assessment of the circumstances of the family, with a focus on safe
21 reunification as the most desirable goal. The assessment must be provided to the parents and to counsel for
22 the parents.
23 (ii) identify appropriate services and help the parents overcome barriers, including actively
24 assisting the parents in obtaining appropriate services;
25 (iii) with parental consent, identify and invite the extended family to participate in providing support
26 and services to the family and to participate in family team meetings, permanency planning, and resolution of
27 placement issues;
