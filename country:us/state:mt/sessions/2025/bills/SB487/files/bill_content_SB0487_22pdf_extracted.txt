4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS RELATED TO THE MONTANA END OF WATCH
5 TRUST; REQUIRING APPLICATIONS FOR PAYMENTS FROM THE END OF WATCH TRUST DUE TO
6 CATASTROPHIC INJURY TO INCLUDE A WORKERS' COMPENSATION DETERMINATION OF
7 PERMANENT AND TOTAL DISABILITY; ALLOWING LEGAL CHILDREN OF AN OFFICER TO RECEIVE
8 PAYMENTS AFTER THEY TURN 18 IN CERTAIN CIRCUMSTANCES; PROVIDING RULEMAKING
9 AUTHORITY TO THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE; REVISING DEFINITIONS; AND AMENDING SECTIONS
10 2-15-2040, 2-15-2041, 2-15-2042, 2-15-2043, 2-15-2044, AND 2-15-2045, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
11 IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Administration by department of justice -- rulemaking authority. (1)
16 The department of justice may assess a fee not to exceed 15% of the amount INTEREST AND EARNINGS
17 appropriated to the department FROM THE TRUST for the purposes of administrating the end of watch trust fund.
18 (2) The department may adopt rules for the purposes of executing its obligations under 2-15-2040
19 through 2-15-2045 and [section 2]. This includes BUT IS NOT LIMITED TO rules that create:
20 (a) a process for distributing payments to applicants approved by the board;
21 (b) a process through which the board approves or denies applications for payments distributed in
22 accordance with this section;
23 (c) guidelines for appeal of board decisions;
24 (d) information security and document retention policies;
25 (e) board training requirements; and
26 (f) reporting requirements.
27

8 Section 3. Section 2-15-2040, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "2-15-2040. Definitions. For the purposes of 2-15-2040 through 2-15-2045 and [sections 1 and 2],
10 the following definitions apply:
11 (1) "Board" means the Montana end of watch trust board established in 2-15-2044.
12 (2) "Catastrophic injury" means an A PHYSICAL, BODILY injury directly related to an individual's
13 required employment duties with direct or proximate consequences that renders the individual in need of 24-
14 hour care continuous care to complete basic activities of daily living, permanently incapacitates the individual,
15 and permanently prevents the individual from performing any gainful form of work, and results in a
16 determination of permanent and total disability, as that term is defined in 39-71- 116, at the time of maximum
17 medical improvement.
18 (3) "Department" means the department of justice.
19 (4) "Immediate family" means a law enforcement officer's spouse and legal dependent children
20 under age 18 years of age, including children to whom the law enforcement officer is a legal guardian.
21 (5) "In the line of duty" means as the direct result of an action taken by a law enforcement officer or
22 an activity in which a law enforcement officer participated:
23 (a) as required or authorized by law, rule, regulation, condition of employment, or professional
24 ethics; and
25 (b) in the course and scope of carrying out the law enforcement officer's sworn obligations; or
26 (b) (c) for which compensation is provided by the officer's employer or would have been provided by
27 the officer's employer if the officer had been on duty at the time the action in question was taken.
28 (6) "Law enforcement officer" means:

7 Section 7. Section 2-15-2044, MCA, is amended to read:
8 "2-15-2044. Montana end of watch trust board. (1) There is a volunteer board to oversee the
9 administration of the Montana end of watch trust provided for in 2-15-2041 and to determine the eligibility of
10 applicants seeking funds from the trust. The board is attached to the department for administrative purposes
11 only, as provided in 2-15-121.
12 (2) The board consists of five members appointed by the attorney general, including:
13 (a) a representative of the Montana sheriffs and peace officers association;
14 (b) a representative of the Montana police protective association;
15 (c) a representative of the association of Montana troopers;
16 (d) a representative of the Montana association of chiefs of police; and
17 (e) a representative from the department.
18 (3) The board shall:
19 (a) meet at least once each fiscal year; and
20 (b) act as an advocate for law enforcement officers catastrophically injured in the line of duty and
21 the surviving immediate family members of officers who died in the line of duty; and convene as necessary to
22 review applications and determine the eligibility of applicants. ; AND
23 (c) settle disputes and concerns regarding trust benefits.;
24 (C) REVIEW THE BOARD OF INVESTMENTS ANNUAL REPORT FOR THE FUND.
25 (4) The representatives in subsection (2) must be sworn actors of a participating agency."
