11 CHAPTER 1
12 Administration
13 S10-10. Officers of the Senate. The officers of the Senate include a president, a president pro
14 tempore, a majority leader, a minority leader, and majority and minority whips.
15 S10-20. Term of officers. The term of office for the officers and employees of the Senate established
16 by rule is until the succeeding Legislature is organized. This rule may not be construed to mean the that short-
17 term session staff will be full-time employees during an interim.
18 S10-30. President, President pro tempore, and other officers. (1) The Senate shall, at the
19 beginning of each regular session, and at other times as may be necessary, elect a Senator as President and a
20 Senator as President pro tempore.
21 (2) The Senate shall choose its other officers and is the judge of the elections, returns, and
22 qualifications of the Senators.
23 S10-40. Voting by presiding officer. Any Senator, when acting as presiding officer of the Senate,
24 shall vote as any other Senator.
25 S10-50. Presiding officer and duties. (1) The presiding officer of the Senate is the President of the
26 Senate, who must be chosen in accordance with law.
27 (2) The President shall take the chair on every legislative day at the hour to which the Senate
28 adjourned at the last sitting.

17 Decorum
18 S20-10. Questions of order -- appeal. The President of the Senate shall decide all questions of order,
19 subject to an appeal by any Senator seconded by two other Senators. A Senator may not speak more than
20 once on an appeal without the consent of a majority of the Senate.
21 S20-20. Violation of rules -- call to order -- appeal. (1) If a Senator, in speaking or otherwise, violates
22 the rules of the Senate, the President shall, or the majority leader or minority floor leader may, call the Senator
23 to order, in which case the Senator called to order must be seated immediately.
24 (2) The Senator called to order may move for an appeal to the Senate, and if the motion is seconded by
25 two Senators, the matter must be submitted to the Senate for determination by majority vote. The motion is
26 nondebatable.
27 (3) If the decision of the Senate is in favor of the Senator called to order, the Senator may proceed. If
28 the decision is against the Senator, the Senator may not proceed.

1 (4) If a Senator is called to order, the matter may be referred to the Rules Committee by the minority or
2 majority leader. The Committee may recommend to the Senate that the Senator be censured or be subject to
3 other action. Censure consists of an official public reprimand of a Senator for inappropriate behavior. The
4 Senate shall act upon the recommendation of the Committee.
5 S20-30. Questions of privilege -- restrictions. (1) Questions of privilege in order of precedence are
6 those:
7 (a) affecting the collective rights, safety, dignity, or integrity of the proceedings of the Senate; and
8 (b) affecting the rights, reputation, or conduct of individual Senators in their capacity as Senators.
9 (2) A Senator may not address the Senate on a question of privilege between the time:
10 (a) an undebatable motion is offered and the vote is taken on the motion;
11 (b) the previous question is ordered and the vote is taken on the proposition included under the
12 previous question;
13 (c) a motion to lay on the table is offered and the vote is taken on the motion; or
14 (d) a bill sponsor closes on the bill and the question is called by the presiding officer with a vote taken
15 on the motion.
16 S20-40. Recognition by chair. A Senator desiring to speak shall indicate to the presiding officer and,
17 once being recognized, shall speak. When two or more Senators indicate a desire to speak at the same time,
18 the presiding officer shall determine the order of the speakers.
19 S20-50. Floor privileges. (1) When the Senate is in session no person is permitted in the chambers
20 except:
21 (a) legislators;
22 (b) legislative officers and employees whose presence is necessary for the conduct of business of the
23 session;
24 (c) registered representatives of the media with a floor pass; and
25 (d) former legislators (not currently registered as lobbyists).
26 (2) The President may make exceptions for visiting dignitaries.
27 (3) Beginning 1 hour before and ending one-half hour after adjournment, no person is permitted in the
28 chambers except those authorized as exceptions under subsection (1) or (2).

2 S40-10. Types of legislation. The only types of legislation that may be introduced in the Senate are
3 those that have been drafted and approved by the Legislative Services Division and signed by a Senator as
4 chief sponsor. The types of legislation allowed include:
5 (1) bills of any subject, except appropriations;
6 (2) joint resolutions, which may be used for any purpose specified in Joint Rule 40-60; and
7 (3) simple resolutions, which may:
8 (a) adopt or amend Senate rules;
9 (b) provide for the internal affairs of the Senate;
10 (c) express confirmation of the Governor's appointments; or
11 (d) make recommendations concerning the districting and apportionment plan as provided by Article V,
12 section 14(4), of the Montana Constitution.
13 S40-20. Introduction -- first reading. (1) Upon receiving a bill or resolution from a Senator, the
14 Secretary of the Senate shall assign an appropriate sequential number, which constitutes introduction of the
15 legislation. Legislation properly introduced or received in the Senate must be announced across the rostrum
16 and public notice provided. This announcement constitutes first reading, and no debate or motion is in order

17 Decorum
18 S20-10. Questions of order -- appeal. The President of the Senate shall decide all questions of order,
19 subject to an appeal by any Senator seconded by two other Senators. A Senator may not speak more than
20 once on an appeal without the consent of a majority of the Senate.
21 S20-20. Violation of rules -- call to order -- appeal. (1) If a Senator, in speaking or otherwise, violates
22 the rules of the Senate, the President shall, or the majority leader or minority floor leader may, call the Senator
23 to order, in which case the Senator called to order must be seated immediately.
24 (2) The Senator called to order may move for an appeal to the Senate, and if the motion is seconded by
25 two Senators, the matter must be submitted to the Senate for determination by majority vote. The motion is
26 nondebatable.
27 (3) If the decision of the Senate is in favor of the Senator called to order, the Senator may proceed. If
28 the decision is against the Senator, the Senator may not proceed.

2 majority leader. The Committee may recommend to the Senate that the Senator be censured or be subject to

4 Senate shall act upon the recommendation of the Committee.

7 (a) affecting the collective rights, safety, dignity, or integrity of the proceedings of the Senate; and

11 Nominations from the Governor
12 S70-10. Nominations -- second term reappointments. (1) The Governor shall nominate and, by and
13 with the consent of the Senate, appoint all officers whose offices are established by the Montana Constitution or
14 which may be created by law and for whom appointment or election is not otherwise provided.
15 (2) If during a recess of the Senate a vacancy occurs in any office subject to Senate confirmation, the
16 Governor shall appoint some fit person to discharge the duties of the office until the next meeting of the Senate,
17 when the Governor shall nominate a person to fill the office.
18 (3) If the Governor is reelected to a second 4-year term of office, the Governor shall renominate all
19 retained officers that were previously confirmed pursuant to subsection (1). The Senate confirmation procedure
20 for a retained officer is the same as the confirmation procedure for a new appointment.
21 (4) A retained officer is an individual whose term would have expired at the end of the Governors first
22 4-year term if the Governor had not been reelected to a second consecutive term.
23 S70-20. Receiving nominations -- requesting bill drafts. (1) Nominations and renominations

25 (a) received by the President;
