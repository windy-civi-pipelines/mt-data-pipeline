3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATING TO ALCOHOL AND
5 SMOKING; CREATING THE CIGAR BAR ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE ENDORSEMENT; APPLYING THE
6 ENDORSEMENT TO CERTAIN ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE LICENSES; PROVIDING REQUIREMENTS
7 RELATING TO THE CIGAR BAR LICENSE ENDORSEMENT; PROVIDING A FEE; EXEMPTING CIGAR
8 BARS FROM CLEAN INDOOR AIR ACT REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING FOR A STUDY AND A REPORT;
9 PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 50-40-103 AND 50-40-104, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Cigar bar -- license endorsement -- requirements. (1) An all-
14 beverages licensee under 16-4-201 may apply to the department for a cigar bar license endorsement. The
15 department shall issue a cigar bar endorsement if the licensee meets the requirements of this section,
16 determined through the department's suitability analysis as provided in 16-3-311. An application for a catering
17 endorsement and an annual fee of $200 must be submitted to the department for its approval.
18 (2) A cigar bar endorsement permits the smoking of premium cigars on the licensed premises,
19 which may constitute an enclosed public place as defined in 50-40-103 but is exempt from the prohibition on
20 smoking in an enclosed public place as provided in 50-40-104.
21 (3) A cigar bar endorsement may be issued only to an all-beverages licensee who holds a gaming
22 or gambling license under Title 23, chapter 5, part 3, 5, or 6.
23 (4) An establishment with a cigar bar endorsement:
24 (a) may only allow premium cigars that are purchased on the licensed premises to be smoked;
25 (b) must generate 10% or more of its annual gross income, excluding gaming or gambling income,
26 from the sale of premium cigars;
27 (c) must have a humidor as defined in this section;

1 (d) may not permit the smoking of any other tobacco, marijuana, or alternative nicotine or vapor
2 products, as defined in 16-11-302, on the licensed premises;
3 (e) must post a notice of the prohibition against smoking products other than premium cigars;
4 (f) may not knowingly sell to or permit entrance to a person less than 21 years of age; and
5 (g) must be located in a freestanding structure occupied solely by the cigar bar, and smoke from a
6 premium cigar may not migrate into an enclosed area where smoking is otherwise prohibited. If indoors, the
7 establishment must have a public place that is enclosed by solid walls or windows, a ceiling, and a solid door
8 and be equipped with a ventilation system as defined in this section.
9 (5) A licensee meeting the requirements of this section shall annually report to the department, on
10 a form prescribed by the department, the revenue generated from the sale of premium cigars as a percentage
11 of annual gross income and any other information considered necessary by the department for renewal of the
12 cigar bar endorsement.
13 (6) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
14 (a) "Cigar" means a premium tobacco product that:
15 (i) is composed of only tobacco leaves, water, and an insignificant amount of vegetable-based
16 adhesive;
17 (ii) is handmade and is not machine-made;
18 (iii) is wrapped in whole leaf tobacco;
19 (iv) contains a 100% leaf tobacco binder;
20 (v) is capped by hand;
21 (vi) does not have a filter, tip, or nontobacco mouthpiece;
22 (vii) has a characterizing flavor that is added through a natural process, such as mixing different
23 types of tobacco leaves, soaking, or aromatic smoking, and is not flavored through a chemical process or other
24 means; and
25 (viii) weighs at least 2.7 grams a cigar.
26 (b)(a) "Enclosed public place" has the same meaning as provided in 50-40-103.
27 (c)(b) "Humidor" means an enclosure affixed to the location that is large enough for an individual to

2 (c) "Premium cigar" means a cigar that is:
3 (i) wrapped in whole-leaf tobacco;
4 (ii) contains a 100% leaf tobacco binder;
5 (iii) is made by manually combining the wrapper, filler, and binder;
6 (iv) has no filter, tip, or nontobacco mouthpiece and is capped by hand; and
7 (v) weighs more than 6 pounds for each 1,000 units.
8 (d) "Ventilation system" means a dedicated system in which exhausted air is not recirculated to
9 nonsmoking areas and smoke is not back streamed into nonsmoking areas.
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Gaming impact study. (1) The gambling control division of the
12 department of justice, in conjunction with the gaming advisory council, shall conduct a study of the gaming
13 industry in Montana.
14 (2) The study must utilize a range of data from different sources, including video gambling machine
15 manufacturers, owners, route operators, casino owners, and applicable trend data from Montana and other
16 states.
17 (3) Upon completion of the study, the department shall publish a report that is accessible to the
18 public. The report must include:
19 (a) a comprehensive assessment of the data as it relates to bet limits, payout limits, bonus games,
20 game structure, and other ancillary issues that are deemed relevant;
21 (b) specific information about the methods used for collecting or acquiring data used in the report;
22 and
23 (c) the identification of specific issues for consideration by the legislature and the department
24 concerning gaming.
25 (4) The study and report must be completed by December 1, 2026.

8 2, that holds a cigar bar endorsement under [section 1] that permits the smoking of premium cigars in an
9 enclosed public place.

11 2201.

14 (a) restaurants;

11 Section 4. Section 50-40-104, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "50-40-104. Smoking in enclosed public places prohibited -- notice to public -- places where
13 prohibition inapplicable. (1) Except as otherwise provided in this section, smoking in an enclosed public place
14 is prohibited.
15 (2) The proprietor or manager of an establishment containing enclosed public places shall post a
16 sign in a conspicuous place at all public entrances to the establishment stating, in a manner that can be easily
17 read and understood, that smoking in the enclosed public place is prohibited.
18 (3) The proprietor or manager of an intrastate bus that is not chartered shall prohibit smoking in all
19 parts of the bus.
20 (4) The prohibition in subsection (1) does not apply to the following places, whether or not the
21 public is allowed access to those places:
22 (a) a private residence unless it is used for any of the following purposes, in which case the
23 prohibition in subsection (1) applies:
24 (i) a family day-care home or group day-care home, as defined in 52-2-703 and licensed pursuant
25 to Title 52, chapter 2, part 7;
26 (ii) an adult foster care home, as defined in 50-5-101 and licensed pursuant to Title 50, chapter 5;
