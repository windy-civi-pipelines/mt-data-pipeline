(Primary Sponsor)
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING THE ADMINISTRATION OF GENE-BASED
5 VACCINES INTO HUMANS IN THE STATE OF MONTANA; PROVIDING A DEFINITION; AND PROVIDING A
6 PENALTY.”
7
8 WHEREAS, the mRNA vaccines have caused enormous numbers of deaths, disabilities, and serious
9 adverse events; and
10 WHEREAS, no long-term studies have been completed regarding fertility, teratogenicity, mutagenicity,
11 or oncogenicity; and
12 WHEREAS, recipients are not given informed consent; and
13 WHEREAS, the mRNA vaccines may integrate into the human genome and be passed onto the next
14 generation; and
15 WHEREAS, the mRNA vaccines are contaminated with DNA, metallic particles, and other unknown
16 nanoparticles; and
17 WHEREAS, the mRNA vaccines may shed to others and cause side effects or harm.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Administration of gene-based vaccines. (1) A person may not provide
22 or administer a gene-based vaccine to a human within the state.
23 (2) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and is subject to a $500 fine for
24 each incident.
25 (3) The appropriate licensure board within the Montana department of labor and industry shall
26 review the license of a person who is licensed under Title 37 and who violates [this section].
27 (4) For the purposes of this section, "gene-based vaccine" means a vaccine developed using
28 messenger ribonucleic acid (mRNA) technology, modified messenger ribonucleic acid technology, self-
