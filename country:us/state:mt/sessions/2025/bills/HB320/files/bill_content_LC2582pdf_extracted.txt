26 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Montana's academic prosperity program for scholars -- legislative
27 findings -- purposes -- intent. (1) The legislature finds, pursuant to the authority and duties provided in Article
28 X, section 1, of the Montana constitution, that:

1 (a) to further the goals of developing the full educational potential of each child in the state and to
2 support equality of educational opportunity for all students, a parent must have maximum flexibility to meet the
3 educational needs of the parent's child;
4 (b) to further the state's recognition of the distinct and unique cultural heritage of American Indians
5 and the state's commitment to advancing the educational goals of the preservation of American Indian cultural
6 integrity, a parent must have maximum flexibility to meet the needs of the parent's children;
7 (c) providing opportunities for the creation of educational options capable of meeting the unique
8 needs of students in all communities is critical to the fulfillment of each person's educational potential;
9 (d) expanding educational options within the state is a valid public purpose necessary to ensure
10 equal educational opportunity for all children of the state and is vital to the preservation of liberty and the
11 economic competitiveness and cultural enrichment of the state; and
12 (e) it is vital to the economic competitiveness and success of the state and its residents to create
13 pathways to accessible educational options that:
14 (i) empower a parent to direct the education of the parent's children;
15 (ii) inspire a student to learn at the student's full potential;
16 (iii) offer an education provider a variety of professional opportunities; and
17 (iv) incentivize educational entrepreneurship.
18 (2) The purposes of [sections 1 through 17] are to:
19 (a) offer a parent greater flexibility to meet the educational needs of the state's diverse student
20 population;
21 (b) support greater familial autonomy and responsibility for the education of children to improve
22 educational outcomes and lifelong success; and
23 (c) provide an option to educate Montana students through a privately funded income tax credit
24 education program whose participation by a parent and donor is voluntary, and which is in addition to the state's
25 obligation to provide and fund the state's share of the cost of a basic system of public schools.
26 (3) The legislature intends that the MAPPS council and the program manager recognize the liberty
27 of a parent to direct the upbringing and education of the parent's child and respect parental autonomy when the
28 parent chooses qualified education expenses to meet the child's individual instructional education needs.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 4. MAPPS council -- membership. (1) (a) There is an autonomous
9 MAPPS council with authority, consistent with legislative intent as stated in [section 2], to:
10 (i) assist the department of administration in choosing a program manager;
11 (ii) oversee the work of the program manager; and
12 (iii) develop a list of qualified education expenses.
13 (b) The council is allocated to the department of administration for administrative purposes, as
14 prescribed in 2-15-121(2), except as provided in this section.
15 (2) The council consists of five members who are appointed by the governor.
16 (3) Members appointed to the council shall represent a diverse group of stakeholders who are
17 supportive of MAPPS, including parents, grandparents, private school leaders and board members, education
18 entrepreneurs, and business leaders. All members of the council must have a demonstrated understanding of
19 and commitment to expanding educational options within the state as a valid public purpose necessary to
20 ensure equal educational opportunity for all children of the state, to preserve the liberty of parents to direct the
21 education of their children, and to enhance the economic competitiveness and cultural enrichment of the state.
22 (4) (a) Initial appointments to the council must be for staggered terms as follows:
23 (i) one member with a 4-year term designated as the initial presiding officer of the council for 2
24 years;
25 (ii) two members with 3-year terms; and
26 (iii) two members with 2-year terms.
27 (b) All terms after the initial term must be for 3 years. Appointment to the initial terms must be
28 made no later than 45 days following [the effective date of this act].

2 presiding officer of the council as provided in the council's bylaws.

19 NEW SECTION. Section 5. MAPPS council and department of administration -- duties related to
20 selection and oversight of program manager. (1) The council shall collaborate with and advise the
21 department during the process of choosing and contracting with a single program manager for MAPPS. When
22 choosing the program manager, the department shall solicit and evaluate proposals from program manager
23 applicants through limited solicitation procedures under 18-4-305 or another expedited procedure. The council
24 shall review applicant proposals promptly to check adherence of the proposals to program manager
25 requirements under MAPPS. In reviewing the program manager applicant proposals, the MAPPS council shall
26 consider:
27 (a) how the applicant intends to meet statutory requirements and why the applicant believes it may
28 best serve the public interest;
- 7 - LC 2582
****

1 (b) the applicant's knowledge based on current or past experience administering tax credit
2 education programs, including those similar to MAPPS;
3 (c) letters of recommendation from clients served by the applicant, if any;
4 (d) the applicant's most recent audit report and proof of tax-exempt status under section 501(c)(3)
5 of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 501(c)(3); and
6 (e) indications that the applicant fully understands the imperative of fulfilling the objectives of the
7 legislature under [section 2] and the educational needs of students and families.
8 (2) (a) The department shall distribute program manager applications to the council as the
9 applications are received. The council shall meet no later than 10 days after the application deadline to review
10 and determine which program manager applicants show an ability to fulfill the statutory requirements of MAPPS
11 and invite qualified applicants to be interviewed by the council. Interviews by the council must occur no later
12 than 14 days after issuing invitations to be interviewed. After interviewing qualified applicants, the council shall
13 recommend to the department the applicant that shows the strongest ability, intent, and commitment to fulfill the
14 legislative objectives and overall goals of MAPPS.
15 (b) After the department selects the best qualified applicant, the MAPPS council shall collaborate
16 with the department to develop a contract with a single program manager that fulfills MAPPS requirements in
17 the least restrictive manner.
18 (3) The council shall review the program manager's contract and performance every 5 years to
19 determine whether to recommend to the department renewal, amendment and renewal, or termination of the
20 contract consistent with the requirements of MAPPS.

22 NEW SECTION. Section 6. MAPPS council -- determining qualified education expenses --
23 appeals process -- reporting. (1) The MAPPS council shall develop and update annually a comprehensive list
24 of qualified education expenses by October 1. The council may update the list throughout the year. The list
25 must be developed and updated after a review of best practices for approved expenses from other states with
26 similar programs and after consultation with the program manager, educators, parents, and others providing
27 educational services. In developing the list, considering requests for review, and handling appeals under this
28 section, the council shall respect and comply with the intent of the legislature as described in [section 2] and be

1 consistent with the legislative goals for education under 20-1-102 and the basic instructional program for pupils
2 referenced in 20-7-111.
3 (2) To provide clarity for parents and the program manager, the council may create a list of
4 expenditures that are not allowable under MAPPS.
5 (3) A parent or the program manager may request that the council review an expenditure and add
6 it to the list of qualified education expenses. The council shall convene in an expedient manner to review,
7 deliberate, and vote on requests under this subsection.
