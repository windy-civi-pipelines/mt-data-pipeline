4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE DISTRIBUTION OF MARIJUANA TAX REVENUE;

14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Marijuana prevention account -- rules. (1) There is a marijuana
15 prevention account in the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102. The account consists of money
16 transferred to the account pursuant to 16-12-111.
17 (2) Money in the account must be used by the department of public health and human services to
18 fund the marijuana tax revenue accountability council provided for in [section 2] and to provide grants for
19 primary substance misuse prevention and youth suicide prevention in consultation with the council.
20 (3) The programs must be designed to:
21 (a) improve access to, utilization of, and engagement and retention in primary substance misuse
22 prevention services; and
23 (b) expand the availability of community-based services that reflect best practices or are evidence-
24 based.
25 (4) The department of public health and human services shall:
26 (a) manage the programs funded by the special revenue account in consultation with the
27 marijuana tax revenue accountability council provided for in [section 2];
28 (b) provide support for schools to administer the behavioral risk factor surveillance system with

11 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Marijuana tax revenue accountability council -- definition. (1) There
12 is a marijuana tax revenue accountability council attached to the department of public health and human
13 services for administrative purposes only as prescribed in 2-15-121.
14 (2) The council is composed of the following members:
15 (a) the director of the department of public health and human services or a designee;
16 (b) a representative of the department of justice appointed by the attorney general;
17 (c) a representative of the department of revenue appointed by the director of the department of
18 revenue;
19 (d) one member who also serves on the board of behavioral health provided for in 2-15-1744
20 appointed by the presiding officer of the board of behavioral health;
21 (e) one member who represents the behavioral health alliance of Montana and who is dually
22 licensed as an addiction counselor and as a licensed practitioner or is a master's social work licensee
23 appointed by the governor;
24 (f) one member who represents the behavioral health alliance of Montana and who is a licensed
25 psychiatrist appointed by the governor;
26 (g) one member who is a certified prevention specialist appointed by the governor; and
27 (h) one public member appointed by the governor.
28 (3) The members provided for in subsections (2)(e) through (2)(h) shall serve 3-year terms. The

2 must be filled for the balance of an unexpired term. A member of the council may be reappointed.
3 (4) Each professional member of a board, while serving as a board member:
4 (a) must be a resident of this state and at least 18 years of age; and
5 (b) must be currently practicing in the profession or occupation and have an active license in this
6 state for at least 1 year and be in good standing for the profession or occupation in which the member is

15 including holding a license under Title 16, chapter 12, part 2.

17 conflicts of interest or relationships that would interfere with the mission of the council.

26 other state agencies on primary substance misuse prevention and youth suicide prevention;
27 (c) encourage and stimulate research, public awareness, education, and prevention activities; and
28 (d) prepare the marijuana impact report provided for in [section 3].

10 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Marijuana impact report. The marijuana tax revenue accountability
11 council shall provide a written marijuana impact report in accordance with 5-11-210 to the children, families,
12 health, and human services interim committee, the economic affairs interim committee, and the law and justice
13 interim committee, no later than September 1 of each year. The report must include:
14 (1) public health and safety objectives that may be impacted by marijuana policy changes,
15 including prevalence and patterns of use; and
16 (2) information on harms, including but not limited to cannabis use disorder, marijuana
17 hospitalizations, environmental impacts of marijuana production, licenses, marijuana business density,
18 marijuana law enforcement actions, criminal and administrative violations, youth access, and cases of driving
19 under the influence of marijuana.

26 (b) 1% for local marijuana law enforcement grants.
27 (3) The local marijuana law enforcement grants provided for in subsection (2)(b) must be
28 administered by the department of justice and provided to local governments that apply for the grants. To apply

5 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Marijuana law enforcement office. (1) There is a marijuana law
6 enforcement office in the department of justice under the supervision and control of the attorney general.
7 (2) The office shall conduct law enforcement operations related to marijuana, including but not
8 limited to combatting black market activities and investigating and prosecuting violations of marijuana
9 regulations.
10 (3) The office must include:
11 (a) two marijuana enforcement teams, one based in western Montana and one based in eastern
12 Montana;
13 (b) a district attorney; and
14 (c) a canine unit.
15 (4) The office is funded with revenue from the marijuana law enforcement operations account
16 provided for in [section 4].
