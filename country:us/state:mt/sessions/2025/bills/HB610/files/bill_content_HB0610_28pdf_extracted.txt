- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0610.2
2 INTRODUCED BY K. SEEKINS-CROWE
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING PREAUTHORIZATION LAWS; PROVIDING
5 DIRECTION TO THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES TO NOT REQUIRE
6 AUTHORIZATION FOR CERTAIN ANTIPSYCHOTIC DRUGS; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE AND
7 AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. SECTION 1. DIRECTIONS TO DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES. (1)
12 FOR THE PURPOSE OF REMOVING BARRIERS TO THE TIMELY TREATMENT OF SERIOUS MENTAL ILLNESSES, THE
13 DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES MAY NOT REQUIRE PRIOR AUTHORIZATION UNDER THE MEDICAID
14 PROGRAM ON ANY ANTIPSYCHOTIC DRUG THAT IS APPROVED BY THE U.S. FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION, FOR ITS
15 APPROVED INDICATIONS, FOR MEDICAID MEMBERS 18 YEARS OF AGE AND OLDER.
16 (2) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY REQUIRE THE DEPARTMENT TO COVER ANTIPSYCHOTIC DRUGS NOT
17 ELIGIBLE FOR COVERAGE IN ACCORDANCE WITH 42 U.S.C. 1396R-8.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Directions to department of public health and human services. The
20 department may not require prior authorization under the medicaid program f or any antipsychotic drug
21 approved by the federal drug administration that is recognized by the most recent edition of the Diagnostic and
22 Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders adopted by the American psychiatric association as effective in the
23 treatment of conditions that are specified in the manual.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Effective date. [This act] is effective [30 days after this act is passed and
26 approved].
27
28 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Applicability. [This act] applies to claims submitted for payment for
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 610
