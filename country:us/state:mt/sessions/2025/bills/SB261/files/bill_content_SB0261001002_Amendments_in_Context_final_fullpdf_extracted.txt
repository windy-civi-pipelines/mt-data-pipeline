1 SENATE BILL NO. 261
2 INTRODUCED BY G. HERTZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING THE OFFENSE OF ENDANGERING THE
5 WELFARE OF CHILDREN; ESTABLISHING THAT A PERSON COMMITS THE OFFENSE OF
6 ENDANGERING THE WELFARE OF CHILDREN BY EXPOSING A CHILD TO MARIJUANA OR
7 DANGEROUS DRUGS OR ASSISTING A CHILD IN ENTERING AN ADULT-USE DISPENSARY OR A PLACE
8 WHERE HUMAN TRAFFICKING OR THE PRODUCTION OF DANGEROUS DRUGS IS TAKING PLACE;
9 AND AMENDING SECTION 45-5-622, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 Section 1. Section 45-5-622, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "45-5-622. Endangering welfare of children. (1) (a) A parent, guardian, or other person supervising
15 the welfare of a child less than 18 years old commits the offense of endangering the welfare of children if the
16 parent, guardian, or other person knowingly endangers the child's welfare by violating a duty of care, protection,
17 or support.
18 (b) A parent or guardian of a child does not violate a duty of care, protection, or support by
19 permitting the child to engage in independent activities consistent with the child's intellectual, emotional, and
20 physical maturity, including:
21 (i) traveling to and from school by walking, running, bicycling, public transit, or other means;
22 (ii) traveling to and from nearby commercial or recreational facilities;
23 (iii) engaging in outdoor play;
24 (iv) remaining for less than 15 minutes in a vehicle if the temperature inside the vehicle is not or will
25 not become dangerously hot or cold;
26 (v) remaining at home if the parent or guardian:
27 (A) returns home the same day on which the parent or guardian gives the child permission to
28 remain at home;

6 the delinquency of a child less than:

8 (i) supplying or encouraging the use of an intoxicating substance by the child;
9 (ii) causing or permitting the child to inhale, be exposed to, or ingest marijuana as defined in 50-
10 32-101, including products containing tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), such as ingestible foods or drinks;or
11 (ii)(iii) assisting, promoting, or encouraging the child to enter a place of prostitution or human
12 trafficking as defined in 45-5-701;
13 (iv) assisting, promoting, or encouraging the child to enter an adult-use dispensary as defined in
14 16-12-102; or
15 (v) assisting, promoting, or encouraging the child to enter a place where the criminal production or
16 manufacture of dangerous drugs is taking place as described in 45-9-110; or
17 (b) 16 years old by assisting, promoting, or encouraging the child to:
18 (i) abandon the child's place of residence without the consent of the child's parents or guardian; or
19 (ii) engage in sexual conduct.
20 (3) A person, whether or not the person is supervising the welfare of a child less than 18 years of
21 age, commits the offense of endangering the welfare of children if the person, in the residence of a child, in a
