4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING DRIVER'S LICENSE LAWS; REVISING THE
5 EXEMPTION FOR OUT-OF-STATE DRIVER'S LICENSES FOR LICENSES ISSUED EXCLUSIVELY TO
6 PERSONS UNLAWFULLY PRESENT IN THE UNITED STATES; REQUIRING THE MOTOR VEHICLE
7 DIVISION OF THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE TO DETERMINE BY RULEMAKING THE CLASSES OF
8 LICENSES THAT ARE ISSUED BY OTHER STATES EXCLUSIVELY TO PERSONS UNLAWFULLY
9 PRESENT IN THE UNITED STATES; REORGANIZING DEPARTMENT RULEMAKING REGARDING
10 DRIVER'S LICENSES; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 61-5-104 AND
11 61-14-201, MCA.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Section 61-5-104, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "61-5-104. Exemptions. (1) The following persons are exempt from licensure under this chapter:
17 (a) a person who is a member of the armed forces of the United States while operating a motor
18 vehicle owned by or leased to the United States government and being operated on official business;
19 (b) a person who is a member of the armed forces of the United States on active duty in Montana
20 who holds a valid license issued by another state and the spouse of the person who holds a valid license
21 issued by another state;
22 (c) a person on active duty in the armed forces of the United States and in immediate possession
23 of a valid license issued to that person in a foreign country by the armed forces of the United States, for a
24 period of 45 days from the date of the person's return to the United States;
25 (d) a person who temporarily drives, operates, or moves a road machine, farm tractor, as defined
26 in 61-9-102, or implement of husbandry for use in intrastate commerce on a highway;
27 (e) a person who is a locomotive engineer, assistant engineer, conductor, brake tender, railroad
28 utility person, or other member of the crew of a railroad locomotive or train being operated upon rails, including
- 1 - LC 2224
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 2224
1 operation on a railroad crossing a public street, road, or highway. A person employed as described in this
2 subsection is not required to display a driver's license to a law enforcement officer in connection with the
3 operation of a railroad train within Montana.
4 (f) a person who temporarily drives, operates, or moves an off-highway vehicle on a forest
5 development road in this state, as defined in 61-8-110, that has been designated and approved for off-highway
6 vehicle use by the United States forest service if the person:
7 (i) is under 16 years of age but at least 12 years of age; and
8 (ii) at the time of driving, operating, or moving the off-highway vehicle, has in the person's
9 possession a certificate showing the successful completion of an off-highway vehicle safety education course
10 approved by the department of fish, wildlife, and parks and is in the physical presence of a person who
11 possesses a license issued under this chapter.
12 (2) A nonresident who is at least 15 years of age and who is in immediate possession of a valid
13 operator's license issued to the nonresident by the nonresident's home state or country may operate a motor
14 vehicle, except a commercial motor vehicle, in this state, unless the license is determined by the department to
15 be of a class issued exclusively to persons unlawfully present in the United States.
16 (3) (a) A nonresident who is in immediate possession of a valid commercial driver's license issued
17 to the nonresident by the nonresident's home jurisdiction, in accordance with the licensing and testing
18 standards of 49 CFR, part 383, may operate a commercial motor vehicle in this state.
19 (b) For the purpose of this chapter, "jurisdiction" means a state, territory, or possession of the
20 United States, the District of Columbia, a province or territory of Canada, or the federal district of Mexico.
21 (4) A nonresident who is at least 18 years of age, whose home state or country does not require
22 the licensing of operators, may operate a motor vehicle as an operator only, for a period of not more than 90
23 days in any calendar year, if the motor vehicle is registered in the home state or country of the nonresident.
24 (5) (a) A driver's license issued under this chapter to a person who enters the United States armed
25 forces, if valid and in effect at the time that the person enters the service, continues in effect so long as the
26 service continues, unless the license is suspended, revoked, or cancelled for a cause as provided by law, and
27 for up to 90 days following the date on which the licensee is honorably separated from the service.
28 (b) A person serving in the United States armed forces may renew the person's driver's license at
- 2 - LC 2224
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 2224
1 any point of the person's service, and any renewed license continues in effect as long as the service continues,
2 unless the license is suspended, revoked, or cancelled for a cause as provided by law.
3 (c) A person serving in the United States armed forces may apply for a Montana driver's license
4 upon meeting the requirements in 61-5-103, and this license continues in effect as long as the service
5 continues, unless the license is suspended, revoked, or cancelled for a cause as provided by law, and for up to
6 90 days following the date on which the licensee is honorably separated from the service."

8 Section 2. Section 61-14-201, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "61-14-201. Rulemaking authority -- driver's licenses and identification cards. (1) The
10 department shall adopt rules governing the calculation of grace periods for renewals and the calculation of
11 other time periods established by statute or federal regulation.
12 (2) The department shall adopt rules to set the standards for driver's license examinations and
13 reexaminations.
14 (3) The department shall adopt rules establishing the functional abilities and skills required to
15 exercise ordinary and reasonable control to safely operate a motor vehicle. The rules:
16 (a) must include operational restrictions based on the driver's ability and skills;
17 (b) may direct the design of one or more types of skills tests. A skills test may consist of:
18 (i) a comprehensive assessment of a person's functional abilities by means of an actual
19 demonstration of the applicant's ability to exercise ordinary and reasonable control in the safe operation of a
20 motor vehicle; or
21 (ii) a more limited assessment of a person's functional abilities, conducted at the discretion of the
22 department, as related to a specific physical or mental condition or conditions or a request for reexamination.
23 (c) must include appropriate licensing criteria relating to the use of adaptive equipment or
24 operational limits that can be readily discerned by law enforcement or a licensing agency in another jurisdiction.
25 (4) The department shall adopt rules establishing vision requirements for a person to safely
26 operate a motor vehicle. The rules:
27 (a) must include the minimum uncorrected or corrected visual acuity requirements for both
28 unrestricted and restricted licenses and operational restrictions based on the visual acuity of an applicant or
- 3 - LC 2224
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 2224
1 licensee, including the use of bioptic lenses; and
2 (b) may include minimum field of vision and depth perception requirements for both unrestricted
3 and restricted licenses.
4 (5) The rules in subsections (3) and (4):
5 (a) may take into consideration any nationally recognized standards or recommended practices or
6 standards of other jurisdictions for assessment of a person's functional abilities and skills;
7 (b) may be derived from medical guidelines and information compiled by driver licensing medical
8 advisory or review boards from other jurisdictions, as well as information received from advocacy groups for
9 persons with disabilities and senior citizens; and
10 (c) except as provided in 61-5-105, may not use a person's age or a person's physical or mental
11 disability, limitation, or condition as a justification for the denial of a license.
12 (6) The department shall adopt rules governing the issuance of a restricted learner license,
13 including when the department may issue a restricted learner license to allow for a driver to practice driving
14 skills.
15 (7) The department shall adopt rules governing the issuance of a hardship license to a person who
16 is at least 13 years of age and because of individual hardship needs a restricted driver's license, including a
17 person who holds a learner license under 61-5-106. The department must consider, among other criteria,
18 whether a hardship license is needed because the applicant's parent or guardian is not available to accompany
19 the licensee, whether due to employment or circumstances related to the operation of a farm or ranch or
20 because the parent or guardian does not hold a valid driver's license, and the licensee is required to drive to the
21 licensee's school bus stop.
22 (8) The department shall adopt rules governing the issuance, renewal, and cancellation of
23 identification cards that align with the proof of identity, residence, and authorized presence standards for a
24 driver's license.
25 (9) The department shall adopt rules governing restrictions for personal communication limitations
26 and other medical information that would be helpful to a peace officer during a traffic stop.
27 (10) The department shall adopt by rule a list of the specific classes of driver's licenses issued by
28 other states that are issued exclusively to persons unlawfully present in the United States.
