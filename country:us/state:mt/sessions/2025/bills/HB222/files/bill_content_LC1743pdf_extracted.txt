4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CREATING A YEAR-ROUND OPEN WOLF HUNTING SEASON;
5 ALLOWING FOR THE SEASON TO BE CLOSED WHEN THE WOLF POPULATION IS AT OR BELOW A
6 CERTAIN NUMBER; AND AMENDING SECTION 87-1-304, MCA.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 Section 1. Section 87-1-304, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "87-1-304. Fixing of seasons and bag and possession limits. (1) Subject to the provisions of 87-5-
12 302 and subsections (7) through (9 ) (10) of this section, the commission may:
13 (a) fix seasons, bag limits, possession limits, and season limits;
14 (b) open or close or shorten or lengthen seasons on any species of game, bird, fish, or fur-bearing
15 animal as defined by 87-2-101;
16 (c) declare areas open to the hunting of deer, antelope, elk, moose, sheep, goat, mountain lion,
17 bear, wild buffalo or bison, and wolf by persons holding an archery stamp and the required license, permit, or
18 tag and designate times when only bows and arrows may be used to hunt deer, antelope, elk, moose, sheep,
19 goat, mountain lion, bear, wild buffalo or bison, and wolf in those areas;
20 (d) subject to the provisions of 87-1-301(6), restrict areas and species to hunting with only
21 specified hunting arms, including bow and arrow, for the reasons of safety or of providing diverse hunting
22 opportunities and experiences; and
23 (e) declare areas open to special license holders only and issue special licenses in a limited
24 number when the commission determines, after proper investigation, that a special season is necessary to
25 ensure the maintenance of an adequate supply of game birds, fish, or animals or fur-bearing animals. The
26 commission may declare a special season and issue special licenses when game birds, animals, or fur-bearing
27 animals are causing damage to private property or when a written complaint of damage has been filed with the
28 commission by the owner of that property. In determining to whom special licenses must be issued, the
- 1 - LC 1743

1 commission may, when more applications are received than the number of animals to be killed, award permits
2 to those chosen under a drawing system. The procedures used for awarding the permits from the drawing
3 system must be determined by the commission.
4 (2) The commission may adopt rules governing the use of livestock and vehicles by archers during
5 special archery seasons.
6 (3) Subject to the provisions of 87-5-302 and subsection (7) of this section, the commission may
7 divide the state into fish and game districts and create fish, game, or fur-bearing animal districts throughout the
8 state. The commission may declare a closed season for hunting, fishing, or trapping in any of those districts
9 and later may open those districts to hunting, fishing, or trapping.
10 (4) Subject to the provisions of subsection (9) of this section, The the commission may declare a
11 closed season on any species of game, fish, game birds, or fur-bearing animals threatened with undue
12 depletion from any cause. The commission may close any area or district of any stream, public lake, or public
13 water or portions thereof to hunting, trapping, or fishing for limited periods of time when necessary to protect a
14 recently stocked area, district, water, spawning waters, spawn-taking waters, or spawn-taking stations or to
15 prevent the undue depletion of fish, game, fur-bearing animals, game birds, and nongame birds. The
16 commission may open the area or district upon consent of a majority of the property owners affected.
17 (5) Subject to the provision of subsection (9) of this section, The the commission may authorize the
18 director to open or close any special season upon 12 hours' notice to the public.
19 (6) The commission may declare certain fishing waters closed to fishing except by persons under
20 15 years of age. The purpose of this subsection is to provide suitable fishing waters for the exclusive use and
21 enjoyment of juveniles under 15 years of age, at times and in areas the commission in its discretion considers
22 advisable and consistent with its policies relating to fishing.
23 (7) In an area immediately adjacent to a national park, the commission may not:
24 (a) prohibit the hunting or trapping of wolves; or
25 (b) close the area to wolf hunting or trapping unless a wolf harvest quota established by the
26 commission for that area has been met.
27 (8) The commission may authorize a wolf trapping season that opens the first Monday after
28 Thanksgiving and closes March 15 of the following calendar year, except that the commission may adjust the

1 dates for specific wolf management units based on regional recommendations.
2 (9) The commission shall authorize a wolf hunting season with an unlimited quota that must
3 continue until the wolf population in the state is at or below 600 wolves.
4 (10) There is established a special muzzleloader heritage hunting season that begins on the second
5 Saturday after the end of the regular season and lasts 9 days. During this season, subject to the provisions of
6 87-6-401(1)(i) and rules adopted by the commission, a person may take a deer or elk with a valid license or
7 permit using plain lead projectiles and a muzzleloading rifle that is charged with loose black powder, loose
8 pyrodex, or an equivalent loose black powder substitute and ignited by a flintlock, wheel lock, matchlock, or
9 percussion mechanism using a percussion or musket cap. The muzzleloading rifle must be a minimum of 45
10 caliber and may not have more than two barrels.
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
13 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
14 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
15 - END -
