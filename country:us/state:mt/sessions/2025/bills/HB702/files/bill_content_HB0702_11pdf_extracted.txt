4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO CRIMINAL ACTS;
5 CREATING THE OFFENSE OF OPERATING AN UNAUTHORIZED, UNMANNED AERIAL VEHICLE OVER A
6 CORRECTIONAL INSTITUTION; REVISING SENTENCING FOR ASSAULT WITH BODILY FLUID; REVISING
7 LAWS RELATED TO POSSESSING AND TRANSFERRING ILLEGAL ARTICLES; PROVIDING A
8 DEFINITION; REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "PERSISTENT FELONY OFFENDER"; AND AMENDING
9 SECTIONS 45-5-214, 45-7-307, AND 46-1-202, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Operating unauthorized, unmanned aerial vehicle over correctional
14 institution. (1) A person commits the offense of operating an unauthorized, unmanned aerial vehicle in
15 proximity to a correctional institution if the person purposely or knowingly operates or assists in the operation of
16 an unmanned aerial vehicle over a correctional institution without the authorization of the correctional institution.
17 (2) A person convicted of the offense of operating an unauthorized, unmanned aerial vehicle in
18 proximity to a correctional institution shall be imprisoned in a state prison for a term of not less than 1 year or
19 more than 5 years.
20
21 Section 2. Section 45-5-214, MCA, is amended to read:
22 "45-5-214. Assault with bodily fluid. (1) A person commits the offense of assault with a bodily fluid if
23 the person purposely causes one of the person's bodily fluids to make physical contact with:
24 (a) a law enforcement officer, a staff person of a correctional or detention facility, or a health care
25 provider, as defined in 50-4-504, including a health care provider performing emergency services, while the
26 health care provider is acting in the course and scope of the health care provider's profession and occupation:
27 (i) during or after an arrest for a criminal offense;
28 (ii) while the person is incarcerated in or being transported to or from a state prison, a county, city,

1 or regional jail or detention facility, or a health care facility; or
2 (iii) if the person is a minor, while the youth is detained in or being transported to or from a county,
3 city, or regional jail or detention facility or a youth detention facility, secure detention facility, regional detention
4 facility, short-term detention center, correctional facility as defined in 41-5-103, health care facility, or shelter
5 care facility; or
6 (b) an emergency responder.
7 (2) A person convicted of the offense of assault with a bodily fluid shall be fined an amount not to
8 exceed $1,000 or incarcerated in a county jail or a state prison for a term not to exceed 1 year 3 years, or both.
9 (3) The youth court has jurisdiction of any violation of this section by a minor.
10 (4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
11 (a) "Bodily fluid" means any bodily secretion, including but not limited to feces, urine, blood, and
12 saliva.
13 (b) "Emergency responder" means a licensed medical services provider, law enforcement officer,
14 firefighter, volunteer firefighter or officer of a nonprofit volunteer fire company, emergency care provider,
15 emergency nurse, ambulance operator, provider of civil defense services, or any other person who in good faith
16 renders emergency care or assistance at a crime scene or the scene of an emergency or accident."
17
18 Section 3. Section 45-7-307, MCA, is amended to read:
19 "45-7-307. Transferring Possessing or transferring illegal articles -- unauthorized
20 communication. (1) (a) A person commits the offense of possessing or transferring illegal articles if the person
21 knowingly or purposely possesses or transfers any illegal article or weapon to a person subject to official
22 detention or is transferred any illegal article or weapon by a person subject to official detention.
23 (b) For the purposes of this section, "illegal article" means a cellphone, tobacco, a weapon, a
24 dangerous drug as defined in 50-32-101, or other article prohibited by the correctional institution or the
25 department of corrections.
26 (b)(c) A person convicted of possessing or transferring illegal articles or a weapon shall be:
27 (i) imprisoned in a state prison for a term not to exceed 20 years, if the item transferred is a
28 weapon;

1 (ii) imprisoned in a state prison for a term not to exceed 10 years, if the illegal article is a
2 dangerous drug, as defined in 50-32-101; or
3 (iii) imprisoned in a state prison for a term not to exceed 13 months or be fined an amount not
4 more than $1,500, or both, if the illegal article, other than a weapon or dangerous drug, is transferred to or from
5 a person incarcerated in a state prison, as defined in 53-30-101, or be fined an amount not more than $100 or

11 purposely communicates with a person subject to official detention without the consent of the person in charge
12 of the official detention.
13 (b) A person convicted of the offense of unauthorized communication shall be fined an amount not
14 to exceed $100 or imprisoned in the county jail for any term not to exceed 10 days, or both."
