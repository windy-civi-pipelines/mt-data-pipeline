1 HOUSE BILL NO. 825
2 INTRODUCED BY M. CUNNINGHAM
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO HEALTH CARE
5 PROXY DECISIONMAKERS; PROVIDING FOR A PATIENT-DESIGNATED TRUSTED DECISIONMAKER;
6 PROVIDING A HIERARCHY IN SELECTING A DECISIONMAKER; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 50-5-110,
7 50-5-1301, AND 50-5-1303, MCA.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Patient designation of trusted decisionmaker as proxy --
12 documentation by attending health care provider -- limitations on other proxies. (1) At any time, after
13 admission to a hospital, before an attending health care provider determines that an adult patient lacks
14 decisional capacity under 50-5-1302, the patient may designate a trusted decisionmaker to make health care
15 decisions on behalf of the patient in the event the patient does not have decisional capacity.
16 (2) (a) The patient may designate a trusted decisionmaker by communicating to the patient's
17 health care provider, or to a health care professional acting under the direction of the patient's health care
18 provider, orally or otherwise, the identity of the individual whom the patient trusts to make health care decisions
19 on the patient's behalf.
20 (b) The health care provider shall ensure that the communication by the patient designating a
21 trusted decisionmaker is witnessed by a third party, who may be another health care professional or who may
22 be a friend or family member of the patient.
23 (3) After the patient designates a trusted decisionmaker, the attending health care provider shall
24 immediately notify the patient's family of the designation and shall document the following information related to
25 the designation in the patient's health record:
26 (a) the identity of the trusted decisionmaker; and
27 (b) contact information of the trusted decisionmaker, to the extent it is available.

1 (4) When an individual is designated as a trusted decisionmaker under this section, an attending
2 health care provider may not seek to select a lay proxy decisionmaker under 50-5-1303 or a medical proxy
3 decisionmaker under 50-5-1304 unless the attending health care provider has made reasonable efforts to
4 contact the trusted decisionmaker and the trusted decisionmaker cannot be contacted.
5 (5) A trusted decisionmaker is designated under this section, and the trusted decisionmaker
6 remains in place until the patient completes an advance directive or, completes a medical durable power of
7 attorney, designates another trusted decisionmaker, or is discharged from the hospital.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Hierarchy of decisionmaker selection. When a decisionmaker is
10 needed for a patient who lacks decisional capacity as determined under 50-5-1302, the decisionmaker must be
11 selected from the following available individuals in the order listed:
12 (1) a guardian appointed by the patient under 72-5-316 who has medical decisionmaking authority;
13 (2) an agent appointed by the patient in a medical durable power of attorney or an individual
14 designated by the patient in an advanced advance medical directive;
15 (3) a trusted decisionmaker designated under [section 1];
16 (4)(3) a spouse of the patient;
17 (5)(4) an adult child of the patient;
18 (6)(5) a parent of the patient;
19 (7)(6) a sibling of the patient;
20 (7) a trusted decisionmaker designated under [section 1];
21 (8) a close friend of the patient who has a deep understanding of the patient's wishes; and
22 (9) any other proxy appointed under Title 50, chapter 5, part 13.

25 "50-5-110. Patient bill of rights. (1) The following rights may be exercised by a patient or, if the
26 patient lacks decisionmaking capacity, is legally incompetent, or is a minor, by a patient's designated surrogate
27 or, lay proxy decisionmaker, as defined in 50-5-1301, or trusted decisionmaker as designated under [section 1]:

1 (a) The patient has the right to be treated with dignity and respect.
2 (b) The patient has the right to and is encouraged to obtain from physicians and other direct
3 caregivers relevant, current, and understandable information concerning diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis.
4 (c) Except in emergencies when the patient lacks decisionmaking capacity and the need for
5 treatment is urgent, the patient is entitled to be informed of information related to the specific procedures and
6 treatments that are recommended or planned, the risks involved that may cause harm to the patient, the
7 possible length of recuperation, and the medically reasonable alternatives and their accompanying risks and
8 benefits.
9 (d) The patient has the right to know the identity of physicians, nurses, and others involved in the
10 patient's care, as well as whether those involved are students, residents, or other trainees
11 (e) The patient has the right to know the immediate and long-term financial implications of
12 treatment choices, to the extent the costs are known.
13 (f) (i) The patient has the right to make decisions about the plan of care prior to and during the
14 course of treatment, to refuse a recommended treatment or plan of care, and to be informed of the medical
15 consequences of the decision.
16 (ii) When the patient refuses a recommended treatment or plan of care, the patient is entitled to
17 other appropriate care and services that the hospital provides or to transfer to another hospital or discharge to
18 home with impunity and without penalty or threat.
19 (iii) A hospital shall notify patients of any policy that might affect patient choices within the facility.
20 (g) (i) The patient has the right to have an advance directive, including but not limited to a living
21 will, health care proxy, or health care power of attorney, concerning treatment or designating a surrogate
22 decisionmaker with the expectation that the hospital will honor the intent of the directive. A hospital shall advise
23 a patient of the patient's rights under state law and hospital policy to make informed medical choices, ask if the
24 patient has an advance directive or surrogate decisionmaker, and include that information in the patient's
25 record.
26 (ii) The patient has the right to timely information about hospital policy that may limit the hospital's
27 ability to implement fully a legally valid advance directive or surrogate decisionmaker.

8 Section 4. Section 50-5-1301, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "50-5-1301. Definitions. As used in this part, the following definitions apply:
10 (1) "Adult" means any person 18 years of age or older.
11 (2) "Advanced practice registered nurse" means an individual who is licensed under Title 37,
12 chapter 8, to practice professional nursing in this state and who has fulfilled the requirements of the board of
13 nursing pursuant to 37-8-202 and 37-8-409.
14 (3) "Attending health care provider" means the physician, advanced practice registered nurse, or
15 physician assistant, whether selected by or assigned to a patient, who has primary responsibility for the
16 treatment and care of the patient.
17 (4) "Decisional capacity" means the ability to provide informed consent to or refuse medical
18 treatment or the ability to make an informed health care decision as determined by a health care provider
19 experienced in this type of assessment.
20 (5) "Health care facility" means a hospital, critical access hospital, rural emergency hospital, or
21 facility providing skilled nursing care as those terms are defined in 50-5-101.
22 (6) "Health care provider" means any individual licensed or certified by the state to provide health
23 care.
24 (7) "Interested person" means a patient's:
25 (a) spouse;
26 (b) parent;
27 (c) adult child, sibling, or grandchild; or
