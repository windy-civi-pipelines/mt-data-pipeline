4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING MEDICAID LAWS RELATED TO DIRECT
5 PRIMARY CARE; ALLOWING FOR COVERAGE OF DIRECT PRIMARY CARE CONTRACTS UNDER THE
6 MONTANA MEDICAID PROGRAM; PROHIBITING THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN
7 SERVICES FROM REQUIRING A MEDICAID ENROLLEE TO PARTICIPATE IN A PRIMARY CARE CASE
8 MANAGEMENT SERVICE IF THE ENROLLEE ENTERS INTO A DIRECT PRIMARY CARE CONTRACT;
9 PROVIDING A DEFINITION OF "DIRECT PRIMARY CARE CONTRACT"; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION;
10 AMENDING SECTIONS 53-6-101, 53-6-116, AND 53-6-124, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”

12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 53-6-101, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "53-6-101. Montana medicaid program -- authorization of services. (1) There is a Montana
16 medicaid program established for the purpose of providing necessary medical services to eligible persons who
17 have need for medical assistance. The Montana medicaid program is a joint federal-state program administered
18 under this chapter and in accordance with Title XIX of the Social Security Act, 42 U.S.C. 1396, et seq. The
19 department shall administer the Montana medicaid program.
20 (2) The department and the legislature shall consider the following funding principles when
21 considering changes in medicaid policy that either increase or reduce services:
22 (a) protecting those persons who are most vulnerable and most in need, as defined by a
23 combination of economic, social, and medical circumstances;
24 (b) giving preference to the elimination or restoration of an entire medicaid program or service,
25 rather than sacrifice or augment the quality of care for several programs or services through dilution of funding;
26 and
27 (c) giving priority to services that employ the science of prevention to reduce disability and illness,
28 services that treat life-threatening conditions, and services that support independent or assisted living, including

6 in 33-22-132;
7 (d) skilled nursing services in long-term care facilities;

15 pregnant women;
16 (j) services that are provided by physician assistants within the scope of their practice and that are
17 otherwise directly reimbursed as allowed under department rule to an existing provider;
18 (k) health services provided under a physician's orders by a public health department;

21 provided in 33-22-153;

27 the appropriate licensing board, that are attended by certified nurse-midwives licensed under Title 37, chapter

4 (5) Services for persons qualifying for medicaid under the medically needy category of assistance,
5 as described in 53-6-131, may be more limited in amount, scope, and duration than services provided to others
6 qualifying for assistance under the Montana medicaid program. The department is not required to provide all of
7 the services listed in subsections (3) and (4) to persons qualifying for medicaid under the medically needy
8 category of assistance.
9 (6) In accordance with federal law or waivers of federal law that are granted by the secretary of the
10 U.S. department of health and human services, the department may implement limited medicaid benefits, to be
11 known as basic medicaid, for adult recipients who are eligible because they are receiving cash assistance, as
12 defined in 53-4-201, as the specified caretaker relative of a dependent child and for all adult recipients of

17 appropriated by the legislature, whether approval has been received, as provided in 53-1-612, and whether the

19 who is pregnant, meets the criteria for disability provided in Title II of the Social Security Act, 42 U.S.C. 416, et
20 seq., or is less than 21 years of age is entitled to full medicaid coverage.
21 (7) The department may implement, as provided for in Title XIX of the Social Security Act, 42
22 U.S.C. 1396, et seq., as may be amended, a program under medicaid for payment of medicare premiums,
23 deductibles, and coinsurance for persons not otherwise eligible for medicaid.
24 (8) (a) The department may set rates for medical and other services provided to recipients of
25 medicaid and may enter into contracts for delivery of services to individual recipients or groups of recipients.
26 (b) The department shall strive to close gaps in services provided to individuals suffering from
27 mental illness and co-occurring disorders by doing the following:
28 (i) simplifying administrative rules, payment methods, and contracting processes for providing

3 (ii) publishing a report on an annual basis that describes the process that a mental health center or
4 chemical dependency facility, as those terms are defined in 50-5-101, must utilize in order to receive payment
5 from Montana medicaid for services provided to individuals of different ages, diagnoses, and treatments.
6 (9) The services provided under this part may be only those that are medically necessary and that
7 are the most efficient and cost-effective.

9 by the department in accordance with Title XIX of the Social Security Act, 42 U.S.C. 1396, et seq., as may be
10 amended.

12 services prescribed under the early and periodic screening, diagnosis, and treatment benefit, including access
13 to prescription drugs and durable medical equipment for which the department has not negotiated a rebate.
14 (11) Services, procedures, and items of an experimental or cosmetic nature may not be provided.
15 (12) (a) Prior to enacting changes to provider rates, medicaid waivers, or the medicaid state plan,
16 the department shall report this information to the following committees:
17 (i) the children, families, health, and human services interim committee;
18 (ii) the legislative finance committee; and
19 (iii) the health and human services budget committee.
20 (b) In its report to the committees, the department shall provide an explanation for the proposed
21 changes and an estimated budget impact to the department over the next 4 fiscal years.
22 (13) If available funds are not sufficient to provide medical assistance for all eligible persons, the
23 department may set priorities to limit, reduce, or otherwise curtail the amount, scope, or duration of the medical
24 services made available under the Montana medicaid program after taking into consideration the funding
25 principles set forth in subsection (2). (Subsection (3)(o) terminates September 30, 2025--sec. 1, Ch. 298, L.
26 2023; bracketed language in subsection (4)(b) terminates June 30, 2031--sec. 10, Ch. 628, L. 2023.)"

1 "53-6-116. Medicaid managed care -- capitated health care. (1) The department of public health
2 and human services, in its discretion, may develop managed care and capitated health care systems for
3 medicaid recipients.
4 (2) The department may contract with one or more persons for the management of comprehensive
5 physical health services and the management of comprehensive mental health services for medicaid recipients.
6 The department may contract for the provision of these services by means of a fixed monetary or capitated
7 amount for each recipient.
8 (3) A managed care system is a program organized to serve the medical needs of medicaid
9 recipients in an efficient and cost-effective manner by managing the receipt of medical services for a
10 geographical or otherwise defined population of recipients through appropriate health care professionals.
11 (4) The provision of medicaid services through managed care and capitated health care systems is
12 not subject to the limitations provided in 53-6-104. The managed care or capitated health care system that is
13 provided to a defined population of recipients may be based on one or more of the medical assistance services
14 provided for in 53-6-101.
15 (5) The proposed systems, referred to in subsection (1), must be submitted to the legislative
16 finance committee. The legislative finance committee shall review the proposed systems at its next regularly
17 scheduled meeting and shall provide any comments concerning the proposed systems to the department. The
18 department shall provide a copy of any reports made to the legislative finance committee concerning the
19 proposed systems to the legislature in accordance with 5-11-210.
20 (6) A managed care or capitated health care system, except for a primary care case management
21 service, that requires for implementation a waiver from the centers for medicare and medicaid is subject to the
22 provisions of Title 53, chapter 6, part 7.
23 (7) The department may not require an enrollee to participate in a primary care case management
24 service if the enrollee enters into a direct primary care contract DIRECT PATIENT CARE AGREEMENT PURSUANT TO
25 50-4-107."

17 scale to each procedure code used to bill for services provided by a physician.

20 of the U.S. department of health and human services."
