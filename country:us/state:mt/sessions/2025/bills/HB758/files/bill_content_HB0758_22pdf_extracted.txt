4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING INSURANCE LAW TO PROHIBIT BALANCE BILLING
5 FOR OUT-OF-NETWORK EMERGENCIES; APPLYING TO HEALTH INSURANCE PLANS; REQUIRING
6 AMBULANCE GROUND TRANSPORTATION SERVICES TO BE PAID BY THE INSURER AT CERTAIN IN-
7 NETWORK RATES; ESTABLISHING RATES; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; REQUIRING
8 REPORTING TO THE COMMISSIONER OF INSURANCE; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND AMENDING
9 SECTIONS 2-18-704, 30-14-2602, 33-31-111, AND 33-35-306, MCA; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED
10 EFFECTIVE DATE.”

12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Balance billing prohibited -- out-of-network emergency --
15 rulemaking. (1) With respect to a health benefit plan, the state employee group benefits plan, the Montana
16 university system group benefits plan, or a medicare supplement insurance policy that provides coverage for
17 ambulance care and transportation, the insurer shall indemnify directly the provider of the ambulance care and
18 transportation.
19 (2) An ambulance service may not bill an enrollee for covered ground transportation services,
20 including nonemergency transportation between health care facilities, if the enrollee has paid the in-network
21 cost-sharing amount specified in the enrollee's health benefit plan.
22 (3) For ambulance ground transportation services covered by a health benefit plan, the insurer:
23 (a) may not impose an out-of-pocket maximum that exceeds $100 for in-network or out-of-network
24 providers;
25 (b) shall apply any out-of-pocket costs toward an applicable deductible;
26 (c) may not impose a deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, copayment, or coinsurance requirement
27 on services provided by out-of-network providers that exceeds the deductible, out-of-pocket maximum,
28 copayment, or coinsurance requirement imposed on services provided by in-network providers; and

1 (d) shall provide an explanation of benefits to the enrollee that reflects the cost-sharing amount.
2 (4) Unless the health benefit plan and the ambulance service have a contracted rate, the health
3 benefit plan must reimburse the ambulance service at the established local rate, or if an established local rate
4 does not exist, in an amount no less than 400% of the medicare rate.
5 (5) The department shall create a database of established local rates for ambulance ground
6 transportation services. The department shall ensure this database is accessible to the public.
7 (6) Ambulance services must submit a catalog of local ground transportation rates to the
8 department annually and within 5 calendar days of a change to the rates.
9 (7) The provisions of this section apply to a self-funded group health plan, whether governed by or
10 exempt from the provisions of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, 29 U.S.C. 1001 et seq.,
11 as amended, only if the self-funded group health plan elects to participate in the provisions of this section by
12 providing notice to the department in the form and manner described by the department by rule.
13 (8) The department shall make rules to implement the provisions described in this section.
14 (9) The department may impose civil penalties and take appropriate action as provided in Title 33,
15 chapter 1.
16 (10) As used in this section:
17 (a) "Department" means the commissioner of securities and insurance, Montana state auditor.
18 (b) "Enrollee" has the same meaning as provided in 33-1-801.
19 (c) "Health benefit plan" has the same meaning as provided in 33-1-801.
20 (d) "Health care facility" means a facility that provides health care services directly to patients,
21 including but not limited to a hospital, clinic, health care provider's office, health maintenance organization,
22 diagnostic or treatment center, mental health facility, hospice, and nursing home.
23 (e) "In-network" means services performed by a provider or health care service contractor that has
24 directly contracted with the insurer.
25 (f) "Out-of-network" means services performed by a provider or provider group that has not
26 contracted or has indirectly contracted with the insurer or health care service contractor.
