69th Legislature 2025 SB0536.2
2 INTRODUCED BY G. HERTZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE CONTRACTOR'S GROSS RECEIPTS TAX;
5 PROVIDING AN EXEMPTION FROM THE TAX FOR CERTAIN INDIVIDUALS AND BUSINESSES THAT ARE
6 IN GOOD STANDING; REQUIRING AN APPLICATION TO RECEIVE THE EXEMPTION; PROVIDING A
7 DEFINITION THAT A CREDIT MAY BE CLAIMED BY AN EMPLOYEE STOCK OWNERSHIP PLAN
8 COMPANY; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AMENDING SECTION 15-50-101 15-50-207, MCA;
9 PROVIDING FOR APPLICABILITY; AND PROVIDING A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Exemption -- compliance with state tax laws considered --
15 rulemaking .. (1) Subject to subsection (2), an individual who is a r esident of the state or a business that is
16 licensed and in good standing in the state is eligible for an exemption from the provisions of this chapter if the
17 individual or business is in full compliance with all state income tax, payroll tax, and property tax obligations.
18
19 (2) The department shall publish a list of individuals and businesses on the department's website
20 that satisfy the provisions of subsection (1) on an application of the individual or business. An individual or
21 business that is listed on the department's website is exempt from the provisions of this chapter.
22
23 (3) The department may adopt rules to ensure the accuracy of exemption requirements, including
24 rules that provide reasonable deadlines to submit an initial application and any renewals ..
25
26 (4) For the purpose of this section, the term "compliance" means all required state tax forms, tax
27 returns, and tax statements were filed on or before the statutory deadline, including any valid extensions, and
28 all state taxes and wage withholdings were paid on or before the statutory time for payment.

2 Section 2 .. Section 15-50-101, MCA, is amended to read:
3
4 "15-50-101. Definitions. As used in this chapter, the following definitions apply:
5
6 (1) "Department" means the department of revenue as provided in 2-15-1301 ..
7 (2) "Gross receipts" means all receipts from sources within the state, whether in the form of
8 money, credits, or other valuable consideration, received from, engaging in, or conducting a business, without
9 deduction on account of the cost of the property sold, the cost of the materials used, labor or service cost,
10 interest paid, taxes, losses, or any other expense whatsoever. However, gross receipts does not include cash
11 discounts allowed and taken on sales and sales refunds, either in cash or by credit, uncollectible accounts
12 written off from time to time, or payments received in final liquidation of accounts included in the gross receipts
13 of any previous return made by the person.
14 (3) (a) "Public contractor" means any person who submits a proposal to perform or enters into a
15 contract for performing public construction work in the state with the federal government or state of Montana;
16 with any board, commission, or department of the state; with any board of county commissioners, any city or
17 town council, or any agency of any of them; or with any other public board, body, commission, or agency
18 authorized to let or award contracts for any public work when the contract cost, value, or price exceeds the sum
19 of $80,000.
20 (b) The term public contractor includes subcontractors undertaking to perform work covered by the
21 original contract or any part of the contract when the contract cost, value, or price exceeds the sum of $80,000.
22
23 (c) The term does not include a person or business that is exempt from this chapter pursuant to
24 [section 1 (2) ]. "
25
26 SECTION 1. Section 15-50-207, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "15-50-207. Credit against other taxes -- credit for personal property taxes and certain fees --
28 employee stock ownership plan company -- rulemaking. (1) (a) The additional license fees withheld or
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – SB 536
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0536.2
1 otherwise paid as provided in this chapter may be used as a credit or a refund, depending on the type of tax the
2 contractor is required to pay under the laws of the state as follows:
3
4 (i) on the contractor's corporate income tax provided for in chapter 31 of this title or ;
5
6 (ii) on the contractor's income tax provided for in chapter 30; or
7 (iii) as a refund using a form promulgated by the department if the contractor is exempt from
8 corporate or individual income taxes because of the contractor's status as an employee stock ownership plan
9 corporation that is 100% owned by the employees, depending upon the type of tax the contractor is required to
10 pay under the laws of the state.
11 (b) The department may adopt rules to administer the refund provided for in subsection (1)(a)(iii).
12 (b) (c) The credit allowed under this subsection (1) may be used as a carryforward against taxes
13 imposed by chapter 30 or 31 for the 5 succeeding tax years. The entire amount of the credit not used in the
14 year earned must be carried first to the earliest tax year in which the credit may be applied and then to each
15 succeeding tax year.
16 (2) Personal property taxes and the fee in lieu of tax on buses, trucks having a manufacturer's
17 rated capacity of more than 1 ton, or truck tractors, as provided in 61-3-529, and the registration fee on light
18 vehicles, as provided in 61-3-321(2) and 61-3-562, paid in Montana on any personal property or vehicle of the
19 contractor that is used in the business of the contractor and is located within this state may be credited against
20 the license fees required under this chapter. However, in computing the tax credit allowed by this section
21 against the contractor's income tax or corporate income tax, the tax credit against the license fees required
22 under this chapter may not be considered as license fees paid for the purpose of the income tax or corporate
23 income tax credit."
24
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 3 .. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
27 integral part of Title 15, chapter 50, part 1, and the provisions of Title 15, chapter 50, part 1, apply to [section 1].
28
