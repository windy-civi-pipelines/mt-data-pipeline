6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ENHANCING LEGISLATIVE UNDERSTANDING OF STATE-
7 TRIBAL RELATIONS; REQUIRING THE LEGISLATIVE SERVICES DIVISION STATE DIRECTOR OF INDIAN
8 AFFAIRS TO WORK COLLABORATIVELY TO PROVIDE INFORMATION AND TRAINING OPPORTUNITIES
9 TO LEGISLATORS; REVISING THE DEADLINE FOR PROVIDING A REPORT TO TRIBAL GOVERNMENTS;
10 AMENDING SECTIONS SECTION 2-15-143 AND 5-11-112, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. State-tribal relations training. (1) In collaboration with the state director
15 of Indian affairs and the state-tribal relations committee THE LEGISLATIVE SERVICES DIVISION AND THE STATE-TRIBAL
16 RELATIONS COMMITTEE, the legislative services division STATE DIRECTOR OF INDIAN AFFAIRS shall make readily
17 available to all legislators information and training on:
18 (a) Indian law;
19 (b) the history of federal Indian policy; AND
20 (c) the legal rights of tribal members ; and ..
21 (d) social, economic, and cultural issues of concern to tribes.
22 (2) The information and training under subsection (1) must include:
23 (a) regularly updated tribal relations handbooks provided to legislators;
24 (b) information and notifications regarding training offered by the governor's office pursuant to 2-
25 15-143;
26 (c) links to Indian education for all resources provided by the office of public instruction under Title
27 20, chapter 1, part 5, including any courses required to be taken by certified school personnel under standards
28 adopted by the board of public education; and

1 (d) any other resources determined to be valuable by the STATE DIRECTOR OF INDIAN AFFAIRS AND
2 THE legislative services division.

4 Section 2. Section 2-15-143, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "2-15-143. Training and consultation. (1) At least once a year, the governor's office and a trainer
6 selected by the tribal governments shall provide training in Helena or a site mutually agreed upon on to state
7 agency managers and key employees who have regular communication with tribes on the legal status of tribes,
8 the legal rights of tribal members, and social, economic, and cultural issues of concern to tribes. Pursuant to
9 [section 1], the state director of Indian affairs shall collaborate with the legislative services division to ensure
10 that legislators are aware of and invited to participate in this training.
11 (2) At least annually, the governor shall convene in Helena a working meeting with representatives
12 of state agencies and tribal officials, including tribal presiding officers, to discuss:
13 (a) tribal concerns with rules and policies that directly impact tribal government and tribal
14 populations;
15 (b) other issues of concern to either the state or the tribes; and
16 (c) potential solutions to the concerns.
17 (3) By August 15 of each year, each state agency shall submit to the governor a report for the prior
18 fiscal year describing the activities of the state agency relating to tribal government and tribal populations. The
19 report must include:
20 (a) any rule or policy changes that the state agency adopted because of discussions under
21 subsection (2)(a);
22 (b) the process that the state agency has established to identify the activities of the state agency
23 that affect tribes;
24 (c) the efforts of the state agency to promote communication and the government-to-government
25 relationship between the state agency and the tribes; and
26 (d) the efforts of the state agency to ensure tribal consultation and the use of American Indian data
27 in the development and implementation of agency programs that directly affect tribes.
28 (4) By September October 15 of each year, the governor shall provide to each tribal government a

1 report with an overview of all state and tribal activities for the prior fiscal year, including a description of the
2 training required under subsection (1). It is the intent of the legislature that this report be prepared within
3 existing levels of funding."
4

6 Section 3. Section 5-11-112, MCA, is amended to read:
7
8 "5-11-112. Functional organization and responsibilities. (1) The legislative council may establish a
9 functional organization within the legislative services division in order to effectively and efficiently carry out all of
10 the responsibilities delegated to the division by law or legislative rule. The responsibilities of the legislative
11 services division include the following:
12 (a) document services:
13 (i) bill drafting and preparation for introduction;
14 (ii) engrossing and enrolling;
15 (iii) distribution of legislative bills and information;
16 (iv) coordination of legislative printing; and
17 (v) publication of legislative records;
18 (b) research and reference services:
19 (i) general and specialized legislative research; and
20 (ii) legislative reference and information;
21 (c) legal services:
22 (i) legal review of draft bills;
23 (ii) legal counseling on legislative matters;
24 (iii) legal support for consolidated entities; and
25
26 (iv) support for the functions of the code commissioner provided in 1-11-201 ;
27 (d) committee services:
28 (i) research, legal, and administrative staff support for consolidated committees as assigned,
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – SB 311
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0311.2
1 including support for interim committees organized under Title 5, chapter 5, part 2; and
2 (ii) research and legal support for legislative standing and select committees;
3 (e) broadcasting services, in accordance with Title 5, chapter 11, part 11;
4 (f) management and business services:
5 (i) financial records;
6 (ii) claims and payrolls;
7 (iii) coordination of procurement of printing, supplies, and equipment; and
8 (iv) maintenance of property inventories;
9 (g) personnel and administrative services:
10 (i) rules for classification and pay; and
11
12 (ii) personnel and administrative policies; and
13 (h) information technology services:
14 (i) legislative branch network support services;
15 (ii) application support and development;
16 (iii) communications support and coordination; and
17 (iv) information technology planning.; and
18 (i) training for legislators, including state-tribal relations training pursuant to [section 1].
19 (2) The responsibilities of the legislative services division must be fulfilled collaboratively with
20 consolidated entities whenever the efficient operation of the legislative branch is served."
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a
23 copy of [this act] to each federally recognized tribal government in Montana.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
26 integral part of Title 5, chapter 11, part 1, and the provisions of Title 5, chapter 11, part 1, apply to [section 1].
27
28 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
