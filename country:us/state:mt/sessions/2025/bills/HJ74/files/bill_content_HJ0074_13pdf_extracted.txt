1 HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION NO. 74
3 STRAND, R. MINER, G. OBLANDER, K. WALSH, J. COHENOUR, D. HAWK, M. MARLER, T. RUNNING
4 WOLF
5
6 A JOINT RESOLUTION OF THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF
7 MONTANA REQUESTING AN INTERIM STUDY EXAMINING WATER REUSE; AND REQUIRING THAT THE
8 FINAL RESULTS OF THE STUDY BE REPORTED TO THE 70TH LEGISLATURE.
9
10 WHEREAS, Montana's water resources are of exceptional quality and are a valuable resource to the
11 residents of the state; and
12 WHEREAS, water reuse, also known as water recycling, is a process in which wastewater is purified
13 for a designated beneficial freshwater purpose; and
14 WHEREAS, the potential uses of recycled water include agricultural, industrial, and municipal
15 purposes; and
16 WHEREAS, water reuse is a tool to promote sustainable and resilient water supplies and protect
17 environmentally sensitive resources; and
18 WHEREAS, there may exist technical, regulatory, financial, and educational roadblocks preventing the
19 implementation of water reuse programs in Montana; and
20 WHEREAS, individualizing levels of treatment for specific uses is more cost and energy efficient than a
21 standardized treatment approach; and
22 WHEREAS, opportunities for water reuse may be underexplored in Montana.
23
24 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF
25 THE STATE OF MONTANA:
26 That the Legislative Council be requested to designate an appropriate interim committee or statutory
27 committee, pursuant to section 5-5-217, MCA to:
28 (1) examine existing statutes and administrative rules related to water reuse;
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HJ 74
69th Legislature HJ 74.1
1 (2) analyze existing reuse infrastructure, including the volume, timing, use, and availability of
2 reused water;
3 (3) examine how other states have implemented water reuse in the public and private sectors;
4 (4) analyze what industries, except agriculture, and geographic areas in the state currently
5 implement water reuse programs and what industries and geographic areas in the state may benefit from water
6 reuse programs;
7 (5) determine what legal, economic, and social barriers exist for industries and communities
8 attempting to implement water reuse programs;
9 (6) analyze what economic, environmental, and population factors necessitate the implementation
10 of water reuse programs; and
11 (7) if necessary, propose legislation to incentivize and facilitate water reuse programs in the state.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that all aspects of the study, including presentation and review
13 requirements, be concluded prior to September 15, 2026.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the final results of the study, including any findings, conclusions,
15 comments, or recommendations of the appropriate committee, be reported to the 70th Legislature.
16 - END -
