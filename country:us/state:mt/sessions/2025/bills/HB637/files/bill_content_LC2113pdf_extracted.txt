4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR MEDICAL AID IN DYING WHEN AN
5 INDIVIDUAL HAS A TERMINAL ILLNESS AND CAPACITY TO MAKE END-OF-LIFE DECISIONS;
6 PROVIDING REQUIREMENTS FOR PRESCRIBING MEDICAL AID IN DYING MEDICATION; REQUIRING A
7 WAITING PERIOD BEFORE A PRESCRIPTION FOR MEDICAL AID IN DYING MEDICATION IS FILLED;
8 PROVIDING IMMUNITIES AND CONSCIENCE-BASED PROTECTIONS FOR HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS;
9 REQUIRING REPORTING TO THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES;
10 ESTABLISHING CRIMINAL PENALTIES FOR ALTERING OR FORGING A REQUEST OR CONCEALING OR
11 DESTROYING A RESCISSION OF A REQUEST FOR MEDICAL AID IN DYING; ESTABLISHING CRIMINAL
12 PENALTIES FOR COERCING OR EXERTING UNDUE INFLUENCE ON AN INDIVIDUAL TO REQUEST
13 MEDICAL AID IN DYING; PROVIDING A CLAIM FOR GOVERNMENT ENTITIES FOR CERTAIN COSTS
14 INCURRED; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND PROVIDING DEFINITIONS.”
15
16 WHEREAS, Montanans may currently seek medical aid in dying under the Montana Supreme Court
17 decision in Baxter v. State, 2009 MT 449, 354 Mont. 234, 224 P.3d 1211; the Legislature seeks to provide
18 protections for Montanans who seek and health care providers who provide medical aid in dying.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 14] may be cited as the "Montana Patient
23 and Physician Protections in Care Act".
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 14], unless the context
26 clearly indicates otherwise, the following definitions apply:
27 (1) "Adult" means an individual who is 18 years of age or older.
28 (2) "Capacity" means an individual's ability to understand and appreciate health care options

8 medication to be used in medical aid in dying:

26 (9) "Self-administer" means taking an affirmative, conscious, voluntary act to ingest a

3 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Medical aid in dying -- prescribing health care provider
4 determination -- form. A prescribing health care provider may provide a prescription for medical aid in dying
5 medication to an adult only after the prescribing health care provider has:
6 (1) determined that the individual has:
7 (a) capacity;
8 (b) a terminal illness;
9 (c) voluntarily made the request for medical aid in dying; and
10 (d) the ability to self-administer the medical aid in dying medication;
11 (2) provided medical care to the individual in accordance with accepted medical standards of care;
12 (3) determined that the individual is making an informed decision after discussing with the
13 individual the:
14 (a) individual's medical diagnosis and prognosis;
15 (b) potential risks associated with self-administering the medical aid in dying medication that the
16 individual has requested the health care provider to prescribe;
17 (c) probable result of self-administering the medical aid in dying medication to be prescribed;
18 (d) individual's option of choosing to obtain the medical aid in dying medication and then deciding
19 not to use it; and
20 (e) feasible alternative, concurrent, or additional treatment opportunities, including hospice care
21 and palliative care focused on relieving symptoms and reducing suffering;
22 (4) determined in good faith that the individual's request does not arise from coercion or undue
23 influence by another person;
24 (5) noted in the individual's health record the prescribing health care provider's determination that
25 the individual qualifies to receive medical aid in dying;
26 (6) affirmed that the individual is:
27 (a) enrolled in a medicare-certified hospice program; or
28 (b) eligible to receive medical aid in dying after the prescribing health care provider has referred

8 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Determining capacity. If an individual has a recent history of a mental
9 health disorder or an intellectual disability that could cause impaired judgment with regard to end-of-life medical
10 decisionmaking, or if, in the opinion of the prescribing health care provider or consulting health care provider,
11 an individual currently has a mental health disorder or an intellectual disability that may cause impaired
12 judgment with regard to end-of-life medical decisionmaking, the individual may not be determined to have
13 capacity to make end-of-life decisions until:
14 (1) THE health care provider refers the individual for evaluation by a mental health professional with
15 the training and expertise to assess a person with such a disorder or disability; and
16 (2) THE mental health professional determines the individual has capacity to make END-OF-LIFE
17 decisions after evaluating the individual during one or more visits with the individual.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Waiting period. (1) A prescription for medical aid in dying medication
20 may not be filled until 48 hours after the prescription for medical aid in dying medication has been written,
21 unless the qualified individual's prescribing health care provider has medically confirmed that the qualified
22 individual may, within reasonable medical judgment, die before the expiration of the waiting period required in
23 this section, in which case the prescription may be filled AFTER the prescribing health care provider affirms that
24 all requirements have been fulfilled pursuant to [section 3].
25 (2) A prescription for medical aid in dying medication must indicate the date and time that the
26 prescription for medical aid in dying medication was written and indicate the first allowable date and time when
27 it may be filled.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Medical aid in dying -- right to know. (1) A health care provider shall
2 inform a terminally ill patient of all reasonable options related to the patient's care that are legally available to
3 terminally ill patients who meet the medical standards of care for end-of-life care.
4 (2) A health care provider fails to obtain informed consent for subsequent medical treatment if an
5 individual requests information about medical aid in dying and within a reasonable time, the provider has failed,
6 at a minimum, to do either of the following:
7 (a) provide information to the individual about medical aid in dying and other legal end-of-life
8 options; or
9 (b) refer the individual to a health care provider who is able and willing to carry out the individual's
10 request or to another individual or entity to assist the requesting individual in seeking medical aid in dying.
11 (3) If the health care provider transfers the individual's care to a new health care provider, the prior
12 health care provider shall transfer, upon request, a copy of the individual's relevant medical records to the new
13 health care provider.
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Safe disposal of unused medication. A person who has custody or
16 control of medication prescribed pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] after the qualified individual's death shall
17 dispose of the medication by lawful means in accordance with state and federal guidelines.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Immunities -- conscience-based decisions. (1) A person may not be
20 subject to civil or criminal liability, licensing sanctions, or other professional disciplinary action for:
21 (a) participating, or refusing to participate, in medical aid in dying in good faith compliance with the
22 provisions of [sections 1 through 14]; or
23 (b) being present when a qualified patient self-administers the prescribed medical aid in dying
24 medication to end the qualified individual's life in accordance with the provisions of [sections 1 through 14].
25 (2) A health care entity, professional organization or association, health insurer, managed care
26 organization, or health care provider may not subject a person to censure, discipline, suspension, loss or denial
27 of license, credential, privileges, or membership, or other penalty for participating, or refusing to participate, in
28 the provision of medical aid in dying in good faith compliance with the provisions of [sections 1 through 14].

14 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Prohibited acts. Nothing in [sections 1 through 14] may be construed to
15 authorize a physician or any other person to end an individual's life by lethal injection, mercy killing, or
16 euthanasia. Actions taken in accordance with [sections 1 through 14] may not be construed, for any purpose, to
17 constitute suicide, assisted suicide, euthanasia, mercy killing, homicide, or adult abuse under the law.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Reporting -- rulemaking. (1) A health care provider who prescribes
20 medical aid in dying to a qualified individual in accordance with the provisions of [sections 1 through 14] shall
21 provide, in accordance with department of public health and human services rules, a report of that provider's
22 participation. The department shall adopt rules that establish the timeframes and forms for reporting pursuant to
23 this section and shall limit the reporting of data relating to qualified individuals who received prescriptions for
24 medical aid in dying medication to the following:
25 (a) the qualified individual's age at death;
26 (b) the qualified individual's race and ethnicity;
27 (c) the qualified individual's gender;
28 (d) whether the qualified individual was enrolled in hospice at the time of death;

1 (e) the qualified individual's underlying medical condition; and
2 (f) whether the qualified individual self-administered the medical aid in dying medication and, if so,
3 the date that this occurred.
4 (2) The department of public health and human services shall promulgate an annual statistical
5 report containing aggregated data on the information collected pursuant to subsection (1) on the total number of
6 medical aid in dying medication prescriptions written statewide and on the number of health care providers who
7 have issued prescriptions for medical aid in dying medication during that year. Data reported pursuant to this
8 subsection may not contain individually identifiable health information and individually identifiable health
9 information is exempt from disclosure.

11 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Effect on insurance and construction of wills, contracts, and
12 statutes. (1) Death resulting from medical aid in dying medication in accordance with [sections 1 through 14]
13 does not constitute, for any purpose, a suicide or a homicide.
14 (2) The sale, procurement, or issuance of a policy of life, health, or accident insurance, or annuity
15 policy, or the rate charged for the policy may not be conditioned on or affected by an individual's act of making
16 or rescinding a request for medication pursuant to [sections 1 through 14].
17 (3) A qualified individual's act of self-administering medication pursuant to [sections 1 through 14]
18 does not invalidate any part of a life, health, or accident insurance, or annuity policy, notwithstanding any term
19 of the policy to the contrary.
20 (4) It is unlawful for an insurer to deny or alter health care benefits otherwise available to an
21 individual with a terminal disease based on the availability of medical aid in dying or otherwise attempt to
22 coerce an individual with a terminal disease to make or rescind a request for medical aid in dying medication.
23 (5) A provision in a contract, will, or other agreement, whether written or oral, that would determine
24 whether an individual may make or rescind a request pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] is not valid.
25 (6) AN obligation owing under any currently existing contract may NOT be conditioned or affected
26 by an individual's act of making or rescinding a request pursuant to [sections 1 through 14].

12 NEW SECTION. Section 13. Liabilities and penalties. (1) A person who intentionally or knowingly
13 alters or forges an individual's request for medication pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] or conceals or
14 destroys a rescission for medication pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] is guilty of a felony and subject to the
15 penalties set forth in 46-18-213.
16 (2) A person who intentionally or knowingly coerces or exerts undue influence on an individual with
17 a terminal disease to request medication pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] or to request or use medication
18 pursuant to [sections 1 through 14] is guilty of a felony and subject to the penalties set forth in 46-18-213.
19 (3) Nothing in [sections 1 through 14] limits civil liability or damages arising from negligent conduct
20 or intentional misconduct by the health care provider or health care entity.
21 (4) The penalties specified in [sections 1 through 14] do not preclude criminal penalties under
22 other laws for conduct inconsistent with [sections 1 through 14].
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 14. Claim by government entity for costs incurred. A government entity
25 that incurs costs resulting from self-administration of medication prescribed under [sections 1 through 14] in a
26 public place has a claim against the estate of the qualified individual to recover the costs and reasonable
27 attorney fees related to enforcing the claim.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Determining capacity. If an individual has a recent history of a mental

17 decisions after evaluating the individual during one or more visits with the individual.
