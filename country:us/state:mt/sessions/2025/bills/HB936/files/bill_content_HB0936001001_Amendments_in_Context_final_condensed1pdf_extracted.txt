1 HOUSE BILL NO. 936
2 INTRODUCED BY J. GILLETTE
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT IMPLEMENTING THE PROVISIONS OF THE GENERAL
5 APPROPRIATIONS ACT; REQUIRING THAT A PORTION OF THE HOSPITAL UTILIZATION FEE BE USED
6 FOR HOSPITAL QUALITY AND EFFICIENCY INCENTIVE PAYMENTS; REQUIRING THAT A PORTION OF
7 THE HOSPITAL UTILIZATION FEE BE USED FOR PAYMENTS TO INDEPENDENT NONAFFILIATED
8 CRITICAL ACCESS HOSPITALS; ALLOWING LOAN REPAYMENT AND FUNDING FOR ADDITIONAL BEDS
9 AT THE MONTANA STATE HOSPITAL TO BE ELIGIBLE USES OF THE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH SYSTEM
10 FOR FUTURE GENERATIONS FUND ON A ONE-TIME BASIS; ESTABLISHING REPORTING
11 REQUIREMENTS; AMENDING SECTIONS 50-1-119 AND 53-6-149, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
12 DATE AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 Section 1. Section 50-1-119, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "50-1-119. Behavioral health system for future generations fund. (1) There is an account in the
18 state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102 to be known as the behavioral health system for future
19 generations fund.
20 (2) There must be deposited in the account money received from legislative general fund transfers.
21 (3) Eligible uses of the fund include:
22 (a) medicaid and CHIP matching funds for payments made to behavioral health settings;
23 (b) medicaid and CHIP matching funds for payments made to intermediate care facilities for
24 individuals with intellectual disabilities;
25 (c) statewide community-based investments to stabilize behavioral health and developmental
26 disabilities service providers and delivery, increase and strengthen the behavioral health and developmental
27 disabilities workforce, increase service capacity to meet identified behavioral health and developmental
28 disabilities services demands, and increase opportunities for Montanans to receive integrated physical and

1 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Montana state hospital reporting. (1) Starting July 1, 2025, and each
2 month thereafter, the department of public health and human services shall submit a written report on the
3 Montana state hospital. The report must outline the department's hiring efforts and following data:
4 (a) the number of applications received;
5 (b) the number of qualified applicants;
6 (c) the number of interviews conducted; and
7 (d) the number of new employees hired.
8 (2) The monthly reports must be submitted to the legislative finance committee, the interim budget
9 committee section B, and the interim budget committee section F.
10
11 Section 5. Section 53-6-149, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "53-6-149. State special revenue fund account -- administration. (1) There is a hospital medicaid
13 reimbursement account in the state special revenue fund provided for in 17-2-102.
14 (2) All money collected under 15-66-102[, except for the money deposited pursuant to 15-66-
15 102(3)(b) into the Montana HELP Act special revenue account provided for in 53-6-1315,] must be deposited in
16 the account.
17 (3) (a) Money in the account must be used by the department of public health and human services
18 to provide funding [no later than May 5 of each year] for increases in medicaid payments to hospitals and for
19 the costs of collection of the fee and other administrative activities associated with the implementation of
20 increases in the medicaid payments to hospitals.
21 (b) The department shall use 2% of the total revenue generated by the inpatient hospital utilization
22 fee in 15-66-102 as a match :
23 (i) for payments to independent nonaffiliated hospitals for the purposes of maintaining access and
24 quality in the most rural and frontier areas in Montana; and
25 (ii) for incentive payments to hospitals that demonstrate exceptional patient-centered and
26 efficiency outcomes. The department may retain a portion of the funds for implementation of the program in this
27 subsection (3)(b)(ii).
28 (c) The department shall use 10% of the total revenue generated by the inpatient hospital

1 utilization fee in 15-66-102 for hospital reimbursement adjustor payments for critical access hospitals as
2 provided for in ARM 37.86.2928(4). (Bracketed language terminates June 30, 2025, on occurrence of
3 contingency--sec. 48, Ch. 415, L. 2019.)"
4
5 COORDINATION SECTION. Section 6. Coordination instruction. The appropriation in House Bill
6 No. 2 for certified community behavioral health centers is contingent on the passage and approval of House Bill
7 No. 574. If House Bill No. 574 is not passed and approved, the appropriation in House Bill No. 2 is void.
8
9 COORDINATION SECTION. Section 7. Coordination instruction. If both House Bill No. 2 and [this
10 act] are passed and approved, then the following language in House Bill No. 2 is void:
11 "The line-item Supplemental Payments to Independent Critical Access Hospitals (Restricted) is
12 restricted to payments to independent critical access hospitals."
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Termination. [This act] terminates June 30, 2027.
17 - END -
