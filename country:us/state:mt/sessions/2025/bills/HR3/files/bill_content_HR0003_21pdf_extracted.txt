6 ROMANO, E. STAFMAN, K. SULLIVAN, M. THANE, P. TUSS, S. DEMAROIS, D. JOY, C. FITZPATRICK
7
8 A RESOLUTION OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF MONTANA URGING THE
9 MONTANA CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION TO ADVOCATE TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED
10 STATES TO PROTECT THE PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION OF MONTANANS FROM THE
11 DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY.
12
13 WHEREAS, online privacy and security is important to Montanans; and
14 WHEREAS, Montana prides itself in its robust privacy laws that protect the identities and financial
15 security of all Montanans, including laws related to online computer crimes, online privacy, and data security;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, the newly established Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE), a federal
18 organization created by executive order and not through an act of the United States Congress, recently gained
19 access to the federal digital infrastructure containing the names, addresses, social security numbers, and other
20 personally identifiable information of millions of American citizens, including thousands of Montanans; and
21 WHEREAS, due to uncertainties about the type of access gained by the DOGE, how the acquired
22 information will be used, and whether there will be any oversight of the DOGE, Americans are afraid that their
23 personally identifiable information is at risk, and many citizens are resorting to freezing their credit as a
24 precaution; and
25 WHEREAS, it is unacceptable to use personally identifiable information in any way that is not entirely
26 secure, and it is the responsibility of the federal government to provide that protection; and
27 WHEREAS, Montanans should have the same protections at the federal level against breaches of
28 personally identifiable information as they do at the state level.
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HR 3
69th Legislature - 2025 HR0003.2
1
2 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE STATE OF
3 MONTANA:
4 That the House of Representatives of the 69th Legislature request that the Montana Congressional
5 Delegation act immediately to secure all databases that contain personally identifiable information of
6 Montanans.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Secretary of State send a copy of this resolution to each
8 member of the Montana Congressional Delegation AND THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES.
9 - END -
