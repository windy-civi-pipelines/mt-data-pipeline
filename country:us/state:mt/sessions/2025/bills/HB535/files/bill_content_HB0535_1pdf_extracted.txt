17 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Timeline for delivering person. After the person is arrested upon a
18 governor's warrant of arrest and rendition of this state, or a waiver of extradition has been executed, the
19 timeline for the transfer of the person to the agent or agents of another state must be set as provided in the
20 federal Extradition Act, 18 U.S.C. 3182, as of December 17, 2024. If a hold exists under 46-30-202, the timeline
21 for the transfer of the person is tolled until the person has been tried and discharged or convicted and punished
22 in this state.

28 the matter; and

4 Section 3. Section 46-30-101, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "46-30-101. Definitions. As used in this chapter, the term:
6 (1) "executive authority" includes the governor and any person performing the functions of
7 governor in a state other than this state or the presiding officer of a federally recognized Indian tribe within the
8 state of Montana United States;
9 (2) "governor" includes any person performing the functions of governor by authority of the law of
10 this state;
11 (3) "governor's warrant of arrest and rendition" refers to the warrant issued by the governor
12 authorizing the extradition of the person to another state;
13 (4) "requisition" refers to the formal, written extradition demand in the form of a warrant issued by
14 the governor or by the executive authority of a demanding state;
15 (3)(5) "state", when referring to a state other than this state, includes any other state or territory,
16 organized or unorganized, of the United States of America or an Indian reservation within the state of Montana
17 United States;
18 (6) "waiver of extradition" means a written agreement stipulating the person's consent to be
19 transported to a demanding state through a voluntary waiver of the person's right to provisions relating to bail,
20 the issuance and service of the governor's warrant of arrest and rendition, and the right to obtain a writ of
21 habeas corpus."
