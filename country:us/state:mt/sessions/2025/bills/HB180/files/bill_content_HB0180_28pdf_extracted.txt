5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING SANITATION IN SUBDIVISION LAWS; PROHIBITING
6 A LOCAL BOARD OF HEALTH FROM ADOPTING REGULATIONS THAT WOULD ALLOW FOR A MIXING
7 ZONE THAT ENCROACHES ON ADJACENT PROPERTY; PROVIDING THAT THE SANITATION IN
8 SUBDIVISIONS ACT DOES NOT APPLY TO CERTAIN DRAINFIELD MIXING ZONES; REVISING A
9 DEFINITION; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 50-2-116 AND 76-4-102, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 Section 1. Section 50-2-116, MCA, is amended to read:
14 "50-2-116. Powers and duties of local boards of health. (1) Except as provided in subsection (5),
15 in order to carry out the purposes of the public health system, in collaboration with federal, state, and local
16 partners, each local board of health shall:
17 (a) recommend to the governing body the appointment of a local health officer who is:
18 (i) a physician;
19 (ii) a person with a master's degree in public health; or
20 (iii) a person with equivalent education and experience, as determined by the department;
21 (b) elect a presiding officer and other necessary officers;
22 (c) adopt bylaws to govern meetings;
23 (d) hold regular meetings at least quarterly and hold special meetings as necessary;
24 (e) identify, assess, prevent, and ameliorate conditions of public health importance through:
25 (i) epidemiological tracking and investigation;
26 (ii) screening and testing;
27 (iii) isolation and quarantine measures;
28 (iv) diagnosis, treatment, and case management;

1 (v) abatement of public health nuisances;
2 (vi) inspections;
3 (vii) collecting and maintaining health information;
4 (viii) education and training of health professionals; or
5 (ix) other public health measures as allowed by law;
6 (f) protect the public from the introduction and spread of communicable disease or other
7 conditions of public health importance, including through actions to ensure the removal of filth or other
8 contaminants that might cause disease or adversely affect public health;
9 (g) supervise or make inspections for conditions of public health importance and issue written
10 orders for compliance or for correction, destruction, or removal of the conditions;
11 (h) bring and pursue actions and issue orders necessary to abate, restrain, or prosecute the
12 violation of public health laws, rules, and local regulations;
13 (i) identify to the department an administrative liaison for public health. The liaison must be the
14 local health officer in jurisdictions that employ a full-time local health officer. In jurisdictions that do not employ a
15 full-time local health officer, the liaison must be the highest ranking public health professional employed by the
16 jurisdiction.
17 (j) subject to the provisions of 50-2-130, propose for adoption by the local governing body
18 necessary regulations that are not less stringent than state standards for the control and disposal of sewage
19 from private and public buildings and facilities that are not regulated by Title 75, chapter 6, or Title 76, chapter
20 4. The regulations must:
21 (i) describe standards for granting variances from the minimum requirements that are identical to
22 standards promulgated by the department of environmental quality and must provide for appeal of variance
23 decisions to the department of environmental quality as required by 75-5-305;
24 (ii) FOR PARCELS CREATED AFTER MARCH 30, 2011, prohibit the construction of a subsurface
25 wastewater treatment system if the associated mixing zone would encroach onto adjacent property, except as
26 allowed in 76-4-104(7)(i); and.
27 (iii) If if the local board of health regulates or permits water well drilling, the regulations must
28 prohibit the drilling of a well if the well isolation zone, as defined in 76-4-102, encroaches onto adjacent private

6 the control and disposal of sewage from private and public buildings and facilities;

8 or rules adopted by the department:
9 (i) for the control of communicable diseases;
10 (ii) for the removal of filth that might cause disease or adversely affect public health;

12 that affects public health and for the maintenance of sewage treatment systems that do not discharge effluent
13 directly into state water and that are not required to have an operating permit as required by rules adopted
14 under 75-5-401;
15 (iv) subject to the provisions of 50-2-130 and Title 50, chapter 48, for tattooing and body-piercing
16 establishments and that are not less stringent than state standards for tattooing and body-piercing
17 establishments;
18 (v) for the establishment of institutional controls that have been selected or approved by the:

21 seq.; or

23 Comprehensive Environmental Cleanup and Responsibility Act, Title 75, chapter 10, part 7; and
24 (vi) to implement the public health laws;

3 (3) A local board of health may provide, implement, facilitate, or encourage other public health
4 services and functions as considered reasonable and necessary.
5 (4) A directive, mandate, or order issued by a local board of health in response to a declaration of
6 emergency or disaster by the governor as allowed in 10-3-303 or by the principal executive officer of a political
7 subdivision as allowed in 10-3-402 and 10-3-403:
8 (a) remains in effect only during the declared state of emergency or disaster or until the governing
9 body holds a public meeting and allows public comment and the majority of the governing body moves to
10 amend, rescind, or otherwise change the directive, mandate, or order; and
11 (b) may not interfere with or otherwise limit, modify, or abridge a person's physical attendance at or
12 operation of a religious facility, church, synagogue, or other place of worship.

14 mandate, or order implemented to carry out the provisions of this part that applies to the entire jurisdictional
15 area of a town, city, or county under the jurisdiction of the local health board may not:
16 (a) compel a private business to deny a customer of the private business access to the premises
17 or access to goods or services;
18 (b) deny a customer of a private business the ability to access goods or services provided by the
19 private business; or
20 (c) include any of the following actions for noncompliance of actions described in subsections
21 (5)(a) and (5)(b):
22 (i) require the assessment of a fee or fine;
23 (ii) require the revocation of a license required for the operation of a private business;
24 (iii) find a private business owner guilty of a misdemeanor; or
25 (iv) bring any other retributive action against a private business owner, including but not limited to
26 an action allowed under 50-2-123, a penalty allowed under 50-2-124, or any other criminal charge.
27 (6) The prohibition provided for in subsection (5)(b) does not apply to persons confirmed to have a
28 communicable disease and who are currently under a public isolation order.

6 entity, a corporation, a sole proprietorship, or a limited liability company."

8 Section 2. Section 76-4-102, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "76-4-102. Definitions. As used in this part, unless the context clearly indicates otherwise, the
10 following definitions apply:
11 (1) "Adequate county water and/or sewer district facilities" means facilities provided by a county
12 water and/or sewer district incorporated under Title 7, chapter 13, that operate in compliance with Title 75,
13 chapters 5 and 6.
14 (2) "Adequate municipal facilities" means municipally, publicly, or privately owned facilities that
15 supply water, treat sewage, or dispose of solid waste for all or most properties within the boundaries of a
16 municipality and that are operating in compliance with Title 75, chapters 5 and 6, including development plans
17 approved by the department pursuant to 75-6-130.
18 (3) "Board" means the board of environmental review.
19 (4) "Certifying authority" means a municipality or a county water and/or sewer district that meets
20 the eligibility requirements established by the department under 76-4-104(7).
21 (5) "Department" means the department of environmental quality.
22 (6) "Extension of a public sewage system" means a sewerline that connects two or more sewer
23 service lines to a sewer main.
24 (7) "Extension of a public water supply system" means a waterline that connects two or more water
25 service lines to a water main.
26 (8) "Facilities" means public or private facilities for the supply of water or disposal of sewage or
27 solid waste and any pipes, conduits, or other stationary method by which water, sewage, or solid wastes might
28 be transported or distributed.

3 (10) "Individual water system" means any water system that serves one living unit or commercial
4 unit and that is not a public water supply system as defined in 75-6-102.
5 (11) "Mixing zone" has the meaning provided in 75-5-103.
6 (12) (a) "Proposed drainfield mixing zone" means a mixing zone submitted for approval under this
7 chapter after March 30, 2011, FOR A PARCEL THAT WAS CREATED AFTER MARCH 30, 2011.

9 chapter prior to March 30, 2011 The term does not include a mixing zone approved under this chapter before

11 (13) (a) "Proposed well isolation zone" means a well isolation zone submitted for approval under
12 this chapter after October 1, 2013.
13 (b) The term does not include well isolation zones that existed or were approved under this chapter
14 prior to October 1, 2013.
15 (14) "Public sewage system" or "public sewage disposal system" means a public sewage system as
16 defined in 75-6-102.
17 (15) "Public water supply system" has the meaning provided in 75-6-102.
18 (16) "Regional authority" means any regional water authority, regional wastewater authority, or

23 chapter 40.

25 to conduct a review under 76-4-104.

27 building requiring facilities for the supply of water or the disposition of sewage or solid waste or the construction

5 (23) "Solid waste" has the meaning provided in 75-10-103.
