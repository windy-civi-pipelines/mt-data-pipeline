10 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING INDIGENOUS PEOPLES' DAY AS A LEGAL
11 HOLIDAY IN MONTANA; AND AMENDING SECTION 1-1-216, MCA.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Section 1-1-216, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "1-1-216. Legal holidays and business days. (1) The following are legal holidays in the state of
17 Montana:
18 (a) Each Sunday;
19 (b) New Year's Day, January 1;
20 (c) Martin Luther King Jr. Day, the third Monday in January;
21 (d) Lincoln's and Washington's Birthdays PRESIDENTS' DAY, the third Monday in February;
22 (e) Memorial Day, the last Monday in May;
23 (f) Independence Day, July 4;
24 (g) Labor Day, the first Monday in September;
25 (h) Indigenous Peoples' Day and Columbus Day and Indigenous Peoples' Day, the second
26 Monday in October;
27 (i) Veterans' Day, November 11;

1 (j) Thanksgiving Day, the fourth Thursday in November;
2 (k) Christmas Day, December 25;
3 (l) State general election day.
4 (2) (a) If any of the holidays in subsection (1)(b) through (1)(l) fall on a Sunday, the Monday
5 following is a holiday.
6 (b) If any of the holidays in subsection (1)(b) through (1)(l) fall on a Saturday, the Friday preceding
7 is a holiday.
8 (c) All other days are business days."
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Notification to Montana supreme court. The secretary of state shall
11 send a copy of [this act] to the Montana supreme court. The Montana supreme court is requested to update the
12 definition of "legal holiday" in its rules of civil procedure to reflect the provisions of [section 1].
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Notification to local governments. The secretary of state shall send a
15 copy of [this act] to the Montana league of cities and towns and to the Montana association of counties.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a
18 copy of [this act] to each federally recognized tribal government in Montana.
19 - END -
