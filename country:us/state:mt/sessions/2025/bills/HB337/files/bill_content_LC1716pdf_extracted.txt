4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING INCOME TAXES; REVISING BRACKETS
5 TO LOWER INCOME TAXES; INCREASING THE AMOUNT OF MONTANA TAXABLE INCOME BEFORE
6 APPLICATION OF A HIGHER RATE OF TAX; REDUCING THE HIGHEST INCOME TAX RATE; AMENDING
7 SECTION 15-30-2103, MCA; AND PROVIDING EFFECTIVE DATES, APPLICABILITY DATES, AND A
8 TERMINATION DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 15-30-2103, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "15-30-2103. Rate of tax -- net long-term capital gains -- definitions. (1) Except as provided in
14 subsections (2) and (3) subsection (2), there must be levied, collected, and paid for each tax year on the
15 Montana taxable income of each taxpayer subject to this chapter a tax on the brackets of taxable income as
16 follows:
17 (a) for every married individual who files a joint return and for every surviving spouse:
18 (i) on the first $41,000 $140,000 of Montana taxable income or any part of that income, 4.7%;
19 (ii) on any Montana taxable income in excess of $41,000 $140,000 or any part of that income,
20 5.9% 5.65%;
21 (b) for every head of household:
22 (i) on the first $30,750 $105,000 of Montana taxable income or any part of that income, 4.7%;
23 (ii) on any Montana taxable income in excess of $30,750 $105,000 or any part of that income,
24 5.9% 5.65%;
25 (c) for every individual other than a surviving spouse or head of household who is not a married
26 individual:
27 (i) on the first $20,500 $70,000 of Montana taxable income or any part of that income, 4.7%;
28 (ii) on any Montana taxable income in excess of $20,500 $70,000 or any part of that income, 5.9%

24 individual:

26 3.0%;

28 income or any part of that income, 4.1%, except that if the total nonqualified taxable income is $20,500 $70,000

7 income or any part of that income, 4.1%, except that if the total nonqualified taxable income is $20,500 $70,000
8 or greater, all of the net long-term capital gains are taxed at 4.1%.
9 (3) By November 1 of each year, the department shall multiply the bracket amounts contained in
10 subsections (1) and (2) by the inflation factor for the following tax year and round the cumulative brackets to the
11 nearest $100. The resulting adjusted brackets are effective for that following tax year and must be used as the
12 basis for imposition of the tax in subsections (1) and (2).
13 (3) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
14 (a) "Net long-term capital gains" means net long-term capital gains as that term is defined in
15 section 1222 of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C. 1222.
16 (b) "Nonqualified taxable income" means Montana taxable income that is not considered net long-
17 term capital gains."
