3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE
5 PROTECTION LAWS; DEFINING BROADBAND AND WIRED COMMUNICATIONS AS A CRITICAL
6 INFRASTRUCTURE FACILITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 82-1-601 AND 82-1-602, MCA.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 Section 1. Section 82-1-601, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "82-1-601. Definitions. As used in 82-1-601 through 82-1-604, the following definitions apply:
12 (1) "Critical infrastructure" means systems and assets, whether physical or virtual, so vital to the
13 United States that the incapacity or destruction of the systems and assets would have a debilitating impact on
14 security, national economic security, national public health or safety, or any combination of those matters.
15 (2) "Critical infrastructure facility" means:
16 (a) one of the following, if completely enclosed by a fence or other physical barrier that is obviously
17 designed to exclude intruders, or if clearly marked with a sign or signs that are posted on the property that
18 indicate that entry is forbidden without site authorization:
19 (i) a petroleum or alumina refinery;
20 (ii) an electric generating facility, substation, switching station, electrical control center, or electric
21 transmission and distribution lines and associated equipment infrastructure;
22 (iii) a chemical, polymer, or rubber manufacturing facility;
23 (iv) a water intake structure, water treatment facility, wastewater treatment plant, or pump station;
24 (v) a natural gas compressor station, including but not limited to pipeline interconnections, a city
25 gate or town border station, a metering station, aboveground piping, and a regulation station and natural gas
26 storage facility;
27 (vi) a liquid natural gas terminal or storage facility;

7 Section 2. Section 82-1-602, MCA, is amended to read:
8 "82-1-602. Criminal penalties. (1) Except as provided in 82-1-604, a person who willfully and
9 knowingly purposely or knowingly trespasses on property containing a critical infrastructure facility:
10 (a) that is completely enclosed by a fence or other physical barrier that is obviously designed to
11 exclude intruders with intent to willfully purposely or knowingly damage, destroy, vandalize, deface, tamper with
12 equipment, or materially impede or inhibit operations of the facility shall, on conviction, be guilty of a felony
13 punishable by a fine of not more than $4,500 or by imprisonment for not more than 18 months or both; and
14 (b) that is clearly marked with a sign or signs that are posted on the property that indicate that
15 entry is forbidden without site authorization with intent to willfully purposely or knowingly damage, destroy,
16 vandalize, deface, tamper with equipment, or materially impede or inhibit operations of the facility shall, on
17 conviction, be guilty of a felony punishable by a fine of not more than $4,500 or by imprisonment for not more
18 than 18 months or both.
19 (2) A person who willfully purposely or knowingly damages, destroys, vandalizes, defaces, or
20 tampers with the equipment in of a critical infrastructure facility:
21 (a) causing less than $1,500 in damages shall, on conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor and may
22 be incarcerated for any term not to exceed 6 months or be fined an amount not to exceed $500, or both, and
23 must be ordered to make restitution in an amount and manner to be set by the court; and
24 (b) causing damages greater than $1,500 shall, on conviction, be guilty of a felony punishable by a
25 fine of not more than $150,000 or by imprisonment for not more than 30 years, or both.

27 are found to have committed any of the crimes provided in subsection (1) or (2) may be punished by a fine up
