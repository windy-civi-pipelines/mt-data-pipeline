5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING EDUCATION LAWS RELATED TO
6 RECRUITMENT AND RETENTION AND THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND; MAKING
7 EDUCATIONAL SIGN LANGUAGE INTERPRETERS ELIGIBLE FOR THE QUALITY EDUCATOR PAYMENT
8 AND THE QUALITY EDUCATOR LOAN ASSISTANCE PROGRAM; EXPANDING THE TYPES OF
9 EDUCATIONAL ENTITIES THAT CAN RECEIVE INCENTIVES FOR MEETING STARTING TEACHER PAY
10 BENCHMARKS AND THAT CAN PARTICIPATE IN THE TEACHER RESIDENCY PROGRAM; ALLOWING
11 THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND TO TRANSPORT RESIDENT STUDENTS BETWEEN
12 HOME AND THE SCHOOL BY SCHOOL BUS; AMENDING SECTIONS 20-4-502, 20-4-503, 20-4-702, 20-8-
13 121, 20-9-324, AND 20-9-327, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
14

18 "20-4-502. Definitions. For the purposes of this part, unless the context requires otherwise, the
19 following definitions apply:
20 (1) "Education cooperative" means a cooperative of Montana public schools as described in 20-7-
21 451.
22 (2) "Educational loans" means all loans made pursuant to a federal loan program, except federal
23 parent loans for undergraduate students (PLUS) loans, as provided in 20 U.S.C. 1078-2.
24 (3) "Federal loan program" means educational loans authorized by 20 U.S.C. 1071, et seq., 20
25 U.S.C. 1087a, et seq., and 20 U.S.C. 1087aa, et seq.
26 (4) "Impacted school" means:
27 (a) a special education cooperative;
28 (b) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;

5 Section 4. Section 20-8-121, MCA, is amended to read:
6 "20-8-121. Transportation of students at school. (1) The school for the deaf and blind shall provide
7 the transportation expenses allowed in subsection (4) for a residential student at the school for the deaf and
8 blind who is a resident of the state of Montana if the student is conveyed to and from the student's residence
9 by:
10 (a) a scheduled air carrier as defined in 67-1-101;
11 (b) charter with a commercial air operator as defined in 67-1-101;
12 (c) a parent or guardian of the student, under an individual transportation contract with the school
13 for the deaf and blind;
14 (d) a school bus as defined in 20-10-101; or
15 (d)(e) other transportation arrangements, provided that the transportation is by a carrier of
16 passengers certified by the public service commission and approved by the superintendent of the school for the
17 deaf and blind, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of public education.
18 (2) The superintendent of the school for the deaf and blind shall determine which method of
19 transportation in subsection (1) is to be provided to a student, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of public
20 education on transportation of residential and boarding students at the school.
21 (3) A parent or guardian who transports a student to or from the school under an individual
22 transportation contract is entitled to reimbursement for transportation, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of
23 public education on reimbursement.
24 (4) The transportation of a residential student provided in subsection (1) is limited to the number of
25 round trips to the student's residence as specified in the school calendar approved by the board of public
26 education. The superintendent of the school for the deaf and blind may grant a variance from this provision, but
27 in no event may a reimbursement for travel expenses be provided for travel in excess of the total number of
28 trips approved in any school fiscal year."
