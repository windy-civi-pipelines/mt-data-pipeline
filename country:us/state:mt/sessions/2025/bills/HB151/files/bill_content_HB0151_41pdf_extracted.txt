5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING EDUCATION LAWS RELATED TO
6 RECRUITMENT AND RETENTION AND THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND; MAKING
7 EDUCATIONAL SIGN LANGUAGE INTERPRETERS ELIGIBLE FOR THE QUALITY EDUCATOR PAYMENT
8 AND THE QUALITY EDUCATOR LOAN ASSISTANCE PROGRAM; EXPANDING THE TYPES OF
9 EDUCATIONAL ENTITIES THAT CAN RECEIVE INCENTIVES FOR MEETING STARTING TEACHER PAY
10 BENCHMARKS AND THAT CAN PARTICIPATE IN THE TEACHER RESIDENCY PROGRAM; ALLOWING
11 THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND TO TRANSPORT RESIDENT STUDENTS BETWEEN
12 HOME AND THE SCHOOL BY SCHOOL BUS; AMENDING SECTIONS 20-4-502, 20-4-503, 20-4-702, 20-8-
13 121, 20-9-324, AND 20-9-327, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE DATES AND AN

18 Section 1. Section 20-4-502, MCA, is amended to read:
19 "20-4-502. Definitions. For the purposes of this part, unless the context requires otherwise, the
20 following definitions apply:
21 (1) "Education cooperative" means a cooperative of Montana public schools as described in 20-7-
22 451.
23 (2) "Educational loans" means all loans made pursuant to a federal loan program, except federal
24 parent loans for undergraduate students (PLUS) loans, as provided in 20 U.S.C. 1078-2.
25 (3) "Federal loan program" means educational loans authorized by 20 U.S.C. 1071, et seq., 20
26 U.S.C. 1087a, et seq., and 20 U.S.C. 1087aa, et seq.
27 (4) "Impacted school" means:
28 (a) a special education cooperative;

1 (b) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;
2 (c) the Montana youth challenge program, as established in 10-1-1401;
3 (d) a correctional facility, as defined in 41-5-103;
4 (e) a public school located on an Indian reservation; and
5 (f) a public school that, driving at a reasonable speed for the road surface, is located more than 20
6 minutes from a Montana city with a population greater than 15,000 based on the most recent federal decennial
7 census.
8 (5) (a) "Quality educator" means a full-time equivalent educator, as reported to the superintendent
9 of public instruction for accreditation purposes in the current school year, who:
10 (i) holds a valid certificate under the provisions of 20-4-106 and is employed by an entity listed in
11 subsection (5)(b) in a position that requires an educator license in accordance with administrative rules adopted
12 by the board of public education; or
13 (ii) is a licensed professional under 37-8-405, 37-8-415, 37-11-301, 37-15-301, 37-17-302, 37-24-
14 301, 37-25-302, 37-39-308, 37-39-309, or 37-39-311 and is employed by an entity listed in subsection (5)(b) to
15 provide services to students; or
16 (iii) is employed by an entity listed in subsection (5)(b) to provide educational sign language
17 interpreting for deaf and hard-of-hearing students and who meets the qualifications for educational sign
18 language interpreting established by the board of public education.
19 (b) For the purposes of subsection (5)(a), "an entity" means:
20 (i) a school district;
21 (ii) an education cooperative;
22 (iii) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;
23 (iv) the Montana youth challenge program; and
24 (v) a correctional facility, as defined in 41-5-103.
25 (6) "School district" means a public school district, as provided in 20-6-101 and 20-6-701."

5 teachers in public school districts -- definitions. (1) A school district, as defined in 20-6-101, must receive
6 an extra quality educator payment for certain quality educators, calculated as provided in 20-9-306(16), if it

9 applicable year:

11 306(16); and

16 (a) that the district has met the legislative goal established in subsection (2) for the current year;
17 and

19 career in the current year. The first 3 years of a teacher's teaching career do not include a year of teaching
20 under an emergency authorization pursuant to 20-4-111.
