69th Legislature 2025 HB 151.1
2 INTRODUCED BY C. KEOGH, D. BEDEY, J. FULLER, L. JONES, E. MATTHEWS, J. WINDY BOY, B.

5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING EDUCATION LAWS RELATED TO
6 RECRUITMENT AND RETENTION AND THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND; MAKING
7 EDUCATIONAL SIGN LANGUAGE INTERPRETERS ELIGIBLE FOR THE QUALITY EDUCATOR PAYMENT
8 AND THE QUALITY EDUCATOR LOAN ASSISTANCE PROGRAM; EXPANDING THE TYPES OF
9 EDUCATIONAL ENTITIES THAT CAN RECEIVE INCENTIVES FOR MEETING STARTING TEACHER PAY
10 BENCHMARKS AND THAT CAN PARTICIPATE IN THE TEACHER RESIDENCY PROGRAM; ALLOWING
11 THE MONTANA SCHOOL FOR THE DEAF AND BLIND TO TRANSPORT RESIDENT STUDENTS BETWEEN
12 HOME AND THE SCHOOL BY SCHOOL BUS; AMENDING SECTIONS 20-4-502, 20-4-702, 20-8-121, 20-9-
13 324, AND 20-9-327, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
16
17 Section 1. Section 20-4-502, MCA, is amended to read:
18 "20-4-502. Definitions. For the purposes of this part, unless the context requires otherwise, the
19 following definitions apply:
20 (1) "Education cooperative" means a cooperative of Montana public schools as described in 20-7-
21 451.
22 (2) "Educational loans" means all loans made pursuant to a federal loan program, except federal
23 parent loans for undergraduate students (PLUS) loans, as provided in 20 U.S.C. 1078-2.
24 (3) "Federal loan program" means educational loans authorized by 20 U.S.C. 1071, et seq., 20
25 U.S.C. 1087a, et seq., and 20 U.S.C. 1087aa, et seq.
26 (4) "Impacted school" means:
27 (a) a special education cooperative;
28 (b) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 151

69th Legislature 2025 HB 151.1
1 (c) the Montana youth challenge program, as established in 10-1-1401;
2 (d) a correctional facility, as defined in 41-5-103;
3 (e) a public school located on an Indian reservation; and
4 (f) a public school that, driving at a reasonable speed for the road surface, is located more than 20
5 minutes from a Montana city with a population greater than 15,000 based on the most recent federal decennial
6 census.
7 (5) (a) "Quality educator" means a full-time equivalent educator, as reported to the superintendent
8 of public instruction for accreditation purposes in the current school year, who:
9 (i) holds a valid certificate under the provisions of 20-4-106 and is employed by an entity listed in
10 subsection (5)(b) in a position that requires an educator license in accordance with administrative rules adopted
11 by the board of public education; or
12 (ii) is a licensed professional under 37-8-405, 37-8-415, 37-11-301, 37-15-301, 37-17-302, 37-24-
13 301, 37-25-302, 37-39-308, 37-39-309, or 37-39-311 and is employed by an entity listed in subsection (5)(b) to
14 provide services to students; or
15 (iii) is employed by an entity listed in subsection (5)(b) to provide educational sign language
16 interpreting for deaf and hard-of-hearing students and who meets the qualifications for educational sign
17 language interpreting established by the board of public education.
18 (b) For the purposes of subsection (5)(a), "an entity" means:
19 (i) a school district;
20 (ii) an education cooperative;
21 (iii) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;
22 (iv) the Montana youth challenge program; and
23 (v) a correctional facility, as defined in 41-5-103.
24 (6) "School district" means a public school district, as provided in 20-6-101 and 20-6-701."
25
26 Section 2. Section 20-4-702, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "20-4-702. Teacher residency program. (1) The superintendent of public instruction shall administer
28 a teacher residency program in collaboration with school districts and professional educator preparation
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 151

1 programs.
2 (2) The teacher residency program must involve:
3 (a) a shared vision and partnership between:
4 (i) a resident;
5 (ii) a teacher-leader;
6 (iii) a school district; and
7 (iv) an educator preparation program;
8 (b) ensuring that a resident possesses the cultural competencies to succeed and be an effective
9 educator in the school hosting the resident;
10 (c) selection and training for teacher-leaders;
11 (d) a resident being matched with a school district that provides a high-quality and supportive
12 experience for residents working with a teacher-leader;
13 (e) flexible course delivery to allow a resident to complete required credits in the resident's
14 preparation program;
15 (f) a cohort model with opportunities for virtual and in-person training and support;
16 (g) financial and other compensation for residents and teacher-leaders, including:
17 (i) housing for the resident or a housing allowance provided to the resident by the host school
18 district;
19 (ii) for a resident in a professional educator preparation program accredited by the board of public
20 education, a last-dollar tuition grant to eliminate tuition costs for the resident for the year of the residency;
21 (iii) a stipend for the teacher-leader; and
22 (iv) compensation for the resident, a portion of which may be conditioned on completion of the
23 program and the signing of a teaching contract in a Montana public school for the following year; and
24 (h) a methodology for evaluating the effectiveness of the program.
25 (3) The superintendent of public instruction may contract with a professional educator preparation
26 program located in a unit of the Montana university system to coordinate the teacher residency program or
27 aspects of the program.
28 (4) If a resident does not complete the program or does not teach in a Montana public school for 3

1 or more years within 5 years of completing the teacher residency program, the superintendent of public
2 instruction shall inform the commissioner of higher education and the commissioner shall convert the amount of
3 any tuition grant provided to the resident under the program to a loan.
4 (5) For the purposes of this part, the following definitions apply:
5 (a) "Professional educator preparation program" means a postsecondary program intended to lead
6 to teacher licensure that is accredited by the board of public education or recognized by the board as an
7 essentially equivalent program for teacher certification purposes.
8 (b) "Resident" means a student enrolled in a professional educator preparation program who is
9 selected for participation in a teacher residency program as described in this section and who commits to
10 teaching in a Montana public school following completion of the teacher residency program.
11 (c) "School district" or "district" means the educational entities eligible for a quality educator
12 payment under 20-9-327.
13 (c)(d) "Teacher-leader" means an experienced classroom teacher who is selected to work with,
14 coach, and mentor a resident.
15 (d)(e) "Teacher residency program" or "program" means a partnership between a district and a
16 professional educator preparation program to provide residents with a year-long, practice-based learning
17 experience working directly with students in a manner that mirrors the experience of teachers in that school and
18 meets the criteria of supervised teaching experience as defined by the board of public education."

20 Section 3. Section 20-8-121, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "20-8-121. Transportation of students at school. (1) The school for the deaf and blind shall provide
22 the transportation expenses allowed in subsection (4) for a residential student at the school for the deaf and
23 blind who is a resident of the state of Montana if the student is conveyed to and from the student's residence
24 by:
25 (a) a scheduled air carrier as defined in 67-1-101;
26 (b) charter with a commercial air operator as defined in 67-1-101;
27 (c) a parent or guardian of the student, under an individual transportation contract with the school
28 for the deaf and blind;
- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 151

1 (d) a school bus as defined in 20-10-101; or
2 (d)(e) other transportation arrangements, provided that the transportation is by a carrier of
3 passengers certified by the public service commission and approved by the superintendent of the school for the
4 deaf and blind, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of public education.
5 (2) The superintendent of the school for the deaf and blind shall determine which method of
6 transportation in subsection (1) is to be provided to a student, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of public
7 education on transportation of residential and boarding students at the school.
8 (3) A parent or guardian who transports a student to or from the school under an individual
9 transportation contract is entitled to reimbursement for transportation, pursuant to rules adopted by the board of
10 public education on reimbursement.
11 (4) The transportation of a residential student provided in subsection (1) is limited to the number of
12 round trips to the student's residence as specified in the school calendar approved by the board of public
13 education. The superintendent of the school for the deaf and blind may grant a variance from this provision, but
14 in no event may a reimbursement for travel expenses be provided for travel in excess of the total number of
15 trips approved in any school fiscal year."

17 Section 4. Section 20-9-324, MCA, is amended to read:
18 "20-9-324. Incentives for school districts meeting legislative goal for competitive base pay of
19 teachers in public school districts -- definitions. (1) A school district, as defined in 20-6-101, must receive
20 an extra quality educator payment for certain quality educators, calculated as provided in 20-9-306(16), if it
21 meets the legislative goal for competitive base pay of teachers in subsection (2).
22 (2) The legislative goal for competitive base pay of teachers is a teacher base pay that in the
23 applicable year:
24 (a) is equal to at least 10 times as much as the quality educator payment amount provided in 20-9-
25 306(16); and
26 (b) for a school district classified as first class pursuant to Title 20, chapter 6, is not less than 70%
27 of the teacher average pay in the school district.
28 (3) A district seeking an incentive for the subsequent school fiscal year under this section shall, by

1 December 1, provide the data necessary, as determined by the superintendent of public instruction, to verify:
2 (a) that the district has met the legislative goal established in subsection (2) for the current year;
3 and
4 (b) the number of full-time equivalent teachers that are in the first 3 years of the teacher's teaching
5 career in the current year. The first 3 years of a teacher's teaching career do not include a year of teaching
6 under an emergency authorization pursuant to 20-4-111.
7 (4) For the purposes of this section, the following definitions apply:
8 (a) "School district" or "district" means the educational entities eligible for a quality educator
9 payment under 20-9-327.
10 (a)(b) "Teacher" means an individual who:
11 (i) holds a current class 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, or 7 license issued by the office of public instruction under
12 rules adopted by the board of public education pursuant to 20-4-102; and
13 (ii) is employed by a school district in an instructional position requiring teacher licensure.
14 (b)(c) "Teacher average pay" means the total compensation paid by a school district to all of its
15 teachers, not including bonuses, stipends, or extended duty contracts, divided by the total full-time equivalent
16 teachers employed in the district, with full-time equivalence rounded to the nearest tenth.
17 (c)(d) "Teacher base pay" means the lowest salary for a beginning teacher incorporated in the
18 district's collective bargaining agreement if the teachers' employment is covered by a collective bargaining
19 agreement pursuant to Title 39, chapter 31, or incorporated in district policy if the teachers' employment is not
20 covered by a collective bargaining agreement, not including bonuses, stipends, or extended duty contracts."

25 (ii) special education cooperatives, as described in 20-7-451;
26 (iii) the Montana school for the deaf and blind, as described in 20-8-101;

28 (v) the Montana youth challenge program.

23 language immersion program pursuant to Title 20, chapter 7, part 14; or
24 (d) meets the qualifications for educational sign language interpreting established by the board of
25 public education and is employed by an entity listed in subsection (1) to provide educational sign language
26 interpreting for deaf and hard-of-hearing students."
