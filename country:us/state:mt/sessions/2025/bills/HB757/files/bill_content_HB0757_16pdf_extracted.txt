69th Legislature 2025 HB 757.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. FITZPATRICK, M. VINTON, S. ROSENZWEIG, J. KASSMIER, E. BOLDMAN, J. ESP, P.
3 TUSS
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING CULTURAL AND AESTHETIC
6 PROJECTS GRANTS; PROVIDING CRITERIA BY WHICH THE CULTURAL AND AESTHETIC PROJECTS
7 ADVISORY COMMITTEE SHALL PRIORITIZE APPLICATIONS FOR GRANTS; PROVIDING AN EXCEPTION
8 ON THE REQUIREMENT TO OBTAIN A FEDERAL SYSTEM FOR AWARD MANAGEMENT NUMBER; AND
9 AMENDING SECTION 22-2-302, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Prioritization of cultural and aesthetic projects grant awards --
14 criteria. After the cultural and aesthetic projects advisory committee provided for in 2-15-1521 has reviewed the
15 applications to ensure compliance and determine whether the application meets the committee's criteria for
16 funding, the committee shall rank the projects by two criteria as follows:
17 (1) the status of an applicant's previous grant awards, for which:
18 (a) first priority will be given to qualified applicants who have not previously received an award;
19 (b) second priority will be given to applicants who have received one grant award in the previous
20 three funding cycles;
21 (c) third priority will be given to applicants who have received two grant awards in the previous
22 three funding cycles; and
23 (d) fourth priority will be given to applicants who have received three grant awards in the previous
24 three funding cycles; and
25 (2) the size of an applicant's annual operating budget, for which:
26 (a) first priority will be given to applicants whose total annual operating budget is less than
27 $99,999;
28 (b) second priority will be given to applicants whose total annual operating budget ranges between
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 757
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 757.1
1 $100,000 and $249,999;
2 (c) third priority will be given to applicants whose total annual operating budget ranges between
3 $250,000 and $499,999; and
4 (d) fourth priority will be given to applicants whose total annual operating budget is equal to or
5 greater than $500,000.
6 (3) Between each funding cycle, the committee, in consultation with the budget director, shall
7 increase the budget limits established in subsection (2) to account for inflation.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Acquisition of federal system for award management number. Grant
10 applicants for a cultural or aesthetic project whose total program budget does not exceed $100,000 and who do
11 not receive or use federal funds to operate may request a waiver from the committee for the requirement to
12 obtain a federal system for award management number, or SAMs number, if complying with the requirement
13 would be burdensome. The committee may not unreasonably withhold the waiver.
14
15 Section 3. Section 22-2-302, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "22-2-302. Advisory committee -- powers and duties. (1) The Subject to [section 1], the cultural
17 and aesthetic projects advisory committee provided for in 2-15-1521 shall review all proposals for cultural and
18 aesthetic project grants before they are submitted to the legislature.
19 (2) Consistent with the rules adopted in accordance with 22-2-303, the committee shall make
20 recommendations to the legislature on each proposal submitted to the committee.
21 (3) The committee's recommendations to the legislature are advisory only.
22 (4) The committee shall present its recommendations to the appropriations committee of the
23 legislature by the 15th day of any regular legislative session."
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 and 2] are intended to be codified
26 as an integral part of Title 22, chapter 2, part 3, and the provisions of Title 22, chapter 2, part 3, apply to
27 [sections 1 and 2].
28 - END -
