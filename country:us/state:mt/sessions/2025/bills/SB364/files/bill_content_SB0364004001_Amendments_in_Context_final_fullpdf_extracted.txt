1 SENATE BILL NO. 364
2 INTRODUCED BY K. BOGNER
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING KILL SWITCH LAWS; PROHIBITING AN ENTITY
5 FROM REMOTELY ACTIVATING A KILL SWITCH WITHOUT A WARRANT; PROVIDING AN EXCEPTION
6 EXCEPTIONS; PROVIDING DAMAGES; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
7
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Kill switches in electronic consumer products ON PERSONALLY OWNED
11 PROPERTY -- warrant required -- exceptions -- definitions. (1) An entity may not activate a kill switch on an
12 individual's electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY without a court order or a warrant
13 issued pursuant to Title 46, chapter 5, part 2.
14 (2) This section does not apply to:
15 (A) subscriptions, memberships, and other recurring-payment programs, or to leased electronic
16 consumer products ELECTRONIC DEVICES USED TO ENFORCE RETAIL INSTALLMENT SALE AGREEMENTS, LEASE
17 AGREEMENTS, RENTAL AGREEMENTS, AND OTHER SECURED CREDITOR AGREEMENTS, OR TO MOTOR VEHICLES SUBJECT
18 TO COLLECTIONS. In the event an individual neglects to make regular payments, the entity may activate a kill
19 switch to turn off the individual's subscribed services. An entity shall disclose the existence of the kill switch and
20 the capability of activating it prior to entering into an agreement or contract.
21 (B) A MECHANISM OR FEATURE THAT:
22 (I) ADDRESSES AN IMMINENT CRITICAL SAFETY ISSUE IMPACTING A MECHANICAL OR SOFTWARE
23 COMPONENT OF A MOTOR VEHICLE;
24 (II) ACTIVATES WHEN A DRIVER OF A MOTOR VEHICLE IS INCAPACITATED, SUFFERS A MEDICAL EMERGENCY,
25 OR EXPERIENCES A LOSS OF CONSCIOUSNESS;
26 (III) TAKES CORRECTIVE ACTION IN A MOTOR VEHICLE WITH AN ENGAGED PARTIAL DRIVING AUTOMATION
27 FEATURE IF THE DRIVER IS NOT ATTENTIVE OR ENGAGED IN THE DRIVING TASK AND DOES NOT RESPOND TO WARNINGS;

1 (IV) BRINGS A MOTOR VEHICLE WITH AN ENGAGED AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM TO A MINIMAL RISK
2 CONDITION; OR
3 (V) AUTOMATICALLY SHUTS OFF THE ENGINE OR MOTOR OF AN IDLING MOTOR VEHICLE THAT HAS BEEN
4 LEFT ON FOR AN EXTENDED PERIOD OF TIME WHILE IN THE PARK POSITION.
5 (3) As used in [sections 1 and 2 THROUGH 3], the following definitions apply:
6 (a) "Electronic consumer product" includes electronic devices as defined in 45-6-310 as well as
7 motor vehicles as defined in 61-1-101.
8 (b)(A) "Entity" means a company that manufactures or distributes an electronic consumer product
9 PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY or that provides service for an electronic consumer product's ITS functionality.
10 (c)(B) “Hard reset” means the restoration of an electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED
11 PROPERTY to the state the electronic consumer product IT was in when it left the factory. Other terms commonly
12 used are "factory reset" and "master reset".
13 (d)(C) "Individual" means a natural person.
14 (e)(D) "Kill switch" means a mechanism that may be engaged remotely, regardless of the owner's
15 consent, and is used to:
16 (i) shut down or perform a hard reset on an electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED
17 PROPERTY;
18 (ii) hasten the battery drainage of an electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY;
19 or
20 (iii) shorten the software lifespan of an electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY.
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Enforcement -- damages. (1) A violation of [section 1] is a violation of
23 30-14-103.
24 (2) The department of justice may bring an action to restrain any unlawful act pursuant to the
25 provisions of Title 30, chapter 14, part 1.
26 (3) An individual whose electronic consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY has a kill
27 switch activated in violation of [section 1] may bring an action pursuant to 30-14-133, except that:

Amendment - 2nd Reading/2nd House-tan - Requested by: Bob Carter - (H) Committee of the

1 (a) the individual needs to show only a violation of [section 1] for that individual’s electronic
2 consumer product PERSONALLY OWNED PROPERTY and is not required to show any other ascertainable loss of
3 money or property; and
4 (b) the individual is entitled to statutory damages of $10,000 for each violation of [section 1] in
5 addition to any other damages or monetary award pursuant to 30-14-133.
6
7 NEW SECTION. SECTION 3. USER CONSENT. A KILL SWITCH MAY BE ACTIVATED ON STOLEN PERSONALLY
8 OWNED PROPERTY WITH CONSENT OF THE PROPERTY OWNER.
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 and 2 THROUGH 3] are intended to
11 be codified as a new part in Title 30, chapter 14, and the provisions of Title 30, chapter 14, apply to [sections 1
12 and 2 THROUGH 3].
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Effective date. [This act] is effective October 1, 2025.
15 - END -
