4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CREATING THE MONTANA GOVERNMENT CLAIMS COURT;
5 PROVIDING FOR APPOINTMENT AND CONFIRMATION OF JUDGES; PROVIDING FOR SUPERVISION
6 AND ADMINISTRATION OF THE COURT AND PERSONNEL; PROVIDING JURISDICTION FOR THE
7 COURT; PROVIDING FOR PROCEDURE, VENUE, HEARINGS, RULES OF PROCEDURE, JURIES,
8 APPEALS, AND A REMOVAL PROCESS; AMENDING SECTIONS 2-4-702, 3-1-101, 3-1-102, 3-1-201, 3-5-
9 115, 3-5-302, 3-20-102, AND 19-5-301, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN
10 APPLICABILITY DATE.”
11
12 WHEREAS, Article VII, section 1, of the Montana constitution allows for "such other courts as may be
13 provided by law"; and
14 WHEREAS, Article VII, section 4, of the Montana constitution allows for other courts to have
15 "jurisdiction concurrent with that of the district court as may be provided by law"; and
16 WHEREAS, the Montana Legislature has used this constitutional authority to create other courts,
17 including the water court, asbestos claims court, workers' compensation court, small claims courts, municipal
18 courts, and city courts.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Government claims court -- appointment -- oath of office -- term of
23 office. (1) There is created a government claims court.
24 (2) The government claims court consists of three judges appointed by the governor and confirmed
25 by the senate in the same manner as provided by Title 3, chapter 1, part 9. The judges must be designated by
26 seat 1, seat 2, and seat 3.
27 (3) Each judge of the government claims court shall, as soon as the judge has taken and
28 subscribed the official oath, file the official oath in the office of the secretary of state.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Qualifications. An individual is not eligible for the office of judge of the
9 government claims court unless the individual is a citizen of the United States, has resided in the state for 2
10 years immediately before taking office, and has been admitted to practice law in Montana for at least 5 years
11 prior to the date of appointment.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Salary and expenses. (1) A judge of the government claims court must
14 be paid a salary that is 20% greater than the salary for an associate justice of the supreme court as provided in
15 2-16-403. The salary of a judge of the government claims court may not be reduced during that judge’s term of
16 office.
17 (2) Actual and necessary expenses for each judge of the government claims court are the travel
18 expenses, as defined and provided in 2-18-501 through 2-18-503, incurred in the performance of the judge’s
19 official duties.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Hearings -- location -- rules of procedure -- staff -- venue. (1) Matters
22 before the government claims court must be heard by one judge, except that the judge:
23 (a) may empanel an advisory jury to assist with fact-finding; and
24 (b) shall empanel a trial jury if a party has not waived the right to a jury trial.
25 (2) The government claims court shall provide rules of procedure by which a party dissatisfied with
26 a final or interlocutory determination by the trial judge may seek review by all judges of the government claims
27 court sitting together.
28 (3) The government claims court must have its offices in Helena. The government claims court

1 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Jurisdiction of judges coextensive with state. (1) The jurisdiction of
2 the judges of the government claims court in rendering and signing judgments, making findings and rendering
3 decrees, and making orders to show cause and all ex parte orders is coextensive with the boundaries of the
4 state as to all matters presented to or heard by the judges and of which they have jurisdiction.
5 (2) The judgments, findings, decrees, and orders of the judges of the government claims court
6 when rendered, made, or signed have the same force and effect as to matters under the judges' jurisdiction as
7 if done in district court in the county in which the action, proceeding, or matter is pending or was heard.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Jurisdiction. (1) The government claims court has jurisdiction over civil
10 actions that involve:
11 (a) claims for equitable or declaratory relief based on the alleged unconstitutionality of a state
12 action, including but not limited to a legislative enactment, an administrative rule, a permit issued by the state or
13 a local government, or a citizen-passed ballot measure, except that this jurisdiction does not extend to
14 challenges to local zoning regulations;
15 (b) claims for equitable or declaratory relief based on the alleged illegality of a state action,
16 including but not limited to an administrative rule, a permit issued by the state or a local government, or a
17 citizen-passed ballot measure, except that this jurisdiction does not extend to challenges to local zoning
18 regulations;
19 (c) petitions for judicial review in which the government claims court has jurisdiction pursuant to 2-
20 4-702; and
21 (d) civil actions involving asbestos-related claims pursuant to Title 3, chapter 20, part 1.
22 (2) The government claims court has supplemental jurisdiction over all other claims that are related
23 to the claims in an action in which the court has jurisdiction under subsection (1), except when:
24 (a) supplemental claims substantially predominate over the claim or claims under subsection (1);
25 (b) the government claims court has dismissed all claims over which it has jurisdiction under
26 subsection (1); or
27 (c) in exceptional circumstances, there are other compelling reasons for declining jurisdiction.
28 (3) This section may not be construed to extend jurisdiction over criminal cases to the government

3 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Rules of procedure. (1) The government claims court shall use the
4 Montana Rules of Civil Procedure and the Montana Rules of Evidence.
5 (2) The government claims court may adopt rules of court governing its practice, but the rules may
6 not conflict with the rules promulgated by the supreme court in accordance with Title 3, chapter 2, part 7.
7
8 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Venue. (1) In an action originally brought in the government claims
9 court, the county of venue is the place of trial as provided in [section 4].
10 (2) In an action removed to the government claims court under [section 12], the county of venue is
11 the county in which the action was originally filed.
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Removal. (1) A party desiring to remove a civil action from a district
14 court to the government claims court shall file in the district court and the government claims court a notice of
15 removal containing a short, plain statement of the grounds for removal, accompanied by a copy of the
16 summons and any complaints served on the party in the action. A notice of removal must be filed within 30
17 calendar days after the first summons is served or an adverse party has appeared.
18 (2) A civil action may be removed under this section if the district court and the government claims
19 court have concurrent jurisdiction.
20 (3) (a) Promptly after the filing of a notice of removal of a civil action or proceeding, the party shall
21 give written notice to all parties and shall file a copy of the notice with the district court and government claims
22 court.
23 (b) The notice of removal effectuates the removal, and the district court may not proceed any
24 further unless the case is remanded.
25 (c) On receiving a notice of removal, the district court shall transmit the pleadings and all papers in
26 the action to the government claims court immediately.
27 (4) If a party believes the removal is improper, the party shall file a motion to remand in the
28 government claims court within 20 days of service of the notice of removal.

14 NEW SECTION. Section 14. Appeals. An appeal from an interlocutory or final decision of the
15 government claims court must be filed directly with the supreme court in the manner provided by law for
16 appeals from the district court in civil cases.

18 NEW SECTION. Section 15. Seal of government claims court. The government claims court must

22 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Government claims court -- appointment -- oath of office -- term of
23 office. (1) There is created a government claims court.

25 by the senate in the same manner as provided by Title 3, chapter 1, part 9. The judges must be designated by
26 seat 1, seat 2, and seat 3.
27 (3) Each judge of the government claims court shall, as soon as the judge has taken and
28 subscribed the official oath, file the official oath in the office of the secretary of state.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Venue. (1) In an action originally brought in the government claims
9 court, the county of venue is the place of trial as provided in [section 4].

13 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Removal. (1) A party desiring to remove a civil action from a district
14 court to the government claims court shall file in the district court and the government claims court a notice of
15 removal containing a short, plain statement of the grounds for removal, accompanied by a copy of the
16 summons and any complaints served on the party in the action. A notice of removal must be filed within 30
17 calendar days after the first summons is served or an adverse party has appeared.
18 (2) A civil action may be removed under this section if the district court and the government claims
19 court have concurrent jurisdiction.
20 (3) (a) Promptly after the filing of a notice of removal of a civil action or proceeding, the party shall
21 give written notice to all parties and shall file a copy of the notice with the district court and government claims
22 court.
23 (b) The notice of removal effectuates the removal, and the district court may not proceed any
24 further unless the case is remanded.
25 (c) On receiving a notice of removal, the district court shall transmit the pleadings and all papers in
26 the action to the government claims court immediately.
27 (4) If a party believes the removal is improper, the party shall file a motion to remand in the
28 government claims court within 20 days of service of the notice of removal.
