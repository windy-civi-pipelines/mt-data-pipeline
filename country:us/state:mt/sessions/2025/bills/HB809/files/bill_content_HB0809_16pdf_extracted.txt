10 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROHIBITING LOCAL GOVERNMENTS FROM ENACTING OR
11 ENFORCING AN EXTREME RISK PROTECTION ORDER; PROHIBITING LOCAL GOVERNMENTS FROM
12 RECEIVING FUNDS RELATED TO EXTREME RISK PROTECTION ORDERS; AND AMENDING SECTION 7-
13 1-111, MCA.”
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Legislative finding -- extreme risk protection order prohibition. (1)
18 The legislature finds that extreme risk protection orders violate a person's right to bear arms under the second
19 amendment of the United States constitution and under Article II, section 12, of the Montana constitution.
20 (2) A local government may not:
21 (a) enact, adopt, or implement a resolution, ordinance, rule, regulation, or policy that would have
22 the effect of enforcing an extreme risk protection order against a resident of the state; or
23 (b) accept a grant or other source of funding for the purpose of aiding in the adoption,
24 implementation, or enforcement of an extreme risk protection order.
25 (3) A local government that violates this section is subject to a civil penalty of up to $10,000,
26 payable to the state general fund. Each instance of enforcement or attempted enforcement constitutes a
27 separate offense.
28 (4) (a) As used in this section, "extreme risk protection order" means a directive, also known as a

1 red flag law, that is an executive order or a written order or warrant issued by a judge, magistrate, or other
2 judicial officer, with the primary purpose of reducing the risk of firearm-related death or injury by doing one or
3 more of the following:
4 (i) prohibiting a named individual from having under the individual's custody or control, owning,
5 possessing, or receiving a firearm; or
6 (ii) removing a firearm from or requiring the surrender of a firearm by a named individual.
7 (b) The term does not include an order of protection issued under Title 40, chapter 15.

11 exercising the following:

14 (2) any power that applies to or affects the provisions of 7-33-4128 or Title 39, except that subject
15 to those provisions, it may exercise any power of a public employer with regard to its employees;
16 (3) any power that applies to or affects the public school system, except that a local unit may
17 impose an assessment reasonably related to the cost of any service or special benefit provided by the unit and
18 shall exercise any power that it is required by law to exercise regarding the public school system;
19 (4) any power that prohibits the grant or denial of a certificate of compliance or a certificate of
20 public convenience and necessity pursuant to Title 69, chapter 12;
21 (5) any power that establishes a rate or price otherwise determined by a state agency;
22 (6) any power that applies to or affects any determination of the department of environmental
23 quality with regard to any mining plan, permit, or contract;
24 (7) any power that applies to or affects any determination by the department of environmental
25 quality with regard to a certificate of compliance;
26 (8) any power that defines as an offense conduct made criminal by state statute, that defines an
27 offense as a felony, or that fixes the penalty or sentence for a misdemeanor in excess of a fine of $500, 6
28 months' imprisonment, or both, except as specifically authorized by statute;
