5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING COUNTY CANVASSING PROCESSES; REQUIRING A
6 BOARD OF COUNTY CANVASSERS TO COMPARE THE NUMBER OF VOTES CAST TO THE NUMBER OF
7 ELECTORS AND THE NUMBER OF PERSONS WHO VOTED DURING CANVASS; REQUIRING A BOARD
8 OF COUNTY CANVASSERS TO INVESTIGATE DISCREPANCIES OR ERRORS IN VOTE COUNTS;
9 PROVIDING THAT A BOARD OF COUNTY CANVASSERS MAY REQUIRE A RECOUNT OF VOTES IN AN
10 INVESTIGATION OF DISCREPANCIES OR ERRORS; AMENDING SECTIONS 13-15-403 AND 13-16-201,
11 MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Section 13-15-403, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "13-15-403. Canvass to be public -- nonessentials to be disregarded -- petition for recount
17 investigation of discrepancies in vote counts. (1) The canvass must be public. It must proceed by opening
18 the returns, auditing the tally books or other records of votes cast, determining the vote for each individual and
19 for and against each ballot issue from each precinct, compiling totals, comparing the number of votes cast to
20 the number of electors and number of persons who voted, and declaring or certifying the results.
21 (2) The board shall record all write-in votes shown in the returns from each precinct.
22 (3) The returns may not be rejected because of failure to show who administered the oath to the
23 election judges, failure to complete all the certificates in a pollbook, or failure of any other act making up the
24 returns that is not essential to determine for whom the votes were cast.
25 (4) If during a canvass the board finds that the votes returned for any candidate or ballot issue
26 exceeds the number of electors or the total number of persons who voted in a precinct or an finds any error in a
27 precinct or precincts affecting the accuracy of vote totals, the board shall immediately investigate the
28 discrepancy or error until the discrepancy or error is reconciled and documented or is determined to have no

4
5 Section 2. Section 13-16-201, MCA, is amended to read:
6 "13-16-201. Conditions under which recount to be conducted. (1) A recount must be conducted if:
7 (a) a candidate for a precinct office or for a county, municipal, or district office voted for in only one
8 county, other than a legislator or a judge of the district court, is defeated by a margin not exceeding 1/4 of 1% of
9 the total votes cast or by a margin not exceeding 10 votes, whichever is greater, and the defeated candidate,
10 within 5 days after the official canvass, files with the election administrator a verified petition stating that the
11 candidate believes that a recount will change the result and that a recount of the votes for the office or
12 nomination should be conducted;
13 (b) a candidate for a congressional office, a state or district office voted on in more than one
14 county, the legislature, or judge of the district court is defeated by a margin not exceeding 1/4 of 1% of the total
15 votes cast for all candidates for the same position and the defeated candidate, within 5 days after the official
16 canvass, files a petition with the secretary of state as set forth in subsection (1)(a). The secretary of state shall
17 immediately notify each election administrator whose county includes any precincts that voted for the office,
18 and a recount must be conducted in those precincts.
19 (c) a question submitted to the vote of the people of a county, municipality, or district within a
20 county is decided by a margin not exceeding 1/4 of 1% of the total votes cast for and against the question and a
21 petition as set forth in subsection (1)(a) is filed with the election administrator. This petition must be signed by
22 not less than 10 electors of the jurisdiction and must be filed within 5 days after the official canvass.
23 (d) a question submitted to the vote of the people of the state is decided by a margin not
24 exceeding 1/4 of 1% of the total votes cast for and against the question and a petition as set forth in subsection
25 (1)(a) is filed with the secretary of state. This petition must be signed by not less than 100 electors of the state,
26 representing at least five counties of the state, and must be filed within 5 days after the official canvass.
27 (e) a question submitted to the vote of the people of a multicounty district is decided by a margin
28 not exceeding 1/4 of 1% of the total votes cast for and against the question and a petition as set forth in
