69th Legislature 2025 SB0067.2
2 INTRODUCED BY D. LOGE
3 BY REQUEST OF THE TRANSPORTATION INTERIM COMMITTEE
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR THE USE OF AUTONOMOUS VEHICLES
6 VEHICLES WITH AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEMS ON STATE PUBLIC HIGHWAYS; PROVIDING
7 RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND PROVIDING DEFINITIONS ; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 WHEREAS, the intention of this legislation is to get all parties to the table to promote the safest ways to
10 promote autonomous technology incorporating the diverse roadways, weather, and animal-human encounters
11 in Montana and to make Montana the showcase for advancing new technologies in transportation.
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 (Refer to Introduced Bill)
16 Strike everything after the enacting clause and insert:
17
18 NEW SECTION. SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE. [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 6] MAY BE CITED AS THE "MONTANA
19 AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEMS ACT."
20
21 NEW SECTION. SECTION 2. PURPOSE. THE PURPOSE OF [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 6] IS TO ALLOW THE USE
22 OF A VEHICLE WITH AN AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM ON THE PUBLIC HIGHWAYS OF THIS STATE IN A WAY THAT:
23 (1) is safe for the traveling public;
24 (2) creates long-term predictability for individuals and businesses;
25 (3) recognizes that this state may have more challenging and variable driving conditions than other
26 states; and
27 (4) integrates as closely as possible with existing state law related to motor vehicles.
28 NEW SECTION. SECTION 3. DEFINITIONS. AS USED IN [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 6], UNLESS THE CONTEXT
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 67
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0067.2
1 CLEARLY INDICATES OTHERWISE, THE FOLLOWING DEFINITIONS APPLY:
2 (1) "Automated driving system" means the hardware and software that are collectively capable of
3 performing the entire dynamic driving task on a sustained basis, regardless of whether the automated driving
4 system is limited to a specific operational design domain.
5 (2) "Department" means the department of transportation.
6 (3) (a) "Dynamic driving task" means all of the real-time operational and tactical functions required
7 to operate a motor vehicle in highway traffic, including:
8 (i) lateral vehicle motion control through steering;
9 (ii) longitudinal vehicle motion control through acceleration and deceleration;
10 (iii) monitoring the driving environment through object and event detection, recognition,
11 classification, and response preparation;
12 (iv) object and event response execution;
13 (v) maneuver planning; and
14 (vi) enhancement of the conspicuousness of the vehicle with lights, signals, and gestures.
15 (b) The term does not include strategic functions such as trip scheduling and the selection of
16 destinations and waypoints.
17 (4) "Fallback ready user" means the user of a vehicle equipped with an engaged level 3 automated
18 driving system who is a human driver and who is responsible to operate the vehicle if the automated driving
19 system issues a request for the user of the vehicle to intervene or a system failure occurs.
20 (5) "Level 1 automated driving system" means an automated driving system that provides a driver
21 with one of the following:
22 (a) braking and accelerating support; or
23 (b) steering support.
24 (6) "Level 2 automated driving system" means an automated driving system that provides a driver
25 with both of the following:
26 (a) braking and accelerating support; and
27 (b) steering support.
28 (7) "Level 3 automated driving system" means an automated driving system that:
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – SB 67
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0067.2
1 (a) has the capability to perform on a sustained basis the entire dynamic driving task within its
2 operational design domain; and
3 (b) requires a fallback ready user to operate the vehicle after the automated driving system issues
4 a request for the fallback ready user to intervene or a system failure occurs.
5 (8) "Level 4 automated driving system" means an automated driving system feature that, without
6 any expectation a human driver will respond to a request to intervene, has the capability to perform:
7 (a) on a sustained basis the entire dynamic driving task within its operational design domain; and
8 (b) any maneuvers necessary to achieve a minimal risk condition in response to:
9 (i) an exit from the operational design domain of the automated driving system; or
10 (ii) a system failure.
11 (9) "Level 5 automated driving system" means an automated driving system feature that, without
12 any expectation a human driver will respond to a request to intervene, has the capability to perform:
13 (a) on a sustained basis the entire dynamic driving task under all conditions that can reasonably
14 be managed by a human driver; and
15 (b) any maneuvers necessary to respond to a system failure.
16 (10) "Operational design domain" means the operating conditions under which an automated driving
17 system or a feature of an automated driving system is specifically designed to function, including:
18 (a) speed-range, environmental, geographical, and time-of-day restrictions; or
19 (b) the requisite presence or absence of certain traffic or roadway conditions.
20 (11) "Public highways of this state" has the same meaning as provided in 60-1-201.
21
22 NEW SECTION. SECTION 4. USE OF AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEMS. (1) A VEHICLE WITH AUTOMATED
23 DRIVING SYSTEMS LEVEL 1, LEVEL 2, AND LEVEL 3 MAY BE USED ON THE PUBLIC HIGHWAYS OF THIS STATE.
24 (2) A vehicle with automated driving systems level 4 and level 5 may be used on the public
25 highways of this state subject to limitations established by department rulemaking as provided in [section 5].
26 (3) [Sections 1 through 6] do not prevent a city, town, or consolidated city-county government from
27 regulating traffic on streets within their jurisdictional boundaries pursuant to 61-12-101 or improving its
28 transportation network, providing that these regulations or improvements do not discriminate against a vehicle
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – SB 67
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0067.2
1 with an automated driving system.
2
3 NEW SECTION. SECTION 5. DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION RULEMAKING AUTHORITY. (1) THE
4 DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION MAY ADOPT RULES THAT LIMIT THE USE OF COMMERCIAL VEHICLES WITH AUTOMATED
5 DRIVING SYSTEMS IN ACTUAL OR PREDICTED PERIODS OF DANGEROUS ROAD CONDITIONS.
6 (2) The department of transportation may exempt from limitation commercial vehicles with an
7 automated driving system that are demonstrated to be capable of operating safely during dangerous road
8 conditions.
9 (3) In conjunction with the department of transportation, the department of justice shall consult
10 interested stakeholders prior to proposing rules on vehicles with an automated driving system, such as
11 members of the transportation interim committee and representatives of law enforcement agencies, motor
12 vehicle and insurance industries, drivers, pedestrians, and tribal and local governments.
13
14 NEW SECTION. SECTION 6. DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE RULEMAKING AUTHORITY. (1) THE DEPARTMENT OF
15 JUSTICE MAY ADOPT RULES FOR THE USE OF A VEHICLE WITH AN AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM.
16 (2) IN CONJUNCTION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION, THE DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE SHALL
17 CONSULT INTERESTED STAKEHOLDERS PRIOR TO PROPOSING RULES ON VEHICLES WITH AN AUTOMATED DRIVING
18 SYSTEM, SUCH AS MEMBERS OF THE TRANSPORTATION INTERIM COMMITTEE AND REPRESENTATIVES OF LAW
19 ENFORCEMENT AGENCIES, MOTOR VEHICLE AND INSURANCE INDUSTRIES, DRIVERS, PEDESTRIANS, AND TRIBAL AND
20 LOCAL GOVERNMENTS.
21
22 NEW SECTION. SECTION 7. CODIFICATION INSTRUCTION. [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 6] ARE INTENDED TO BE
23 CODIFIED AS AN INTEGRAL PART OF TITLE 61, AND THE PROVISIONS OF TITLE 61 APPLY TO [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 6].
24 - END -
