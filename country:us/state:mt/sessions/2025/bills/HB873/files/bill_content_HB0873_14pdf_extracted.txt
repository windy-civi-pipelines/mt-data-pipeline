4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO MOBILE HOME
5 PARKS; ESTABLISHING A MOBILE HOME PARK EMERGENCY RELOCATION ACCOUNT; PROVIDING
6 THAT TENANTS WHO ARE REQUIRED TO MOVE DUE TO A CHANGE IN USE OF THE MOBILE HOME
7 PARK MAY RECEIVE FUNDING FOR RELOCATION COSTS; ALLOWING FOR A PERSONAL PROPERTY
8 ASSESSMENT ON MOBILE HOMES; REVISING THE ALLOCATION OF REVENUE COLLECTED FROM
9 CAMPGROUND AND TRAILER COURT LICENSE FEES; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY;
10 PROVIDING A STATUTORY APPROPRIATION; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AMENDING SECTIONS
11 17-7-502, 50-52-202, AND 70-33-103, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE AND A TERMINATION
12 DATE.”

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Mobile home park change of use -- compensation of moving
17 expenses -- applicability. (1) (a) If a landlord plans to change the use of all or part of a mobile home park, the
18 landlord shall follow the process provided for in 70-33-433(2).
19 (b) The landlord shall inform all tenants in writing of the mobile home park emergency relocation
20 account established in [section 3].
21 (2) If a tenant is required to move due to a change in use or redevelopment of a mobile home park,
22 the tenant may:
23 (a) collect payment from the mobile home park emergency relocation account for the lesser of
24 actual moving expenses of relocating the mobile home to a new location that is within a 100-mile radius of the
25 vacated mobile home park or the maximum of $10,000 for a single section mobile home or $15,000 for a
26 multisection mobile home. Moving expenses include the cost of stabilizing, taking down, moving, and setting up
27 the mobile home in the new location.
28 (b) abandon the mobile home in the mobile home park and collect an amount equal to 50% of the

1 maximum allowable moving expense for that mobile home from the mobile home park emergency relocation
2 account. To qualify for abandonment payment pursuant to this subsection (2)(b), the tenant shall deliver to the
3 landlord the current title to the mobile home with the notarized endorsement of the owner of record or a
4 notarized ownership change request form if the title does not exist with complete releases of all liens that are
5 shown on the title and proof that all taxes owing on the mobile home have been paid to date. The tenant shall
6 provide a copy of these documents to the department in support of the tenant's application for payment.
7 (3) If a tenant is vacating the premises and has informed the landlord before the change-in-use
8 notice has been given, the tenant is not eligible for compensation under this section.
9 (4) A person who purchases a mobile home already situated in a park or moves a mobile home
10 into a park in which a change in use notice has been given is not eligible for compensation under this section.
11 (5) This section does not apply to a change in use if the landlord moves a tenant to another space
12 in the mobile home park at the landlord's expense.
13 (6) The department may contract with a nonprofit entity to administer funds allowed under this
14 section and may collect an administrative fee of up to 10% of the funds distributed to the nonprofit entity.
15 (7) The department shall promulgate rules necessary to administer and enforce this section.
16
17 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Assessment for mobile home park emergency relocation account --
18 waiver. (1) Each owner of a mobile home located in a mobile home park who does not own the land on which
19 the mobile home is located shall pay each year to the state an assessment equal to a rate of $1.50 for each
20 $100 of the taxable value of the mobile home for each mobile home the person owns.
21 (2) The county treasurer shall collect the assessment imposed under this section at the same time
22 and in the same manner as personal property taxes. The county treasurer shall separately list the assessment
23 on the tax bill and shall transfer the revenue collected to the state for deposit in the mobile home park
24 emergency relocation account established in [section 3].
25 (3) The department of revenue shall:
26 (a) notify the county treasurers to waive the assessment for any year in which the amount in the
27 account established in [section 3] exceeds $1 million; and
28 (b) if, at the end of a fiscal year, the amount of money in the account established in [section 3] is

3 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Mobile home park emergency relocation account. (1) (a) There is a
4 special revenue account within the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102 called the mobile home
5 park emergency relocation account to aid mobile home owners who rent a mobile home lot and are required to
6 relocate as provided for in [section 1].
7 (b) There must be deposited in the account:
8 (i) all monetary contributions, gifts, and donations received from public or private sources;
9 (ii) all revenue collected from assessments pursuant to [section 2]; and
10 (iii) all revenue collected from license fees pursuant to 50-52-202.
11 (c) Money in the account is statutorily appropriated, as provided in 17-7-502, to the department of
12 commerce and may be used only for those purposes provided in this section.
13 (2) Money may be released from the special revenue account to:
14 (a) eligible tenants meeting the requirements of [section 1]; or
15 (b) with agreement from the tenant or tenants, any individual or organization that applies for
16 relocation assistance from the account who intends to use the funds in combination with public or private
17 sources.
