5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS FOR STATE AGENCIES PROVIDING
6 INFORMATION TO LEGISLATORS; ALLOWING LEGISLATORS TO SUBMIT A COMPLAINT TO A
7 COMMITTEE WHEN A STATE AGENCY HAS NOT PRODUCED REQUESTED INFORMATION WITHIN 5
8 LEGISLATIVE DAYS OF A WRITTEN REQUEST; PROVIDING FOR A HEARING BY A COMMITTEE;
9 PROVIDING FOR THE COMMITTEE TO VOTE TO REDUCE A STATE AGENCY BUDGET BY NO MORE
10 THAN 5% 3%; PROVIDING THAT INFORMATION FROM THE HEARING BE PRESENTED TO CERTAIN
11 ELECTED OFFICIALS; PROVIDING A DEFINITION; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Legislator request for information from state agency -- complaint
16 process. (1) A legislator may make informal requests for information from a state agency.
17 (2) (a) If a legislator makes a written request for information from a state agency during the regular
18 legislative session, the state agency shall respond with the requested information within 5 legislative days of
19 receiving the legislator's written request.
20 (b) The legislator's written request must be:
21 (i) provided to the director of the agency on the same day the request is made to that agency's
22 staff; and
23 (ii) limited to one bill or one specific issue; and
24 (iii) clearly intended as information of use for legislation or oversight.
25 (3) A legislator may not make more than one written request as outlined in subsection (2) a week.
26 (4) A state agency need only respond to 10 formal requests in a week. If a legislator makes a
27 formal request after the agency has responded to 10 formal requests that week, the agency shall notify the
28 legislator that the request will be responded to the following week.

1 (4)(5) After the deadline for responding has passed, the legislator may submit a written complaint with
2 the committee that has the general appropriations act in its possession. The complaint must contain the
3 following information:
4 (a) the name of the legislator;
5 (b) the state agency from which information was requested;
6 (c) a copy of the written request for information;
7 (d) a copy of the director's response to the written request, if any; and
8 (e) the legislator's proposed reduction to that agency's budget.
9 (5)(6) The committee in possession of the general appropriations act shall schedule a hearing on the
10 complaint within 3 legislative days of receiving the complaint.
11 (a) Notice of the time and place of the hearing must be provided to the legislator and the director of
12 the agency within 48 hours of the hearing.
13 (b) The hearing may be postponed by the presiding officer of the committee if either the legislator
14 or the director of the agency is unavailable to attend in person or cannot attend remotely by electronic means
15 due to hospitalization or catastrophic event. The hearing must be rescheduled by the presiding officer of the
16 committee at the earliest date when both the legislator and the director of the agency are available to attend the
17 hearing.
18 (c) The hearing, discussion of the committee, and vote of the committee must be recorded.
19 (6)(7) (a) After the hearing, the committee may vote to reduce the agency's budget by no more than
20 5% 3% for failing to provide the requested information.
21 (b) An agency's funding may not be reduced under this statute more than 3% in any one legislative
22 session.
23 (7)(8) The recording of the hearing, discussion of the committee, and vote of the committee must be
24 provided by the committee clerk to the president of the senate, the speaker of the house, and the governor
25 within 10 days of the committee's vote.
26 (8)(9) The governor shall include the committee's vote in the director's personnel record and may
27 subject the director to disciplinary measures, including termination.
28 (10) The employee directed to retrieve the information by the legislator and the employee's director

4 (a) "State agency" means a department, board, quasi-judicial board, commission, committee,
