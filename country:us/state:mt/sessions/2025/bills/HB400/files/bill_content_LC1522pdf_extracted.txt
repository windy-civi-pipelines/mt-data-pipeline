4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE "FREE TO SPEAK ACT"; PROHIBITING
5 PUBLIC SCHOOLS OR THE STATE FROM IMPOSING DISCIPLINARY OR OTHER ADVERSE ACTIONS ON
6 A STUDENT OR EMPLOYEE WHO REFUSES TO USE CERTAIN SPEECH; PROVIDING A CAUSE OF
7 ACTION FOR VIOLATIONS OF THE FREE TO SPEAK ACT; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AND PROVIDING
8 AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 4] may be cited as the "Free to Speak
13 Act".
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 4], the following definitions
16 apply:
17 (1) "Employee" means an individual who is employed or contract by a public school or the state.
18 (2) "Noncharter public school" has the meaning provided in 20-6-803.
19 (3) "Public charter school" has the meaning provided in 20-6-803.
20 (4) (a) "Public school" means a noncharter public school, a public charter school, or a public
21 institution of higher education.
22 (b) The term does not include a nonpublic school or home school as described in 20-5-102(2)(e).
23 (5) "Sex" has the meaning provided in 1-1-201.
24 (6) "State" means the state of Montana or a county, municipality, board, commission, department,
25 institution, or special district, or a subdivision or agency of the state of Montana or a county, municipality, board,
26 commission, department, institution, or special district.
27 (7) "Student" means an individual who is enrolled in a public school on a full-time or part-time
28 basis.

1
2 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Protection against compelled speech. (1) A student may not be
3 subject to a disciplinary action for declining to:
4 (a) identify the student's pronouns; or
5 (b) address a person by using a name other than the person's legal name or a derivative of the
6 person's legal name or by using a pronoun or a title that is inconsistent with the person's sex.
7 (2) An employee, regardless of the scope of the employee's official duties, may not be subject to
8 an adverse employment action for declining to:
9 (a) identify the employee's pronouns while acting within the scope of employment; or
10 (b) address a person by using a name other than the person's legal name or a derivative of the
11 person's legal name or by using a pronoun or a title that is inconsistent with the person's sex.
12 (3) The state may not penalize or take an adverse action against a person because the person
13 declines to:
14 (a) identify the person's pronouns; or
15 (b) address another person by using a name other than the other person's legal name or a
16 derivative of the other person's legal name or by using a pronoun or a title that is inconsistent with the other
17 person's sex.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Private cause of action -- penalties -- limitation. (1) A person who is
20 harmed by a violation of [sections 1 through 4] may bring a cause of action against a public school or the state
21 for injunctive relief, monetary damages, reasonable attorney fees and costs, and any other appropriate relief.
22 (2) A civil action brought pursuant to this section must be initiated within 2 years after the violation
23 of [sections 1 through 4] occurs.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 4] are intended to be
26 codified as an integral part of Title 49, chapter 1, part 1, and the provisions of Title 49, chapter 1, part 1, apply
27 to [sections 1 through 4].
28

1 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
2 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
3 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval.
