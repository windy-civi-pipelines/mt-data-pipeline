4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR THE REGULATION AND SALE OF
5 AQUACULTURE; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING EXEMPTIONS; PROVIDING RULEMAKING
6 AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 30-12-301, 50-49-203, 50-50-102, AND 80-3-302, MCA; AND

9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

11 Section 1. Section 30-12-301, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "30-12-301. Method of sale of commodities -- general. (1) Commodities in liquid form may be sold
13 only by liquid measure or by weight, and, except as otherwise provided in parts 1 through 5, commodities not in
14 liquid form may be sold only by weight, by measure of length or area, or by count. Liquid commodities may be
15 sold by weight and commodities not in liquid form may be sold by count only if those methods give accurate
16 information as to the quantity of commodity sold. This section does not apply to:
17 (a) commodities when sold for immediate consumption on the premises where sold;
18 (b) vegetables when sold by the head or bunch;
19 (c) commodities in containers standardized by a law of this state or by federal law;
20 (d) commodities in package form when there exists a general consumer usage to express the
21 quantity in some other manner;
22 (e) concrete aggregates, concrete mixtures, and loose solid materials such as earth, soil, gravel,
23 crushed stone, and the like, when sold by cubic measure;
24 (f) unprocessed vegetable and animal fertilizer when sold by cubic measure;
25 (g) cottage food products as defined in 50-50-102; or
26 (h) homemade food or homemade food products sold pursuant to Title 50, chapter 49, part 2; or
27 (i) aquaculture products as defined in 80-3-302.
28 (2) The department may adopt reasonable rules necessary to ensure that amounts of commodity

4 Section 2. Section 50-49-203, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "50-49-203. Exemptions from regulations -- transactions -- information required -- exceptions.
6 (1) (a) A state agency or an agency of a political subdivision of the state may not require licensure, permitting,
7 certification, packaging, labeling, testing, sampling, or inspection that pertains to the preparation, serving, use,
8 consumption, delivery, or storage of homemade food or a homemade food product under this part.
9 (b) This part does not preclude an agency from providing assistance, consultation, or inspection
10 requested by a producer.
11 (c) A producer is not:
12 (i) a retail food establishment, a cottage food operation, or a temporary food establishment, as
13 each term is defined in 50-50-102;
14 (ii) a wholesale food manufacturing establishment, as defined in 50-57-102; or
15 (iii) a dairy or a manufactured dairy products plant, as defined in 81-22-101.
16 (d) A producer is not subject to labeling, licensure, inspection, sanitation, or other requirements or
17 standards of 30-12-301; Title 50, chapters 31 and 50; or Title 81, chapters 2, 9, 21, 22, or 23.
18 (2) Transactions pursuant to this part:
19 (a) must be directly between the producer and the informed end consumer;
20 (b) must be only for home consumption or consumption at a traditional community social event;
21 (c) must occur only in this state and may not involve interstate commerce; and
22 (d) are not subject to regulation by a board of county commissioners pursuant to 7-21-3301.
23 (3) Except as provided in subsection (7), a producer shall inform an end consumer that any
24 homemade food or homemade food product sold through ranch, farm, or home-based sales pursuant to this
25 part has not been licensed, permitted, certified, packaged, labeled, or inspected per any official regulations.
26 (4) Except for raw, unprocessed fruit, and vegetables, and aquaculture products as defined in 80-
27 3-302, homemade food may not be sold or used in a retail food establishment, as defined in 50-50-102, unless
28 the food has been licensed, permitted, certified, packaged, labeled, and inspected as required by law.

1 (5) Except as provided in subsection (6) and pursuant to this part, a producer may donate
2 homemade food or homemade food products to a traditional community social event.
3 (6) A producer may not donate milk to a traditional community social event.
4 (7) (a) Except for a temporary food establishment subject to 50-50-120, meat or meat products
5 processed at a state-licensed establishment or a federally approved meat establishment, by the producer, or by
6 any third party may not be used in preparation of homemade food that is sold pursuant to a transaction
7 provided for in this part.
8 (b) Subsection (7)(a) does not apply to a producer who slaughters fewer than 1,000 poultry birds a
9 year except that the producer is subject to the requirements of 9 CFR 381.10(c) and the recordkeeping
10 requirements of 9 CFR 381.175. The poultry or poultry products may not be adulterated or misbranded.
11 (8) A small dairy shall:
12 (a) sample, test, or retest every 6 months for standard plate count, coliform count, and somatic cell
13 count of milk or cream sold as homemade food pursuant to this part;
14 (b) sample, test, or retest every year for brucellosis for every lactating cow, lactating goat, or
15 lactating sheep that is part of the small dairy; and
16 (c) maintain records for 2 years of all previous samples, tests, or retests, which must be provided
17 to the department of livestock if the department suspects the small dairy is causing a foodborne illness."

3 Section 4. Section 80-3-302, MCA, is amended to read:
4 "80-3-302. Definitions. As used in this part, unless the context indicates otherwise, the following
5 definitions apply:
6 (1) "Aquaculture" means the COMMERCIAL production of aquatic organisms under controlled
7 conditions throughout part or all of their life cycle.
8 (2) "Aquaculture products" means articles used for human consumption produced by aquaculture.
9 The term includes but is not limited to fish, shellfish, and aquatic plants.
10 (1)(3) "Container" means any package, sack, box, crate, carton, basket, or other object used for the
11 shipping of produce.
12 (4) (a) "Controlled conditions" means the management of water quality, feed, stocking density, and
13 environmental parameters within an aquatic system to optimize the growth and health of farmed aquatic
14 organisms. CONTROLLED CONDITIONS INCLUDE THE CONDITIONS REQUIRED BY A CONTROLLED SPECIES PERMIT OR A
15 PROHIBITED SPECIES PERMIT ISSUED PURSUANT TO TITLE 87, CHAPTER 5, PART 7.
16 (b) The term does not include a pond, lake, reservoir, river, OCEAN, or stream.
17 (2)(5) "Director" means the director of agriculture appointed pursuant to 2-15-3001.
18 (3)(6) "Person" means an individual, firm, association, corporation, partnership, or any other form of
19 business enterprise.
20 (4)(7) (a) "Produce" means any fruit, vegetable, aquaculture product, or other natural product
21 designated by department rule.
22 (b) The term does not include livestock and its byproducts, poultry and its byproducts, apiary
23 products, dairy products, or grain.
24 (5)(8) "Produce dealer" means a person who engages in a business involving or who as part of a
25 business participates in purchasing, exchanging, negotiating, or soliciting the sale, resale, exchange, or transfer
26 of produce in this state, except vegetative seed potato products intended or used for planting purposes.
27 (6)(9) "Produce unit" means the standard size container for a given type of produce. A produce unit
28 may be established by department rule.
