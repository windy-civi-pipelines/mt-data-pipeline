4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE MONTANA HERITAGE PRESERVATION AND
5 DEVELOPMENT COMMISSION; REVISING THE QUALIFICATION AND NUMBER OF THE MEMBERS OF
6 THE COMMISSION; REVISING THE ADMINISTRATIVE REQUIREMENTS AND PROCESSES BETWEEN
7 THE COMMISSION AND THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE; AMENDING SECTION 22-3-1002, MCA;
8 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 22-3-1002, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "22-3-1002. Montana heritage preservation and development commission. (1) There is a
14 Montana heritage preservation and development commission. The commission is attached to the department of
15 commerce for administrative purposes only, pursuant to 2-15-121. The commission and the department shall
16 negotiate a specific indirect administrative rate annually. The rate determined by the commission and the
17 department must be reported to the economic affairs interim committee in accordance with 5-11-210.
18 (2) (a) The commission consists of 149 14 members. The members shall must broadly represent
19 the state. Nine Four Nine members must be appointed by the governor, one member must be appointed by the
20 president of the senate, and one member must be appointed by the speaker of the house.
21 (b) If the president of the senate and the speaker of the house do not appoint the members for
22 which they are responsible within 6 months of a vacancy having occurred in those positions, the members must
23 be appointed by the governor.
24 (c) The director of the Montana historical society, the director of the department of fish, wildlife,
25 and parks, and the director of the department of commerce shall serve as members.
26 (d) Of the members appointed by the governor When appointing a member under subsection
27 (2)(a), the governor shall prioritize selection based on whether the member Of the members appointed by the

15 (4) (a) The commission may employ:
16 (i) an executive director who has general responsibility for the selection and management of
17 commission staff, developing recommendations for the purchase of property, and overseeing the management
18 of acquired property;
19 (ii) a curator who is responsible for the display and preservation of the acquired property; and
20 (iii) other staff that the commission and the executive director determine are necessary to manage
21 and operate commission properties.
22 (b) The commission shall prescribe the duties and annual salary of the executive director, the
23 curator, and other commission staff.
24 (4) In compliance with the state pay plan, the department shall provide all staff and services to the
25 commission that the commission, in conjunction with the department, determines are necessary for carrying out
26 the commission's programs and statutory requirements. The department shall assess the commission for
27 reasonable costs."
