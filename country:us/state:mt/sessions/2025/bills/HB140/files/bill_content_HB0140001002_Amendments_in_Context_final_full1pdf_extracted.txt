5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING PROPERTY TAX ASSISTANCE FOR FIRST
6 RESPONDERS INJURED IN THE LINE OF DUTY AND SURVIVING SPOUSES OF FIRST RESPONDERS
7 KILLED IN THE LINE OF DUTY; PROVIDING ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS;
8 EXPANDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AMENDING SECTIONS 15-6-301, 15-6-302, AND 15-6-312, MCA;
9 AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Injured first responder program. (1) The residential real property of a
14 qualified first responder or a qualified first responder's spouse is eligible to receive a tax rate reduction as
15 provided in 15-6-302 and this section.
16 (2) Property qualifying under subsection (1) and owned by a qualified first responder is taxed at
17 the rate provided in 15-6-134 multiplied by a percentage figure based on the applicant's qualifying income
18 determined from the following table:
19 Income Income Percentage
20 Single Person Married Couple Multiplier
21 Head of Household
22 $0 - $46,977 $0 - $56,372 0%
23 $46,978 - $51,676 $56,373 - $61,071 20%
24 $51,677 - $56,372 $61,072 - $65,768 30%
25 $56,373 - $61,071 $65,769 - $70,467 50%
26 (3) For a surviving spouse who owns property qualifying under subsection (4), the property is
27 taxed at the rate established by 15-6-134 multiplied by a percentage figure based on the spouse's qualifying
28 income determined from the following table:

7 (4) The property tax exemption under this section remains in effect as long as the qualifying
8 income requirements are met and the property is the primary residence owned and occupied by the qualified
9 first responder or, if the first responder is deceased, by the first responder's spouse, and the spouse:
10 (a) is the owner and occupant of the house;
11 (b) is unmarried; and
12 (c) has obtained a letter from the first responder's employer indicating that the first responder was
13 killed in the line of duty or died from a disability resulting from an injury in the line of duty.
14 (5) The qualifying income levels contained in subsections (2) and (3) must be adjusted annually by
15 using the PCE inflation factor defined in 15-6-301, rounded to the nearest whole dollar amount. If the
16 adjustment results in a decrease in qualifying income levels from the previous year, the qualifying income levels
17 must remain the same for that year.

19 Section 2. Section 15-6-301, MCA, is amended to read:
20 "15-6-301. Definitions. As used in this part, the following definitions apply:
21 (1) "Annual verification" means the use of a process to:
22 (a) verify an applicant's income;
23 (b) approve, renew, or deny benefits for the current year based upon the applicant's eligibility; and
24 (c) terminate participation based upon death or loss of status as a qualified veteran or veteran's
25 spouse.
26 (2) "Firefighter" means a firefighter qualified under 7-33-4107 who receives full or partial pay or a
27 volunteer firefighter as defined in 19-17-102.
28 (3) "First responder" means a law enforcement officer, or a firefighter, or volunteer emergency care

19 Section 3. Section 15-6-302, MCA, is amended to read:
20 "15-6-302. Property tax assistance -- rulemaking. (1) The requirements of this section must be met
21 for a taxpayer to qualify for property tax assistance under 15-6-305, or 15-6-311, or [section 1].
22 (2) For the property tax assistance programs provided for in 15-6-305, and 15-6-311, and [section
23 1], the residential real property must be owned by the applicant or under contract for deed and be the primary
24 residence as defined in 15-6-301. The department shall make rules specifying the indicators used for
25 determining whether a residence is a primary residence for purposes of property tax assistance programs.
26 (3) An applicant's qualifying income, as defined in 15-6-301, may not exceed the threshold
27 established in 15-6-305, or 15-6-311, or [section 1], or in rules established pursuant to those sections.
28 (4) (a) A claim for assistance must be submitted on a form prescribed by the department.

15 spouse's income, and approve, renew, or deny benefits for the current year based upon the findings.

17 [section 1], applicants are subject to the false swearing penalties established in 45-7-202.

9 Section 4. Section 15-6-312, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "15-6-312. Time period for property tax assistance. (1) A person who qualifies for assistance under
11 15-6-305, or 15-6-311, or [section 1] is entitled to assistance as provided for in this section.
12 (2) The property tax assistance is provided for the full tax year:
13 (a) in the first year in which the applicant qualifies for assistance if the applicant resides in the
14 qualifying property for the remainder of the tax year;
15 (b) if the applicant resides in the qualifying property for the full tax year;
16 (c) for qualifying property owned by an applicant at the time the tax roll is provided to the county
17 treasurer for billing if 15-6-301(4)(b)(8)(b) applies.
18 (3) If an applicant who qualifies for assistance sells the qualifying property and does not purchase
19 a new residence during the tax year, the assistance is provided for the number of days the taxpayer owned the
20 qualifying property during the tax year based on the date of sale.
21 (4) (a) Except as provided in subsection (4)(b), a person who purchases a qualifying property is
22 not entitled to assistance for the partial tax year during which the person owns the property. The property must
23 be assessed at the full tax rate for the portion of the year the person owns the property based on the date of
24 sale.
25 (b) If the sale date is after the county treasurer sends the tax notice provided for in 15-16-101(2),
26 the tax notice may not be revised based on the change in ownership."
