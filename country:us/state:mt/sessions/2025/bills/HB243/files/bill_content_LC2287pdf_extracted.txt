4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING AN INCOME TAX CREDIT FOR VOLUNTEER
5 EMERGENCY FIRST RESPONDERS; PROVIDING ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS; PROVIDING THAT THE
6 CREDIT IS REFUNDABLE; AMENDING SECTION 15-30-2303, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY
7 DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Credit for volunteer emergency first responders. (1) There is allowed
12 a credit against the tax imposed by 15-30-2103 for a qualified volunteer emergency first responder.
13 (2) To be entitled to the credit authorized in subsection (1), a taxpayer, during the year for which
14 the credit is claimed, must have provided active, uncompensated volunteer service as:
15 (a) a member of a volunteer fire company organized under the provisions of Title 7, chapter 33,
16 part 21, 22, 23, or 24, or a member of an emergency medical service as defined in 50-6-302;
17 (b) an auxiliary officer, a reserve officer, or a special services officer under the provisions of Title 7,
18 chapter 32, part 2;
19 (c) a volunteer firefighter as defined in 19-17-102;
20 (d) a volunteer emergency care provider as defined in 50-6-202; or
21 (e) an air search and rescue volunteer as provided in 67-2-105.
22 (3) The credit is equal to the lesser of $15 an hour of volunteer service provided or $1,500.
23 (4) A taxpayer claiming the credit allowed in this section shall include, on forms provided by the
24 department, evidence of the number of hours of active volunteer service performed during the tax year.
25 (5) If the amount of the credit exceeds the claimant's liability under this chapter, the amount of the
26 excess must be refunded to the claimant. The credit may be claimed even if the claimant has no taxable
27 income under this chapter.
28 (6) Pursuant to 5-4-104, the legislature finds that the purpose of the tax credit provided for in this
