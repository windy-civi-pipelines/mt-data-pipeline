5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT IMPLEMENTING THE RECLAMATION AND DEVELOPMENT
6 GRANTS PROGRAM; APPROPRIATING MONEY TO THE DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES AND
7 CONSERVATION FOR GRANTS UNDER THE RECLAMATION AND DEVELOPMENT GRANTS PROGRAM;
8 PRIORITIZING PROJECT GRANTS AND AMOUNTS; ESTABLISHING CONDITIONS FOR GRANTS;
9 PROVIDING FOR A TRANSFER OF FUNDS FROM THE STATE GENERAL FUND TO THE NATURAL
10 RESOURCES PROJECTS STATE SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
11 DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Appropriations for reclamation and development grants. (1) There is
16 appropriated up to $1.5 million $750,000 from the natural resources projects state special revenue account
17 established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants for planning
18 reclamation and development projects to be awarded by the department over the course of the biennium
19 beginning July 1, 2025.
20 (2) There is appropriated up to $5.3 million from the natural resources projects state special
21 revenue account established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants
22 for pilot water storage planning and projects to be awarded by the department over the course of the biennium
23 beginning July 1, 2025.
24 (3)(2) There is appropriated $7,110,975 $6,342,275 from the natural resources projects state special
25 revenue account established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants to
26 political subdivisions and local governments during the biennium beginning July 1, 2025. The funds in this
27 subsection (3) (2) must be awarded by the department to the named entities for the described purposes and in
28 the grant amounts set out in subsection (5) (4) subject to the conditions set forth in [sections 2 and 3] and the

19 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Coordination of fund sources for grants to political subdivisions
20 and local governments. A grant recipient listed under [section 1(5)] [section 1(4)] may not receive funds from
21 both the reclamation and development grants program and the renewable resource grant and loan program for
22 the same project during the same biennium.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Condition of grants. The disbursement of funds under [section 1] is
25 subject to the following conditions that must be met by the grant recipient:
26 (1) The grant recipient must have a scope of work, schedule, and budget for the project that is
27 approved by the department of natural resources and conservation. Any changes in scope of work or budget
28 subsequent to legislative approval may not alter project goals and objectives. Changes in activities that would

1 reduce the public or natural resource benefits as presented in department of natural resources and
2 conservation reports and applicant testimony to the 69th legislature may result in a proportional reduction in the
3 grant amount.
4 (2) The grant recipient shall document that other matching funds required for the completion of the
5 project are firmly committed.
6 (3) The grant recipient must have a project management plan that clearly outlines the roles of
7 participating entities and that is approved by the department of natural resources and conservation.
8 (4) The grant recipient shall comply with the auditing and reporting requirements provided for in 2-
9 7-503 and establish a financial accounting system that the department can reasonably ensure conforms to
10 generally accepted accounting principles. Tribal governments shall comply with the auditing and reporting
11 requirements provided for in 2 CFR 200.
12 (5) The grant recipient shall submit documentation to the department demonstrating satisfactory
13 completion of the conditions of described in the recommendation section of the project narrative of the program
14 report to the legislature for the biennium ending June 30, 2027, or, in the case of planning grants issued under
15 [section 1(1)], complete the conditions specified at the time of written notification of the approved grant
16 authority.
17 (6) The grant recipient shall execute a grant agreement with the department of natural resources
18 and conservation.
19 (7) Any other specific requirements considered necessary by the department must be met to
20 accomplish the purpose of the grant as evidenced from the application to the department or from the proposal
21 as presented to the legislature.
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Appropriations established. There is appropriated to any entity of state
24 government that receives a grant under [section 1] the amount of the grant upon award of the grant by the
25 department of natural resources and conservation. Grants to entities from prior biennia are reauthorized for the
26 completion of contract work.
27

5 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Transfer of funds. By July 1, 2025, the state treasurer shall transfer

9 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a

12 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Coordination instruction. (1) If both [this act] and an act that provides
13 additional funding for reclamation and development grants from a source other than the natural resources
14 projects state special revenue account established in 15-38-302 are passed and approved, the projects listed in
15 [section 1(5) of this act] [section 1(4) of this act] that do not receive funding from the appropriations in [section
16 1(3) of this act] [section 1(2) of this act] may receive funding from the appropriation in the other act designated
17 for reclamation and development grants as follows:
18 (a) in the order of completion of the conditions of [section 3 of this act]; and
19 (b) to the extent that there is appropriation authority available.
20 (2) If both [section 1(1)(b) of House Bill No. 6] and [section 1(1) of this act] are passed and
21 approved and if all of the $1.5 million $750,000 in grant funds authorized in [section 1(1) of this act] are not
22 expended for planning reclamation and development projects by the end of the biennium, then projects eligible
23 for funding under [section 1(1)(b) of House Bill No. 6] are eligible to apply for funding under [section 1(1) of this
24 act] for renewable resource project planning grants.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
27 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
28 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
