5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT IMPLEMENTING THE RECLAMATION AND DEVELOPMENT
6 GRANTS PROGRAM; APPROPRIATING MONEY TO THE DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES AND
7 CONSERVATION FOR GRANTS UNDER THE RECLAMATION AND DEVELOPMENT GRANTS PROGRAM;
8 PRIORITIZING PROJECT GRANTS AND AMOUNTS; ESTABLISHING CONDITIONS FOR GRANTS; AND
9 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Appropriations for reclamation and development grants. (1) There is
14 appropriated up to $1.5 million TO $750,000 from the natural resources projects state special revenue account
15 established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants for planning
16 reclamation and development projects to be awarded by the department over the course of the biennium
17 beginning July 1, 2025.
18
19 (2) There is appropriated up to $5.3 million from the natural resources projects state special
20 revenue account established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants
21 for pilot water storage planning and projects to be awarded by the department over the course of the biennium
22 beginning July 1, 2025.
23 (3) (2) There is appropriated $7,110,975 $3,774,208 from the natural resources projects state special
24 revenue account established in 15-38-302 to the department of natural resources and conservation for grants to
25 political subdivisions and local governments during the biennium beginning July 1, 2025. The funds in this
26 subsection (3) (2) must be awarded by the department to the named entities for the described purposes and in
27 the grant amounts set out in subsection (5) (4) subject to the conditions set forth in [sections 2 and 3] and the
28 contingencies described in the reclamation and development grants program report to the 69th legislature titled:

18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Coordination of fund sources for grants to political subdivisions
19 and local governments. A grant recipient listed under [section 1(5)] [SECTION 1(4)] may not receive funds from
20 both the reclamation and development grants program and the renewable resource grant and loan program for
21 the same project during the same biennium.
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Condition of grants. The disbursement of funds under [section 1] is
24 subject to the following conditions that must be met by the grant recipient:
25 (1) The grant recipient must have a scope of work, schedule, and budget for the project that is
26 approved by the department of natural resources and conservation. Any changes in scope of work or budget
27 subsequent to legislative approval may not alter project goals and objectives. Changes in activities that would
28 reduce the public or natural resource benefits as presented in department of natural resources and
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – HB 7
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0007.2
1 conservation reports and applicant testimony to the 69th legislature may result in a proportional reduction in the
2 grant amount.
3 (2) The grant recipient shall document that other matching funds required for the completion of the
4 project are firmly committed.
5 (3) The grant recipient must have a project management plan that clearly outlines the roles of
6 participating entities and that is approved by the department of natural resources and conservation.
7 (4) The grant recipient shall comply with the auditing and reporting requirements provided for in 2-
8 7-503 and establish a financial accounting system that the department can reasonably ensure conforms to
9 generally accepted accounting principles. Tribal governments shall comply with the auditing and reporting
10 requirements provided for in 2 CFR 200.
11 (5) The grant recipient shall submit documentation to the department demonstrating satisfactory
12 completion of the conditions of described in the recommendation section of the project narrative of the program
13 report to the legislature for the biennium ending June 30, 2027, or, in the case of planning grants issued under
14 [section 1(1)], complete the conditions specified at the time of written notification of the approved grant
15 authority.
16 (6) The grant recipient shall execute a grant agreement with the department of natural resources
17 and conservation.
18 (7) Any other specific requirements considered necessary by the department must be met to
19 accomplish the purpose of the grant as evidenced from the application to the department or from the proposal
20 as presented to the legislature.
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Appropriations established. There is appropriated to any entity of state
23 government that receives a grant under [section 1] the amount of the grant upon award of the grant by the
24 department of natural resources and conservation. Grants to entities from prior biennia are reauthorized for the
25 completion of contract work.
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Approval of grants -- completion of biennial appropriation. The
28 legislature, pursuant to 90-2-1111, approves the reclamation and development grants listed in [section 1]. The
- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 7
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 HB0007.2
1 authorization of these grants constitutes a biennial appropriation from the natural resources projects state
2 special revenue account established in 15-38-302.
3
4 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a
5 copy of [this act] to each federally recognized tribal government in Montana.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Coordination instruction. (1) If both [this act] and an act that provides
8 additional funding for reclamation and development grants from a source other than the natural resources
9 projects state special revenue account established in 15-38-302 are passed and approved, the projects listed in
10 [section 1(5) of this act] [SECTION 1(4) OF THIS ACT] that do not receive funding from the appropriations in [section
11 1(3) of this act] IN [SECTION 1(2) OF THIS ACT] may receive funding from the appropriation in the other act
12 designated for reclamation and development grants as follows:
13 (a) in the order of completion of the conditions of [section 3 of this act]; and
14 (b) to the extent that there is appropriation authority available.
15 (2) If both [section 1(1)(b) of House Bill No. 6] and [section 1(1) of this act] are passed and
16 approved and if all of the $1.5 million $750,000 in grant funds authorized in [section 1(1) of this act] are not
17 expended for planning reclamation and development projects by the end of the biennium, then projects eligible
18 for funding under [section 1(1)(b) of House Bill No. 6] are eligible to apply for funding under [section 1(1) of this
19 act] for renewable resource project planning grants.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Severability. If a part of [this act] is invalid, all valid parts that are
22 severable from the invalid part remain in effect. If a part of [this act] is invalid in one or more of its applications,
23 the part remains in effect in all valid applications that are severable from the invalid applications.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
26 - END -
