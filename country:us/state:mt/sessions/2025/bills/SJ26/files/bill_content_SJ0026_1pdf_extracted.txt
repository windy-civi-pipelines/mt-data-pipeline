1 SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION NO. 26
2 INTRODUCED BY K. BOGNER
3
5 MONTANA URGING THE UNITED STATES CONGRESS TO PROHIBIT THE TRADING OF INDIVIDUAL
6 STOCKS BY MEMBERS OF CONGRESS AND THEIR IMMEDIATE FAMILIES.
7
8 WHEREAS, the citizens of the United States expect that their elected representatives act in the public
9 interest and not for personal financial gain; and
10 WHEREAS, members of the United States Congress have access to sensitive, nonpublic information
11 and wield significant influence over legislation and regulatory actions that can directly impact the stock market
12 and individual companies; and
13 WHEREAS, public trust in government is undermined when members of Congress engage in stock
14 trading, creating the appearance or reality of conflicts of interest, insider trading, and self-dealing; and
15 WHEREAS, prohibiting members of Congress and their immediate families from trading individual
16 stocks would strengthen ethical standards, restore public confidence in government institutions, and affirm
17 Congress's commitment to serving the American people above personal financial interests; and
18 WHEREAS, numerous bipartisan proposals have been introduced in Congress to address this issue,
19 reflecting widespread agreement across the political spectrum that stronger restrictions on congressional stock
20 trading are necessary.
21
22 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF
23 THE STATE OF MONTANA:
24 That the 69th Legislature of Montana urges the United States Congress to pass a law prohibiting the
25 trading of individual stocks by members of Congress and their immediate families to restore the American
26 people's confidence in government institutions.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Secretary of State send a copy of this resolution to the
28 President of the United States, the Speaker of the United States House of Representatives, the President Pro
