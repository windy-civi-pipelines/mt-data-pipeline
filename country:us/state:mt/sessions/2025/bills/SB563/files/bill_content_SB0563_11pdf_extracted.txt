69th Legislature 2025 SB 563.1
2 INTRODUCED BY K. BOGNER
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE MONTANA BRIDGE TO MEDICAL
5 RESIDENCY ACT; PROVIDING FOR THE LICENSURE OF A SUPERVISED MEDICAL GRADUATE,
6 INCLUDING QUALIFICATIONS AND FEES; OUTLINING THE SCOPE OF PRACTICE OF A SUPERVISED
7 MEDICAL GRADUATE; REQUIRING A SUPERVISED MEDICAL GRADUATE TO BE SUPERVISED BY A
8 LICENSED PHYSICIAN; AND PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Short title. [Sections 1 through 5] may be cited as the "Montana Bridge
13 to Medical Residency Act".
14
15 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Purpose. The purpose of [sections 1 through 5] is to expand
16 opportunities for quality health care in Montana by creating educational and mentorship opportunities in the
17 state for medical graduates who do not match with a residency program.
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Supervised medical graduate -- qualifications for licensure -- fee for
20 licensure and renewal -- limitations. (1) Before being issued a license, an applicant may not practice as a
21 supervised medical graduate in this state.
22 (2) (a) The department may issue a supervised medical graduate license under the seal of the
23 board for a period of 1 year and shall charge a fee to applicants for licensure.
24 (b) The department may renew a supervised medical graduate license once, for a period of 1
25 additional year and shall charge a fee to the licensee for the renewal of the license.
26 (c) An individual may not practice as a supervised medical graduate for a period of more than 2
27 years.
28 (3) To be eligible for licensure as a supervised medical graduate, an individual must:

1 (a) have a degree of doctor of medicine from an accredited medical school or a degree of doctor of
2 osteopathy from an accredited school of osteopathy; and
3 (b) have passed parts 1 and 2 of the United States medical licensing examination.
4 (4) A supervised medical graduate license does not grant the licensee the ability to engage in the
5 practice of medicine in this state.
6
7 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Refusal of license -- suspension of license. If the board determines
8 that an applicant for a supervised medical graduate license or a licensed supervised medical graduate does not
9 possess the qualifications required by this chapter or that the applicant has committed unprofessional conduct,
10 the board may deny or suspend the license.
11
12 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Supervised medical graduate -- scope of practice -- rulemaking. (1)
13 (a) To the extent permitted by this section and the rules promulgated by the board, a supervised medical
14 graduate licensed pursuant to [sections 1 through 5] may perform duties as delegated by a physician licensed
15 pursuant to Title 37, chapter 3, part 3.
16 (b) A supervised medical graduate may not be supervised by an individual licensed as a resident
17 pursuant to Title 37, chapter 3, part 3.
18 (2) A licensed supervised medical graduate may perform duties delegated to the licensee by a
19 licensed physician if the licensed physician is:
20 (a) personally treating the patient;
21 (b) in the same medical facility as the patient and the supervised medical graduate; and
22 (c) immediately available to provide assistance and guidance to the supervised medical graduate
23 for the duration of the completion of a delegated duty.
24 (3) The board shall adopt rules to delineate the scope of duties that a licensed physician may
25 delegate to a supervised medical graduate.
26 (4) For the purposes of this section, a licensed physician is not required to be in the presence of a
27 patient and a supervised medical graduate during the completion of a delegated duty.
28
