****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 324.1
2 INTRODUCED BY S. FITZPATRICK
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING VOLUNTARY PAYMENTS LAWS TO INCLUDE ALL
5 CLAIMS; REVISING LEGISLATIVE POLICY FOR VOLUNTARY PAYMENTS TO INCLUDE ALL CLAIMS;
6 PROVIDING THAT CERTAIN BAD FAITH INSURANCE ACTIONS DO NOT APPLY TO VOLUNTARY
7 PAYMENTS; AMENDING SECTIONS 26-1-701, 26-1-703, AND 26-1-706, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
8 APPLICABILITY DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Section 26-1-701, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "26-1-701. Legislative policy. The legislature declares that the health, welfare, and safety of the
14 people of the state of Montana would be enhanced by the expeditious handling of liability claims. The
15 legislature further declares that the handling of liability claims would be expedited if voluntary payment by or on
16 behalf of one person to or on behalf of a person who has sustained injury to that person or damage to that
17 person's property could possesses a claim may not be construed as an admission of fault or liability as to any
18 claim arising out of the occurrence that gave rise to the injury or damage."
19
20 Section 2. Section 26-1-703, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "26-1-703. Voluntary partial payment of claim not an admission of fault or waiver. (1) No A
22 voluntary partial payment of a claim against any person based on alleged liability of that person for injury to
23 person, including death, or damage to property arising out of any occurrence shall may not be construed as an
24 admission of fault or liability or as a waiver or release of claim by the person to whom or in whose behalf such
25 the payment was made. No A voluntary partial payment shall may not be construed to reduce the amount of
26 damages which that may be pleaded or proved in any action arising out of such the occurrence.
27 (2) This section does not apply to actions brought under 33-18-201 or 33-18-242.
28 (3) This section does not apply to proceedings to determine attorney fees relating to breach of
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 324
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 324.1
1 contract, including but not limited to proceedings involving:
2 (a) offers of judgment; or
3 (b) claims seeking attorney fees from an insurer."
4
5 Section 3. Section 26-1-706, MCA, is amended to read:
6 "26-1-706. Effect of payment on judgment. After entry of a judgment in an action for damages for
7 personal injuries, including death, or for damage to property arising out of any occurrence, any voluntary partial
8 payment theretofore made shall up to that time must be treated as a credit against such the judgment and shall
9 must be deductible from the amount of such the judgment. If after partial voluntary payments are made as
10 herein provided for in this section, it shall must be determined by a court of competent jurisdiction that the
11 person who made such the payments or on whose behalf such the payments were are made is liable for an
12 amount which that is less than the amount of the voluntary payments already made, such the person shall may
13 not have no a right of action for the recovery of the amount by which the voluntary payments exceeded the
14 amount of the judgment."
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Applicability. [This act] applies to liability claims in which a cause of
17 action has accrued on or after October 1, 2025.
18 - END -
