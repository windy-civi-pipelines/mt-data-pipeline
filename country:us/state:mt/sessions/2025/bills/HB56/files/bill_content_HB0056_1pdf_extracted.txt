5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ESTABLISHING AN AMBULANCE PROVIDER ASSESSMENT
6 FEE; ESTABLISHING PROCEDURES FOR COLLECTING AND DISTRIBUTING THE ASSESSMENT FEE;
7 ALLOWING AUDITING OF AMBULANCE PROVIDER REPORTS AND PAYMENTS; ALLOWING FOR
8 PENALTIES AND INTEREST; REQUIRING REVENUES GENERATED BY THE ASSESSMENT FEE TO BE
9 USED FOR SUPPLEMENTING AMBULANCE PROVIDER MEDICAID PAYMENTS; PROVIDING
10 DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING A STATUTORY APPROPRIATION;
11 AMENDING SECTION 17-7-502, MCA; PROVIDING FOR CONTINGENT VOIDNESS; AND PROVIDING A
12 CONTINGENT EFFECTIVE DATE.”
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Definitions. As used in [sections 1 through 13], the following definitions
17 apply:
18 (1) (a) "Ambulance provider" means a person licensed pursuant to 50-6-306 to provide ground
19 ambulance transport, including transport for a municipal fire or police department or other government entity.
20 (b) The term does not include:
21 (i) an entity that exclusively provides air ambulance services; or
22 (ii) an entity operated by the United States, an Indian tribe, or any facility authorized under the
23 Indian Health Care Improvement Act.
24 (2) "Assessment fee" means the ambulance provider assessment fee as provided in [section 2].
25 (3) "Department" means the department of revenue provided for in 2-15-1301.
26 (4) "Emergency ambulance services" means any service delivered by an ambulance provider other
27 than air ambulance services.
28 (5) (a) "Net operating revenue" means gross revenue collected by ambulance providers for the
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 56
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 56.1
1 delivery of emergency ambulance services, minus amounts deducted for bad debt, charity care, and payer
2 discounts.
3 (b) The term does not include nonpatient service-related revenue.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Ambulance provider assessment fee. (1) Each ambulance provider
6 shall pay to the department a uniform assessment fee of 5.75% of net operating revenues.
7 (2) The department shall deposit the proceeds from collection of the assessment fee in the
8 ambulance provider special revenue account provided for in [section 14].
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Relation to other taxes and fees. The assessment fee imposed under
11 [section 2] is, in addition to any other taxes and fees, required to be paid by ambulance providers.
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Rulemaking authority. The department may adopt rules necessary to
14 administer [sections 1 through 13].
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Reporting and collection of assessment fee. (1) On or before March
17 31 each year, an ambulance provider shall file with the department a report of its net operating revenue
18 received during the previous calendar year. The report must be:
19 (a) in the form prescribed by the department; and
20 (b) accompanied by a payment in an amount equal to the assessment fee required to be paid
21 under [section 2].
22 (2) Revenue received for all emergency ambulance services provided during the calendar year
23 must be included in the calculation of the ambulance provider's net operating revenue regardless of the source
24 of payment for the services rendered, including services covered under fee-for-service and managed care
25 arrangements.
26 (3) In the case of a transfer of ownership, the successor in interest to the ambulance provider
27 assumes the liability for the assessment fee.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Audit -- records. (1) The department may audit the records and other
2 documents of an ambulance provider to ensure that the proper assessment fee has been collected.
3 (2) The department may require the ambulance provider to provide records and other
4 documentation, including books, ledgers, and registers, necessary for the department to verify the proper
5 amount of the assessment fee paid.
6 (3) An ambulance provider shall maintain and make available for inspection by the department
7 sufficient records and other documentation to demonstrate how the ambulance provider's net operating revenue
8 was calculated. The ambulance provider shall maintain the records for at least 5 years from the date the report
9 is due.
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Periods of limitation. (1) Except as otherwise provided in this section, a
12 deficiency may not be assessed or collected with respect to the year for which a report is filed unless the notice
13 of additional fees proposed to be assessed is mailed within 5 years from the date the report was filed. For the
14 purposes of this section, a report filed before the last day prescribed for filing is considered filed on the last day.
15 If, before the expiration of the period prescribed for the assessment of the fees, the ambulance provider
16 consents in writing to an assessment after the 5-year period, the fees may be assessed at any time prior to the
17 expiration of the period agreed on.
18 (2) A refund or credit may not be paid or allowed with respect to the year for which a report is filed
19 after 5 years from the last day prescribed for filing the report or after 1 year from the date of the overpayment,
20 whichever period expires later, unless before the expiration of the period, the ambulance provider files a claim
21 or the department has determined the existence of the overpayment and has approved the refund or credit. If
22 the ambulance provider has agreed in writing under the provisions of subsection (1) to extend the time within
23 which the department may propose an additional assessment, the period for filing a claim for a refund or credit
24 or for allowing a refund or credit if no claim is filed, is automatically extended.
25
26 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Penalty and interest for delinquent fee. If an ambulance provider does
27 not pay the required assessment fee on or before the due date of the report as provided in [section 5], penalty
28 and interest, as provided in 15-1-216, must be added to the assessment fee.

2 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Estimated assessment fee on failure to file. For the purposes of
3 ascertaining the correctness of any report, the department may:
4 (1) examine or cause to have examined by a designated agent or representative any books,
5 papers, records, or memoranda bearing on the information required to be included in the report;
6 (2) require the attendance of any officer or employee of the ambulance provider making the report
7 or the attendance of any other persons having relevant knowledge; and
8 (3) take testimony and require the production of any other material for its information.
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Deficiency assessment -- penalty and interest -- hearing. (1) (a) If
11 the department determines that the amount of the assessment fee due is greater than the amount disclosed by
12 the report, it shall mail the ambulance provider a notice of the additional fees proposed to be assessed. Within
13 30 days after the mailing of the notice, the ambulance provider may file with the department a written protest
14 against the proposed additional fees stating the grounds on which the protest is based. The ambulance
15 provider may request in its protest an oral hearing or an opportunity to present additional evidence relating to its
16 fee liability.
17 (b) If a protest is not filed, the amount of the additional fees proposed to be assessed becomes
18 final on the expiration of the 30-day period.
19 (c) If a protest is filed, the department shall reconsider the proposed assessment and, if the
20 ambulance provider has requested, shall grant the provider an oral hearing. After consideration of the protest
21 and the evidence presented at an oral hearing, the department's action on the protest is final when it mails
22 notice of its action to the ambulance provider.
23 (2) When a deficiency is determined and the fees become final, the department shall mail a notice
24 and demand to the ambulance provider for payment. Penalty and interest may be added to any deficiency
25 assessment as provided in 15-1-216.
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 11. Closing agreements. (1) The director of the department or a person
28 authorized in writing by the director may enter into an agreement with an ambulance provider relating to the
- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 56
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 56.1
1 liability of the provider in respect to fees imposed by [sections 1 through 13].
2 (2) An agreement under this section is final and conclusive, and, except on a showing of fraud,
3 malfeasance, or misrepresentation of a material fact:
4 (a) the case may not be reopened as to matters agreed on or the agreement modified by an
5 officer, employee, or agent of this state; and
6 (b) in a suit, action, or proceeding under the agreement or a determination, assessment, collection,
7 payment, abatement, refund, or credit made in accordance with the agreement, the agreement may not be
8 annulled, modified, set aside, or disregarded.
9
10 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Credit for overpayment -- interest on overpayment. (1) If the
11 department determines that the amount of fees, penalty, or interest due for any year is less than the amount
12 paid, the amount of the overpayment must be credited against any fees, penalty, or interest then due from the
13 ambulance provider and the balance must be refunded to the ambulance provider or its successor through
14 reorganization, merger, or consolidation or to its shareholders on dissolution.
15 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), interest is allowed on overpayments at the same rate as
16 is charged on unpaid taxes, as provided in 15-1-216. Interest is due from the due date of the report or from the
17 date of overpayment, whichever date is later, to the date the department approves refunding or crediting of the
18 overpayment. Interest does not accrue during a period during which the processing of a claim for refund is
19 delayed more than 30 days by reason of failure of the ambulance provider to furnish information requested by
20 the department for the purpose of verifying the amount of the overpayment.
21 (3) Interest is not allowed:
22 (a) if the overpayment is refunded within 6 months from the date the report is due or from the date
23 the return is filed, whichever is later; or
24 (b) if the amount of interest is less than $1.
25 (4) A payment not made incident to a discharge of actual ambulance provider assessment fee
26 liability or payment reasonably assumed to be imposed under [sections 1 through 13] is not considered an
27 overpayment with respect to which interest is allowable.

1 NEW SECTION. Section 13. Warrant for distraint. If the assessment fee is not paid when due, the
2 department may issue a warrant for distraint as provided in Title 15, chapter 1, part 7.

4 NEW SECTION. Section 14. Ambulance medicaid reimbursement special revenue account --
5 statutory appropriation. (1) There is an ambulance medicaid reimbursement account in the state special
6 revenue account provided for in 17-2-102 to the credit of the department of public health and human services.
7 (2) The account consists of:
8 (a) money from the assessment fee provided for in [section 2];
9 (b) any penalties and interest on penalties collected pursuant to [sections 1 through 13];
10 (c) other money authorized by the legislature to be credited to the account; and
11 (d) income earned on the account.
12 (3) Money in the account must be used by the department of public health and human services to
13 provide funding no later than June 30 of each year for increases in medicaid payments to emergency
14 ambulance services up to the average commercial rate for the service and for the cost of collection of the fees
15 and other administrative activities associated with the implementation of increases in the medicaid payments to
16 ambulance providers.
17 (4) Money remaining in the account at the end of a fiscal year may not be expended or transferred
18 for any other purpose.
19 (5) Money in the account is statutorily appropriated, as provided in 17-7-502, for the purposes
20 provided for in this section.

3 NEW SECTION. Section 18. Contingent effective date. [This act] is effective on approval by the
4 United States department of health and human services of all waivers and approvals necessary to implement
5 the assessment fee and supplemental payments as provided in [sections 1 through 15]. The department of
6 public health and human services shall notify the code commissioner within 15 days of the occurrence of the
7 contingency.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 19. Contingent voidness. (1) [Sections 1 through 15] are void on the date
10 that federal law or policy is amended so that the assessment fee collected pursuant to [sections 1 through 15]
11 may not be considered as the state's share in claiming federal financial participation under the medicaid
12 program. The department of public health and human services shall submit certification of the change in federal
13 law or policy to the code commissioner within 15 days of the occurrence of the contingency.
14 (2) If [sections 1 through 15] are rendered void under the provisions of this section, all fees
15 received or collected by the department of revenue prior to the date on which the act becomes void must be
16 deposited in accordance with [section 2], and a person or party may not receive a refund of any fees received
17 or collected by the department prior to the date on which [sections 1 through 15] become void.
18 (3) If the United States department of health and human services fails to approve the ambulance
19 provider supplemental payments, [sections 1 through 15] are void. The department of public health and human
20 services shall notify the code commissioner within 15 days of the occurrence of the contingency.

22 notice of its action to the ambulance provider.
