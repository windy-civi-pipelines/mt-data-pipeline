9 section, by BY complying with [sections 1 through 6 9], a domestic entity may become a domestic entity of the
10 same type of entity in a foreign jurisdiction if the domestication is authorized by the law of the foreign

17 company, professional limited liability company, limited liability partnership, professional limited liability
18 partnership, benefit corporation, or nonprofit corporation.

23 became an interest holder after the adoption of that provision.
24 (2) A domestication of a foreign domesticating entity is not effective unless it is approved in
25 accordance with the law of the foreign entity’s jurisdiction of formation.

8 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Effect of domestication. (1) When a domestication becomes effective:
9 (a) the domesticated entity:
10 (i) is organized under and subject to the organic law of the domesticated entity; and
11 (ii) is the same entity without interruption as the domesticating entity;
12 (b) all property of the domesticating entity continues to be vested in the domesticated entity
13 without transfer, reversion, or impairment;
14 (c) all debts, obligations, and other liabilities of the domesticating entity continue as debts,
15 obligations, and other liabilities of the domesticated entity;
16 (d) except as otherwise provided by law or by the plan of domestication, all of the rights, privileges,
17 immunities, powers, and purposes of the domesticating entity remain in the domesticated entity;
18 (e) the name of the domesticated entity may be substituted for the name of the domesticating
19 entity in any pending action or proceeding;
20 (f) if the domesticated entity is a filing entity, its public organic record is effective;
21 (g) the private organic rules of the domesticated entity that are to be in a record, if any, approved
22 as part of the plan of domestication are effective; and
23 (h) the interests in the domesticating entity are converted to the extent of and as approved in
24 connection with the domestication and the domesticating entity's organic law. The interest holders of the
25 domesticating entity are entitled only to the rights provided to them under the plan of domestication and to any
26 appraisal rights THEY MAY HAVE UNDER THE ORGANIC LAWS OF THE DOMESTICATING ENTITY AND ANY CONTRACTUAL
27 APPRAISAL RIGHTS THEY MAY HAVE UNDER THE ORGANIC RULES OF THE DOMESTICATING ENTITY.

3 NEW SECTION. SECTION 8. NONEXCLUSIVITY. A TRANSACTION UNDER [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 9] THAT
4 PRODUCES A CERTAIN RESULT MAY NOT PRECLUDE THE SAME RESULT FROM BEING ACCOMPLISHED IN ANY OTHER
5 MANNER PERMITTED BY LAW OTHER THAN IN [SECTIONS 1 THROUGH 9].
6
7 NEW SECTION. SECTION 9. REFERENCE TO EXTERNAL FACTS. A PLAN OF DOMESTICATION MAY REFER TO
8 FACTS ASCERTAINABLE OUTSIDE THE PLAN IF THE MANNER IN WHICH THE FACTS WILL OPERATE UPON THE PLAN IS
9 SPECIFIED IN THE PLAN. THE FACTS MAY INCLUDE THE OCCURRENCE OF AN EVENT OR A DETERMINATION OR ACTION BY A
10 PERSON, WHETHER OR NOT THE EVENT, DETERMINATION, OR ACTION IS WITHIN THE CONTROL OF A PARTY TO THE
11 TRANSACTION.

13 NEW SECTION. Section 10. Codification instruction. [Sections 1 through 6 9] are intended to be
14 codified as a new part in Title 35, chapter 8, and the provisions of Title 35, chapter 8, apply to [sections 1
15 through 6 9].
