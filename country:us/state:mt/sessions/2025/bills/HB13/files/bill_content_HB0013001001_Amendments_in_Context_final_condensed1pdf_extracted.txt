5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS GOVERNING STATE EMPLOYEE
6 COMPENSATION; REVISING STATE EMPLOYEE PER DIEM RATES; INCREASING THE EMPLOYER
7 CONTRIBUTION FOR GROUP BENEFITS; SETTING THE HOURLY PAY RATE FOR LEGISLATORS IN
8 FUTURE BIENNIA; APPROPRIATING FUNDS TO IMPLEMENT PAY AND BENEFIT REVISIONS AND PER
9 DIEM ADJUSTMENTS; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AMENDING SECTIONS 2-18-303, 2-18-501, 2-
10 18-703, AND 5-2-301, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 2-18-303, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "2-18-303. Procedures for administering broadband pay plan. (1) On the first day of the first
16 complete pay period in fiscal year 2024 2026, each employee is entitled to the amount of the employee's base
17 salary as it was on June 30, 2023 2025.
18 (2) To the extent that the plan applies to employees within a collective bargaining unit, the
19 implementation of the plan is a negotiable subject under 39-31-305.
20 (3) Effective on the first day of the first complete pay period that includes July 1, 2023 2025, the
21 base salary of each employee must be increased by $1.50 $1.00 an hour or by 4% 2.5%, whichever is greater.
22 Effective on the first day of the first complete pay period that includes July 1, 2024 2026, the base salary of
23 each employee must be increased by $1.50 $1.00 an hour or by 4% 2.5%, whichever is greater. All full-time
24 employees must receive a one-time, lump-sum payment of $1,040 in the first full pay period after April 11,
25 2023. All employees who are regularly scheduled to work 20 hours or more a week but less than 40 hours a
26 week must receive a one-time, lump-sum payment of $780 in the first full pay period after April 11, 2023. All
27 employees who are regularly scheduled to work less than 20 hours a week must receive a one-time, lump-sum
28 payment of $520 in the first full pay period after April 11, 2023. These payments are applicable for fiscal year

1 Section 2. Section 2-18-501, MCA, is amended to read:
2 "2-18-501. Meals, lodging, and transportation of persons in state service. All elected state
3 officials, appointed members of boards, commissions, or councils, department directors, and all other state
4 employees must be reimbursed for meals and lodging while away from the person's designated headquarters
5 and engaged in official state business in accordance with the following provisions:
6 (1) Except as provided under subsection (3), for travel within the state of Montana, the following
7 provisions apply:
8 (a) lodging Lodging must be authorized at the actual cost of lodging and taxes on the allowable
9 cost of lodging., except as provided in subsection (3), plus $8.25 for the morning meal, $9.25 for the midday
10 meal, and $16.00 for the evening meal except as provided in subsection (9). All claims for lodging expense
11 reimbursement allowed under this section must be documented by an appropriate receipt.
12 (b) Except as provided in subsection (9), meals must be authorized at 70% of the standard federal
13 rate of reimbursement for breakfast, lunch, and dinner in Montana established by the United States general
14 services administration in accordance with the federal travel regulation.
15 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), for travel outside the state of Montana including foreign
16 travel, the following provisions apply:
17 (a) Lodging must be reimbursed at actual cost, not to exceed the prescribed maximum standard
18 federal rate per day for the location involved plus taxes on the allowable cost.
19 (b) Meal reimbursement may not exceed the prescribed maximum standard federal rate per meal.
20 (3) Except as provided in subsection (9), the department of administration shall designate the
21 locations and circumstances under which the governor, other elected state officials, appointed members of
22 boards, commissions, or councils, department directors, and all other state employees may be authorized the
23 actual cost of the following:
24 (a) meals, not including alcoholic beverages, when the actual cost exceeds the maximum
25 established in subsection (2)(b); and
26 (b) lodging when the actual cost exceeds the maximum established in subsection (2)(a).
27 (4) When other than commercial, nonreceiptable lodging facilities are used by a state official or
28 employee while conducting official state business in a travel status, the amount of $12 is authorized for lodging
