9 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Duties of K-12 data task force regarding student data privacy. (1)
10 The superintendent of public instruction shall continually inform and seek input from the K-12 data task force
11 provided for in 20-7-105 regarding the data inventory and dictionary of data elements created by the education
12 and workforce data governing board pursuant to 20-7-138, including but not limited to:
13 (a) education data that is required to be reported by state and federal education mandates; and
14 (b) education data that has been proposed for inclusion in the statewide K-12 data system by the
15 board with a statement regarding the purpose or reason for the proposed collection.
16 (2) The superintendent of public instruction shall continually work in consultation with the K-12
17 data task force to:
18 (a) develop a detailed data security plan that includes:
19 (i) privacy compliance standards;
20 (ii) privacy and security audits;
21 (iii) breach planning, notification, and procedures; and
22 (iv) data retention and disposition policies;
23 (b) develop, publish, and make publicly available policies and procedures to comply with the
24 Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. 1232g, as amended, and its implementing
25 regulations at 34 CFR, part 99, and other relevant federal and state privacy laws; and
26 (c) provide annual notification to students and parents regarding their privacy rights under federal
27 and state law, including the right to be forgotten under [section 1].

4 Section 5. Section 20-7-136, MCA, is amended to read:
5 "20-7-136. Legislative findings -- purpose. (1) The legislature finds that:
6 (a) the utilization of education and workforce data holds great promise for developing the full
7 educational potential of Montanans and in maximizing the effectiveness of state investments in education and
8 workforce systems; and
9 (b) a systems approach with shared governance between relevant agencies is the best way to
10 utilize education and workforce data while ensuring that the data is only used for appropriate purposes and in
11 compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C. 1232g, as amended, and its
12 implementing regulations at 34 CFR, part 99, and all other relevant federal and state privacy laws, including the
13 right to be forgotten pursuant to [section 1], and any other privacy measures required by the education and
14 workforce data governing board.
15 (2) The purpose of 20-7-136 through 20-7-138 is to create a strong and transparent education and
16 workforce data governing board with authority over the linkage of education and workforce data gathered and
17 maintained by state agencies to ensure that the data is used to benefit the people of the state in a secure
18 manner and only for appropriate purposes."
