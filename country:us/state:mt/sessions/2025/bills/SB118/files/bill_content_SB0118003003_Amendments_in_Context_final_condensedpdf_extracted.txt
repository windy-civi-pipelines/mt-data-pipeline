69th Legislature 2025 Drafter: Pad McCracken, SB0118.003.003
1 SENATE BILL NO. 118
2 INTRODUCED BY D. ZOLNIKOV
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATED TO EDUCATION
5 DATA; ESTABLISHING THE RIGHT TO BE FORGOTTEN; ENHANCING STUDENT DATA PRIVACY
6 PROTECTIONS IN THE STATEWIDE K-12 STUDENT DATA SYSTEM AND IN THE WORK OF THE
7 EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE DATA GOVERNING BOARD; PROHIBITING THE DIRECT USE IN WHOLE
8 OR IN PART OF A STUDENT'S SOCIAL SECURITY NUMBER IN ANY AS A DISTRIBUTED UNIQUE
9 IDENTIFIER USED TO LINK EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE DATA; PROVIDING ADDITIONAL
10 RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE DATA GOVERNING BOARD, THE
11 SUPERINTENDENT OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION, AND THE K-12 DATA TASK FORCE; PROVIDING
12 DEFINITIONS; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; PROVIDING AN APPROPRIATION; AMENDING
13 SECTIONS 20-7-104, 20-7-105, 20-7-136, 20-7-137, AND 20-7-138, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
14 DATE.”
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Right to be forgotten -- verifiable request -- timeline -- rulemaking.
19 (1) A student or parent may request that the superintendent of public instruction EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE
20 DATA GOVERNING BOARD ESTABLISHED IN 20-7-138 OFFICE OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION delete any education data on
21 the student collected and stored in the statewide K-12 data system PURSUANT TO THE BOARD'S AUTHORITY IN THE
22 K-12 STUDENT DATA SYSTEM by submitting a verifiable request pursuant to subsection (4).
23 (2) Except as provided in subsection (3), on receipt of a verifiable request submitted pursuant to
24 this section, the superintendent of public instruction EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE DATA GOVERNING BOARD OFFICE
25 OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION shall delete or have deleted FROM THE K-12 STUDENT DATA SYSTEM from the statewide K-
26 12 data system any education data on that student COLLECTED AND STORED IN THE K-12 STUDENT DATA SYSTEM
27 collected and stored in the statewide K-12 data system within the time provided for in subsection (6).

1 (f) develop and implement policies and procedures regarding data and research requests;
2 (g) develop and make available a model data-sharing agreement that allows for reciprocal sharing
3 of information between public schools, public, private, or tribal institutions of higher education, and state and
4 local workforce entities; and
5 (h) develop and implement policies:
6 (i) to ensure compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, 20 U.S.C.
7 1232g, as amended, and its implementing regulations at 34 CFR, part 99, and all other relevant federal and
8 state privacy laws;
9 (ii) to ensure compliance with the right to be forgotten pursuant to [section 1];
10 (iii) to prohibit the DIRECT use of a student's social security number in whole or in part as a unique
11 identifier by a contributing agency or a school district; and. THE POLICY MAY ALLOW THE USE OF A STUDENT'S
12 SOCIAL SECURITY NUMBER FOR THE PURPOSES OF GENERATING A MATCHING KEY TO LINK DATA.
13 (ii)(iv) to provide for additional privacy protections determined to be necessary by the board.
14 (8) The board may form committees, work groups, or advisory councils to accomplish the board's
15 purposes.
16 (9) The board shall, in accordance with 5-11-210, report to the education interim committee and
17 the education interim budget committee on the board's work."
18
19 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Appropriation. There is appropriated $25,000 from the general fund to
20 the office of public instruction for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, for the purposes of implementing [this
21 act].
22
23 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Codification instruction -- directions to code commissioner.
24 [Sections 1 and 4] are intended to be codified as an integral part of Title 20, chapter 1, and the provisions of
25 Title 20, chapter 1, apply to [sections 1 and 4].
26 (2) Sections 20-7-103, 20-7-104, 20-7-105, 20-7-136, 20-7-137, and 20-7-138 are intended to be
27 renumbered and codified with [sections 1 and 4] as a new part in Title 20, chapter 1.
