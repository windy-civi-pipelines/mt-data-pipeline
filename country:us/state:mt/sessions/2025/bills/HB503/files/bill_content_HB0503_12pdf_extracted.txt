69th Legislature 2025 HB 503.1
2 INTRODUCED BY D. BAUM, J. ISALY, S. KELLY, P. STRAND, J. SECKINGER, M. CUFFE, C. SPRUNGER,
3 G. LAMMERS, S. VINTON, J. WINDY BOY, G. OBLANDER, J. COHENOUR, D. HAWK, D. HAYMAN, S.
4 MORIGEAU, S. ESSMANN
5
6 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING LAWS REGARDING FLEEING FROM OR ELUDING A
7 PEACE OFFICER; CLASSIFYING MISDEMEANOR AND FELONY CONVICTIONS; PROVIDING PENALTIES;
8 REORGANIZING PROVISIONS TO CONFORM TO THE PATTERN IN TITLE 61, CHAPTER 8; AND
9 AMENDING SECTION 61-8-316, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Fleeing from or eluding peace officer. (1) Except as provided in
14 subsections (2) and (3), a person convicted of or pleading guilty or no contest to an offense under 61-8-316 is
15 guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be imprisoned for a term not to exceed 1 year or fined an amount not to
16 exceed $2,000, or both.
17 (2) A person convicted of the offense of aggravated fleeing from or eluding a peace officer under
18 61-8-316 is guilty of a felony and shall be imprisoned for a term not to exceed 5 years or fined an amount not to
19 exceed $5,000, or both.
20 (3) A person convicted of an offense of fleeing from or eluding a peace officer during which the
21 person causes serious bodily injury to or the death of any other person is guilty of a felony and shall be
22 imprisoned for a term not to exceed 10 years or fined an amount not to exceed $10,000, or both.
23
24 Section 2. Section 61-8-316, MCA, is amended to read:
25 "61-8-316. Fleeing from or eluding peace officer. (1) A person operating a motor vehicle commits
26 the offense of fleeing from or eluding a peace officer if a uniformed peace officer operating a police vehicle in
27 the lawful performance of the peace officer's duty gives the person a visual or audible signal by hand, voice,
28 emergency light, or siren directing the person to stop the motor vehicle and the person knowingly fails to obey

****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 503.1
1 the signal by refusing to bring the motor vehicle to a stop, increasing the speed of the motor vehicle, continuing
2 at a speed that is 10 or more miles an hour above the applicable speed limit, extinguishing the motor vehicle's
3 lights, or otherwise fleeing from, eluding, or attempting to flee from or elude the peace officer.
4 (2) (a) Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), a person convicted of or pleading guilty or nolo
5 contendere to an offense under subsection (1) shall be imprisoned for a term not to exceed 1 year or fined an
6 amount not to exceed $2,000, or both.
7 (b) A person convicted of an offense of fleeing from or eluding a peace officer during which the
8 person causes serious bodily injury to or the death of any other person shall be imprisoned for a term not to
9 exceed 10 years or fined an amount not to exceed $10,000, or both.
10 (2) A person commits the offense of aggravated fleeing from or eluding a peace officer if:
11 (a) the person has previously been convicted of fleeing from or eluding a peace officer under this
12 section; or
13 (b) in the course of fleeing from or eluding a peace officer, the person:
14 (i) exhibits reckless driving behavior as provided in 61-8-301, including intentionally steering the
15 motor vehicle toward any other person;
16 (ii) causes property damage to the property of any other person in an amount exceeding $5,000;
17 or
18 (iii) leaves the state.
19 (3) A person who is convicted of or pleads guilty or no contest to a violation of this section is
20 subject to the penalties provided in [section 1]."
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
23 integral part of Title 61, chapter 8, part 7, and the provisions of Title 61, chapter 8, part 7, apply to [section 1].
24 - END -
