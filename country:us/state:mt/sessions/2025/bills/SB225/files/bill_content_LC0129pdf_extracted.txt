4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A RENTER'S INCOME TAX CREDIT;
5 PROVIDING ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS; AMENDING SECTIONS 15-30-2303, 15-30-2337, AND 15-30-
6 2340, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE, A RETROACTIVE APPLICABILITY DATE,
7 AND A TERMINATION DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Renter's tax credit. (1) There is a renter's credit against the taxes
12 imposed by this chapter for a person with gross household income of less than $45,000 who pays rent-
13 equivalent property taxes as provided in this section.
14 (2) The eligibility, application, and limitation provisions of 15-30-2338, 15-30-2339, and 15-30-2341
15 that apply to renters, excluding the age requirement, apply to the renter's credit.
16 (3) (a) Except as provided in subsection (3)(b), the credit is equal to the lesser of:
17 (i) rent-equivalent tax paid that exceeds 4% of gross household income; or
18 (ii) $500.
19 (b) For a claimant whose gross household income is $35,000 or more but less than $45,000, the
20 amount of the credit is equal to the credit calculated under this section multiplied by the decimal equivalent of a
21 percentage figure according to the following table:
22 Gross household income Percentage of credit allowed
23 $35,000 - $37,500 40%
24 $37,501 - $40,000 30%
25 $40,001 - $42,500 20%
26 $42,501 - $44,999 10%
27 $45,000 or more 0%
28 (4) If the amount of the credit exceeds the claimant's liability under this chapter, the amount of the
- 1 - LC 129

1 excess must be refunded to the claimant. The credit may be claimed even though the claimant has no income
2 taxable under this chapter.
3 (5) A person may not claim this credit and the residential property tax credit for the elderly provided
4 for in 15-30-2340.

12 recommendations in accordance with 5-11-210 at the conclusion of the full review to the legislature about

14 or termination date that is not listed in this section in the biennium before the credit is scheduled to expire or
15 terminate.

17 (a) whether the credit changes taxpayer decisions, including whether the credit rewards decisions
18 that may have been made regardless of the existence of the tax credit;
19 (b) to what extent the credit benefits some taxpayers at the expense of other taxpayers;
20 (c) whether the credit has out-of-state beneficiaries;
21 (d) the timing of costs and benefits of the credit and how long the credit is effective;
22 (e) any adverse impacts of the credit or its elimination and whether the benefits of continuance or
23 elimination outweigh adverse impacts; and
24 (f) the extent to which benefits of the credit affect the larger economy. (Subsection (3)(c) (3)(d)
25 terminates December 31, 2028--sec. 4, Ch. 391, L. 2023; subsection (3)(d) (3)(e) terminates December 31,
26 2028--sec. 2, Ch. 576, L. 2023; subsection (1)(c) terminates December 31, 2031--sec. 6, Ch. 493, L. 2023.)"

1 "15-30-2337. Residential property tax credit for elderly -- definitions. As used in 15-30-2337
2 through 15-30-2341 and [section 1], the following definitions apply:
3 (1) "Claim period" means the tax year for individuals required to file Montana individual income tax
4 returns and the calendar year for individuals not required to file returns.
5 (2) "Claimant" means a person who is eligible to file a claim under 15-30-2338.
6 (3) "Department" means the department of revenue.
7 (4) "Gross household income" means all income received by all individuals of a household while
8 they are members of the household.
9 (5) "Gross rent" means the total rent in cash or its equivalent actually paid during the claim period
10 by the renter or lessee for the right of occupancy of the homestead pursuant to an arm's-length transaction with
11 the landlord.
12 (6) "Homestead" means:
13 (a) a single-family dwelling or unit of a multiple-unit dwelling that is subject to property taxes in
14 Montana and as much of the surrounding land, but not in excess of 1 acre, as is reasonably necessary for its
15 use as a dwelling; or
16 (b) a single-family dwelling or unit of a multiple-unit dwelling that is rented from a county or
17 municipal housing authority as provided in Title 7, chapter 15.
18 (7) (a) "Household" means an association of persons who live in the same dwelling, sharing its
19 furnishings, facilities, accommodations, and expenses.
20 (b) The term does not include bona fide lessees, tenants, or roomers and boarders on contract.
21 (8) "Household income" means the amount obtained by subtracting [$12,600] from gross
22 household income.
23 (9) (a) "Income" means, except as provided in subsection (9)(b), federal adjusted gross income,
24 without regard to loss, as that quantity is defined in the Internal Revenue Code of the United States, plus all
25 nontaxable income, including but not limited to:
26 (i) the amount of any pension or annuity, including Railroad Retirement Act benefits and veterans'
27 disability benefits;
28 (ii) the amount of capital gains excluded from adjusted gross income;
