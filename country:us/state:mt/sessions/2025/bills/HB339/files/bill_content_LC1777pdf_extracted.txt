4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING SCHOOL FUNDING LAWS TO PROVIDE THAT 6TH
5 GRADERS IN ACCREDITED MIDDLE SCHOOLS ARE FUNDED AT THE HIGH SCHOOL ANB RATES
6 ALONG WITH 7TH AND 8TH GRADERS; AMENDING SECTIONS 20-9-306 AND 20-9-311, MCA; AND
7 PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 WHEREAS, nearly all Montana school districts have embraced the middle school model and 98% of
10 Montana 6th graders attend a middle school; and
11 WHEREAS, currently 6th graders in a middle school are funded at the lower elementary per-ANB rate,
12 while their 7th and 8th grade schoolmates are funded at the high school ANB rates; and
13 WHEREAS, there is no educationally relevant justification for this disparity in funding; and
14 WHEREAS, if 6th graders were funded on par with 7th and 8th graders, middle schools would be able
15 to offer more robust program offerings as required in the accreditation standards for middle schools, including
16 career and technical education courses and pathways.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:

4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING SCHOOL FUNDING LAWS TO PROVIDE THAT 6TH
5 GRADERS IN ACCREDITED MIDDLE SCHOOLS ARE FUNDED AT THE HIGH SCHOOL ANB RATES
6 ALONG WITH 7TH AND 8TH GRADERS; AMENDING SECTIONS 20-9-306 AND 20-9-311, MCA; AND
7 PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 WHEREAS, nearly all Montana school districts have embraced the middle school model and 98% of
10 Montana 6th graders attend a middle school; and
11 WHEREAS, currently 6th graders in a middle school are funded at the lower elementary per-ANB rate,
12 while their 7th and 8th grade schoolmates are funded at the high school ANB rates; and
13 WHEREAS, there is no educationally relevant justification for this disparity in funding; and
14 WHEREAS, if 6th graders were funded on par with 7th and 8th graders, middle schools would be able
15 to offer more robust program offerings as required in the accreditation standards for middle schools, including
16 career and technical education courses and pathways.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
