69th Legislature 2025 HB 159.1
2 INTRODUCED BY A. GRIFFITH
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING RURAL IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT LAWS; ALLOWING
5 FOR A DISTRICT TO BE CREATED FOR THE MAINTENANCE OF EXISTING PUBLIC IMPROVEMENTS;
6 ALLOWING FOR A PETITION TO CREATE A DISTRICT FOR THE PURPOSE OF MAINTAINING EXISTING
7 IMPROVEMENTS; REMOVING THE REQUIREMENT FOR A PETITION WHEN ALL OWNERS OF
8 PROPERTY WITHIN THE AREA OF A PROPOSED RURAL IMPROVEMENT DISTRICT HAVE WAIVED
9 THEIR RIGHT TO PROTEST THE CREATION OF THE DISTRICT; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 7-12-2102,
10 7-12-2103, 7-12-2105, AND 7-12-2113, MCA.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 7-12-2102, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "7-12-2102. Authorization to create rural improvement districts -- property owners may petition
16 for creation. (1) Whenever the public interest or convenience may require, the board of county commissioners
17 may order and create special improvement districts outside of the limits of incorporated towns and cities for the
18 purpose of maintaining existing public improvements or building, constructing, or acquiring by purchase one or
19 more of the improvements of the kind described in 7-12-4102, in or for the benefit of the special improvement
20 district.
21 (2) (a) Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), the board of county commissioners may order and
22 create a special improvement district upon on the receipt of a petition to create a special improvement district
23 that contains the consent of all of the owners of property to be included in the district.
24 (b) The board of county commissioners may order and create a special improvement district solely
25 for the purpose of road maintenance:
26 (i) upon on the receipt of a petition to create the district that contains the consent of the owners of
27 more than 85% of the area of the property proposed in the petition to be included in the district; or.
28 (ii) if all of the roads to be maintained are public improvements, on the receipt of a petition of more
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – HB 159
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 159.1
1 than 50% of the area of the property proposed in the petition to be included in the district.
2 (c) The property proposed to be included in the district allowed in subsection (2)(b) must be
3 located in a residential subdivision, except that the owner of property located outside of a residential subdivision
4 may consent to the inclusion of the property in the proposed district.
5 (d) If all of the owners of property in the area proposed in the petition to be included in the district
6 have waived their right to protest the creation of a rural improvement district, no petition is necessary, and the
7 board of county commissioners may order and create the district.
8 (3) The board of county commissioners may order and create special improvement districts
9 covering projects abutting the city limits and include properties inside the city where the rural improvement
10 district abuts and benefits that property. Properties within the proposed district boundaries inside the city may
11 not be included in the rural special improvement district if, under the assessment methodology provided in the
12 resolution of intention, the owners of lots, tracts, or parcels in the city representing not less than 40% of the total
13 projected assessments against properties in the city protest the creation of the rural special improvement
14 district. The property inside the city must be treated in a similar manner as to improvements, notices, and
15 assessments as the property outside the city limits. A joint resolution of the city and county must be passed
16 agreeing to the terms of the rural special improvement district prior to passing the resolution of intention or
17 resolution creating the rural special improvement district. A copy of the resolution of intention and the resolution
18 creating the rural special improvement district must be provided to the city clerk upon on the passage of the
19 respective resolutions."
20
21 Section 2. Section 7-12-2103, MCA, is amended to read:
22 "7-12-2103. Resolution of intention to create rural improvement district. (1) Before creating a
23 special improvement district for the purpose of making any of the improvements, maintaining existing public
24 improvements, or acquiring any private property for any purpose authorized by this part, the board of county
25 commissioners shall pass a resolution of intention.
26 (2) The resolution must:
27 (a) designate the number of the district;
28 (b) describe the boundaries of the district;
- 2 - Authorized Print Version – HB 159
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 159.1
1 (c) state in the resolution the general character of the improvements that are to be made;
2 (d) designate the name of the engineer who is to have charge of the work and an approximate
3 estimate of the cost of the work;
4 (e) specify the method or methods by which the costs of the improvements will be assessed
5 against property in the district; and
6 (f) if the method of assessment is that described in 7-12-2151(1)(d), specify that if an increase
7 occurs in the number of benefited lots, tracts, or parcels within the boundaries of the district during the term of
8 the bonded indebtedness, the assessment per lot, tract, or parcel then in the district will be recalculated as
9 provided in 7-12-2151(4).
10 (3) The board of county commissioners may include, in one proceeding under one resolution of
11 intention and in one contract, any of the different kinds of improvements or work provided for in this part and
12 may include any number of streets and rights-of-way or portions of streets and rights-of-way, and it may exempt
13 any of the work already done upon on a street to the official grade."
14
15 Section 3. Section 7-12-2105, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "7-12-2105. Notice of resolution of intention to create district -- hearing -- exception. (1) Upon
17 On passage of a resolution of intention pursuant to 7-12-2103, the board of county commissioners shall publish
18 notice of the passage as provided in 7-1-2121.
19 (2) A copy of the notice must be mailed, as provided in 7-1-2122, to each person, firm, or
20 corporation or the agent of the person, firm, or corporation owning real property within the proposed district
21 listed in the owner's name upon on the last-completed assessment roll for state, county, and school district
22 taxes.
23 (3) (a) The notice must describe the general character of the improvements proposed to be made
24 or acquired by purchase, identify the public improvements that will be improved or maintained, state the
25 estimated cost of the improvements, describe generally the method or methods by which the costs of the
26 improvements will be assessed, and designate the time when and the place where the board will hear and pass
27 upon on all protests that may be made against the making or maintenance of the improvements or the creation
28 of the district. If the method of assessment described in 7-12-2151(1)(d) is used, the notice must state that if an
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – HB 159
****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 159.1
1 increase occurs in the number of benefited lots, tracts, or parcels within the boundaries of the district during the
2 term of the bonded indebtedness, the assessment per lot, tract, or parcel then in the district will be recalculated
3 as provided in 7-12-2151(4).
4 (b) If the revolving fund is to be pledged to secure the payment of bonds and warrants, the notice
5 must include a statement that, subject to the limitations in 7-12-2182:
6 (i) the county general fund may be used to provide loans to the revolving fund; or
7 (ii) a general tax levy may be imposed on all taxable property in the county to meet the financial
8 requirements of the revolving fund.
9 (c) The notice must refer to the resolution on file in the office of the county clerk for the description
10 of the boundaries. If the proposal is for the purchase of an existing improvement, the notice must state the
11 exact purchase price of the existing improvement.
12 (4) The provisions of this section do not apply to a resolution of intention to create a district that is
13 passed upon on receipt of a petition as provided in 7-12-2102(2)(a)."
14
15 Section 4. Section 7-12-2113, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "7-12-2113. Resolution creating district -- power to order improvements. (1) Before ordering any
17 of the proposed improvements or maintenance, the board of county commissioners shall pass a resolution
18 creating the special improvement district in accordance with the resolution of intention that is introduced and
19 passed by the board.
20 (2) The board has jurisdiction to order improvements or maintenance immediately upon on the
21 occurrence of the following conditions:
22 (a) when sufficient protests have not been delivered to the county clerk within 30 days after the
23 date of the first publication of the notice of the passing of the resolution of intention;
24 (b) when a protest has been found by the board to be insufficient or has been overruled;
25 (c) when a protest against extending the proposed district has been heard and denied; or
26 (d) when a resolution creating the district is passed upon on receipt of a petition as provided in 7-
27 12-2102(2)(a)."
28 - END -
