4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT CREATING THE MONTANA FAMILY AND MEDICAL LEAVE
5 INSURANCE ACT; CREATING AN ENTERPRISE FUND WITH CONTRIBUTIONS FROM COVERED

11 HEALTH INFORMATION PRIVACY TO THOSE USING BENEFITS; PROVIDING FOR PUBLIC OUTREACH

16 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Creation of fund -- uses of fund -- rulemaking. (1) (a) There is an
17 enterprise fund known as the family and medical leave insurance fund, which is to be kept separate from all
18 other public money and used exclusively for the purposes of [sections 1 through 20].
19 (b) The fund includes:
20 (i) contributions from covered employers and eligible employees of covered employers;
21 (ii) contributions from self-employed individuals who have elected coverage under [section 5];
22 (iii) interest earned on any money in the fund; and
23 (iv) any gifts, grants, or donations acquired to conduct the actuarial valuation and fund the
24 administrative costs of setting up and maintaining the fund and program under [sections 1 through 20].
25 (2) The commissioner of labor and industry is the ex officio treasurer of the fund.
26 (3) Expenditures from the fund may be used only for the purposes and administration of the family
27 and medical leave insurance benefits program outlined in [sections 1 through 20] and rules adopted to
28 implement [sections 1 through 20]. Only the commissioner or the commissioner's designated agent may

4 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Contribution schedule -- rulemaking. (1) (a) The department shall
5 evaluate and determine on an annual basis the amount of contributions needed to finance the family and
6 medical leave insurance benefits program and shall determine by rule the amount of contributions needed to
7 ensure solvency of the fund provided for in [section 3]. Subject to subsection (2), the department shall set the
8 total contribution as a percentage of wages, split equally into employer and employee shares.
9 (b) The department may not require contributions by the covered employer and the eligible
10 employee combined that exceed 1% of an eligible employee's monthly wages from a covered employer as
11 calculated on the contribution and benefit base in 42 U.S.C. 430.
12 (2) (a) The department shall assess and collect contributions at the contribution rate as provided in
13 subsection (1), which must be remitted as follows:
14 (i) a covered employer with 15 or more employees shall remit an amount equal to the total
15 contribution rate;
16 (ii) a covered employer with not less than 10 but not more than 15 employees shall remit an
17 amount equal to the employee share plus 75% of the employer share of the contributions;
18 (iii) a covered employer with fewer than 10 employees shall remit an amount equal to the
19 employee share of contributions plus 50% of the employer share of contributions; and
20 (iv) a self-employed person participating in the program shall remit an amount equal to the
21 employee share of contributions plus 50% of the employer share of contributions.
22 (b) If a covered employer pays both the covered employer's and the eligible employee's share of
23 the contribution, the eligible employee's share is considered to have been contributed by the eligible employee.
24 (c) If a covered employer assesses to eligible employees their share of the overall contribution, the
25 covered employer shall collect each eligible employee's amount as a payroll deduction from the eligible
26 employee's wages each payroll period and remit the full contribution to the department as provided in
27 subsection (3).

4 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Elective coverage for self-employed individuals and tribal
5 employees -- definition. (1) A self-employed individual may elect coverage under [sections 1 through 20]
6 during an open enrollment period and become a covered individual for an initial period of not less than 3 years
7 and for subsequent periods of not less than 1 year immediately following a period of coverage.
8 (2) A self-employed individual electing coverage under this section:
9 (a) shall make payments in accordance with rules adopted by the department as to the covered
10 employer and eligible employee contribution and payment schedules; and
11 (b) is responsible for 100% of the covered employer and eligible employee contributions, as
12 provided in [section 4].
13 (3) (a) The department shall notify the self-employed individual who fails to make timely and full
14 payment of contributions required under this section.
15 (b) Notice to the self-employed individual must:
16 (i) be in writing sent by common carrier with tracking capability, unless the self-employed
17 individual chooses to receive electronic notifications; and
18 (ii) include information as to when payment must be received to avoid disenrollment for
19 nonpayment. Any contributions made prior to disenrollment become those of the program and may not be
20 returned.
21 (c) If payment is not received by the department within 3 months of receipt of the notice, the
22 department shall notify the self-employed individual that the self-employed individual is being disenrolled from
23 the program and is ineligible for benefits under [section 7]. After the department receives payment of the past-
24 due amount, the self-employed individual may reenroll in the program consistent with the requirements in this
25 section and as provided by rule.
26 (4) Subject to subsection (5)(b), beginning January 1, 2027, a self-employed individual who
27 previously elected coverage and withdrew from the program is ineligible to receive benefits pursuant to [section
28 7] for the first year after enrolling or reenrolling in the program.

16 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Rights to benefits subject to availability of funds -- state not liable
17 for fund shortage -- dissolution. (1) Family and medical leave insurance benefits payable under [sections 1
18 through 20] are available only to the extent that money is available in the family and medical leave insurance
19 fund.
20 (2) The state is subject to [sections 1 through 20] as a covered employer.
21 (3) The state has no more liability than any other covered employer if the department declares a
22 shortage of funds.
23 (4) A shortage of funds must be addressed by using the payment determination in [section 3].
24 (5) There is no vested private right of any kind against amendment or repeal of [sections 1 through
25 20]. If repealed and a balance remains in the family and medical leave insurance fund, the money not paid out
26 in family and medical leave insurance benefits under [sections 1 through 20] must be transferred to the general
27 fund.

6 NEW SECTION. Section 8. Benefit calculation -- duration. (1) (a) Family and medical leave
7 insurance benefits under [sections 1 through 20] correspond to the covered individual's base wages or earnings
8 during the base period, subject to the provisions of subsection (1)(b) or (1)(c).
9 (b) The wages may be the sum of all covered employment for which the covered individual is
10 taking leave under [sections 1 through 20].
11 (c) The department shall determine by rule the basis used for determining a self-employed
12 individual's earnings during the base period.
13 (2) Subject to subsection (3), a weekly benefit amount is calculated as follows:
14 (a) for a covered individual whose wages, or earnings in the case of a self-employed individual, in
15 the base period are not more than 50% of the average annual wage, the department shall pay weekly benefits
16 in an amount equal to 1.92% of the covered individual's wages in the base period; or
17 (b) for a covered individual whose wages, or earnings in the case of a self-employed individual, in
18 the base period are more than 50% of the average annual wage, the department shall pay weekly benefits
19 equal to 1.92% of 50% of the average annual wage, plus 0.96% of the amount of wages in the base period that
20 are in excess of 50% of the average annual wage.
21 (3) (a) A weekly benefit may not exceed $1,000 or the average weekly wage, as defined in 39-51-
22 2201, whichever is greater.
23 (b) Benefits for partial weeks of leave must be prorated.
24 (c) If a covered individual is eligible and seeking family and medical leave insurance benefits for
25 more than one job, the benefits must be calculated by the department based on a combination of wages from
26 all jobs from which the covered individual is taking leave based on [sections 1 through 20].

3 NEW SECTION. Section 9. Coordination of benefits. (1) (a) The provisions of [sections 1 through
4 20] run concurrently with the benefits covered by the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, 29 U.S.C. 2601, et
5 seq.
6 (b) A covered employer may require that the covered individual take family and medical leave
7 insurance benefits payments concurrently, or otherwise coordinated, with payment made or leave allowed
8 under the terms of disability or family care leave under a collective bargaining agreement or employer policy.
9 The covered employer shall give employees written notice of this requirement.
10 (2) The provisions of [sections 1 through 20] do not:
11 (a) supersede any provision of law, collective bargaining agreement, or other contract that
12 provides paid leave rights in addition to the rights under [sections 1 through 20]; or
13 (b) prevent a covered employer from adopting or retaining a paid leave policy that provides greater
14 benefits than are required by [sections 1 through 20].
15 (3) The department may not provide family and medical leave insurance benefits for any period in
16 which the covered individual is receiving unemployment insurance benefits or workers' compensation,
17 excluding medical benefits.
