5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR A SPECIAL REVENUE ACCOUNT FOR
6 REVIEW OF PUBLIC WATER SUPPLY OR PUBLIC SEWAGE SYSTEM PLANS AND SPECIFICATIONS;
7 AMENDING SECTIONS 75-6-104, 75-6-108, AND 75-6-115, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Plan review special revenue account. (1) There is a plan review
12 special revenue account within the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102.
13 (2) There must be credited to the account the revenue from fees assessed, collected, and
14 allocated pursuant to 75-6-108(3).
15 (3) Funds from the account may be used only to pay department costs in implementing plan and
16 specification review, as described in this part.
17
18 Section 2. Section 75-6-104, MCA, is amended to read:
19 "75-6-104. Duties of department. (1) The department has general supervision over all state waters
20 that are directly or indirectly being used by a person for a public water supply system, for domestic purposes, or
21 as a source of ice.
22 (2) The department shall, subject to the provisions of 75-6-116 and as provided in 75-6-131, adopt
23 rules and standards concerning:
24 (a) maximum contaminant levels for waters that are or will be used for a public water supply
25 system;
26 (b) fees, as described in 75-6-108, for services rendered by the department;
27 (c) monitoring, recordkeeping, and reporting by persons who own or operate public water supply
28 systems;

4 sewage system, including requirements to remedy:

11 supply system, for domestic purposes, or as a source of ice;
12 (f) the review of the technical, managerial, and financial capacity of a proposed public water
13 supply system or public sewage system, as necessary to ensure the capability of the system to meet the
14 requirements of this part;

7 (a) except as provided in 75-6-131, a water supply or water distribution facility reviewed and
8 approved by the department is not subject to changes in department design and construction criteria for a
9 period of 36 months after written approval of the facility is issued by the department;
10 (b) except for facilities subject to permit requirements under Title 75, chapter 5, part 4, and except
11 as provided under rules adopted pursuant to 75-6-131, a system of water supply, drainage, wastewater, or
12 sewage reviewed and approved under this section is not subject to changes in department design or
13 construction criteria for a period of 36 months after written approval is issued by the department;
14 (c) plans and specifications for a portion of a facility or system subject to a 36-month limit on
15 criteria changes pursuant to subsections (3)(a) and (3)(b) but not constructed within the 36-month timeframe
16 must be resubmitted for department review and approval before construction of that portion of the facility;

7 (e) consult with persons engaged in or intending to engage in manufacturing or other business

19 (i) enforce and administer the provisions of this part;
20 (j) establish a plan for the provision of safe drinking water under emergency circumstances;
21 (k) maintain an inventory of public water supply systems and establish a program for conducting
22 sanitary surveys;

7 Section 3. Section 75-6-108, MCA, is amended to read:
8 "75-6-108. Department to prescribe fees -- opportunity for appeal. (1) The department shall by
9 rule prescribe fees to be assessed annually on owners of public water supply systems to recover department
10 costs in providing services under this part. The annual fee for a public water supply system is no more than
11 $2.25 for each service connection to the public water supply system for the biennium beginning July 1, 1991,
12 and ending June 30, 1993, and thereafter is no more than $2 for each service connection to the public water
13 supply system, although the minimum fee for any system is $100, except that the fee for a transient
14 noncommunity water system is $50.
15 (2) Public water supply systems in a municipality may raise the rates to recover costs associated
16 with the fees prescribed in this section without the public hearing required in 69-7-111.
17 (3) The department shall by rule prescribe fees assessed on persons who submit plans and
18 specifications or development plans for construction, alteration, or extension of a public water supply system or
19 public sewage system. The fees must be commensurate with the cost to the department of reviewing the plans
20 and specifications.
21 (4) Fees collected pursuant to this section subsection (1) must be deposited in the public drinking
22 water special revenue fund established in 75-6-115.
23 (5) Fees collected pursuant to subsection (3) must be deposited in the plan review special revenue
24 account established in [section 1].
25 (5)(6) (a) The department shall notify the owner of a public water supply system in writing of the
26 amount of the fee to be assessed and the basis for the assessment. The owner may appeal the fee
27 assessment in writing to the board within 20 days after receipt of the written notice.
28 (b) An appeal must be based on the allegation that the fee is erroneous or excessive. An appeal
- 5 - Authorized Print Version – HB 40

1 may not be based only on the fee schedule adopted by the department.
2 (c) If any part of the fee assessment is not appealed, it must be paid to the department on receipt
3 of the notice provided for in subsection (5)(a) (6)(a)."
4
5 Section 4. Section 75-6-115, MCA, is amended to read:
6 "75-6-115. Public drinking water special revenue fund. (1) There is a public drinking water special
7 revenue fund within the state special revenue fund established in 17-2-102.
8 (2) There must be credited to the public drinking water special revenue fund the revenue from fees
9 assessed, collected, and allocated pursuant to 75-6-108 75-6-108(4). Funds from the public drinking water
10 special revenue fund may be used only to pay department costs in implementing the public drinking water
11 supply program, as described in this part."
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Codification instruction. [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
14 integral part of Title 75, chapter 6, part 1, and the provisions of Title 75, chapter 6, part 1, apply to [section 1].
15
16 NEW SECTION. Section 6. Effective date. [This act] is effective July 1, 2025.
