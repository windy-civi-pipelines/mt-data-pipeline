69th Legislature 2025 HB 756.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. FITZPATRICK, M. VINTON, S. ROSENZWEIG, J. KASSMIER, E. BOLDMAN, J. ESP, P.
3 TUSS
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE MONTANA MUSEUMS ACT OF 2020; REVISING
6 THE CRITERIA USED TO MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS FOR GRANTS AWARDED UNDER THE HISTORIC
7 PRESERVATION GRANT PROGRAM; AND AMENDING SECTION 22-3-1306, MCA.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 22-3-1306, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "22-3-1306. Priorities for funding -- rulemaking. (1) The department of commerce shall make
13 recommendations for grants awarded under the historic preservation grant program to eligible entities for the
14 preservation of historic sites, historical societies, or history museums in the state.
15 (2) Projects funded under this section must provide that a significant portion of the facility be open
16 to public access and use. Projects that confer only a private a limited public benefit are not eligible.
17 (3) The recommendations must be based on competitive criteria created by the department, as
18 guided by the legislature. The criteria may include:
19 (a) the degree to which the project will protect:
20 (i) the building envelope, consisting of the structure's walls, roof, doors and windows, and
21 foundation; and
22 (ii) its internal utility systems, including electricity, plumbing, and heat;
23 (b) the degree of economic stimulus or economic activity, including job creation and work creation
24 for Montana contractors and service workers;
25 (b) (c) the purpose of the project, including whether it provides features that establish or enhance
26 security, climate control, or fire protection for museums or address infrastructure, maintenance, or building code
27 issues at historic sites;
28 (c) (d) the timing of the project, including access to matching funds, if needed, and approval of permits

****
69th Legislature 2025 HB 756.1
1 so that work can be completed without delay;
2 (d) (e) the historic or heritage value related to the state of Montana;
3 (e) (f) the successful track record or experience of the organization directing the project;
4 (f) the expected ongoing economic benefit to the state as a result of the project completion;
5 (g) the degree of local contribution to the project;and
6 (h) (g) the anticipated public benefit, including the extent the site or building will be open to the public
7 and the degree of immediate facility use after project completion; and
8 (h) the degree of local contribution to the project. For-profit entities must provide a match equal to
9 $1 for every $1 of grant funding received.
10 (4) The department of commerce shall adopt rules necessary to implement the historic
11 preservation grant program. In adopting rules, the department shall look to the rules adopted for the Montana
12 coal endowment program, the cultural and aesthetic grant program, and other similar state programs. To the
13 extent feasible, the department shall make the rules compatible with those other programs.
14 (5) Government applicants shall comply with Article II, sections 8 and 9, of the Montana
15 constitution and with Title 2, chapter 3, parts 1 and 2, and Title 7. Nongovernment applicants are exempt from
16 this provision."
17 - END -
