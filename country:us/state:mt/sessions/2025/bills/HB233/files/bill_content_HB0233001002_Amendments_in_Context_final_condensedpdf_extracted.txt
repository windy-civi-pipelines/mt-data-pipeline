1 HOUSE BILL NO. 233
2 INTRODUCED BY A. GRIFFITH
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING FOR THE TRANSFER OF TITLE OF OPEN SPACES
5 IN A RECORDED FINAL PLAT TO A HOMEOWNERS' ASSOCIATION; REQUIRING A PUBLIC HEARING;
6 PROVIDING A DEFINITION; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 76-3-103, 76-8-101, 76-25-103, AND 76-25-411,
7 MCA.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Transfer of title of open spaces in recorded final plat. (1) After a final
12 subdivision plat has been filed for record with the county clerk and recorder, a homeowners' association may
13 file a petition with the governing body to transfer title to the homeowners' association of any open spaces,
14 parks, or similar common-use areas set aside in the final plat if the following conditions are met:
15 (a) the homeowners' association is organized in accordance with the covenants;
16 (b) the covenants require the homeowners' association to maintain, repair, or insure the open
17 spaces, parks, or similar common-use areas; and
18 (c) the governing body finds that it was the intent of the subdivider that the open spaces, parks, or
19 similar common-use areas be owned by the homeowners' association; and
20 (d) the property to be transferred is not owned by a person.
21 (2) The petition under subsection (1) must be signed by an authorized representative of the
22 homeowners' association.
23 (3) After a public hearing on the petition, the governing body shall issue written findings of fact and
24 a decision based on the record. If the requirements of subsection (1) have been met, the governing body shall
25 approve the transfer. If the governing body approves the transfer, the approval must be recorded with the clerk
26 and recorder.

6 Section 5. Section 76-25-411, MCA, is amended to read:
7 "76-25-411. Filing and recordation of plats and certificates of survey. (1) (a) Except as provided
8 in subsection (1)(b), every final plat or certificate of survey must be filed for record with the county clerk and
9 recorder before title to the land may be sold or transferred in any manner. The clerk and recorder of the county
10 may not accept any final plat or certificate of survey for record that has not been approved in accordance with
11 this part unless the final plat or certificate of survey is located in an area over which the state does not have
12 jurisdiction.
13 (b) After the preliminary plat of a subdivision has been approved or conditionally approved, the
14 subdivider may enter into contracts to sell lots in the proposed subdivision if all of the following contract
15 conditions are imposed and met:
16 (i) the purchasers of lots in the proposed subdivision make payments to an escrow agent, which
17 must be a bank or savings and loan association chartered to do business in the state of Montana;
18 (ii) the payments made by purchasers of lots in the proposed subdivision may not be distributed by
19 the escrow agent to the subdivider until the final plat of the subdivision is filed with the county clerk and
20 recorder;
21 (iii) if the final plat of the proposed subdivision is not filed with the county clerk and recorder within
22 the approval period of the preliminary plat, the escrow agent shall immediately refund to each purchaser any
23 payments the purchaser has made under the contract;
24 (iv) the county treasurer has certified that no real property taxes assessed and levied on the land to
25 be divided are delinquent; and
26 (v) the following language is conspicuously set out in each contract: "The real property that is the
27 subject of this contract has not been finally platted, and until a final plat identifying the property has been filed
28 with the county clerk and recorder, title to the property may not be transferred in any manner".

1 (2) (a) Subject to subsection (2)(b), no division of land may be made unless the county treasurer
2 has certified that all real property taxes and special assessments assessed and levied on the land to be divided
3 have been paid.
4 (b) (i) If a division of land includes centrally assessed property and the property taxes applicable to
5 the division of land are not specifically identified in the tax assessment, the department of revenue shall prorate
6 the taxes applicable to the land being divided on a reasonable basis. The owner of the centrally assessed
7 property shall ensure that the prorated real property taxes and special assessments are paid on the land being
8 sold before the division of land is made.
9 (ii) The county treasurer may accept the amount of the tax prorated pursuant to this subsection
10 (2)(b) as a partial payment of the total tax that is due.
11 (3) (a) The county clerk and recorder shall maintain an index of all recorded and filed subdivision
12 plats and certificates of survey.
13 (b) The index must list plats and certificates of survey by the quarter section, section, township,
14 and range in which the platted or surveyed land lies and must list the recording or filing numbers of all plats or
15 certificates of survey depicting lands lying within each quarter section. Each quarter section list must be
16 definitive to the exclusion of all other quarter sections. The index must also list the names of all subdivision
17 plats in alphabetical order and the place where filed.
18 (4) The recording of any plat made in compliance with the provisions of this chapter must serve to
19 establish the identity of all lands shown on and being part of the plat. When lands are conveyed by reference to
20 a plat, the plat itself or any copy of the plat properly certified by the county clerk and recorder as being a true
21 copy thereof must be regarded as incorporated into the instrument of conveyance and must be received in
22 evidence in all courts of this state.

25 4115. Upon vacation, the governing body or the district court, as provided in 7-5-2502, shall determine to which
26 properties the title to the streets and alleys of the vacated portions must revert. The governing body or the
27 district court, as provided in 7-5-2502, shall take into consideration:
