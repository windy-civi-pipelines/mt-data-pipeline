19 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Board of facility ALLIED health care professionals. (1) In accordance
20 with 37-1-123, there is a board of facility ALLIED health care professionals.
21 (2) The board consists of seven members:
22 (a) two clinical laboratory scientists;
23 (b) two radiologic technologists;
24 (c) two respiratory therapists; and
25 (d) one public member.
26 (3) The board is allocated to the department as prescribed in 2-15-121.

5 telephonic, electronic, or written communication.

20 under the provisions of this chapter for the issuance of a license to perform diagnostic x-ray procedures on

22 (a) operates x-ray equipment to reveal the internal condition of patients for the diagnosis of
23 fractures, diseases, and other injuries;
24 (b) prepares and positions patients for x-ray procedures;
25 (c) selects the proper radiographic technique for visualization of specific internal structures of the
26 human body;

- 4 - Authorized Print Version – HB 502

2 of respiratory problems.
3 (3) (a) "Respiratory care" means the care provided by a member of the allied health profession
4 responsible for the treatment, management, diagnostic testing, and control of patients with deficiencies and
5 abnormalities associated with the cardiopulmonary system. The term includes but is not limited to:
6 (i) administration of pharmacological, diagnostic, and therapeutic agents related to respiratory
7 care procedures that are necessary to implement a treatment, disease prevention, pulmonary rehabilitative, or
8 diagnostic regimen prescribed by a physician;
9 (ii) transcription and implementation of the written or verbal orders of a physician regarding the
10 practice of respiratory care;
11 (iii) observation and monitoring of a patient's signs and symptoms, general behavior, and physical
12 response to respiratory care treatment and diagnostic testing, including determination of abnormal
13 characteristics;
14 (iv) implementation of respiratory care protocols pursuant to a prescription by a physician; and
15 (v) initiation of emergency procedures prescribed by board rules.
16 (b) Respiratory care is not limited to a hospital setting but must be performed pursuant to a
17 physician's order and under qualified medical direction. The term includes inhalation and respiratory therapy.
18 (4) "Respiratory care practitioner" means a person who has the knowledge and skill necessary to
19 administer respiratory care and who is licensed under the provisions of part 2 of this chapter.
20 (5) "Student respiratory care practitioner" means a person:
21 (a) enrolled in a respiratory care educational program recognized by the joint review committee for
22 respiratory therapy education and the American medical association's committee on allied health education and
23 accreditation, or their successors;
24 (b) permitted to provide respiratory care under clinical supervision; and
25 (c) identified as a student respiratory care practitioner or "SRCP"."
