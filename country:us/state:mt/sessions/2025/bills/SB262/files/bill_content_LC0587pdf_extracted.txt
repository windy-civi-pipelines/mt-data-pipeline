4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT EXEMPTING THE REVIEW OF CERTAIN SUBDIVISION
5 APPLICATIONS AND WATER OR SEWER PLANS FROM ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW; PROVIDING AN
6 EXEMPTION TO LOCAL GOVERNMENTS AND INDEPENDENT REVIEWERS; REVISING RULEMAKING
7 AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 75-6-121, 76-4-104, AND 76-4-136, MCA.”

9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Exemption from environmental review. The department is exempt
12 from the provisions of Title 75, chapter 1, parts 1 and 2 when reviewing water or sewer system plans or
13 specifications under this part.
14
15 Section 2. Section 75-6-121, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "75-6-121. Delegation of review of small public water and sewer construction. (1) If a local
17 government requests a delegation and the appropriate division of the local government has established
18 satisfactory review programs, the department shall delegate to the division of local government the review of:
19 (a) small public water and sewer systems; and
20 (b) extensions or alterations of existing public water and sewer systems.
21 (2) The department shall adopt rules regarding the delegation of review authority to divisions of
22 local government.
23 (3) A division of local government conducting a review under this section:
24 (a) must receive 90% of the review fee, and the department must receive the remaining 10% of the
25 review fee; and
26 (b) shall complete documents necessary to complete the review and to comply with:
27 (i) the Montana Environmental Policy Act provided for in Title 75, chapter 1, parts 1 through 3;
28 (ii )real property takings requirements in accordance with Title 70; and

5 provisions of 76-4-135, adopt reasonable rules, including adoption of sanitary standards, necessary for

8 public and private water supplies, sewage disposal facilities, storm water drainage ways, and solid waste
9 disposal. The rules and standards must be related to:
10 (a) size of lots;
11 (b) contour of land;
12 (c) porosity of soil;
13 (d) ground water level;
14 (e) distance from lakes, streams, and wells;
15 (f) type and construction of private water and sewage facilities; and
16 (g) other factors affecting public health and the quality of water for uses relating to agriculture,
17 industry, recreation, and wildlife.
18 (3) The storm drainage review requirements of this chapter do not apply to divisions or parcels of
19 land that are exempt from review under 76-3-207(1)(a), (1)(d), (1)(e), or (1)(f) that:
20 (a) are used for a single-family residential purpose; and
21 (b) include no more than 25% that is impervious.

23 subdivisions consistent with 76-4-114 by a local department or board of health, as described in Title 50, chapter

28 board of health may not review public water supply systems, public sewage systems, or extensions of or
