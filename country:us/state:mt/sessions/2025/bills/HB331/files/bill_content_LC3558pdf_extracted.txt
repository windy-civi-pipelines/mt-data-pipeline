10 Section 1. Section 16-12-102, MCA, is amended to read:
11 "16-12-102. Definitions. As used in this chapter, the following definitions apply:
12 (1) "Adult-use dispensary" means a licensed premises from which a person licensed by the
13 department may:
14 (a) obtain marijuana or marijuana products from a licensed cultivator, manufacturer, dispensary, or
15 other licensee approved under this chapter; and
16 (b) sell marijuana or marijuana products to registered cardholders, adults that are 21 years of age
17 or older, or both.
18 (2) (a) "Advertise" or "advertising" means the publication, dissemination, solicitation, or circulation
19 of visual, oral, or written communication to directly induce a person to purchase or consume marijuana or
20 marijuana products.
21 (b) The term does not include branding on packaging and labeling of marijuana and marijuana
22 products for sale to registered cardholders or consumers.
23 (3) "Affiliate" means a person that directly, or indirectly through one or more intermediaries,
24 controls or is controlled by, or is under common control with, another person.
25 (4) "Beneficial owner of", "beneficial ownership of", or "beneficially owns an" is determined in
26 accordance with section 13(d) of the federal Securities and Exchange Act of 1934, as amended.

28 entertainment, or attraction that is sold, offered, or existing elsewhere than on the same premises where the

4 (7) "Commercial mascot" means a live human being, animal, or mechanical device used for
5 attracting the attention of motorists and passersby so as to make them aware of marijuana products or the
6 presence of a marijuana business. Commercial mascots include but are not limited to inflatable tube displays, a
7 person in costume, and a person wearing, holding, or spinning a sign with a marijuana-related commercial
8 message or image when the intent is to draw attention to a marijuana business or its products.

26 (3) and to which an individual may be ordered by any court of competent jurisdiction.

28 (a) plant, cultivate, grow, harvest, and dry marijuana; and

10 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Limitations on advertising. (1) A licensee may not promote their
11 business or market their brand or products.
12 (2) A licensee may use the terms "marijuana" and "cannabis" in its signage and on its website.
13 (3) A licensee's outdoor signage may not use colloquial terms for marijuana or marijuana products
14 and may not use an image or visual representation of usable marijuana, marijuana-infused products, marijuana
15 concentrates, marijuana paraphernalia, or an image that indicates the presence of a product, including but not
16 limited to smoke or edibles.
17 (4) A licensee's outdoor signage must comply with any applicable local jurisdiction sign ordinances
18 and regulations.
19 (5) A marijuana business that maintains a website shall utilize appropriate measures to verify that
20 individuals visiting the website are 21 years of age or older, including the requirement to establish an account to
21 sign in and verify the visitor's age and Montana residency.
22 (6) Marijuana businesses may not create social media accounts, including but not limited to
23 platforms such as facebook, tiktok, or instagram.
24 (7) A marijuana business may not:
25 (a) engage in advertising or brand marketing directed toward location-based devices, including but
26 not limited to cellular phones;
27 (b) utilize targeted marketing, including unsolicited pop-up or push-to advertising on the internet;
28 (c) advertise or promote its business, brand, or products on television, radio, social media,
- 7 - LC 3558
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 3558
1 internet, or in print, such as newspapers, magazines, flyers, or mailers;
2 (d) engage in advertising or utilize signage that asserts its products are safe;
3 (e) utilize a billboard;
4 (f) make a statement or use images that imply health benefits, including but not limited to crosses
5 or medical symbology;
6 (g) use objects, names, images, or depictions, such as toys or inflatables, movie or cartoon
7 characters, animals, robots, fruits, references to foods, or other images or depictions likely to be appealing to
8 youth, when the objects, names, images, or depictions indicate an intent to cause youth to become interested in
9 the purchase or consumption of marijuana products;
10 (h) use or employ a commercial mascot outside of or in proximity to a licensed marijuana business;
11 (i) establish rewards or referral programs or other programs that could incentivize more use; or
12 (j) provide sponsorship for business or events.
13 (8) The prohibition in subsection (7)(c) does not prohibit the use of informational pamphlets for
14 dissemination at a marijuana trade conference or the use or distribution of business cards.
15 (9) The prohibition in subsection (7)(d) does not prohibit a marijuana business from asserting that
16 its products have been tested by a licensed marijuana laboratory.

10 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Limitations on advertising. (1) A licensee may not promote their

16 limited to smoke or edibles.

18 and regulations.
