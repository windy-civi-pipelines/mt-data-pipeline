8 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT MOVING MUNICIPAL ELECTIONS TO EVEN-NUMBERED
9 YEARS; PROVIDING A TRANSITION FOR MUNICIPAL OFFICERS; AMENDING SECTION SECTIONS 13-1-
10 104, AND 13-1-107, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE A DELAYED EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN
11 APPLICABILITY DATE.”
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
14
15 Section 1. Section 13-1-104, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "13-1-104. Times for holding general elections. (1) A general election must be held throughout the
17 state on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.
18 (2) In every even-numbered year, the following elections must be held on general election day:
19 (a) an election on any ballot issue submitted to electors pursuant to Article III, section 6, unless the
20 legislature orders a special election, or Article XIV, section 8, of the Montana constitution;
21 (b) an election of federal officers, members of the legislature, state officers, multicounty district
22 officers elected at a statewide election, district court judges, and county officers; and
23 (c) an election of officers for municipalities required by law to hold the election; and
24 (c)(d) any other election required by law to be held on general election day in an even-numbered
25 year.
26 (3) In every odd-numbered year, the following elections must be held on the same day as the
27 general election:

1 (a) an election of officers for municipalities required by law to hold the election; and
2 (b) any other Only an election required by law to be held on general election day in an odd-
3 numbered year may be held in an odd-numbered year."
4
5 SECTION 2. SECTION 13-1-107, MCA, IS AMENDED TO READ:
6 "13-1-107. Times Time for holding primary elections -- cost of municipal election. (1) On the first
7 Tuesday after the first Monday in June preceding a general election held in an even-numbered year, a primary
8 election must be held throughout the state.
9 (2) On the Tuesday following the second Monday in September preceding a general election held
10 in an odd-numbered year, a primary election, if required, must be held throughout the state.
11 (3)(2) The cost of a municipal election must be paid by the municipality."
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Transition. (1) For a municipal officer whose term of office ends in
14 January 2026, there must be an election in 2025 for a one-year term of office. The terms of office for municipal
15 officers as provided in Title 7, chapter 4, that are set to end in January 2028 are extended for 1 year to January
16 2029 until the qualification of a successor elected in the general election held in 2028.
17 (2) For a municipal officer whose term of office ends in January 2028 or January 2030, there must
18 be a special election for a one-year term proceeding when their term of office is set to end. This special election
19 must take place during the preceding even-numbered year general election.
20 (2) The terms of office for municipal officers as provided in Title 7, chapter 4, that are set to end in
21 January 2030 are extended for 1 year to January 2031 until the qualification of a successor elected in the
22 general election held in 2030.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Saving clause. [This act] does not affect rights and duties that matured,
25 penalties that were incurred, or proceedings that were begun before [the effective date of this act].
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Effective date. [This act] is effective on passage and approval January
