1 HOUSE BILL NO. 25

4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REQUIRING EXEMPT ENTITIES TO REPORT BENEFICIAL USE
6 OF PROPERTY TO THE DEPARTMENT OF REVENUE; PROVIDING FOR A REVISED ASSESSMENT
7 WHEN BENEFICIAL USE IS NOT PROPERLY REPORTED; PROVIDING AN EXEMPTION FROM THE
8 PRIVILEGE TAX FOR CERTAIN PROPERTY; PROVIDING RULEMAKING AUTHORITY; AND AMENDING
9 SECTION 15-6-201 SECTIONS 15-24-1203 AND 15-24-1207, MCA.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 (Refer to Introduced Bill)
14 Strike everything after the enacting clause and insert:
15
16 Section 1. Section 15-24-1203, MCA, is amended to read:
17 "15-24-1203. Privilege tax on industrial, trade, or other business use of tax-exempt property --
18 exceptions. (1) There is imposed and must be collected a tax upon the possession or other beneficial use for
19 industrial, trade, or other business purposes enjoyed by any private individual, association, or corporation of
20 any property, real or personal, that for any reason is exempt from taxation. The tax is imposed upon the
21 possession or other beneficial use of an electric transmission line and associated facilities, except that lines and
22 facilities of a design capacity of less than 500 kilovolts are not subject to the tax.
23 (2) The tax may not be imposed upon:
24 (a) the possession or other beneficial use of railroad right-of-way or track owned by the United
25 States or acquired by the state pursuant to Title 60, chapter 11, part 1, as long as the state or the United States
26 retains ownership and the right-of-way or track is used exclusively for rail transportation;
27 (b) the beneficial use by a person of property held by a port authority, created under Title 7,
28 chapter 14, part 11, or by a port authority owned by the United States or an agency of the United States unless

1 the port authority provides for the exclusive use of the property by the person;
2 (c) the possession or other beneficial use of public lands occupied under the terms of recreational,
3 mineral, timber, or grazing leases or permits issued by the United States or the state of Montana or upon any
4 easement unless the lease, permit, or easement entitles the lessee or permittee to exclusive possession of the
5 premises to which the lease, permit, or easement relates; or
6 (d) the possession or other beneficial use of land and buildings owned by a municipality,
7 consolidated city-county, or county and used as a golf course or as an athletic facility for a sports team; or
8 (d)(e) the possession or other beneficial use of buildings owned by public entities and located upon
9 public airports. However, privately owned buildings located on public airport property are subject to taxation."
10
11 Section 2. Section 15-24-1207, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "15-24-1207. Reporting requirements -- penalty -- rules. (1) Each private person who is a
13 possessor or a beneficial user of tax-exempt property for industrial, trade, or other business purposes subject to
14 tax pursuant to 15-24-1203 shall, on or before March 1 each year, file with the department a statement of the
15 possession or other beneficial use of tax-exempt property in the preceding calendar year and the value of the
16 property. The statement must be in the form prescribed by the department and must be verified by the
17 possessor or beneficial user or, in the case of an association or corporation, by an officer of the association or
18 corporation. The statement must include:
19 (a) the name and address of the person, association, or corporation;
20 (b) the location of the tax-exempt property; and
21 (c) the market value in dollars and cents of the tax-exempt property.
22 (2) Entities listed in 15-6-201(1)(a) that lease or otherwise allow the possession or beneficial use of
23 tax-exempt property for industrial, trade, or other business purposes shall file an annual report with the
24 department. The report must include the following information for each lease or agreement:
25 (a) the name and address of the lessee or user;
26 (b) the location of the leased property; and
27 (c) the lease terms, including the duration and payment amounts.
28 (2)(3) A person who fails to file the statement required by subsection (1) must be assessed the
