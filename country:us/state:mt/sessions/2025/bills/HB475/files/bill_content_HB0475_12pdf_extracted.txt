69th Legislature 2025 HB 475.1
2 INTRODUCED BY J. REAVIS, L. DEMING, E. BOLDMAN
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT RESTRICTING THE USE OF RESTRAINTS ON PREGNANT
5 INMATES; PROVIDING THAT RESTRAINTS MAY NOT BE USED ON A PREGNANT INMATE DURING
6 LABOR AND DELIVERY EXCEPT IN EXTRAORDINARY CIRCUMSTANCES; AND PROVIDING A
7 DEFINITION.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Use of restraints on pregnant inmates restricted -- extraordinary
12 circumstances -- definition. (1) Restraints may not be used on an inmate known to be pregnant during labor
13 and delivery, except as provided in subsection (2).
14 (2) (a) Except as provided in subsection (2)(b), restraints may be used on a pregnant inmate
15 during labor and delivery in extraordinary circumstances in which the detention center makes an individualized
16 determination that:
17 (i) the inmate is an established flight risk; or
18 (ii) there is a clear threat that the inmate could harm themself or others.
19 (b) Leg or waist restraints may not be used on a pregnant inmate during any stage of labor and
20 delivery under any circumstances.
21 (c) Both the type of restraint applied and the application of the restraint must be done in the least
22 restrictive manner necessary.
23 (d) Restraints must be removed under the following circumstances:
24 (i) when the flight risk or threat to harm has been mitigated; or
25 (ii) at the request of a doctor, nurse, or other health care professional treating the inmate during
26 labor and delivery.
27 (3) For the purposes of this section, "restraints" means handcuffs, leg shackles, leg irons, belly
28 belts, belly chains, or other restraint devices used to restrict free movement of limbs or appendages, including
