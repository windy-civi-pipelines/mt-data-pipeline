2 NEW SECTION. Section 12. Qualified supervisor requirements. (1) If supervising a student or
3 trainee who is providing medical nutrition therapy, a qualified supervisor must be one of the following:
4 (a) a licensed dietitian, licensed nutritionist, or a health care provider licensed or certified in any
5 state or territory, including licensed or certified dietitians, dietitian nutritionists, or nutritionists whose scope of
6 practice includes the provision of medical nutrition therapy;
7 (b) in the case of the supervisor supervising a student or trainee in a state that does not provide for
8 licensure or certification of dietitians, dietitian nutritionists, or nutritionists, the supervisor meets other criteria as
9 the board may establish, including being a registered dietitian or a licensed health care provider whose scope
10 of practice includes the provision of medical nutrition therapy; or
11 (c) an employee of the federal government authorized within the discharge of official duty to
12 provide medical nutrition therapy.
13 (2) Unless the person is an employee of the federal government authorized within the discharge of
14 official duty to provide medical nutrition therapy, a qualified supervisor must be licensed in this state to provide
15 medical nutrition therapy if the qualified supervisor is supervising a student or trainee who is providing medical
16 nutrition therapy to a patient located in this state.
17 (3) A qualified supervisor may only supervise a clinical activity or nutrition care service for which
18 the qualified supervisor is qualified and is authorized to perform.
19 (4) A qualified supervisor shall develop and carry out a program for advancing and optimizing the
20 quality of care provided by the student or trainee being supervised. A qualified supervisor and a student or
21 trainee being supervised shall identify and document goals for supervised practice experience, the assignment
22 of clinical tasks as appropriate to the supervisee's evolving level of competence, the supervisee's relationship
23 and access to the qualified supervisor, and a process for evaluating the student or trainee's performance.
24 (5) A qualified supervisor shall oversee the activities of and approve and accept responsibility for
25 the nutrition care services rendered by the student or trainee.
26 (6) A qualified supervisor shall, at a minimum, be physically onsite and present where the
27 supervisee is providing nutrition care services or be immediately and continuously available to the supervisee
28 by means of two-way real-time audiovisual technology that allows for the direct, contemporaneous interaction
