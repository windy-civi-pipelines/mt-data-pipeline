13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Property right in use of names, voices, and likenesses -- penalties
14 for unauthorized use. (1) Each individual must have AN INDIVIDUAL HAS property rights in their name, voice,
15 and VISUAL likeness, IF THE INDIVIDUAL IS:
16 (A) A RESIDENT OF THIS STATE; OR
17 (B) DOMICILED IN THIS STATE AT THE TIME OF THEIR DEATH.
18 (2) Whether or not an individual commercially benefits during their lifetime, their rights:
19 (a) must b e ARE freely transferable, LICENSABLE, and descendible, in whole or in part;
20 (b) may DO not expire on the death of the individual TO WHOM THE RIGHT APPLIES; and
21 (c) must b e ARE exclusive to the individual TO WHOM THE RIGHT APPLIES during their lifetime,
22 subject to the transfer of rights as provided in subsection (1)(a) (2)(A), and to the RIGHTS HOLDER'S executors,
23 heirs, transferees, or devisees for a period of 10 years after the death of the individual unless:
24 (i) the name, voice, or likeness of an individual remains in use for commercial purposes by all
25 executors, heirs, transferees, or devisees; or
26 (ii) all executors, heirs, transferees, or devisees of the individual exercising the individual's
27 property rights are deceased ; AND
28 (D) TERMINATE 20 YEARS AFTER THE DATE OF THE INDIVIDUAL'S DEATH IF NO LEGAL ACTION HAS BEEN

21 for $5,000 for each violation for each person or the actual damages suffered by the person, whichever is
22 greater, plus any profits from the unauthorized use of the individual's name, voice, or VISUAL likeness.
23 (6) IF A PERSON WHO VIOLATED SUBSECTION (3)(B) HAD AN OBJECTIVELY REASONABLE BELIEF THAT
24 MATERIAL THAT IS CLAIMED TO BE AN UNAUTHORIZED DIGITAL VOICE DEPICTION OR DIGITAL VISUAL DEPICTION DID NOT

26 ACTUAL DAMAGES REGARDLESS OF WHETHER THE MATERIAL IS ULTIMATELY DETERMINED TO BE AN UNAUTHORIZED
27 DIGITAL VOICE DEPICTION OR DIGITAL VISUAL DEPICTION.

3 deductible from this violation.
4 (c) Punitive damages and reasonable attorney fees may also be awarded to the injured party.
5 (6) A defense to an allegation of a violation of subsection ( 2 ) may not be that the unauthorized
6 user displays or otherwise communicates to the public a disclaimer stating that use is unauthorized or that the
7 individual or owner of the individual's rights did not participate in the creation, development, distribution, or
8 dissemination of the unauthorized digital voice replica or digital depiction.
9 (7)(8) An action to enforce this section may be brought by:
10 (a) the individual whose name, voice, or VISUAL likeness is at issue; or
11 (b) any other person to which the individual has assigned, bequeathed, transferred, or exclusively
12 licensed their name, voice, or VISUAL likeness rights.
13 (8)(9) To the extent the use is protected by the first amendment to the United States constitution, it IT
14 may not be a violation if the use is:
15 (a) by a news-gathering organization;
16 (b) in connection with a news report, public affairs report, sports broadcast, or account;
17 (c) for the purposes of comment, criticism, scholarship, satire, or parody;
18 (d) a representation of an individual as themself in audiovisual works as defined in 17 U.S.C. 101,
19 unless the work containing the use is intended to create, and does create, the false impression that the work is
20 an authentic recording in which the individual participated;
21 (e) fleeting or negligible; or
22 (f) in an advertisement or commercial announcement for a work described in this subsection (7)
23 (9).
24 (9)(10) No civil action may be maintained under the provisions of this section unless it is commenced
25 within 4 years after the party seeking to bring the claim discovered, or should have reasonably discovered, the
26 violation.

23 or deceased individual.

26 creation, that is readily identifiable as the individual by virtue of the individual's face, outward appearance, or
27 other distinguishing characteristic, or other information displayed in connection with the readily identifiable
28 characteristics of the individual.

1 (e) "Person" means an individual, firm, association, partnership, corporation, joint stock company,
2 syndicate, receiver, common law trust, conservator, statutory trust, or any other concern by whatever name
3 known or however organized, formed, or created, and includes not-for-profit corporations, associations,
4 educational and religious institutions, political parties, and community, civic, and other organizations.
5 (F) "VISUAL LIKENESS" MEANS AN ACTUAL OR SIMULATED PHYSICAL DEPICTION OR REPRESENTATION,
6 DIGITAL DEPICTION OR REPRESENTATION, OR OTHER FORM OF DEPICTION OR REPRESENTATION, REGARDLESS OF THE
7 MEANS OF ITS CREATION, THAT IS READILY IDENTIFIABLE AS AN INDIVIDUAL'S FACE, OUTWARD APPEARANCE, OR OTHER
8 DISTINGUISHING CHARACTERISTIC OR INFORMATION DISPLAYED WITH THE READILY IDENTIFIABLE CHARACTERISTICS OF
9 THE INDIVIDUAL.
10 (f)(G) "Voice" means sounds in any medium containing the actual voice or a simulation of the voice of
11 an individual, whether recorded or generated by computer, artificial intelligence, algorithm, or other digital
12 technology, service, or device, to the extent that the individual depicted or simulated is readily identifiable from
13 the sound of the voice depicted or simulated, or from other information displayed in connection with the voice or
14 simulation of the voice.
15 (12) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A PROVIDER OF BROADBAND INTERNET ACCESS SERVICE.
