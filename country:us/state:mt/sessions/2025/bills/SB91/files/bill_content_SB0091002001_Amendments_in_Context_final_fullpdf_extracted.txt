5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REQUIRING OFFERING THE OPTION FOR DRIVER'S LICENSES
6 AND IDENTIFICATION CARDS TO DISPLAY CITIZENSHIP STATUS; AMENDING SECTIONS 61-5-111 AND
7 61-12-501, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 61-5-111, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "61-5-111. Contents of driver's license, renewal, license expirations, license replacements,
13 grace period, and fees for licenses, permits, and endorsements -- notice of expiration. (1) (a) The
14 department may appoint county treasurers and other qualified officers to act as its agents for the sale of driver's
15 license receipts. In areas in which the department provides driver licensing services 3 days or more a week, the
16 department is responsible for sale of receipts and may appoint an agent to sell receipts.
17 (b) The department may enter into an authorized agent agreement with the county treasurer of any
18 county in which the department no longer maintains a driver examination station for the purpose purposes of
19 providing driver's license renewal services.
20 (2) (a) The department, upon on receipt of payment of the fees specified in this section, shall issue
21 a driver's license to each qualifying applicant. The license must contain:
22 (i) a full-face photograph of the licensee in the size and form prescribed by the department;
23 (ii) a distinguishing number issued to the licensee;
24 (iii) the full legal name, date of birth, and Montana residence address unless the licensee requests
25 use of the mailing address, except that the Montana residence address must be used for a REAL ID-compliant
26 driver's license unless authorized by department rule;
27 (iv) a brief description of the licensee;

8 (c) A license issued to a person who is under 21 years of age expires on the licensee's 21st
9 birthday.
10 (d) (i) Except as provided in subsection (4)(d)(ii), a commercial driver's license expires on the
11 anniversary of the licensee's birthday 4 years or less after the date of issue.
12 (ii) When a person obtains a Montana commercial driver's license with a hazardous materials
13 endorsement after surrendering a comparable commercial driver's license with a hazardous materials
14 endorsement from another licensing jurisdiction, the license expires on the anniversary of the licensee's
15 birthday 4 years or less after the date of the issue of the surrendered license if, as reported in the commercial
16 driver's license information system, a security threat assessment was performed on the person as a condition of
17 issuance of the surrendered license.
18 (e) A license issued to a person who is a foreign national whose presence in the United States is
19 temporarily authorized under federal law expires, as determined by the department, no later than the expiration
20 date of the official document issued to the person by the bureau of citizenship and immigration services of the
21 department of homeland security authorizing the person's presence in the United States.
22 (5) When the department issues a driver's license to a person under 18 years of age, the license
23 must be clearly marked with a notation that conveys the restrictions imposed under 61-5-133.
24 (6) (a) Upon On application for a driver's license or commercial driver's license and any
25 combination of the specified endorsements, the following fees must be paid:
26 (i) driver's license, except a commercial driver's license -- $5 a year or fraction of a year;
27 (ii) motorcycle endorsement -- 50 cents a year or fraction of a year;

2 (A) interstate -- $10 a year or fraction of a year; or

4 (b) A renewal notice for either a driver's license or a commercial driver's license is 50 cents.

6 the department of military affairs pursuant to 10-2-1301 and upon on receiving the information and fees
7 required in this part, the department shall include the word "veteran" on the face of the license.
8 (b) After a person's status as a veteran is denoted on a driver's license, the department may not
9 require further documentation of that status from the holder of the license upon on subsequent renewal or
10 replacement.
11 (8) (a) Except as provided in subsection (8)(b), an applicant may request a replacement driver's
12 license online or by mail.
13 (b) If the department does not have a digitized photograph and signature record of the applicant,
14 the applicant shall apply in person.
15 (c) The term of the replacement license must be the term of the applicant's current driver's license.
16 (9) (a) An applicant may request an expedited delivery service for a driver's license or identification
17 card. The department shall set a fee for expedited delivery based on the cost of providing this service.
18 (b) The fees for expedited delivery must be deposited in the motor vehicle division administration
19 account established in 61-3-112 and used for the purposes of expediting delivery, including actual costs for
20 delivery, personnel, and related technology.
21 (10) On or after January 1, 2026, if the applicant is a citizen of the United States, the applicant may
22 request an image of an eagle to signify the licensee's status as a United States citizen."

24 Section 2. Section 61-12-501, MCA, is amended to read:
25 "61-12-501. Authority of department to issue identification cards -- lawful presence verification.
26 (1) The department may issue an identification card to any person who maintains a residence in this state and
27 whose presence in the United States is authorized under federal law.
