11 Section 1. Section 41-3-112, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "41-3-112. Appointment of court-appointed special advocate -- guardian ad litem. (1) In every
13 judicial proceeding, the court shall may appoint a court-appointed special advocate as the guardian ad litem for
14 any child alleged to be abused or neglected. If a court-appointed special advocate is not available for
15 appointment, the court may appoint an attorney or other qualified person to serve as the guardian ad litem. The
16 A governmental department or any member of its staff who has a direct conflict of interest may not be
17 appointed as the guardian ad litem special advocate in a judicial proceeding under this title. When necessary,
18 the guardian ad litem may serve at public expense.
19 (2) A prospective special advocate must meet the following minimum qualifications:
20 (a) be at least 21 years old and have demonstrated an interest in children and children's welfare;
21 (b) be willing to commit to the court for a minimum of 1 year of service to a child;
22 (c) have completed an application and have provided required background information; and
23 (d) have completed a screening interview.
24 (3) An appointed special advocate may not:
25 (a) accept any compensation for the duties and responsibilities of the special advocate's
26 appointment;
27 (b) have any association that creates a conflict of interest with the special advocate's duties;
28 (c) be related to any party or attorney involved in a case;
