Section 1:

69th Legislature 2025 SB 249.1
2 INTRODUCED BY D. LENZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING CHILD ABUSE AND NEGLECT LAWS INVOLVING
5 ADJUDICATION; REQUIRING THAT A COURT HEAR ADDITIONAL EVIDENCE FROM INDIVIDUALS
6 CARING FOR A CHILD; AMENDING SECTION 41-3-437, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE
7 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 41-3-437, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "41-3-437. (Temporary) Adjudication -- temporary disposition -- findings -- order. (1) Upon the
13 filing of an appropriate petition, an adjudicatory hearing must be held within 90 days of a show cause hearing
14 under 41-3-432. Adjudication may take place at the show cause hearing if the requirements of subsection (2)
15 are met or may be made by prior stipulation of the parties pursuant to 41-3-434 and order of the court.
16 Exceptions to the time limit may be allowed only in cases involving newly discovered evidence, unavoidable
17 delays, stipulation by the parties pursuant to 41-3-434, and unforeseen personal emergencies.
18 (2) The court may make an adjudication on a petition under 41-3-422 if the court determines by a
19 preponderance of the evidence, except as provided in the federal Indian Child Welfare Act or the Montana
20 Indian Child Welfare Act provided for in Title 41, chapter 3, part 13, if applicable, that the child is a youth in
21 need of care. Except as otherwise provided in this part, the Montana Rules of Civil Procedure and the Montana
22 Rules of Evidence apply to adjudication and to an adjudicatory hearing. Adjudication must determine the nature
23 of the abuse and neglect and establish facts that resulted in state intervention and upon which disposition, case
24 work, court review, and possible termination are based.
25 (3) The court shall hear evidence regarding the residence of the child, paternity, if in question, the
26 whereabouts of the parents, guardian, or nearest adult relative, and any other matters the court considers
27 relevant in determining the status of the child. Hearsay evidence of statements made by the affected youth is
28 admissible according to the Montana Rules of Evidence.

1 (4) In a case in which abandonment has been alleged by the county attorney, the attorney general,
2 or an attorney hired by the county, the The court shall hear offered evidence, including evidence offered by a
3 person appearing pursuant to 41-3-422(9)(a) or (9)(b), regarding any of the following subjects:
4 (a) the extent to which the child has been cared for, nurtured, or supported by a person other than
5 the child's parents; and
6 (b) whether the child was placed or allowed to remain by the parents with another person for the
7 care of the child, and, if so, then the court shall accept evidence regarding:
8 (i) the intent of the parents in placing the child or allowing the child to remain with that person;
9 (ii) the continuity of care the person has offered the child by providing permanency or stability in
10 residence, schooling, and activities outside of the home; and
11 (iii) the circumstances under which the child was placed or allowed to remain with that other
12 person, including:
13 (A) whether a parent requesting return of the child was previously prevented from doing so as a
14 result of an order issued pursuant to Title 40, chapter 15, part 2, or of a conviction pursuant to 45-5-206; and
15 (B) whether the child was originally placed with the other person to allow the parent to seek
16 employment or attend school.
17 (5) In all civil and criminal proceedings relating to abuse or neglect, the privileges related to the
18 examination or treatment of the child do not apply, except the attorney-client privilege granted by 26-1-803 and
19 the mediation privilege granted by 26-1-813.
20 (6) (a) If the court determines that the child is not an abused or neglected child, the petition must
21 be dismissed and any order made pursuant to 41-3-427 or 41-3-432 must be vacated.
22 (b) If the child is adjudicated a youth in need of care, the court shall set a date for a dispositional
23 hearing to be conducted within 20 days, as provided in 41-3-438(1), and order any necessary or required
24 investigations. The court may issue a temporary dispositional order pending the dispositional hearing. The
25 temporary dispositional order may provide for any of the forms of relief listed in 41-3-427(2).
26 (7) (a) Before making an adjudication, the court may make oral findings, and following the
27 adjudicatory hearing, the court shall make written findings on issues, including but not limited to the following:
28 (i) which allegations of the petition have been proved or admitted, if any;

2 (iii) whether the department has made reasonable efforts to avoid protective placement of the child

5 (i) terms for visitation, support, and other intrafamily communication pending disposition if the
6 child is to be placed or to remain in temporary out-of-home care prior to disposition;
7 (ii) examinations, evaluations, or counseling of the child or parents in preparation for the
8 disposition hearing that does not require an expenditure of money by the department unless the court finds
