4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING EDUCATION LAWS RELATED TO ENGLISH
5 LEARNERS; ESTABLISHING STATE FUNDING TO SUPPORT SCHOOL DISTRICTS SERVING ENGLISH
6 LEARNERS; ESTABLISHING PROFESSIONAL STIPENDS FOR HIGHLY QUALIFIED TEACHERS SERVING
7 ENGLISH LEARNERS; PROVIDING A DEFINITION; PROVIDING APPROPRIATIONS; AND PROVIDING AN
8 EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 WHEREAS, the Legislature has defined, in section 20-9-309, MCA, the basic system of free quality
11 public elementary and secondary schools to include educational programs to provide for students with limited
12 English proficiency; and
13 WHEREAS, the Legislature is required under section 20-9-309, MCA, to consider the needs of students
14 with limited English proficiency in developing a mechanism to fund the basic system of free quality public
15 elementary and secondary schools; and
16 WHEREAS, currently there is no mechanism in Montana's school funding formula that addresses the
17 needs of students with limited English proficiency.
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Programs to serve English learners -- funding. (1) A public school
22 district or a consortium of public school districts receiving subgrants under 20 U.S.C. 6825 from the office of
23 public instruction for the purposes of conducting programs serving students with limited English proficiency, as
24 defined in 20-1-101 and referred to in 20-9-309, is entitled to state matching funds under this section.
25 (2) The office of public instruction shall distribute from money appropriated for this purpose by the
26 last day of January a maximum amount of $10 for each dollar that the office of public instruction allocated as a
27 subgrant to a public school district under 20 U.S.C. 6825 in the prior year. The office of public instruction shall
28 prorate payments to districts based on the available appropriation. The money received by a school district
- 1 - LC 1220

69th Legislature 2025 LC 1220
1 must be deposited in the miscellaneous programs fund and administered as provided in 20-9-507 and may be
2 expended only for programs serving students with limited English proficiency.
3
4 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Professional stipends for highly qualified teachers serving English
5 learners. (1) Pursuant to subsection (5), an annual stipend of up to $500 must be provided to each teacher
6 who holds a culturally and linguistically diverse certificate or a teaching certificate with an English as a second
7 language endorsement if the teacher is:
8 (a) a classroom teacher serving students with limited English proficiency as defined in 20-1-101;
9 (b) certified to teach in the state under the provisions of 20-4-103; and
10 (c) a full-time employee of:
11 (i) a Montana public school district as defined in 20-6-101;
12 (ii) an education cooperative as described in 20-7-451;
13 (iii) the Montana school for the deaf and blind as described in 20-8-101; or
14 (iv) a correctional facility as defined in 41-5-103.
15 (2) An annual stipend of up to $1,000 must be provided to each teacher who meets the criteria for
16 the stipend in subsection (1) and who has an instructional assignment in a school identified as:
17 (a) a school in a high-poverty area eligible to participate in the community eligibility provision under
18 Public Law 111-296; or
19 (b) an impacted school as defined in 20-4-502.
20 (3) A teacher becomes eligible for the stipend in subsection (1) in the school year beginning July 1
21 after the teacher obtains a culturally and linguistically diverse certificate or a teaching certificate with an English
22 as a second language endorsement.
23 (4) By March 1, the superintendent of public instruction shall distribute stipend payments to any
24 entity listed in subsections (1)(c)(i) through (1)(c)(iv) that employs an eligible teacher.
25 (5) The obligation for funding a portion of the professional stipends is an obligation of the state.
26 This section may not be construed to require a school district to provide its matching portion of a stipend to a
27 qualifying teacher without a payment from the state to the district. If the money appropriated for the stipends is
28 not enough to provide the full amount for each eligible teacher, the superintendent of public instruction shall
- 2 - LC 1220
****
69th Legislature 2025 LC 1220
1 request the state budget director to submit a request for a supplemental appropriation in the second year of the
2 biennium that is sufficient to complete the funding of the stipends.
3 (6) (a) For a stipend under subsection (1), the state shall pay $200 and another $1 for each $1
4 contributed by the teacher's school district, up to a maximum state contribution of $350.
5 (b) For a stipend under subsection (2), the state shall pay $400 and another $2 for each $1
6 contributed by the teacher's school district, up to a maximum state contribution of $800.
7 (7) For the purposes of this section, "culturally and linguistically diverse certificate" means a
8 certificate issued by an accredited postsecondary institution that:
9 (a) requires a minimum of 12 postsecondary credit hours; and
10 (b) is designed to enhance a teacher's ability to develop the full educational potential of a student
11 with limited English proficiency.
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Appropriation. (1) There is appropriated $5 MILLION from the state
14 general fund to the office of public instruction for BOTH fiscal years 2026 and 2027 for the purposes described in
15 [section 1].
16 (2) There is appropriated $25,000 from the state general fund to the office of public instruction for
17 BOTH fiscal years 2026 and 2027 for the purposes described in [section 2].
18 (3) The legislature intends that the appropriations in subsections (1) and (2) be considered part of
19 the ongoing base for the next legislative session.
20
21 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Notification to tribal governments. The secretary of state shall send a
22 copy of [this act] to each federally recognized tribal government in Montana.
23
24 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Codification instruction. (1) [Section 1] is intended to be codified as an
25 integral part of Title 20, chapter 9, part 3, and the provisions of Title 20, chapter 9, part 3, apply to [section 1].
26 (2) [Section 2] is intended to be codified as an integral part of Title 20, chapter 4, part 1, and the
27 provisions of Title 20, chapter 4, part 1, apply to [section 2].
28
