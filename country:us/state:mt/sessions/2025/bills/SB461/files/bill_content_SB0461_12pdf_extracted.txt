4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT GENERALLY REVISING LAWS RELATING TO INTOXICATING
5 PRODUCTS INTENDED FOR HUMAN CONSUMPTION; PROHIBITING CONSUMPTION OF INTOXICATING
6 PRODUCTS THAT ARE NOT REGULATED IN THE STATE; REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "HEMP";
7 PUTTING HEMP PRODUCTS UNDER THE REGULATION OF THE DEPARTMENT OF REVENUE;

9 MCA.”

11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Intoxicating beverages or products prohibited unless regulated. (1)
14 A beverage or product that is made for human consumption that has an intoxicating effect on the consumer is
15 prohibited unless it is specifically authorized and regulated in this state. A person who consumes a beverage or
16 product not regulated in this state is guilty of a misdemeanor punishable as provided in 45-9-121.
17 (2) For the purposes of this section, the term "intoxicating effect" includes but is not limited to
18 beverages or products that have the purpose of altering the person's mental or physical state or cause
19 intoxication.
20

26 other licensee approved under this chapter; and
27 (b) sell marijuana or marijuana products to registered cardholders, adults that are 21 years of age
28 or older, or both.

9 Section 3. Section 16-12-117, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "16-12-117. Synthetic marijuana products prohibited -- restriction on sale of marijuana
11 products. (1) A person may not manufacture, process, or offer for sale a synthetic marijuana product.
12 (2) Products containing or consisting of cannabinoids produced and processed for any type of
13 consumption into a human body, whether marketed as containing or consisting of cannabinoids or not, that
14 exceed a THC concentration of 0.3% may only be sold by a manufacturer licensed under 16-12-222 or a
15 dispensary licensed under 16-12-224 unless the products are authorized as a drug by the United States food
16 and drug administration and those products produce no intoxicating effects when consumed. Products under
17 this section may not exceed the potency levels established in 16-12-224.
18 (3) Products containing a THC concentration of 0.3% or less sold by any person other than a
19 licensed manufacturer under 16-12-222 or a licensed dispensary under 16-12-224 may not exceed 0.5
20 milligrams of THC for each serving and may not exceed 2 milligrams per package.
21 (4) This section does not apply to unadulterated hemp flower that is not further processed into
22 extracts, infused products, or concentrates."
