1 HOUSE BILL NO. 12
2 INTRODUCED BY J. FITZPATRICK
3 BY REQUEST OF THE OFFICE OF BUDGET AND PROGRAM PLANNING
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT APPROPRIATING MONEY FROM THE HISTORIC
6 PRESERVATION GRANT PROGRAM ACCOUNT TO THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE FOR HISTORIC
7 PRESERVATION PROJECTS; AUTHORIZING GRANTS FROM THE HISTORIC PRESERVATION GRANT
8 PROGRAM ACCOUNT; PLACING CONDITIONS ON GRANTS AND FUNDS; AMENDING SECTION 4(1),
9 CHAPTER 710, LAWS OF 2023; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
12
13 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Appropriation for Montana historic preservation grant program. (1)
14 There is appropriated $6,229,375 from the historic preservation grant program account established in 22-3-
15 1307 to the department of commerce for the biennium beginning July 1, 2025, to finance projects authorized in
16 subsection (2).
17 (2) The following projects and applicants are authorized for grants in their order of priority:
18 Rank Project/Applicant Grant Amount
19 1 Carter County Museum
20 Carter County Geological Society $500,000
21 2 Yaak Community Hall
22 Lincoln County $168,000
23 2 John Engen Building
24 Missoula Local Government Building Special District $498,800
25 4 Paris Gibson Square Museum of Art
26 Paris Gibson Square Museum of Art $356,377
27 5 Lincoln School
28 Lincoln School Foundation $364,980

1 requirements of the department of commerce as described in the most recent edition of the Montana Historic
2 Preservation Grant Program Project Administration Manual adopted by the department through the
3 administrative rulemaking process.
4
5 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Supplemental appropriations. In the event that the total cost of a
6 project exceeds the amount of the grant and matching funds, the grant recipient is fully responsible for funding
7 the cost overrun. A supplemental appropriation may not be granted to complete the project.
8
9 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Other powers and duties of department of commerce. (1) The
10 department of commerce shall disburse grant funds on a reimbursement basis as grant recipients incur eligible
11 project expenses.
12 (2) If actual project expenses are lower than the projected expense of the project, the department
13 may, at its discretion, reduce the amount of grant funds provided to grant recipients in proportion to all other
14 project funding sources.
15
16 Section 6. Section 4(1), Chapter 710, Laws of 2023, is amended to read:
17 (1) The portion of section 4(1), Chapter 710, Laws of 2023, appropriating money from the historic
18 preservation grant program account to the department of commerce for the indicated project is amended to
19 read:
20 "15 Rocky Mountain Building
21 Community Health Care Center, Inc. dba Alluvion Health $400,000"
22 (2) The portion of section 4(1), Chapter 710, Laws of 2023, appropriating money from the historic
23 preservation grant program account to the department of commerce for the indicated project is amended to
24 read:
25 "20 Montana Club
26 Original Montana Club Cooperative Association Rathskeller, LLC $90,040"
27
