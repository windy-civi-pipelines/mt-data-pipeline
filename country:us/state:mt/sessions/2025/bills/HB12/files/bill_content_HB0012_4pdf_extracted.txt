1 (a) The grant recipient shall document that other matching funds required for the completion of the
2 project are firmly committed.
3 (b) The grant recipient must have a project management plan that is approved by the department
4 of commerce.
5 (c) The grant recipient must be in compliance with the auditing and reporting requirements
6 provided for in 2-7-503 and establish a financial accounting system that the department can reasonably ensure
7 conforms to generally accepted accounting principles. Tribal governments shall comply with the auditing and
8 reporting requirements provided for in 2 CFR 200.
9 (d) The grant recipient shall satisfactorily comply with any conditions described in the application or
10 project summaries section of the Montana historic preservation grant program 2025 biennium report to the 69th
11 legislature.
12 (e) The grant recipient shall satisfy other specific requirements considered necessary by the
13 department of commerce to accomplish the purpose of the project as evidenced by the application to the
14 department.
15 (f) The grant recipient shall execute a grant agreement with the department of commerce.
16 (2) Recipients of Montana historic preservation grant program funds are subject to the
17 requirements of the department of commerce as described in the most recent edition of the Montana Historic
18 Preservation Grant Program Project Administration Manual adopted by the department through the
19 administrative rulemaking process.

21 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Supplemental appropriations. In the event that the total cost of a
22 project exceeds the amount of the grant and matching funds, the grant recipient is fully responsible for funding
23 the cost overrun. A supplemental appropriation may not be granted to complete the project.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 5. Other powers and duties of department of commerce. (1) The
26 department of commerce shall disburse grant funds on a reimbursement basis as grant recipients incur eligible
27 project expenses.
