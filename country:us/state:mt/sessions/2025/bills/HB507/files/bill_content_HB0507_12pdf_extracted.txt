2026
69th Legislature 2025 HB 507.1
2 INTRODUCED BY B. BARKER, M. VINTON, S. KELLY, E. ALBUS, D. BEDEY, S. GIST, L. JONES, G.
3 OBLANDER, J. FITZPATRICK, G. HERTZ, G. KMETZ, B. MERCER, G. NIKOLAKAKOS, J. DARLING
4
5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT SUBMITTING TO THE QUALIFIED ELECTORS OF MONTANA AN
6 AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VIII, SECTION 16, OF THE MONTANA CONSTITUTION TO PROVIDE FOR A
7 4% SALES TAX AND USE TAX TO REDUCE PROPERTY TAXES THAT FUND K-12 EDUCATION; AND
8 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.”
9
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
11
12 Section 1. Article VIII, section 16, of The Constitution of the State of Montana is amended to read:
13 "Section 16. Limitation on sales tax or and use tax rates for education. The rate of legislature
14 shall dedicate the net revenue from a general statewide sales tax or and use tax may not exceed of 4% to
15 reduce property taxes that fund K-12 education unless appropriated for other purposes by a vote of three-
16 fourths of the members of each house of the legislature."
17
18 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Two-thirds vote required. Because [section 1] is a legislative proposal
19 to amend the constitution, Article XIV, section 8, of the Montana constitution requires an affirmative roll call vote
20 of two-thirds of all the members of the legislature, whether one or more bodies, for passage.
21
22 NEW SECTION. Section 3. Effective date. If approved by the electorate, [this act] is effective July 1,
23 2027.
24
25 NEW SECTION. Section 4. Submission to electorate. [This act] shall be submitted to the qualified
26 electors of Montana at the general election to be held in November 2026 by printing on the ballot the full title of
27 [this act] and the following:
28 [] YES on Constitutional Amendment ____.
