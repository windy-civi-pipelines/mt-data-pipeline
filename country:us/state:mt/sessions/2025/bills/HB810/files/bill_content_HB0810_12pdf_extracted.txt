4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING RENTAL LAWS; SPECIFYING ACCEPTABLE FORMS
5 OF PAYMENT; PROHIBITING ADDITIONAL FEES BASED ON RENT PAYMENT TYPE EXCEPT TO
6 RECOUP AN ELECTRONIC BANK FEE INCURRED FOR ELECTRONIC PAYMENT; PROVIDING A
7 DEFINITION; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 70-24-103, 70-24-201, 70-33-103, AND 70-33-201, MCA.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Section 70-24-103, MCA, is amended to read:
12 "70-24-103. General definitions. Subject to additional definitions contained in subsequent sections
13 and unless the context otherwise requires, in this chapter the following definitions apply:
14 (1) "Abandon" means to give up possession of the premises unless the landlord does not accept
15 abandonment or surrender as provided in 70-24-426 or unless the rental agreement has been terminated as
16 provided by law.
17 (2) "Action" includes recoupment, counterclaim, setoff suit in equity, and any other proceeding in
18 which rights are determined, including an action for possession.
19 (3) "Actual and reasonable cost" means the actual amount of expenses and labor incurred or
20 expended and the reasonable amount of expenses and labor estimated to be incurred or expended.
21 (4) "Case of emergency" means an extraordinary occurrence beyond the tenant's control requiring
22 immediate action to protect the premises or the tenant. A case of emergency may include the interruption of
23 essential services, including heat, electricity, gas, running water, hot water, and sewer and septic system
24 service, or life-threatening events in which the tenant or landlord has reasonable apprehension of immediate
25 danger to the tenant or others.
26 (5) "Court" means the appropriate district court, small claims court, justice's court, or city court.
27 (6) "Dwelling unit" means a structure or the part of a structure that is used as a home, residence,
28 or sleeping place by a person who maintains a household or by two or more persons who maintain a common

1 household. Dwelling unit, in the case of a person who rents space in a mobile home park and rents the mobile
2 home, means the mobile home itself.
3 (7) "Good faith" means honesty in fact in the conduct of the transaction concerned.
4 (8) "Guest" means a person staying with a tenant for a temporary period of time as defined in the
5 rental agreement or, if not defined in the rental agreement, for a period of time no more than 7 days unless the
6 tenant has received the landlord's written consent to a longer period of time.
7 (9) "Landlord" means:
8 (a) the owner of the dwelling unit or the building of which it is a part;
9 (b) a person who has written authorization from the owner to act as the owner's agent or assignee
10 for purposes related to the premises or the rental agreement;
11 (c) a person who has written authorization from the owner to act as a manager of the premises for
12 the purposes of the tenancy or the rental agreement; or
13 (d) a lessor who has written authorization from the owner of the premises to sublease the
14 premises.
15 (10) "Organization" includes a corporation, government, governmental subdivision or agency,
16 business trust, estate, trust, or partnership or association, two or more persons having a joint or common
17 interest, and any other legal or commercial entity.
18 (11) "Owner" means one or more persons, jointly or severally, in whom is vested all or part of:
19 (a) the legal title to property; or
20 (b) the beneficial ownership and a right to present use and enjoyment of the premises, including a
21 mortgagee in possession.
22 (12) "Person" includes an individual or organization.
23 (13) "Premises" means a dwelling unit and the structure of which it is a part, the facilities and
24 appurtenances in the structure, and the grounds, areas, and facilities held out for the use of tenants generally
25 or promised for the use of a tenant.
26 (14) "Rent" means all payments to be made to the landlord, including rent, late fees, or other
27 charges as agreed on in the rental agreement, except money paid as a security deposit.
28 (15) "Rent payment type" means all forms of payment types, including:

2 (b) check;
3 (c) electronic; or
4 (d) other forms agreed on in the rental agreement that must comply with 70-24-203.
5 (15) (16) "Rental agreement" means all agreements, written or oral, and valid rules adopted
6 under 70-24-311 embodying the terms and conditions concerning the use and occupancy of a dwelling unit and
7 premises.
8 (16) (17) "Roomer" means a person occupying a dwelling unit that does not include a toilet, a
9 bathtub or a shower, a refrigerator, a stove, or a kitchen sink, all of which are provided by the landlord and one
10 or more of which are used in common by occupants in the structure.
11 (17) (18) "Single-family residence" means a structure maintained and used as a single dwelling
12 unit. A dwelling unit that shares one or more walls with another dwelling unit is a single-family residence if it has
13 direct access to a street or thoroughfare and does not share heating facilities, hot water equipment, or any
14 other essential facility or service with another dwelling unit.
15 (18) (19) "Tenant" means:
16 (a) a person entitled under a rental agreement to occupy a dwelling unit to the exclusion of others;
17 or
18 (b) a person who, with the written approval of the landlord and pursuant to the rental agreement,

20 agreement.

23 permission by the landlord to remain on the premises;
24 (b) is in violation of 45-6-201;
25 (c) is in violation of 45-6-203; or
26 (d) is in violation of 70-27-102."

28 Section 2. Section 70-24-201, MCA, is amended to read:
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – HB 810

1 "70-24-201. Rental agreement -- terms and conditions. (1) A landlord and a tenant may include in
2 a rental agreement terms and conditions not prohibited by this chapter or other rule or law, including rent, term
3 of the agreement, and other provisions governing the rights and obligations of the parties.
4 (2) Unless the rental agreement provides otherwise:
5 (a) the tenant shall pay as rent the rental value for the use and occupancy of the dwelling unit as
6 determined by the landlord;
7 (b) rent is payable at the landlord's address or using electronic funds transfer to an account
8 designated for the payment of rent by the landlord;
9 (c) periodic rent is payable at the beginning of a term of a month or less and otherwise in equal
10 monthly installments at the beginning of each month;
11 (d) rent is uniformly apportionable from day to day;
12 (e) the tenancy is week to week in the case of a roomer who pays weekly rent and in all other
13 cases month to month; and
14 (f) if either party terminates the rental agreement without cause prior to the expiration date of the
15 lease term, the aggrieved party is entitled to monetary damages up to 1 month's rent or an amount that is
16 agreed on in the rental agreement, which may not exceed 1 month's rent. Landlords shall follow 70-24-426(3)
17 and are entitled to rent from defaulting tenants up to the date a new tenancy starts or the date the rental
18 agreement term expires.
19 (3) Rent is payable without demand or notice at the time and place agreed upon by the parties or
20 provided for by subsection (2).
21 (4) A landlord may not charge an additional fee based on rent payment type except to recoup an
22 electronic bank fee incurred for electronic payment."
