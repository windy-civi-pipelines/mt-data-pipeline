1 SENATE BILL NO. 214
2 INTRODUCED BY G. HERTZ

4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING ZONING LAWS; PROVIDING THAT THE FREE USE
5 OF PROPERTY MUST BE FAVORED IN CASES INVOLVING AN INTERPRETATION OF USE OR
6 WHETHER A NONCONFORMING USE OF LAND IS ALLOWED AND THERE IS AMBIGUITY AS TO THE
7 USE OR PRIOR USE; REMOVING THE EXCEPTION THAT HISTORIC PRESERVATION BOARDS MAY
8 REVIEW APPLICATIONS FOR ZONING PERMITS AND VARIANCES; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 76-2-
9 105, 76-2-113, 76-2-208, 76-2-210, 76-2-302, 76-2-308, AND 76-25-301, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
10 IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 76-2-105, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "76-2-105. Continuation of prior nonconforming uses. (1) Existing or prior nonconforming uses
16 may be continued although not in conformity with such the zoning regulations The zoning regulations must
17 allow for the nonconforming use of land or buildings legal at the time or prior to the time that a zoning regulation
18 or amendment to the regulation was adopted, but the board of county commissioners may provide grounds for
19 discontinuing nonconforming uses based on changes to or abandonment of the use of the land or buildings
20 after the adoption of a zoning regulation or amendment.
21 (2) Any ambiguity or uncertainty in the zoning regulations as to whether a nonconforming use is
22 allowed or whether the use was allowed when it was commenced must be interpreted in favor of the free use of
23 property nonconforming use."
24
25 Section 2. Section 76-2-113, MCA, is amended to read:
26 "76-2-113. Enforcement and interpretation of zoning provisions. (1) If any building or structure is
27 erected, constructed, reconstructed, altered, repaired, converted, or maintained or if any building, structure, or
28 land is used in violation of this part or of any resolution adopted under this part, the county, in addition to other

14 Section 5. Section 76-2-302, MCA, is amended to read:
15 "76-2-302. Zoning districts. (1) For the purposes of 76-2-301, the local city or town council or other
16 legislative body may divide the municipality into districts of the number, shape, and area as are considered best
17 suited to carry out the purposes of this part. Within the districts, it may regulate and restrict the erection,
18 construction, reconstruction, alteration, repair, or use of buildings, structures, or land, including the creation of
19 zoning districts that allow tiny dwelling units.

21 the regulations in one district may differ from those in other districts.

23 zoning district, there is a rebuttable presumption that placement of a manufactured home will not adversely
24 affect property values of conventional housing.

26 (a) "Manufactured housing" means a single-family dwelling, built offsite in a factory, that is in
