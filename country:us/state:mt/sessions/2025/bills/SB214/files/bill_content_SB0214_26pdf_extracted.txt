4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING ZONING LAWS; PROVIDING THAT THE FREE USE
5 OF PROPERTY MUST BE FAVORED IN CASES INVOLVING AN INTERPRETATION OF USE OR
6 WHETHER A NONCONFORMING USE OF LAND IS ALLOWED AND THERE IS AMBIGUITY AS TO THE
7 USE OR PRIOR USE; REMOVING THE EXCEPTION THAT HISTORIC PRESERVATION BOARDS MAY
8 REVIEW APPLICATIONS FOR ZONING PERMITS AND VARIANCES; AND AMENDING SECTIONS 76-2-
9 105, 76-2-113, 76-2-208, 76-2-210, 76-2-302, 76-2-308, AND 76-25-301, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN
10 IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”

12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 Section 1. Section 76-2-105, MCA, is amended to read:

17 ALLOW FOR THE NONCONFORMING USE OF LAND OR BUILDINGS LEGAL AT THE TIME OR PRIOR TO THE TIME THAT A

19 MAY PROVIDE GROUNDS FOR DISCONTINUING NONCONFORMING USES BASED ON CHANGES TO OR ABANDONMENT OF THE
20 USE OF THE LAND OR BUILDINGS AFTER THE ADOPTION OF A ZONING REGULATION OR AMENDMENT.
21 (2) Any ambiguity or uncertainty IN THE ZONING REGULATIONS as to whether a nonconforming use is
22 allowed or whether the use was allowed when it was commenced must be interpreted in favor of the free use of
23 property NONCONFORMING USE."
24

15 Section 5. Section 76-2-302, MCA, is amended to read:
16 "76-2-302. Zoning districts. (1) For the purposes of 76-2-301, the local city or town council or other
17 legislative body may divide the municipality into districts of the number, shape, and area as are considered best
18 suited to carry out the purposes of this part. Within the districts, it may regulate and restrict the erection,
19 construction, reconstruction, alteration, repair, or use of buildings, structures, or land, including the creation of
20 zoning districts that allow tiny dwelling units.
21 (2) All regulations must be uniform for each class or kind of buildings throughout each district, but
22 the regulations in one district may differ from those in other districts.
23 (3) In a proceeding for a permit or variance to place manufactured housing within a residential
24 zoning district, there is a rebuttable presumption that placement of a manufactured home will not adversely
25 affect property values of conventional housing.
26 (4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
27 (a) "Manufactured housing" means a single-family dwelling, built offsite in a factory, that is in
28 compliance with the applicable prevailing standards of the United States department of housing and urban

1 development at the time of its production. A manufactured home does not include a mobile home or
2 housetrailer, as defined in 15-1-101.
3 (b) (i) "Tiny dwelling unit" means a residential dwelling unit that is 350 to 750 square feet, is on a
4 permanent foundation, and is used as a single-family dwelling for at least 45 days or longer.
5 (ii) Appendix Q, tiny houses, of the International Building Code as it was printed on January 1,
6 2023, may govern all other requirements of a tiny dwelling unit that is 350 to 750 square feet.
7 (5) This section may not be construed to limit conditions imposed in historic districts, local design
8 review standards, existing covenants, or the ability to enter into covenants pursuant to Title 70, chapter 17, part
9 2. Local design review standards imposed by a local government must be clear, objective, and necessary to
10 protect public health or safety or to comply with federal law.
11 (6) Zoning regulations may not include a requirement to:
12 (a) pay a fee for the purpose of providing housing for specified income levels or at specified sale
13 prices; or
14 (b) dedicate real property for the purpose of providing housing for specified income levels or at
15 specified sale prices.
16 (7) A dedication of real property as prohibited in subsection (6)(b) includes a payment or other
17 contribution to a local housing authority or the reservation of real property for future development of housing for
18 specified income levels or specified sale prices.
19 (8) (a) Except as provided in subsection (8)(b), when When reviewing an application for a zoning
20 permit or variance from local design review standards, the determination of compliance with local design review
21 standards as provided in subsection (5) must be conducted by employees of the municipality, and the
22 municipality may not require review by an external board.
23 (b) Subsection (8)(a) does not apply to historic preservation boards reviewing an application for a
24 permit or variance to structures or districts that the local government has designated as historic or that are
25 listed on the national register of historic places as defined in the National Historic Preservation Act of 1966 as it
26 read on October 1, 2023."

17 NEW SECTION. Section 7. Continuation of nonconforming uses. (1) The zoning regulations must
18 allow for the nonconforming use of land or buildings legal at the time or prior to the time that a zoning regulation
19 or amendment to the regulation is adopted, but the local government may provide grounds for discontinuing
20 nonconforming uses based on changes to or abandonment of the use of the land or buildings after the adoption
21 of a zoning regulation or amendment.
22 (2) Any ambiguity or uncertainty IN THE ZONING REGULATIONS as to whether a nonconforming use is
23 allowed or whether the use was allowed when it was commenced must be interpreted in favor of the free use of
24 property IN QUESTION.

26 Section 8. Section 76-25-301, MCA, is amended to read:
27 "76-25-301. Authority to adopt local zoning regulations. (1) (a) A local government subject to this
28 chapter, within its respective jurisdiction, has the authority to and shall regulate the use of land in substantial

1 compliance with its adopted land use plan by adopting zoning regulations.
2 (b) The governing body of a county or city has the authority to adopt zoning regulations in
3 accordance with this part by an ordinance that substantially complies with 7-5-103 through 7-5-107.
4 (c) A municipality shall adopt zoning regulations for the portions of the jurisdictional area outside of
5 the boundaries of the municipality that the governing body anticipates may be annexed into the municipality
6 over the next 20 years. Unless otherwise agreed to by the applicable jurisdictions, zoning regulations on

18 Title 76, chapter 2, into the zoning regulations meeting the requirements of this chapter.
19 (4) The local government shall adopt a zoning map for the jurisdiction in substantial compliance
20 with the land use plan and future land use map and the zoning regulations adopted pursuant to this section,

23 implementation of this chapter.

25 development or a specific type of development, such as unmitigable natural hazards, insufficient water supply,
26 inadequate drainage, lack of access, inadequate public services, or the excessive expenditure of public funds
27 for the supply of the services.
