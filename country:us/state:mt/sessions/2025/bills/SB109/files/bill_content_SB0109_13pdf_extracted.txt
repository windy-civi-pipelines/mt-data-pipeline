69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
2 INTRODUCED BY G. HERTZ
3
4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT REVISING THE DEFINITION OF "TREATING PHYSICIAN" TO
5 INCLUDE A PHYSICAL THERAPIST; AND AMENDING SECTION 39-71-116, MCA.”
6
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
8
9 Section 1. Section 39-71-116, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "39-71-116. Definitions. Unless the context otherwise requires, in this chapter, the following
11 definitions apply:
12 (1) "Actual wage loss" means that the wages that a worker earns or is qualified to earn after the
13 worker reaches maximum healing are less than the actual wages the worker received at the time of the injury.
14 (2) "Administer and pay" includes all actions by the state fund under the Workers' Compensation
15 Act necessary to:
16 (a) investigation, review, and settlement of claims;
17 (b) payment of benefits;
18 (c) setting of reserves;
19 (d) furnishing of services and facilities; and
20 (e) use of actuarial, audit, accounting, vocational rehabilitation, and legal services.
21 (3) "Aid or sustenance" means a public or private subsidy made to provide a means of support,
22 maintenance, or subsistence for the recipient.
23 (4) "Beneficiary" means:
24 (a) a surviving spouse living with or legally entitled to be supported by the deceased at the time of
25 injury;
26 (b) an unmarried child under 18 years of age;
27 (c) an unmarried child under 22 years of age who is a full-time student in an accredited school or is
28 enrolled in an accredited apprenticeship program;
- 1 - Authorized Print Version – SB 109
****
69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
1 (d) an invalid child over 18 years of age who is dependent, as defined in 26 U.S.C. 152, upon the
2 decedent for support at the time of injury;
3 (e) a parent who is dependent, as defined in 26 U.S.C. 152, upon the decedent for support at the
4 time of the injury if a beneficiary, as defined in subsections (4)(a) through (4)(d), does not exist; and
5 (f) a brother or sister under 18 years of age if dependent, as defined in 26 U.S.C. 152, upon the
6 decedent for support at the time of the injury but only until the age of 18 years and only when a beneficiary, as
7 defined in subsections (4)(a) through (4)(e), does not exist.
8 (5) "Business partner" means the community, governmental entity, or business organization that
9 provides the premises for work-based learning activities for students.
10 (6) "Casual employment" means employment not in the usual course of the trade, business,
11 profession, or occupation of the employer.
12 (7) "Child" includes a posthumous child, a dependent stepchild, and a child legally adopted prior to
13 the injury.
14 (8) (a) "Claims examiner" means an individual who, as a paid employee of the department, of a
15 plan No. 1, 2, or 3 insurer, or of an administrator licensed under Title 33, chapter 17, examines claims under
16 chapter 71 to:
17 (i) determine liability;
18 (ii) apply the requirements of this title;
19 (iii) settle workers' compensation or occupational disease claims; or
20 (iv) determine survivor benefits.
21 (b) The term does not include an adjuster as defined in 33-17-102.
22 (9) (a) "Construction industry" means the major group of general contractors and operative
23 builders, heavy construction (other than building construction) contractors, and special trade contractors listed
24 in major group 23 in the North American Industry Classification System Manual.
25 (b) The term does not include office workers, design professionals, salespersons, estimators, or
26 any other related employment that is not directly involved on a regular basis in the provision of physical labor at
27 a construction or renovation site.
28 (10) "Days" means calendar days, unless otherwise specified.

1 (11) "Department" means the department of labor and industry.
2 (12) "Direct result" means that a diagnosed condition was caused or aggravated by an injury or
3 occupational disease.
4 (13) "Fiscal year" means the period of time between July 1 and the succeeding June 30.
5 (14) "Health care provider" means a person who is licensed, certified, or otherwise authorized by
6 the laws of this state to provide health care in the ordinary course of business or practice of a profession.
7 (15) (a) "Household or domestic employment" means employment of persons other than members
8 of the household for the purpose of tending to the aid and comfort of the employer or members of the
9 employer's family, including but not limited to housecleaning and yard work.
10 (b) The term does not include employment beyond the scope of normal household or domestic
11 duties, such as home health care or domiciliary care.
12 (16) (a) "Indemnity benefits" means any payment made directly to the worker or the worker's
13 beneficiaries, other than a medical benefit. The term includes payments made pursuant to a reservation of
14 rights.
15 (b) The term does not include stay-at-work/return-to-work assistance, auxiliary benefits, or
16 expense reimbursements for items such as meals, travel, or lodging.
17 (17) "Insurer" means an employer bound by compensation plan No. 1, an insurance company
18 transacting business under compensation plan No. 2, or the state fund under compensation plan No. 3.
19 (18) "Invalid" means one who is physically or mentally incapacitated.
20 (19) "Limited liability company" has the meaning provided in 35-8-102.
21 (20) "Maintenance care" means treatment designed to provide the optimum state of health while
22 minimizing recurrence of the clinical status.
23 (21) "Medical stability", "maximum medical improvement", "maximum healing", or "maximum
24 medical healing" means a point in the healing process when further material functional improvement would not
25 be reasonably expected from primary medical services.
26 (22) "Objective medical findings" means medical evidence, including range of motion, atrophy,
27 muscle strength, muscle spasm, or other diagnostic evidence, substantiated by clinical findings.
28 (23) (a) "Occupational disease" means harm, damage, or death arising out of or contracted in the
- 3 - Authorized Print Version – SB 109
****
69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
1 course and scope of employment caused by events occurring on more than a single day or work shift.
2 (b) The term does not include a physical or mental condition arising from emotional or mental
3 stress or from a nonphysical stimulus or activity.
4 (24) "Order" means any decision, rule, direction, requirement, or standard of the department or any
5 other determination arrived at by the department.
6 (25) "Palliative care" means treatment designed to reduce or ease symptoms without curing the
7 underlying cause of the symptoms.
8 (26) "Payroll", "annual payroll", or "annual payroll for the preceding year" means the average annual
9 payroll of the employer for the preceding calendar year or, if the employer has not operated a sufficient or any
10 length of time during the calendar year, 12 times the average monthly payroll for the current year. However, an
11 estimate may be made by the department for any employer starting in business if average payrolls are not
12 available. This estimate must be adjusted by additional payment by the employer or refund by the department,
13 as the case may actually be, on December 31 of the current year. An employer's payroll must be computed by
14 calculating all wages, as defined in 39-71-123, that are paid by an employer.
15 (27) "Permanent partial disability" means a physical condition in which a worker, after reaching
16 maximum medical healing:
17 (a) has a permanent impairment, as determined by the sixth edition of the American medical
18 association's Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment, that is established by objective medical
19 findings for the ratable condition. The ratable condition must be a direct result of the compensable injury or
20 occupational disease and may not be based exclusively on complaints of pain.
21 (b) is able to return to work in some capacity but the permanent impairment impairs the worker's
22 ability to work; and
23 (c) has an actual wage loss as a result of the injury.
24 (28) "Permanent total disability" means a physical condition resulting from injury as defined in this
25 chapter, after a worker reaches maximum medical healing, in which a worker does not have a reasonable
26 prospect of physically performing regular employment. Lack of immediate job openings is not a factor to be
27 considered in determining if a worker is permanently totally disabled.
28 (29) "Primary medical services" means treatment prescribed by the treating physician, for conditions

69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
1 resulting from the injury or occupational disease, necessary for achieving medical stability.
2 (30) "Prosthetic device" or "prosthesis" means an artificial substitute for a missing body part.
3 (31) "Public corporation" means the state or a county, municipal corporation, school district, city, city
4 under a commission form of government or special charter, town, or village.
5 (32) "Reasonably safe place to work" means that the place of employment has been made as free
6 from danger to the life or safety of the employee as the nature of the employment will reasonably permit.
7 (33) "Reasonably safe tools or appliances" are tools and appliances that are adapted to and that are
8 reasonably safe for use for the particular purpose for which they are furnished.
9 (34) "Regular employment" means work on a recurring basis performed for remuneration in a trade,
10 business, profession, or other occupation in this state.
11 (35) (a) "Secondary medical services" means those medical services or appliances that are
12 considered not medically necessary for medical stability. The services and appliances include but are not
13 limited to spas or hot tubs, work hardening, physical restoration programs and other restoration programs
14 designed to address disability and not impairment, or equipment offered by individuals, clinics, groups,
15 hospitals, or rehabilitation facilities.
16 (b) (i) As used in this subsection (35), "disability" means a condition in which a worker's ability to
17 engage in gainful employment is diminished as a result of physical restrictions resulting from an injury. The
18 restrictions may be combined with factors, such as the worker's age, education, work history, and other factors
19 that affect the worker's ability to engage in gainful employment.
20 (ii) Disability does not mean a purely medical condition.
21 (36) "Sole proprietor" means the person who has the exclusive legal right or title to or ownership of
22 a business enterprise.
23 (37) "State's average weekly wage" means the mean weekly earnings of all employees under
24 covered employment, as defined and established annually by the department before July 1 and rounded to the
25 nearest whole dollar number.
26 (38) "Temporary partial disability" means a physical condition resulting from an injury, as defined in
27 39-71-119, in which a worker, prior to maximum healing:
28 (a) is temporarily unable to return to the position held at the time of injury because of a medically
- 5 - Authorized Print Version – SB 109
****
69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
1 determined physical restriction;
2 (b) returns to work in a modified or alternative employment; and
3 (c) suffers a partial wage loss.
4 (39) "Temporary service contractor" means a person, firm, association, partnership, limited liability
5 company, or corporation conducting business that hires its own employees and assigns them to clients to fill a
6 work assignment with a finite ending date to support or supplement the client's workforce in situations resulting
7 from employee absences, skill shortages, seasonal workloads, and special assignments and projects.
8 (40) "Temporary total disability" means a physical condition resulting from an injury, as defined in
9 this chapter, that results in total loss of wages and exists until the injured worker reaches maximum medical
10 healing.
11 (41) "Temporary worker" means a worker whose services are furnished to another on a part-time or
12 temporary basis to fill a work assignment with a finite ending date to support or supplement a workforce in
13 situations resulting from employee absences, skill shortages, seasonal workloads, and special assignments
14 and projects.
15 (42) "Treating physician" means the person who, subject to the requirements of 39-71-1101, is
16 primarily responsible for delivery and coordination of the worker's medical services for the treatment of a
17 worker's compensable injury or occupational disease and is:
18 (a) a physician licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 3, and has admitting
19 privileges to practice in one or more hospitals, if any, in the area where the physician is located;
20 (b) a chiropractor licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 12;
21 (c) a physician assistant licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 20, if there is not
22 a treating physician, as provided for in subsection (42)(a), in the area where the physician assistant is located;
23 (d) an osteopath licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 3;
24 (e) a dentist licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 4;
25 (f) for a claimant residing out of state or upon approval of the insurer, a treating physician defined
26 in subsections (42)(a) through (42)(e) and (42)(h) who is licensed or certified in another state; or
27 (g) an advanced practice registered nurse licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter
28 8; or
- 6 - Authorized Print Version – SB 109

69th Legislature 2025 SB 109.1
1 (h) a physical therapist licensed by the state of Montana under Title 37, chapter 11.
2 (43) "Work-based learning activities" means job training and work experience conducted on the
3 premises of a business partner as a component of school-based learning activities authorized by an
4 elementary, secondary, or postsecondary educational institution.
5 (44) "Year", unless otherwise specified, means calendar year."
