24 entity is alleging fraud or other intentional or willful misrepresentation that is evidenced by the review of claims
25 data, statements, physical review, or other investigative methods;
26 (4) use extrapolation or other statistical expansion techniques in calculating the amount of any
27 recoupment or penalty;

8 Section 2. Section 33-22-170, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "33-22-170. Definitions. As used in 33-22-170 through 33-22-177 and 33-22-180, the following
10 definitions apply:
11 (1) "Contract pharmacy" means a pharmacy operating under contract with a federally certified
12 health entity to provide dispensing services to the federally certified health entity.
13 (2) "Effective rate contracting" means an agreement or arrangement between a pharmacy or a
14 contracting agent acting on behalf of a pharmacy and a pharmacy benefit manager or third-party payer that
15 establishes a reimbursement rate for pharmaceuticals based on the effective rate of payment rather than on a
16 predetermined fixed price or a fixed discount percentage. AN EFFECTIVE RATE OF PAYMENT INVOLVES ANY
17 CALCULATION FOR WHICH PHARMACY REIMBURSEMENT IS BASED ON AN AGGREGATION OF MORE THAN ONE CLAIM,
18 RATHER THAN ON A PER-CLAIM BASIS.

20 256b(a)(4).
21 (4) "INDEPENDENT PHARMACY" MEANS A PHARMACY AS DEFINED IN 37-7-101 THAT:
22 (A) IS LICENSED WITH THE BOARD OF PHARMACY AS A PHARMACY;
23 (B) IS LOCATED IN THE STATE; AND
24 (C) IS NOT OWNED OR OPERATED BY OR A SUBSIDIARY OR AFFILIATE OF:
25 (I) A FOR-PROFIT ENTITY WITH MORE THAN 10 PHARMACY LOCATIONS NATIONWIDE;
26 (II) A PHARMACY BENEFIT MANAGER; OR
27 (III) A PUBLICLY TRADED ENTITY.
28 (3)(4)(5) "Maximum allowable cost list" means the list of drugs used by a pharmacy benefit

5 licensed by the board of pharmacy pursuant to Title 37, chapter 7, and that has entered into a network contract

9 Section 5. Section 33-22-175, MCA, is amended to read:
10 "33-22-175. Allowable and prohibited fees on pharmacies. (1) A pharmacy benefit manager, or a
11 third-party payer, or a discount card processor may not directly or indirectly charge or hold a pharmacy
12 responsible for a fee, related to a claim including but not limited to the following:
13 (a) a fee for submission of a claim;
14 (b) any other claim-related fee;
15 (c) a fee for enrollment or participation in a retail pharmacy network;
16 (d) a credentialing or recredentialing fee;
17 (e) a fee for the development or management of claims processing services or claims payment
18 services; or
19 (f) a fee on remittance advice or a fee that is retroactive if the fee is not apparent at the time the
20 claim is processed;
21 (b) if the fee is not reported on the remittance advice of an adjudicated claim; or
22 (c) after the initial claim is adjudicated.
23 (2) A pharmacy benefit manager or third-party payer may collect a performance-based fee from a
24 pharmacy only if the pharmacy fails to meet the criteria established by a pharmacy performance measurement
25 entity. The fee may be applied only to the professional dispensing fee outlined in the contract with the
26 pharmacy and may not be imposed on the cost of goods sold by a pharmacy.
27 (3) Only criteria established by a pharmacy performance measurement entity may be used to
28 measure a pharmacy's performance for the purposes of this section.

22 Section 7. Section 39-71-727, MCA, is amended to read:
23 "39-71-727. Payment for prescription drugs -- limitations. (1) For payment of prescription drugs,
24 an insurer is liable only for the purchase of generic-name drugs if the generic-name product is the therapeutic
25 equivalent of the brand-name drug prescribed by the physician, unless the generic-name drug is unavailable.
26 (2) If an injured worker prefers a brand-name drug, the worker may pay directly to the pharmacist
27 the difference in the reimbursement rate between the brand-name drug and the generic-name product, and the
28 pharmacist may bill the insurer only for the reimbursement rate of the generic-name drug.

4 was unavailable.
5 (5) The department shall establish a schedule of fees for prescription drugs.
