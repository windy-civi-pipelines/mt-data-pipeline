5 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT ADDRESSING CAREER FATIGUE AND WELLNESS IN CERTAIN
6 HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS; PROVIDING CIVIL IMMUNITY FOR MEMBERS OF OR HEALTH CARE
7 PROFESSIONAL CONSULTANTS TO CERTAIN ENTITIES ADDRESSING CAREER FATIGUE AND
8 WELLNESS IN HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS; REVISING MANDATORY AND PERMISSIVE REPORTING
9 LAWS RELATED TO CERTAIN HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS; AMENDING SECTIONS 37-3-401 AND 37-4-
10 312, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN IMMEDIATE EFFECTIVE DATE.”
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
13
14 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Health care provider professional wellness program -- reporting --
15 nonliability -- evidentiary privilege -- definitions. (1) A person or entity may not be obligated to report
16 information regarding a health care provider to the provider's respective licensing board solely because the
17 health care provider who is a participant in a professional program to the provider's respective licensing board.
18 A person or entity may report information regarding a health care provider to the provider's respective licensing
19 board if the person or entity reasonably and in good faith has determined that the participant is not competent
20 to continue in practice or is a danger to themselves or to the health and welfare of their patients or the public.
21 (2) Every member of, or a health care professional consultant to, any committee, board, group,
22 commission, or other entity that functions primarily to review, evaluate, or make recommendations on a A
23 professional program is and its agents are immune from civil liability for any act, decisions decision, omission,
24 or utterance done or made in performance of the professional program the member's or health care
25 professional consultant's duties while serving as a member of or as a health care professional consultant to the
26 committee, board, group, commission, or other entity. An active participant in a professional program may not
27 be employed or engaged by the professional program or have a financial ownership interest in the professional

1 program.
2 (3) (a) The proceedings, minutes, records, reports, analyses, findings, conclusions,
3 recommendations, and deliberative process, including oral or written opinions and reports, of a professional
4 program originating in or provided to the professional program are not subject to discovery or introduction into
5 evidence in any civil action unless a court of competent jurisdiction, after a hearing in camera, determines that
6 the evidence is not otherwise available and that extraordinary circumstances exist such that the need for the
7 evidence substantially outweighs the interest in protecting the evidence from disclosure and orders the
8 disclosure of the proceedings, minutes, records, reports, or communications information. The information is not
9 admissible in a licensing action unless the office of administrative hearings determines, after in an in camera
10 review, that the factors of public safety and health outweigh the individual right of privacy of the licensee subject
11 to the licensing action.
12 (b) A person involved in the work of a professional program may not be questioned as a witness in
13 a civil action regarding the person's knowledge of any factual information regarding specific patient health care
14 or treatment by virtue of the person's involvement in the professional program.
15 (c) Exchange of information between professional programs does not constitute a waiver of any
16 privilege provided in this subsection (3).
17 (4) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
18 (a) "Board" has the same meaning as provided in 37-1-302. The term specifically includes means
19 the board of medical examiners established in 2-15-1731, the board of dentistry established in 2-15-1732, the
20 board of pharmacy established in 2-15-1733, the board of nursing established in 2-15-1734, and the board of
21 behavioral health established in 2-15-1744.
22 (b) "Health care provider" means:
23 (i) a physician, physician assistant, dentist, mental health professional, behavioral health
24 professional as defined in 37-39-102, pharmacist, or nurse licensed, registered, or certified under Title 37; or
25 (ii) students enrolled in a school of medicine, osteopathic medicine, physician assistant medicine,
26 nursing, dentistry, or pharmacy or in a behavioral health or mental health educational program.
27 (c) "Professional program" means a program created to address issues related to career fatigue

8 Section 2. Section 37-3-401, MCA, is amended to read:
9 "37-3-401. Report of incompetence or unprofessional conduct. (1) Notwithstanding Except as
10 provided in [section 1], notwithstanding any provision of state law dealing with confidentiality, each licensed
11 physician, professional standards review organization, and the Montana medical association or any component
12 society of the association shall and any other person may report to the board any information that the physician,
13 organization, association, society, or person has that appears to show that a physician is:
14 (a) medically incompetent;
15 (b) mentally or physically unable to safely engage in the practice of medicine; or
16 (c) guilty of unprofessional conduct.
17 (2) (a) Information that relates to possible physical or mental impairment connected to habitual
18 intemperance or excessive use of addictive drugs, alcohol, or any other drug or substance by a licensee or to
19 other mental or chronic physical illness of a licensee may be reported to the appropriate personnel of the
20 medical assistance program established by the board under 37-3-203, in lieu of reporting directly to the board.
21 (b) The medical assistance program personnel referred to in subsection (2)(a) shall report to the
22 board the identity of a licensee and all facts and documentation in their possession if:
23 (i) the licensee fails or refuses to:
24 (A) comply with a reasonable request that the licensee undergo a mental, physical, or chemical
25 dependency evaluation or a combination of evaluations;
26 (B) undergo a reasonable course of recommended treatment, including reasonable aftercare;
27 (C) satisfactorily complete a reasonable evaluation, a course of treatment, or aftercare;
