12 Section 1. Section 41-3-205, MCA, is amended to read:
13 "41-3-205. Confidentiality -- disclosure exceptions. (1) The case records of the department and its
14 local affiliate, the local office of public assistance, the county attorney, and the court concerning actions taken
15 under this chapter and all records concerning reports of child abuse and neglect must be kept confidential
16 except as provided by the provisions of 41-3-431 and this section. Except as provided in 41-3-431 and
17 subsections (9) and (10) of this section, a person who purposely or knowingly permits or encourages the
18 unauthorized dissemination of the contents of case records is guilty of a misdemeanor.
19 (2) Records may be disclosed to a court for in camera inspection if relevant to an issue before it.
20 The court may permit public disclosure if it finds disclosure to be necessary for the fair resolution of an issue
21 before it.
22 (3) Records, including case notes, correspondence, evaluations, videotapes, and interviews,
23 unless otherwise protected by this section or unless disclosure of the records is determined to be detrimental to
24 the child or harmful to another person who is a subject of information contained in the records, must, upon
25 request, be disclosed to the following persons or entities in this state and any other state or country:
26 (a) a department, agency, or organization, including a federal agency, military enclave, or Indian
27 tribal organization, that is legally authorized to receive, inspect, or investigate reports of child abuse or neglect
28 and that otherwise meets the disclosure criteria contained in this section;

20 Section 2. Section 41-3-431, MCA, is amended to read:
21 "41-3-431. Discovery procedure. (1) On request of a parent who is a party to the a proceeding under
22 Title 40 or Title 41, the department shall, pursuant to Rules 26 through 37 of the Montana Rules of Civil
23 Procedure, make available for examination and reproduction the following material and information within the
24 department's possession or control:
25 (a) the names, addresses, and statements of all persons who the department may call to provide
26 testimony;
27 (b) all written or oral statements, reports, case notes, correspondence, evaluations, interviews, and
28 documentation produced by the department or in the department's possession that addresses the parent or
- 7 - Authorized Print Version – SB 256
- 2025
69th Legislature 2025 SB0256.2
1 child;
2 (c) all written reports or statements of experts who have personally examined the child or any
3 evidence, together with the results of any physical or psychological examinations;
4 (d) all papers, documents, photographs, videotapes, or tangible objects that the department may
5 use at trial or that were obtained from or purportedly belong to the parent; and
6 (e) all material or information that tends to support, mitigate, or negate the department's case
7 concerning the custody of and parental rights to the child.
8 (2) The department may impose reasonable conditions, including an appropriate stipulation
9 concerning the chain of custody, to protect physical evidence produced under subsection (1)(d).
10 (3) The department's obligation of disclosure extends to material and information in the possession
11 or control of members of the department's staff and of any other persons who have participated in the
12 investigation or evaluation of the a case.
13 (4) On motion showing that the parent has requested discovery relevant to the preparation of the a
14 case for additional material or information not otherwise provided for and that the parent is unable to obtain the
15 substantial equivalent by other means, the court shall order the department or any person to make it available
16 to the parent. The court may, on the request of any person affected by the order, vacate or modify the order if
17 compliance would be unreasonable or oppressive.
18 (5) If at any time during the course of the proceeding it is brought to the attention of the court that a
19 party has failed to comply with any of the provisions of this section or any order issued pursuant to this section,
20 the court may order any remedy that it finds just under the circumstances, including but not limited to:
21 (a) ordering disclosure of the information not previously disclosed;
22 (b) granting a continuance;
23 (c) holding a witness, party, or counsel in contempt for an intentional violation; or
24 (d) precluding a party from calling a witness, offering evidence, or raising a defense not disclosed.
25 (6) The identity of any person who reported or provided information on an alleged child abuse or
26 neglect incident is protected from disclosure as provided under 41-3-205.
27 (7) Any materials furnished to an attorney under this section may not be disclosed to the public but
28 may be disclosed to others only to the extent necessary for the proper conduct of the case.
