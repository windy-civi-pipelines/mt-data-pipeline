4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT SUBMITTING TO THE QUALIFIED ELECTORS OF MONTANA AN
5 AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE VII, SECTION 8, OF THE MONTANA CONSTITUTION TO REVISE THE
6 METHOD OF SELECTION FOR JUSTICES OF THE MONTANA SUPREME COURT; AND PROVIDING AN
7 EFFECTIVE DATE AND AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 Section 1. Article VII, section 8, of The Constitution of the State of Montana is amended to read:
12 "Section 8. Selection. (1) Supreme court justices and district court judges shall be elected by the
13 qualified electors as provided by law shall be selected as provided in subsection (3).
14 (2) District court judges shall be elected by the qualified electors as provided by law.
15 (3) (a) Supreme court justices shall be appointed by the governor from a list of nominees provided
16 by a nomination committee consisting of the president of the senate, the speaker of the house of
17 representatives, and the minority leaders of each house and two current or former attorneys. The two attorneys
18 must be selected by the first four members, with the majority and minority party members of the committee
19 each selecting one attorney to serve on the committee.
20 (b) For a vacancy in the office of supreme court justice caused by the end of the justice's term, the
21 nomination committee shall provide to the governor the names of at least three but no more than five qualified
22 nominees at least 60 days prior to the end of the justice's term. The governor shall appoint a justice from those
23 names within 60 days after receiving the names. If the nomination committee fails to provide at least three
24 qualified nominees by the required date, the governor shall appoint an individual from a list of 10 nominees
25 chosen by lottery from a full list of qualified nominees. The full list of qualified nominees must be created using
26 a method determined by the nomination committee.
27 (c) For a vacancy in the office of supreme court justice caused by any other reason, the
28 nomination committee shall provide to the governor the names of at least three but no more than five qualified

1 nominees within 60 days after receiving notification of the vacancy from the chief justice of the supreme court.
2 The governor shall appoint a justice from those names within 60 days after receiving the names. If the
3 nomination committee fails to provide at least three qualified nominees by the required date, the governor shall
4 appoint an individual from a list of 10 nominees chosen by lottery from a full list of qualified nominees. The full
5 list of qualified nominees must be created using a method determined by the nomination committee.
6 (d) Appointments of supreme court justices made under this subsection (3) shall be subject to
7 confirmation by the senate, as provided in law.
8 (e) A supreme court justice shall serve until the expiration of the term for which the justice was
9 appointed or until the expiration of the term of the justice's predecessor if the appointment was to fill an
10 unexpired term.
11 (2) (4) For any vacancy in the office of supreme court justice or district court judge, the governor shall
12 appoint a replacement from nominees selected in the manner provided by law. If the governor fails to appoint
13 within thirty days after receipt of nominees, the chief justice or acting chief justice shall make the appointment
14 from the same nominees within thirty days of the governor's failure to appoint. Appointments made under this
15 subsection shall be subject to confirmation by the senate, as provided by law. If the appointee is not confirmed,
16 the office shall be vacant and a replacement shall be made under the procedures provided for in this section.
17 The appointee shall serve until the election for the office as provided by law and until a successor is elected
18 and qualified. The person elected or retained at the election shall serve until the expiration of the term for which
19 his predecessor was elected. No appointee, whether confirmed or unconfirmed, shall serve past the term of his
20 predecessor without standing for election.
21 (3) (5) If an incumbent files for election for the office of district court judge and there is no election
22 contest for the office, the name of the incumbent district court judge shall nevertheless be placed on the general
23 election ballot to allow the voters of the state or district to approve or reject him. If an incumbent district court
24 judge is rejected, the vacancy in the office for which the election was held shall be filled as provided in
25 subsection (2) (4)."
26
27 NEW SECTION. Section 2. Two-thirds vote required. Because [section 1] is a legislative proposal
28 to amend the constitution, Article XIV, section 8, of the Montana constitution requires an affirmative roll call vote
