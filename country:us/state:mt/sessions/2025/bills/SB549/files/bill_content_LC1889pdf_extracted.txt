4 A BILL FOR AN ACT ENTITLED: “AN ACT PROVIDING AN INCOME TAX BENEFIT FOR PRIMARY AND
5 SECONDARY EDUCATION EXPENSES; ESTABLISHING AN INCOME TAX CREDIT FOR CERTAIN
6 PRIMARY AND SECONDARY EDUCATION EXPENSES; PROVIDING DEFINITIONS; AMENDING SECTION
7 15-30-2303, MCA; AND PROVIDING AN APPLICABILITY DATE.”
8
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MONTANA:
10
11 NEW SECTION. Section 1. Primary and secondary education expenses credit -- definitions. (1)
12 There is allowed a credit against the tax imposed by 15-30-2103 for education expenses that are paid by a
13 parent, guardian, or teacher as provided in this section.
14 (2) The credit is equal to the lesser of the amount paid for education expenses or $1,250.
15 (3) If the amount of the credit exceeds the claimant's liability under this chapter, the amount of the
16 excess must be refunded to the claimant. The credit may be claimed even if the claimant has no taxable
17 income under this chapter.
18 (4) Pursuant to 5-4-104, the legislature finds that the purpose of the tax credit provided for in this
19 section is to assist parents, guardians, and teachers with the increasing costs of providing a primary and
20 secondary education to a child.
21 (5) As used in this section, the following definitions apply:
22 (a) "Education expenses" means the following expenses for the benefit of an individual to receive
23 an education from an education provider:
24 (i) school tuition charged by an education provider, fees, textbooks, software, or other
25 instructional materials or services;
26 (ii) an educational program or course using electronic or offsite delivery methods, including but not
27 limited to tutoring, distance learning programs, online programs, and technology delivered learning programs;
28 (iii) curriculum, including supplemental materials necessary for the curriculum;

1 (iv) tutoring;
2 (v) educational therapies or services, including but not limited to occupational, behavioral,
3 physical, speech-language, and audiology therapies from licensed or certified practitioners or providers,
4 including licensed or certified paraprofessionals or educational aides;
5 (vi) state or nationally recognized assessment tests, advanced placement exams, entrance
6 examinations at an eligible postsecondary institution, or other assessment instruments;
7 (vii) services provided by a public school in the state, including individual classes and
8 extracurricular activities;
9 (viii) consumable education supplies, such as paper, pens, and markers;
10 (ix) transportation required for another allowable educational service; and
11 (x) fees paid to a cooperative educational program.
12 (b) "Education provider" means an education provider for individuals in kindergarten through 12TH
13 grade in the state that is:
14 (i) a public school;
15 (ii) accredited, has applied for accreditation, or is provisionally accredited by a state, regional, or
16 national accreditation organization;
17 (iii) a nonaccredited provider or tutor and has informed the child's parents or legal guardian in
18 writing at the time of enrollment that the provider OR TUTOR is not accredited and is not seeking accreditation; or
19 (iv) a home school that complies with the provisions of 20-5-109.
20 (c) "Teacher" means a person that provides instruction to students that is employed by an
21 education provider. The term includes licensed or certified paraprofessionals or educational aides that are
22 employed by an education provider.

1 (5) The revenue interim committee shall review the tax credits scheduled for review and make
2 recommendations in accordance with 5-11-210 at the conclusion of the full review to the legislature about
3 whether to eliminate or revise the credits. The committee shall also review any tax credit with an expiration date
4 or termination date that is not listed in this section in the biennium before the credit is scheduled to expire or
5 terminate.
6 (6) The revenue interim committee shall review the credits using the following criteria:
7 (a) whether the credit changes taxpayer decisions, including whether the credit rewards decisions
8 that may have been made regardless of the existence of the tax credit;
9 (b) to what extent the credit benefits some taxpayers at the expense of other taxpayers;
10 (c) whether the credit has out-of-state beneficiaries;
11 (d) the timing of costs and benefits of the credit and how long the credit is effective;
12 (e) any adverse impacts of the credit or its elimination and whether the benefits of continuance or
13 elimination outweigh adverse impacts; and
14 (f) the extent to which benefits of the credit affect the larger economy. (Subsection (3)(c)
15 terminates December 31, 2028--sec. 4, Ch. 391, L. 2023; subsection (3)(d) terminates December 31, 2028--
16 sec. 2, Ch. 576, L. 2023; subsection (1)(c) terminates December 31, 2031--sec. 6, Ch. 493, L. 2023.)"
